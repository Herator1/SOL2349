In _SOL 2349_, characters are not defined by supernatural powers, but by **training, equipment, and controlled augmentation**. Technology does not replace skill, but it greatly expands what a character can do and how effectively they can do it.

Implants, genetic modifications, weapons, tools, drones, and consumables all shape how characters operate under pressure. Together, they form one of the central pillars of the system: **specialization through technology, always constrained by risk, limits, and availability**.

## Implants

Implants are the core augmentation system of _SOL 2349_. They are **active systems** integrated into the body and nervous system, designed for short bursts of enhanced capability.

Their use is limited by two key mechanics:

- **[[Load]]** limits how far systems can be pushed in the short term
- **Mod Space** limits how many implants a character can install overall

Implants are tied to distinct operational categories, and each character can only access **two categories**, forcing meaningful specialization.

For details, see [[Implant Overview]].

## Genetic Modifications

Genetic modifications are **passive, permanent adaptations**. Unlike implants, they do not generate Load and do not need to be activated. In exchange, they cannot normally be switched off, removed, or adjusted, and often come with trade-offs.

They represent long-term adaptation rather than active system use.

For details, see [[Genetic Modifications]].

## Equipment

Equipment defines how characters engage with the world on a practical level.

- **Weapons and tactical gear** shape combat style and engagement options
- **Tools and devices** enable technical, medical, and operational tasks
- **Drones and robotics** extend reach, support, and remote presence
- **Consumables** provide short-term advantages at limited supply

In many situations, the right equipment is not a bonus, but a requirement.

## System Limits

Technology in _SOL 2349_ is powerful, but never unlimited.

- **Load** creates immediate pressure and the risk of overload
- **Mod Space** forces long-term build choices
- **Wounds** and **mental strain** limit how much pressure a character can survive
- access to advanced systems is shaped by cost, legality, and availability

Power is never free. Every advantage comes with a constraint, a risk, or a trade-off.

## Design Principle

Augmentations and gear are meant to create **clear technological identities** without turning the game into a class system or a power fantasy. Characters are defined by what systems they rely on, how they combine them, and how far they are willing to push them.

Technology creates opportunity. Judgment determines survival.
