Tools and devices extend a character’s ability to interact with the world. They do not replace [[Skills]], but they often determine **whether an action is possible at all**.

In _SOL 2349_, the right tool can matter just as much as training. Many technical, medical, digital, or EVA-related tasks cannot be attempted properly without suitable equipment.

## Core Rule

Most tools require **1 Action** to use unless stated otherwise.

There are **no bonus actions** in _SOL 2349_. Using a tool always competes with:

- attacking
- moving
- activating implants
- other meaningful actions

A tool may:

- enable an action
- improve an action
- reduce difficulty
- grant **Tactical Edge**
- or make a task safe enough to attempt at all

## Tools and Skills

Tools do not replace skill.

As a rule of thumb:

- the **tool** determines whether the action can be attempted properly
- the **skill** determines how well the character performs it

A trained engineer without tools is limited.  
A character with the right tools but no skill is still unreliable.

## Tool Tags

These tags describe what a tool is primarily used for.

|Tag|Effect|
|---|---|
|**Sensor**|Supports detection, scanning, analysis, or observation|
|**Digital**|Required for hacking, system access, or digital interface work|
|**Engineering**|Required for repair, maintenance, or mechanical intervention|
|**Medical**|Required for diagnosis, treatment, or stabilization|
|**Industrial**|Built for heavy-duty work on structures and materials|
|**Explosive**|Used for forced entry or destructive breaching|
|**EVA**|Designed for safe use in vacuum or hazardous external conditions|
|**Encrypted**|Harder to intercept, spoof, or monitor|
|**Utility**|Broad-purpose gear with general but limited application|

## Tool Requirements

Some actions cannot be performed properly without the correct tool.

Typical examples:

- hacking requires a **Digital** tool
- advanced repair requires **Engineering** tools
- treatment and diagnosis require **Medical** tools
- safe external operation requires **EVA** gear

Without the proper tool:

- the action may be impossible
- may suffer **Setback**
- or may become slower, riskier, or less effective

The GM decides which of these applies based on the situation.

## Field Tools vs Full Kits

**Field tools** are portable and fast, but limited.  
**Full kits** are more capable, but bulkier and slower to use well.

Examples:

- a **Repair Kit** supports quick field fixes
- an **Engineering Toolkit** supports serious repair work
- a **Med Scanner** helps diagnosis quickly
- full treatment still depends on medical gear and skill

This keeps tools practical without turning every carried item into a complete workshop.

## Improvised Weapon Use

Some industrial tools can be used as improvised weapons.

If used this way:

- use an appropriate combat-relevant Attribute and Skill, as the GM judges
- apply the item’s listed weapon profile if it has one elsewhere
- otherwise treat it as an improvised close-range weapon with limited combat efficiency

Industrial tools should be dangerous, but should not outperform purpose-built weapons.

## EVA Dependency

In vacuum or external operations, suitable **EVA** equipment is mandatory.

Without proper EVA gear, a character may suffer:

- **Setback**
- failed task execution
- movement problems
- direct damage
- or even **Wounds** in severe exposure

For full vacuum and exposure rules, see [[Environmental Hazards]] and [[Movement]].
