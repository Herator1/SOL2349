During combat and other high-intensity situations, time is measured in **rounds**.  
One round represents roughly **five seconds** of in-world time.

Within each round, every character gets a turn in initiative order. A character normally has **3 Actions per round**.

## Actions

Actions are the basic unit of activity in _SOL 2349_. A character can spend Actions on:

- movement
- attacks
- abilities
- implants
- interactions
- other significant tasks

There are **no separate bonus actions or reactions**. Everything uses the same Action economy.

Unless a rule says otherwise, each action costs **1 Action**.

## Turns and Unused Actions

A character does not need to spend all of their Actions during their own turn.

Any unused Action may be held and spent **later in the same round**, including outside the character’s turn. These are called **Reactive Actions**.

At the start of a new round, Action counts reset. Unused Actions from the previous round are lost.

## Reactive Actions

A **Reactive Action** is a normal Action that is delayed until a specific event occurs.

Examples:

- dodging an incoming attack
- firing at a target that leaves cover
- activating an implant in response to danger

Reactive Actions follow these rules:

- the triggering event must be clear in the fiction
- the character must still have an unused Action available
- the GM decides whether the action is fast enough to be used reactively
- slow, complex, or preparation-heavy actions cannot usually be used as Reactive Actions

If multiple characters want to react to the same event, the GM decides the order, usually based on logic, timing, or initiative.

A Reactive Action may interrupt the flow of another turn if the trigger justifies it, but the GM has final say on exact timing.

## Action Limit

A character normally has **3 Actions per round**.

Some implants, abilities, or special effects may grant additional Actions. No character can use more than **5 Actions in a single round** unless a rule explicitly says otherwise.

## Diminishing Returns

Repeated use of the same type of action in a single round becomes less effective.  
Each category tracks its own use separately.

This means, for example, that making multiple attacks increases attack penalties, but does not reduce movement distance or increase implant Load unless those actions are also repeated.

### Attacks

|Use|Modifier|
|---|---|
|1st|0|
|2nd|−2|
|3rd|−4|
|4th|−6|
|5th|−8|

### Implants and Abilities

|Use|Additional Cost|
|---|---|
|1st|no additional cost|
|2nd|+1 Load|
|3rd|+2 Load|
|4th|+2 Load|
|5th|+3 Load|

### Movement

|Use|Distance|
|---|---|
|1st|10 m|
|2nd|5 m|
|3rd|3 m|
|4th|3 m|
|5th|3 m|

Each movement action is resolved separately. Unless a rule says otherwise, its distance cannot be pooled together first and split afterward.

### Utility Actions

Some simple interactions do not scale and can be performed without additional penalties beyond their normal Action cost.

Examples include:

- pressing a button
- opening an unlocked door
- picking up a small object
- other brief, low-complexity interactions

The GM decides whether an action counts as a utility action. If it requires precision, stress, timing, or meaningful effort, it is usually **not** a utility action.

## Example: Take Aim

**Take Aim** is a standard action.

- - **1 Action**
- gain **Tactical Edge** on your next ranged attack this round
- Effect: Gain **+2 to your next attack**

The bonus applies only to the next qualifying attack and is lost if not used before the end of the round, unless another rule says otherwise.

## Load and Overuse

Using more actions, especially implants and abilities, can increase [[Load]].

High Load creates mounting risk:

- actions may become less safe or less reliable
- systems may destabilize
- prolonged overuse can create mental or physiological strain

The exact effects are explained in [[Load]].
