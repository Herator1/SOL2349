# Ship Types (Reference Classes)

This section provides **baseline ship classes** used as reference templates for space combat.

These are not specific ship models, but **generic performance profiles** that can be used to:
- build encounters quickly
- benchmark custom ships
- improvise civilian or military vessels during play

Each reference class lists:
- core ship statistics
- hardpoints
- a typical loadout

GMs and players may freely modify these templates.

## Civilian Ships

Most civilian ships are **not built for sustained combat**.

They usually rely on:
- speed
- escorts
- stealth
- compliance
- or simple defensive systems

Civilian vessels rarely carry serious offensive weapons and often mount only limited point defense.

## Military Ships

Military ships are built for:
- patrol
- escort
- interdiction
- missile combat
- or sustained armed conflict

They carry offensive systems, stronger defensive coverage, and better combat-grade sensors.

## Battleship

Battleships are the largest and most heavily armed warships commonly encountered in the Solar System.

They are extremely rare due to:
- cost
- logistical demands
- crew requirements
- political consequences of deployment

Most factions maintain only a few, if any.

Battleships are usually assigned to:
- major orbital shipyards
- planetary defense zones
- key trade hubs
- strategic infrastructure
- fleet command roles

Player characters will almost never command a battleship. In most campaigns, battleships serve as **strategic background elements, major threats, or plot devices**.

Battleships dominate open engagements through reach, defensive depth, and staying power, but are rarely risked casually.
