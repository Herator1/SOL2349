# Body Purity Movements

Body Purity Movements are not a single church or ideology, but a broad spectrum of religious, ethical, and political groups opposed to radical human modification.

All of them share the belief that there must be a limit to how far the human body and mind should be altered. They differ sharply, however, in why they believe this and in how far they are willing to act on it.

## Internal Spectrum

These movements exist on two major axes.

The first runs from **spiritual and theological purity** to **pragmatic and socio-economic resistance**. Some groups oppose heavy augmentation because they believe the human body is sacred. Others are less concerned with holiness than with coercion, labor pressure, inequality, and dependence on corporate biotech systems such as those associated with [[Shukra]].

The second runs from **personal conviction and communal withdrawal** to **activism, sabotage, and violent extremism**. Many purity communities simply refuse modification for themselves. Others campaign against augmentation culture in public life. A radical minority justifies direct action against enhancement infrastructure and those who promote it.

## Major Currents

### Sacred Body Traditions

The spiritual wing of the purity spectrum. These groups regard the human body as something that should not be fundamentally redesigned beyond necessity. They often accept ordinary medicine and even limited restorative intervention, but reject enhancement, deep neural integration, germline redesign, and identity-altering modification.

Most are strict, conservative, and separatist rather than openly violent.

### Human Integrity Movements

The socio-political wing. These groups argue that enhancement under current Solar System conditions is rarely a free choice. Instead, it creates new forms of coercion, class division, labor pressure, and permanent dependence on corporate infrastructure.

They are especially critical of augmentation as an employment expectation, of proprietary implant ecosystems, and of the emergence of modified elites insulated from ordinary human limits.

Many of these movements are politically active and sometimes influential well beyond explicitly religious circles.

### Purity Fronts

The radical fringe of the spectrum. These groups believe that modification culture has already crossed into corruption and that stronger action is justified. Some remain at the level of intimidation, sabotage, or militant protest. Others cross into terrorism.

They are a minority even within Body Purity Movements, but their actions shape the public reputation of the whole spectrum.
