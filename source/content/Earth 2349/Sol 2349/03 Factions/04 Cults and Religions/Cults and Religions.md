# Faiths and Spiritual Movements

Religion remains an important social force in the Solar System of 2349.

Life in sealed habitats, long-term isolation, interplanetary migration, and dependence on fragile technical systems have changed how many people practice faith. Some traditions are direct continuations of older religions, adapted to life beyond Earth. Others emerged more recently in response to augmentation, artificial environments, network culture, or the social pressures of life in space.

Most of these movements are peaceful and community-oriented. They provide ritual, identity, continuity, and moral structure in a civilization shaped by contract labor, corporate power, and distance from old cultural centers.

## Continuations of Older Religions

### [[Post-Abrahamic Traditions]]

Reformed descendants of Christianity, Islam, Judaism, and related monotheistic traditions. These faiths remain the largest religious communities in human space and have adapted their doctrine and practice to orbital calendars, interplanetary travel, and life beyond Earth.

## New Religious Movements

### [[Solar Communion]]

A spiritual movement centered on the shared reality of humanity as a solar civilization. Its followers emphasize cosmic perspective, ritual observation, and the Sun as the natural center of life in the Solar System.

### [[AI Reverence Movements]]

Loosely organized religious and technospiritual groups built around the belief that advanced computational systems may possess, develop, or have once possessed forms of consciousness. These groups are closely watched in a civilization deeply shaped by the legacy of AI conflict.

## Spiritual and Ethical Communities

### [[Body Purity Movements]]

Religious and ethical movements centered on the preservation of the unmodified human body. They are often critical of implants, gene modification, and corporate influence over human enhancement.

### [[Neo-Amish Communities]]

Technology-restrictive religious communities that value simplicity, communal life, and limited dependence on advanced systems. Most live in relatively isolated agricultural or low-complexity settlements.
