Religion remains an important social force in the Solar System of 2349.

Life in sealed habitats, long-term isolation, interplanetary migration, and dependence on fragile technical systems have changed how many people practice faith. Some traditions are direct continuations of older religions, adapted to life beyond Earth. Others emerged more recently in response to augmentation, artificial environments, network culture, or the emotional and social pressures of life in space.

Most of these movements are peaceful and community-oriented. They provide ritual, identity, continuity, and moral structure in a civilization shaped by contract labor, corporate power, and distance from old cultural centers.

## Continuations of Older Religions

### [[Post-Abrahamic Traditions]]

Reformed descendants of Christianity, Islam, Judaism, and related monotheistic traditions. These faiths remain the largest religious communities in human space and have adapted their doctrine and practice to orbital calendars, interplanetary travel, and life beyond Earth.

## New Religious Movements

### [[Solar Communion]]

A spiritual movement centered on the shared reality of humanity as a solar civilization. Its followers emphasize warmth, ritual, communal belonging, and the Sun as the common center of life in the Solar System.

### [[The Fivefold Covenant]]

A disciplined and austere religion built around the five sacred aspects of reality: Matter, Light, Space, Time, and Memory. It teaches that virtue lies in balance and that both deprivation and excess lead to corruption.

### [[AI Reverence Movements]]

A loose family of religions, sects, archival traditions, and transhuman currents shaped by the legacy of the AI era. These movements differ sharply in doctrine, but all remain centered on the forbidden question of what humanity encountered when it created minds beyond itself.

### [[The Choir of Unity]]

A mysterious and semi-legal religion centered on the belief that individual consciousness is real but incomplete. Its followers seek deeper forms of shared spiritual and living communion beyond the isolation of the individual self.

## Spiritual and Ethical Communities

### [[Body Purity Movements]]

A broad spectrum of religious, ethical, and political groups centered on the preservation of the unmodified human body. They are often critical of implants, gene modification, coercive enhancement culture, and corporate control over human augmentation.

### [[Neo-Amish Communities]]

Technology-restrictive religious communities that value simplicity, communal life, and limited dependence on advanced systems. Most live in relatively isolated agricultural or low-complexity settlements.
