Medicine in **SOL 2349** is advanced, routine, and deeply integrated into ordinary life.

In a civilization built on stations, ships, controlled habitats, and long-distance work, medicine cannot remain a rare specialist service reserved for emergencies. Basic care, diagnostics, preventive treatment, and mental healthcare are part of normal infrastructure across most of human space. Every serious station has medical staff. Every long-term mission carries trained medical personnel. In space, people are too valuable, isolation is too real, and evacuation is too unreliable for anything else to work.

That does not mean all care is equal. Routine treatment is widely available, but the best outcomes are not. Once medicine moves into life extension, premium recovery, elite nanomedical intervention, or top-tier body replacement, money, location, and institutional backing begin to matter much more.

## Regional Differences

Medical inequality in 2349 is real, but it does not follow a simple center-versus-frontier pattern.

Poor regions on [[Earth]] can have worse access than many stations. [[Mars]] and [[Venus]] both support highly advanced medicine, but unevenly. The [[Asteroid Belt]] varies heavily by station and posting. The Jovian sphere is known for combining high medical capacity with unusually broad access to advanced treatment.

In most of human space, the question is not whether medicine exists, but what level of recovery, adaptation, and long-term care a person can actually reach.
