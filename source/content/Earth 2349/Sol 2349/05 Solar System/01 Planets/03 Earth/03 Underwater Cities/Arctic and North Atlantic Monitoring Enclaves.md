The **Arctic and North Atlantic Monitoring Enclaves** are not cities and not major permanent settlements. They are a sparse network of **small research stations, sensor platforms, subsea monitoring posts, and cold-water technical outposts** spread across the northern Atlantic and Arctic maritime regions.

Their main purpose is limited and highly specialized: **climate observation, seabed monitoring, ocean-system research, and strategic sensing** in some of the most environmentally sensitive waters on Earth. Most sites are lightly staffed, heavily automated, and only support small rotating crews of researchers, engineers, and systems personnel.

What makes them distinctive is their atmosphere. Unlike the more industrial underwater zones, these enclaves are quiet, isolated, and often located far from dense human infrastructure. Some are tied indirectly to the sparse inhabited pockets of the [[Rewilded North]], but most exist primarily as technical observation points rather than places people truly live.

They are among the loneliest inhabited sites on Earth—  
small, cold, and surrounded by water that still feels far less known than the land above it.
