The major planets of the Solar System form the **anchor points of human civilization** in **SOL 2349**. They structure trade, population distribution, political influence, and long-distance transport across the system. While the inner planets—especially [[Earth]], [[Mars]], and [[Venus]]—remain the main centers of population, industry, and scientific development, the outer systems provide strategic resources, energy, and industrial reach.

Planets function as hubs of governance, culture, logistics, and large-scale infrastructure. Together with their moons, orbital installations, and surrounding transport networks, they shape the movement of goods, people, and information across interplanetary space.
