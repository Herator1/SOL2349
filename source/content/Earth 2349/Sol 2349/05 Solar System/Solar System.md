In **SOL 2349**, the Solar System has become humanity’s extended home. Over the past centuries, permanent settlements, industrial infrastructure, and research installations have spread far beyond [[Earth]]. Planets, moons, asteroids, orbital habitats, and transfer hubs are linked by transport networks, energy systems, and the logistical frameworks that hold interplanetary civilization together.  
  
Human presence varies enormously across the system. [[Earth]] remains the largest population center, while [[Mars]] has become a major center of science, agriculture, and planetary governance. Beyond the major worlds, much of civilization depends on **orbital stations, mining bodies, transfer infrastructure, industrial outposts, and remote research installations** operating under hostile conditions.  
  
This section provides an overview of the **major regions and locations of the Solar System in 2349**—from inhabited planets and Belt habitats to orbital hubs, Lagrange infrastructure, and the thin frontier of the outer system.
