# Energy and Drive Systems

In *SOL 2349*, technology is not built around convenience. It is built around survival, endurance, and control under extreme conditions.

Fusion powers the Solar System. It sustains habitats, industrial infrastructure, and long-range travel, but it does so at high cost and under constant constraint. Ships do not move because propulsion is easy. They move because civilization depends on systems that operate near thermal, material, and operational limits.

Drive systems, reactors, heat control, and orbital mechanics are therefore not background details. They shape trade, travel times, ship design, maintenance culture, and the everyday risks of life in space. A vessel under thrust is a managed hazard. A damaged power system is often a death sentence. Even routine movement through the Solar System depends on careful planning, strict tolerances, and constant compromise.

This section covers the technical logic behind energy generation, propulsion, and ship movement in the year 2349.
