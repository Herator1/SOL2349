## 🧭 Notes

Idris is strongest when he can **control space and tempo**.

- excels at holding chokepoints and protecting others
- strongest when fights become short, direct, and positional
- can switch cleanly between ranged pressure and close control
- built to stop escalation physically, not to chase kills

He is not subtle, and he is not socially flexible. But when a corridor turns into a problem, he is exactly the kind of person you want standing in it.

## 📖 Optional Background Lore

Idris Halvorsen grew up in the kind of places where structure failure was never abstract. Jovian industrial habitats taught him early that systems do not collapse all at once. They drift, strain, compensate, and finally fail exactly where someone decided not to reinforce them.

Security work came naturally after that. Not because he liked authority, but because he understood pressure. Freight corridors, loading hubs, contract enforcement, access control — all of it was the same lesson in different uniforms. Problems were rarely solved by aggression alone. Most were solved by standing in the right place, reading the room early, and making the next step expensive for whoever wanted to push it.

Aphrodite Prime unsettles him for a reason he would never phrase cleanly: it hides strain too well. In the Jovian habitats, failure looked like failure. Here, it looks like calm.

Players stepping into Idris should focus on three things:

- keep your cool
- take space deliberately
- treat every environment like it can turn unstable faster than the briefing claims

He is not built to be flashy.

He is built to be there when things go wrong.
