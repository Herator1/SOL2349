## 🖧 System Interface Node

**Cat:** Operations  
**Type:** Neural / Interface  
**mSpace:** 2

A neural control implant that allows Elias to interact with nearby systems as if he were already plugged in.

### Remote Interface

**Type:** Passive

You may interact with a connected or exposed system within **20 m** if you have:

- line of sight
- a valid access point
- or a clear local interface path

This allows you to:

- open or close doors
- trigger panels
- cycle locks
- operate cameras
- activate simple mechanisms

This does **not** bypass security by itself.

### Fast Interface

**Type:** Augmented  
**Load:** +1  
**Limit:** 1× per round

Perform one simple system interaction **without spending an Action**.

This may be used on your turn or reactively if a valid trigger exists.

### Extended Control

**Type:** Active  
**Cost:** 1 Action  
**Load:** +2

Take control of one simple connected system within range until the start of your next turn.

Typical examples:

- a door
- a camera
- a lift control
- a local mechanism
- a turret or sensor on standby, if lightly secured

## 🧭 Notes

Elias is built around **information control**.

He is strongest when he can:

- operate systems remotely
- shape the flow of a tense scene
- disrupt enemy action before it happens
- support allies through coordination and pressure rather than direct violence

He is not a frontline combatant. He is most effective when he can stay one step removed from the fight and control what other people believe, notice, or can access.

## 📖 Optional Background Lore

Elias Varga did not come aboard the _Asterion_ by accident. Officially, he is the ship’s communications and operations specialist — a sensible assignment on a long-range [[Helios]] survey contract. Unofficially, he was placed there to observe, report, and quietly ensure that the mission stayed within parameters that were never fully explained to the rest of the crew.

He is good at this kind of work because he rarely looks important. Elias does not project force. He listens, notices, and stores things away. Systems logs, stress patterns, the exact moment a conversation turns from routine to dangerous — that is his natural terrain.

For a long time, that made him useful to people higher up the chain. But extended field work has a way of eroding clean loyalties. Out in deep space, after enough accidents, enough half-truths, and enough exposure to the gap between official language and material reality, certainty becomes harder to maintain.

On the _Asterion_, Elias is the crew member most likely to understand both what the systems are doing and what the people around him are not saying.

Players who want to step into Elias quickly should focus on three things:

- observe first, speak second
- treat information as leverage
- stay calm when others start reacting emotionally

He is not built to dominate a room by presence alone. He is built to shape what happens in it before anyone realizes he already has.
