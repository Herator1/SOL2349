## Purpose of This Act

Act 1 brings the crew from routine work into a crisis.

The Asterion is almost finished with its survey assignment. Only one asteroid remains on the scan route. What begins as a final routine pass becomes a salvage emergency when the crew detects a damaged signal, discovers a hidden station, and suffers damage from station debris.

By the end of this act, the crew should understand three things:

- the asteroid contains a damaged station
- the Asterion has been hit and cannot safely leave without help
- boarding the station is the most practical path forward

Act 1 should feel technical, grounded, and fast.

---

# Starting Situation

The player characters are aboard the **Asterion**, a small survey vessel operating in the Belt.

They are approaching the final asteroid on their assigned scan route. The mission has been routine so far: approach, scan, classify, record, transmit, move on.

This final target appears unremarkable at first. It is a depleted rock body with old surface irregularities, no clean active transponder, and no obvious settlement profile.

The crew expects to finish the scan and leave.

They are wrong.

# Scene 1 — Routine Survey Pass

## Goal

Let the crew begin as professionals doing their job.

This scene should establish normal operations before the situation turns dangerous.

## What the Crew Can Do

The crew may scan the asteroid, check surface composition, map old excavation scars, analyze thermal data, monitor radio traffic, look for transponders, prepare the final survey package, or compare readings with the mission profile.

Keep this scene short.

It exists to make the disruption feel earned.

## Initial Findings

At first, the asteroid appears mostly uninteresting.

Possible findings include:

- old mining scars
- irregular internal voids
- traces of artificial metal near one side
- faint thermal irregularities
- weak radio noise
- no stable station transponder
- no clear docking beacon

Do not reveal the full station immediately.

The first impression should be:

**Something is wrong with this asteroid, but the shape of the problem is unclear.**

## Useful Checks

Use any appropriate system or skill names from your rules.

Examples:

- **Operations / Sensors:** detect non-natural geometry or artificial heat
- **Science:** identify hollowed structure and old mining traces
- **Engineering:** recognize industrial material signatures
- **Spacefaring:** judge safe approach vectors
- **Communication / Operations:** isolate the weak signal

Failure should not block the scenario.

It should cost time, worsen uncertainty, degrade the scan quality, or lead the crew closer before they fully understand the risk.

# Scene 2 — The Corrupted Signal

## Goal

Introduce the station through a damaged technical signal.

The signal comes from the station’s crippled communication array. It cannot send a clean message.

## Signal Fragments

Use short fragments. Do not over-explain.

Examples:

text
...station identifier corrupted...
...emergency channel active...
...dock control not responding...
...pressure loss in spine sector...
...repeat, do not approach without vector correction...
...transfer interrupted...
...crew status unknown...
...array damage severe...

Optional audio fragment:

—not a raid, they’re still inside—  
—seal the spine—  
—container is not on the—  
—repeat, do not trust—

Cut the fragment before it answers too much.

## What the Crew Can Learn

With analysis, the crew can determine:

- the signal is artificial
- it is coming from inside or near the asteroid
- the communication array is damaged
- the transmission is repeating automatically
- some fragments are recent
- location data is inconsistent because the array is damaged
- the source is probably a station or large embedded structure

## Tone

The signal should feel like broken infrastructure, not horror.

It is not a ghost voice.  
It is not an alien message.  
It is a damaged station trying and failing to communicate.

# Scene 3 — The Station Revealed

## Goal

The crew realizes the asteroid contains a station.

As they refine scans, the artificial structure becomes clearer: a hollowed asteroid body with a damaged **Main Docking Spine** protruding from it.

The station is not a clean facility. It is an old industrial retrofit built inside a mined-out asteroid.

## Description

Use or adapt:

> The asteroid profile changes as the sensor model updates.
> 
> The rock is hollow.
> 
> Not naturally fractured — mined out.
> 
> Inside the cavity, the scans pick up pressure structures, old power routing, cargo rails, and a large rotating mass still moving inside the rock.
> 
> A long industrial spine protrudes from one side of the asteroid, half-buried in shadow and damaged framework.
> 
> It is not a settlement beacon.
> 
> It is a station.
> 
> Hidden, damaged, and still partially alive.

## What Is Visible

The crew can identify:

- the asteroid was hollowed by mining
- the main station lies inside the old cavity
- the Main Docking Spine protrudes from the rock
- the communication array is damaged
- limited debris surrounds the approach path
- parts of the station still have heat and power
- an internal rotating structure is still moving, but not smoothly

The station’s internal layout should remain unclear.

The **Secondary Spine** and docked freighter should not be clearly visible from the first approach angle. At most, careful scans may detect a secondary mass shadow, an intermittent heat source, or ambiguous docking hardware deeper in the structure.

Do not identify it as a freighter yet unless the crew earns that information through strong scanning, a changed angle, or later station records.

## What They Should Not Know Yet

The crew should not yet know:

- who lives there
- what caused the fighting
- who Arman Volkov or Mira Volkov are
- that the station split into Holdouts and Breakaway Crew
- where the Secondary Spine leads
- whether a docked freighter is usable
- what the container is
- where the artifact is
- whether anyone inside can be trusted

# Scene 4 — The Debris Strike

## Goal

Damage the Asterion and force the crew into the scenario.

The debris strike should come from the station’s wreckage field, not from random natural rock or an enemy attack.

The debris field should be limited but dangerous: enough to threaten the Asterion and complicate approach, not so dense that the station looks buried in wreckage.

## Trigger

The strike can happen when the crew:

- approaches for a better scan
- attempts a docking vector
- maneuvers around the asteroid
- launches a drone
- aligns with the damaged signal source
- tries to confirm the artificial structure
- delays too long near the debris field

The GM should make the danger visible before impact: sensor clutter, moving fragments, unreliable beacon data, broken range estimates, or drifting material coming out of the asteroid’s shadow.

## Read-Aloud

Use or adapt:

> The debris warning flashes too late.
> 
> A cluster of station fragments comes out of the asteroid’s shadow: plating, cable, something that might once have been part of a docking frame.
> 
> The Asterion rolls hard.
> 
> Something hits the hull with a sound that travels through the ship like a struck bell.
> 
> For one second, every system display becomes noise.
> 
> Then the damage alarms start.

## Damage Options

Choose two or three consequences. Do not disable everything.

|System|Effect|
|---|---|
|Maneuvering|Departure burn becomes unsafe without repair or recalibration.|
|Sensors|External scans are degraded; close inspection becomes harder.|
|Communications|Long-range transmission is unreliable.|
|Radiators|High-power operation risks overheating.|
|Docking Hardware|Automated docking is unsafe; manual or EVA assistance is needed.|
|Hull Integrity|One section requires patching before hard maneuvers.|
|Power Routing|Some ship systems cannot run together without overload.|
|Navigation Alignment|Departure trajectory cannot be trusted without recalibration.|

## Mechanical Use

The exact damage should create choices, not dead ends.

Good damage does one or more of the following:

- makes immediate departure risky
- makes docking harder
- creates a need for station parts or data
- gives Engineering or Spacefaring characters work
- makes time matter
- keeps the Asterion usable but vulnerable

Avoid total ship loss unless you want a much harsher scenario.

---

# Scene 5 — Damage Control

## Goal

Let the crew respond to the crisis and understand their options.

This scene should be tense but controlled. The players should feel that competent action matters.

## Possible Crew Actions

The crew may:

- patch hull damage
- stabilize power routing
- inspect external damage by drone or EVA
- recalculate safe vectors
- identify needed spare parts
- search the station scan profile for compatible systems
- attempt remote contact again
- choose a safer docking path
- prepare boarding equipment

## What They Learn

After damage control, the crew should know:

- the Asterion can survive for now
- leaving immediately is possible only with serious risk, or not practical at all
- repairs or recalibration would greatly improve their chances
- the damaged station may contain compatible parts, docking data, power systems, or shelter
- the debris field makes repeated maneuvering dangerous
- boarding is the best available option

## Optional Partial Discovery

If the crew performs strong scan work here, give them one limited extra clue.

Possible clues:

- a secondary mass signature deeper inside the asteroid cavity
- power draw inconsistent with the visible Main Docking Spine
- cargo-transfer geometry continuing away from the main approach path
- an internal rotating ring with structural arms connected to a central hub
- a faint heat source behind the asteroid shell

Do not label the freighter yet unless the crew has a very strong success.

This clue should create curiosity, not solve the layout.

## Keep the Pressure Practical

Do not frame boarding as heroic adventure.

Frame it as a bad but rational decision.

The crew boards because staying outside is dangerous and leaving without repairs may kill them.

# Scene 6 — Choosing the Approach

## Goal

Let the crew decide how to enter the station.

This choice gives them agency before Act 2 begins.

## Possible Approaches

### Manual Docking

The crew attempts to dock with a damaged collar or emergency port on the Main Docking Spine.

Risk: additional ship damage, unstable clamps, poor seal.

Reward: direct access and easier movement of equipment.

### EVA Entry

The crew crosses from the Asterion to the Main Docking Spine by suit.

Risk: debris, distance, bad lighting, uncertain handholds.

Reward: avoids stressing damaged docking systems.

### Drone Scout First

The crew sends a drone ahead.

Risk: drone loss, limited signal, incomplete information.

Reward: safer route, early layout data, possible airlock access.

### Cargo Intake Route

The crew enters through a cargo handling structure attached to the Main Docking Spine.

Risk: decompression, unstable cargo machinery, unclear pressure zones.

Reward: early access to cargo clues.

### Emergency Airlock

The crew uses an old or damaged emergency lock.

Risk: manual operation, bad atmosphere, jammed cycling.

Reward: safest pressure transition if restored.

## GM Advice

Do not make one option obviously correct.

Each approach should carry a different risk and provide a different first impression of the station.

The crew should not be able to directly board the freighter in Act 1. The freighter is still hidden at the Secondary Spine and should become relevant later through investigation, station records, faction movement, or a changed approach angle.

# Optional Complications

Use one if Act 1 needs more pressure.

## Unstable Signal Burst

The station briefly transmits a clearer fragment before collapsing back into noise.

It may reveal one useful phrase:

...they moved the container...  
...spine pressure failing...  
...do not let her reach the freighter...  
...habitat rotation unstable...

Keep it fragmented. Do not explain who “her” is yet unless you want to foreshadow Mira early.

## Secondary Debris Drift

Another debris cluster begins moving into the Asterion’s safe position. The crew must dock, move, or risk another impact.

## Asterion Heat Problem

Radiator damage forces the crew to reduce power use. They cannot keep all systems active while analyzing the station.

## Bad Docking Beacon

The station’s docking beacon is still active but wrong. Following it blindly leads into dangerous wreckage.

## Movement Inside

A sensor contact appears briefly inside the Main Docking Spine or rotating habitat. It is unclear whether it is a person, loose machinery, or sensor error.

## Hidden Mass Shadow

A scan briefly suggests something large attached deeper in the station structure, but the asteroid shell and damaged systems prevent a clean read.

This should hint at the Secondary Spine or freighter without revealing it clearly.

---

# Act 1 Information Control

The crew should learn enough to act, not enough to solve the scenario.

## They Should Learn

- the final asteroid contains a hidden station
- the station is built into an old hollowed mining body
- the signal comes from damaged station communications
- the debris field comes from the station
- the Main Docking Spine is the visible first access route
- the Asterion is damaged
- the station still has heat, power, and possibly survivors
- boarding is the best practical option

## They Should Not Yet Learn

- the full faction history
- the Volkov family conflict
- the exact cause of the firefight
- the identity of all survivors
- the clear location of the Secondary Spine
- whether the freighter exists or can leave
- the container’s current location
- the full nature of the artifact
- which faction, if any, can be trusted

---

# Transition to Act 2

Act 1 ends when the crew commits to boarding.

Use or adapt:

> The Asterion holds position near the damaged spine.
> 
> Behind you, the ship is alive but wounded.
> 
> Ahead, the station turns slowly inside the hollowed asteroid, its signal still pulsing through static.
> 
> The docking lights flicker once.
> 
> Then the outer hatch indicator changes from red to amber.
> 
> Something inside still has power.

The next act begins with the approach to the Main Docking Spine and the first entry into the station.
