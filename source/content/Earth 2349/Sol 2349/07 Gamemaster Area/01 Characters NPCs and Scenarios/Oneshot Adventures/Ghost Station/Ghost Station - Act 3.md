## Purpose of This Act

Act 3 is not a linear finale.

It is a pressure sandbox.

By this point, the crew understands the basic situation: the Asterion is damaged, the station is failing, Arman Volkov’s Holdouts and Mira Volkov’s Breakaway Crew are still active, and a sealed container connected to the conflict is missing somewhere inside the station.

The crew may also have learned that a freighter is docked at the Secondary Spine, deeper in the station structure.

What happens next depends on the players.

They may search for the container, repair the Asterion, stabilize station systems, negotiate with factions, reach the freighter, rescue survivors, salvage useful equipment, or leave with only partial answers.

The GM’s job is not to push them toward one objective.

The GM’s job is to keep the situation moving.

---

# Starting Situation

Act 3 begins when the crew has enough information to make meaningful choices.

They know the station is unstable.  
They know the factions are involved.  
They know the container matters.  
They know the Asterion cannot be ignored.  
They may know the freighter exists, but not whether it can fly.

They do not yet know what matters most.

That decision belongs to the players.

---

# Core Sandbox Rule

Every major action should do at least one of three things:

1. reveal information
2. change the station state
3. change faction behavior

If the crew spends time, something should shift.

The station is not a static map.  
The factions are not waiting.  
The Asterion is not magically safe.

---

# Main Pressures

Use three pressure tracks during Act 3.

A simple 0–6 clock for each track is enough.

## Asterion Condition

This track shows how safely the crew can leave with their own ship.

It worsens when the crew delays repairs, keeps the ship exposed in the debris field, stresses damaged docking systems, runs unstable power, or allows factions to approach the ship.

It improves when the crew finds compatible parts, repairs key systems, recalibrates navigation, clears a safer departure vector, or retrieves useful station data.

At high pressure, leaving with the Asterion becomes dangerous, partial, or impossible without sacrifice.

## Station Collapse

This track shows how quickly the station is failing.

It worsens when life support degrades, power reroutes fail, pressure zones collapse, the rotating habitat destabilizes, fire or fumes spread, or factions sabotage systems.

It improves when the crew stabilizes life support, restores pressure control, reroutes power, repairs doors or pumps, restarts scrubbers, or stabilizes spin-transfer access.

At high pressure, routes close, survivors die, systems fail, and escape becomes harder.

## Faction Movement

This track shows how actively the factions respond to the crew and each other.

It worsens when the crew is seen, helps one side, approaches the container trail, controls a valuable route, reveals the Asterion can fly, exposes a lie, or makes the Secondary Spine reachable.

It improves when the crew negotiates a temporary deal, repairs a shared survival system, trades useful supplies, creates distance between factions, or gives one side a reason to wait.

At high pressure, factions force action: ambush, ship seizure, sabotage, hostage-taking, betrayal, or a rush toward the container or freighter.

# Player Objectives

The crew may pursue any combination of the following objectives.

They will probably not have time to do all of them.

---

## Repair the Asterion

This is the most practical survival objective.

The crew may need parts, calibration data, radiator patches, maneuvering fixes, docking repairs, power components, or a safe departure vector.

Possible sources include maintenance stores, stripped station systems, cargo crates, faction-controlled workshops, old mining equipment, surviving technicians, or components from the docked freighter.

Complications may include blocked access, faction control, EVA work, incompatible parts, failing power, unstable docking clamps, or a faction realizing that the Asterion is the best escape option.

Success means the crew can leave on their own terms.

Failure may force them toward the freighter, a faction deal, or a risky burn.

---

## Search for the Container

This is the investigation objective.

The container can only be found if the crew actively reconstructs where it went.

Possible investigation paths include:

- restoring damaged video footage
- decoding cargo movement logs
- comparing mass records
- following scraped rails or broken restraints
- tracking sensor glitches
- questioning survivors
- recovering access data from bodies or faction terminals
- inspecting the Secondary Spine transfer route
- checking freighter transfer records
- searching old mining service pockets

The container should not be found through one roll.

Use a chain of evidence. Each clue should narrow the search area, create a new risk, or reveal faction involvement.

Possible final locations include:

- jammed cargo lift
- sealed maintenance bay
- old mining service pocket
- spin-transfer buffer
- Secondary Spine transfer corridor
- decompressed cargo cell

Success means the crew finds the artifact or learns enough to decide what to do about it.

Failure means the container remains missing, or a faction reaches it first.

---

## Stabilize Station Systems

This is the technical and humanitarian objective.

The crew can buy time, save lives, and open new routes by repairing damaged infrastructure.

Useful systems include life support, pressure doors, local power, camera nodes, cargo lifts, spin-transfer machinery, emergency lighting, fire suppression, the comm array, or thermal control.

Stabilizing systems may reduce Station Collapse pressure, open blocked routes, reveal useful logs, earn trust from survivors, delay faction violence, or improve evacuation options.

Complications may include time pressure, faction control, unstable maps, old mining infrastructure, missing parts, bad air, heat, radiation, decompression, or the need to take power from another useful system.

Success changes the map.

Failure makes the station more dangerous.

---

## Deal With Arman’s Holdouts

This is the old-station-order objective.

Arman Volkov and his Holdouts control parts of the rotating habitat, improvised medical space, and some life support access. They are more numerous than Mira’s crew, but less mobile and more dependent on the station staying alive.

Arman may offer:

- habitat access
- old station maps
- access codes
- medical supplies
- spare parts
- technicians
- old mining route knowledge
- permission to move through Holdout-controlled sections

Arman may demand:

- life support repairs
- protection from Mira
- help blocking the Secondary Spine
- proof the crew will not loot and leave
- evacuation for wounded or noncombatants
- the container kept away from Mira

Complications:

- Arman may value the station’s survival over individual lives
- some Holdouts may be hiding sabotage they committed
- helping Arman may make Mira hostile
- Arman may try to control the crew through access to air, doors, or survivors

A deal with Arman should feel useful, heavy, and not entirely clean.

---

## Deal With Mira’s Breakaway Crew

This is the escape-and-leverage objective.

Mira Volkov and the Breakaway Crew are smaller, more mobile, and better positioned around cargo routes, the Secondary Spine, and the freighter berth. They believe the station is dying and that escape matters more than preserving the old order.

Mira may offer:

- cargo route information
- freighter access codes
- repair parts from cargo stores
- armed support
- information about the route break
- a promised share of the container’s value
- help reaching the Secondary Spine

Mira may demand:

- help finding the container
- access to the Asterion
- help reaching or securing the freighter
- protection from Arman’s systems control
- evacuation for selected Breakaway crew
- silence about what happened during the fight

Complications:

- Mira may abandon people outside her crew
- she may betray the crew if the container is found
- helping Mira may doom parts of the station
- the Breakaway Crew may include people who acted without her orders
- she may try to seize the Asterion if the freighter fails

A deal with Mira should feel practical, urgent, and dangerous.

---

## Reach the Freighter

This is the alternate escape and leverage objective.

The freighter is docked at the Secondary Spine / Freighter Berth. It was not obvious from the first approach and becomes relevant only after the crew learns about the secondary docking route.

The freighter may have brought the container, may hold useful cargo logs, and may be more repairable than the Asterion. It is also a valuable prize.

The crew may try to reach it to escape, recover cargo records, find compatible repair parts, inspect the transfer route, deny it to a faction, evacuate survivors, or strip it for components.

Complications may include unstable docking clamps, locked access, traps, faction control, low power, decompressed cargo spaces, damaged flight systems, or the possibility that using it means abandoning the Asterion.

The freighter should be useful, but never simple.

It is not where the artifact automatically waits.

---

## Rescue Survivors

This is the humanitarian objective.

The crew may choose to save wounded, trapped, or noncombatant station survivors.

Survivors may be trapped behind pressure doors, wounded in the rotating habitat, stuck in spin-transfer machinery, held by a faction, hiding in bad air, or carrying key information but unable to move.

Rescue may earn trust, reveal information, reduce faction hostility, provide technical help, or give moral weight to the ending.

Complications may include time, limited air, limited medical supplies, faction resistance, evacuation capacity, or the need to abandon salvage or the container.

Rescue should matter mechanically and morally.

---

## Salvage the Station

This is the pragmatic Belt objective.

The crew may decide that survival and salvage are enough.

Useful salvage may include repair parts, fuel or propellant components, medical supplies, tools, sensor packages, encrypted data, station maps, spare suits, weapons, rare materials, cargo records, or components from the freighter.

Complications may include time, mass, ownership disputes, faction response, unstable zones, and the possibility that some useful systems are keeping people alive.

Salvage should feel practical, not like video-game looting.

---

## Leave

The crew can decide to leave.

This is valid.

They may leave because the station is too dangerous, the Asterion is barely repairable, the factions are unstable, the freighter is not worth the risk, or the container is not worth dying for.

Leaving may mean escaping with partial repairs, using the freighter, abandoning the container, abandoning survivors, transmitting a warning, taking evidence but not the artifact, making a deal, or breaking one.

Do not punish leaving as wrong.

But make the consequences clear.

They may survive without the truth.

# Running the Container Search

The container should feel discoverable, not hidden behind a single secret.

Use a three-step investigation structure.

## Step 1 — Establish the Planned Route

The crew learns where the container was supposed to go.

Possible sources include the cargo manifest, freighter transfer log, station intake record, damaged camera footage, Sera Nox, Pavel Kest, or survivor testimony.

Example reveal:

SEALED CONTAINER ACCEPTED.  
PLANNED ROUTE: FREIGHTER LINK → CARGO HOLDING 3 → SECONDARY TRANSFER.  
TRANSFER STATUS: INTERRUPTED.

## Step 2 — Reveal the Route Break

The crew learns that the container did not follow the planned route.

Possible sources include mass discrepancies, pressure-door logs, access overrides, broken cargo switches, camera blackouts, physical scrape marks, Ilya Ro, Talia Serrano, or faction testimony.

Example reveal:

ROUTE CHANGE AUTHORIZED.  
AUTHORITY STATE: DISPUTED.  
OVERRIDE SOURCE: LOCAL MANUAL INPUT.  
DESTINATION: [CORRUPTED]

## Step 3 — Narrow the Final Location

The crew combines evidence to identify the likely location.

Useful narrowing clues include stronger sensor glitches near one branch, a cargo lift stopped between levels, a survivor who saw the container moved toward maintenance, a pressure log showing one bay sealed after the fighting, physical tracks ending near spin-transfer, a faction secretly guarding an otherwise unimportant area, or a transfer record that never reaches the freighter.

The final reveal should feel earned.

The players should be able to say:  
“That makes sense. We found it because we followed the evidence.”

---

# Faction Behavior

During Act 3, factions should move with purpose.

They may fortify routes, send scouts, demand supplies, hunt the container, try to reach the freighter, threaten survivors, offer false information, offer real information for a price, sabotage systems, attempt to board the Asterion, abandon weaker members, or turn on each other.

Use factions to create pressure, not constant combat.

A faction should act when:

- the crew controls something valuable
- the crew spends too much time
- the crew gets close to the container
- the crew makes a deal with the other side
- the station state changes
- the Secondary Spine becomes reachable
- escape becomes possible

---

# Escalation Events

Use escalation events whenever the pace slows or the crew spends significant time.

## Station Event

A pressure door fails, air quality drops, a fire starts, lights die, a route decompresses, the spin-transfer jams, or the rotating habitat shudders.

## Asterion Event

The ship reports rising heat, unstable power, worsening hull stress, navigation drift, docking strain, or debris movement.

## Faction Event

A patrol appears, a barricade moves, a faction makes an offer, a hostage situation develops, someone tries to reach the Asterion, one side pushes toward the Secondary Spine, or a firefight breaks out elsewhere.

## Freighter Event

The freighter’s docking clamps shift, a lock cycles unexpectedly, emergency power drops, a hidden alarm activates, a faction tries to board it, or Pavel Kest claims the window to undock is closing.

## Information Event

A camera node briefly restores, a corrupted log resolves, a survivor reveals a contradiction, a sensor glitch narrows the container search, or an old map reveals a hidden route.

## Cost Event

A useful route remains open only if the crew sacrifices one of the following:

- time
- air
- power
- parts
- faction trust
- medical supplies
- access to another option

# Possible Endgames

The ending should emerge from what the players prioritized.

## Asterion Escape

The crew repairs the Asterion enough to leave.

They may carry survivors, salvage, evidence, the container, or only themselves.

## Freighter Escape

The crew uses the freighter instead of the Asterion.

This may save them, but it raises hard questions about ownership, capacity, and who gets left behind.

## Container Recovered

The crew finds and removes the container.

This gives them leverage and future consequences. Arman may demand it stay. Mira may pursue it. Buyers may become interested. Authorities may ask questions. The artifact may be worth far more than the crew understands.

## Container Destroyed or Abandoned

The crew decides the artifact is too dangerous, too heavy, too politically toxic, or not worth the risk.

Destroying it may not be simple.

Abandoning it may leave it for someone else.

## Station Partially Stabilized

The crew saves enough infrastructure to keep survivors alive until help arrives or until someone else claims the station.

This may cost them the container, the freighter, or salvage opportunity.

## Arman Victory

Arman’s Holdouts keep control of the habitat, station systems, or the container.

The station may survive longer, but Mira’s people may be trapped, killed, or left behind.

## Mira Victory

Mira’s Breakaway Crew gains access to the freighter, container, Asterion, or escape route.

Some people may get out, but the station and its weaker survivors may be abandoned.

## Both Factions Broken

Neither Arman nor Mira gains control.

The crew may escape with greater freedom, but the remaining survivors face a power vacuum.

## Hard Exit

The crew leaves under fire, during decompression, through debris, with bad repairs, or with only partial information.

They survive, but not cleanly.

---

# Ending Questions

At the end, answer these questions:

- Who escaped?
- What happened to the Asterion?
- Was the freighter used, lost, or left behind?
- Was the container found?
- What happened to the artifact?
- What happened to Arman and the Holdouts?
- What happened to Mira and the Breakaway Crew?
- Which survivors were saved or abandoned?
- What evidence did the crew carry away?
- Who might come looking for them later?

The scenario should end with consequences, not closure.

---

# Final GM Principle

Act 3 is not about solving everything.

It is about choosing what matters before the station, the factions, and the damaged ship remove the choice.
