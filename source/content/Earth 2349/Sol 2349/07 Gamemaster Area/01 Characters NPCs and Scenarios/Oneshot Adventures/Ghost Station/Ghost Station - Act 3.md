# 🧭 Act 3 — Escalation

By the start of Act 3, the players are no longer just investigating Ghost Station. They are part of its internal balance.

They know the station is still inhabited, the two remaining factions are hostile, the failed cargo transfer lies at the center of the crisis, the _Asterion_ cannot simply leave, and the freighter matters. If they found **Talia Serrano**, they also have a witness who ties the freighter directly to the transfer dispute.

Act 3 is a **pressure sandbox** built around three linked problems: the station is getting worse, the factions want incompatible outcomes, and the players need a ship.

---

## Scene Purpose

Act 3 should force real decisions, escalate pressure without railroading, make the ships and relic materially relevant, and drive the scenario toward a believable endgame. At this point, exploration gives way to leverage, repair work, bargaining, and commitment.

---

# ⚙️ Situation Overview

By now, the players should understand that Ghost Station is failing, the **Old Guard** controls the spinward side of the inhabited core, the **Recovery Team** controls the counterspinward side, the **spin-transfer lift** is the unstable link between dead shell and living core, the _Asterion_ remains usable only as shelter and fallback, and the **freighter** is docked elsewhere in the outer shell as the main material objective.

Delay now has costs.

---

# 🌐 Station Layout

Keep the station simple:

|Area|Function|
|---|---|
|**Dock 1**|_Asterion_, fallback shelter, tools, calibration support|
|**Dead Corridors**|dangerous transit space between ships and factions|
|**Spin-Transfer Lift / Threshold**|choke point, meeting point, stand-off zone|
|**Spinward Core**|Old Guard territory|
|**Counterspinward Core**|Recovery Team territory|
|**Dock 2**|freighter, relic, repair parts, proof of the transfer dispute|

This keeps Act 3 readable: the inhabited core is the social center, the freighter is the material center, and the outer shell is the hazardous space between them.

---

# ⏳ Active Pressures

The act moves because several pressures are running at the same time: life support is degrading, power failures are spreading, spin stability is worsening, both factions are watching the players, and every delay makes ship repair harder.

The station will not stabilize on its own. If the players hesitate too long, the environment and the factions start deciding for them.

---

# 🧪 The Relic

The relic is an [[AI Relic]]: a degraded machine-age signal-routing and processing node. It is not an active intelligence, not a weapon, and not mystical. Its value comes from rarity, research potential, deniability, and the legal danger around possession.

It is hidden inside the **freighter**, in a concealed secondary compartment near the secured hold. By Act 3, the freighter should clearly feel like the real center of the conflict.

The relic should feel unusual but not supernatural: compact, dense, engineered, and difficult to identify. Its effects stay subtle — inconsistent sensor readings, slight desynchronization, timing discomfort, and equipment that behaves almost correctly.

---

# 🚀 Escape Options

There are **two real ways out**, and both depend on access, time, and faction pressure.

## Repair the _Asterion_

The _Asterion_ suffered critical drive damage. Its magnetic nozzle containment and alignment system is compromised. The reactor still supports ship systems, but the ship cannot safely perform a full departure burn.

Repairing it requires drive-control parts from the freighter, time for major engineering work, tools and power, and enough freedom from faction interference to finish the job.

This is the **harder but better** option: the players keep their own ship and leave with better long-term odds. If rushed, it still becomes spaceworthy enough to escape, but with real risk.

## Patch the Freighter

The freighter is battered, but its propulsion section is in much better condition. It has structural damage, local hull failures, sensor problems, and control-routing faults, but it is easier to patch into emergency flight than the _Asterion_ is to fully restore.

This requires moderate repair work, structural patching, restored flight control, and limited support from the _Asterion_.

This is the **easier but worse** option: the players leave sooner, but they abandon their own ship and escape in a compromised freighter.

## Repair Logic at a Glance

|Option|Difficulty|Speed|Needs|Result|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**Repair _Asterion_**|Harder|Slower|parts from freighter|keep own ship|
|**Patch freighter**|Easier|Faster|support from _Asterion_|abandon own ship|

Keep this choice visible throughout the act. It should drive movement, negotiation, and conflict.

---

# 👥 Main NPCs

Keep them simple.

|NPC|Wants|Fears|Use in Play|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Elena Markovic**|relic, witness control, extraction|relic disappearing, Talia talking, losing timeline control|pressure, deals, mission logic|
|**Arman Volkov**|keep people alive, deny Recovery Team a clean win, secure survival options|pointless losses, players siding with Markovic, losing the relic cleanly|local authority, bargaining, station-rooted perspective|
|**Talia Serrano**|protection, extraction, not being handed over|being silenced, traded, or abandoned|witness, freighter lead, leverage|

Markovic is calm, precise, and controlled. Volkov is plainspoken, tired, and pragmatic. Talia is frightened, suspicious, and useful in fragments rather than speeches.

---

# 🧩 Talia’s Role

If the players found **Talia**, decide early how she matters. She should not fade into the background.

In Act 3 she is best used as a witness, burden, bargaining asset, or liability. She can confirm that the freighter brought the cargo, that the transfer into station storage failed, that authority broke down during the move, that the freighter crew split, and that the station was still inhabited when the Recovery Team pushed in. She does not know the full origin of the relic or the full corporate chain behind the operation.

If protected, she can push the players toward the freighter, help a little with systems, and make the human cost visible. If exposed, she becomes a target and can turn a tense negotiation into an urgent one.

---

# 📦 Freighter Discovery

The freighter is where suspicion becomes proof.

If the players reach the secured hold, they find a damaged cargo panel on emergency power, a breached transfer frame, a broken containment bracket, two dead freighter crew in vac-suits, and the concealed compartment containing the relic.

The recovered log should be easy to understand:

> **CARGO TRANSFER ABORTED**  
> **DESTINATION: INTERNAL HOLD**  
> **EXTERNAL BUYER CONFIRMATION: PENDING**  
> **COMMAND OVERRIDE: CONTESTED**  
> **SECURED ITEM REMAINS IN HOLD**  
> **LOG END: MANUAL INTERRUPTION / WEAPONS DISCHARGE**

The players should clearly understand that the relic was physically here, that there was an intended outside buyer, that the transfer broke down into armed conflict, and that the relic never reached station storage.

Do not hide that meaning behind hard rolls.

---

# ⚔️ Escalation Triggers

Act 3 works best when escalation feels like reaction, not punishment.

|Trigger|Likely Result|
|---|---|
|**Players start visible ship repair**|both factions push for influence, access, or guarantees|
|**Relic is found, moved, or mentioned**|tension spikes immediately; both sides refocus|
|**Talia changes hands**|witness pressure becomes urgent|
|**Players clearly back one side**|the other closes access and hardens posture|
|**Too much time passes**|environment worsens and routes become less reliable|
|**A lie or theft is exposed**|trust collapses and both sides may treat players as hostile|

Use these to keep the act moving.

---

# 🛠️ High-Value Pressure Points

The most useful things to bargain, fight, or threaten over are the **spin-transfer lift**, the **freighter**, the **_Asterion_**, and one surviving **local systems node**. That is enough.

|Pressure Point|Why It Matters|
|---|---|
|**Spin-Transfer Lift**|clearest choke point and stand-off zone|
|**Freighter**|contains proof, relic, parts, and possible escape route|
|**_Asterion_**|fallback shelter, tools, calibration support|
|**Local Systems Node**|buys time, supports repairs, offers non-combat leverage|

Do not overcomplicate this with too many sub-objectives.

---

# 🧠 Likely Player Approaches

No path should be treated as correct.

|Approach|What It Means|
|---|---|
|**Negotiate**|trade access, repair help, protection, or transport|
|**Choose a Side**|back Old Guard or Recovery Team and accept the consequences|
|**Stay Independent**|focus on ships and avoid deep commitment|
|**Escalate**|threaten, sabotage, attack, or seize key ground|
|**Exploit Chaos**|take the relic, finish a ship, and run|

All of these remain viable as long as the players act decisively.

---

# 🎯 Endgame Triggers

Act 3 tips into endgame once one of these becomes true: one ship becomes launchable, the relic changes hands, the factions openly clash, the threshold fails, or the players commit to a final plan.

At that point, stop broadening the situation. Narrow it.

The final phase should answer three questions:

1. **Which ship leaves, if any?**
    
2. **Who leaves with the players, if anyone?**
    
3. **What happens to the relic?**
    

Those answers determine the ending.

---

# 🧠 GM Guidance

Keep the act visible and concrete. Keep reminding the players of ship condition, route distance, faction posture, and station deterioration. Use specific obstacles: blocked routes, missing parts, demanded transport, failing pressure, armed stand-offs, or unstable access to the freighter.

If the session stalls, advance one pressure: worsen a system, move a faction, expose Talia, force a repair choice, or make the threshold unsafe.

For quick play, remember:

- **Markovic** = control, extraction, secrecy
    
- **Volkov** = survival, people, local authority
    
- **Talia** = witness, freighter link, liability
    

Do not force a preferred ending. Let the players decide, let the factions react, and let the station keep failing.

---

# 🎯 Summary

Act 3 is about pressure, repair, leverage, and consequence.

The station is getting worse, the factions want different outcomes, the ships make the conflict practical, and the relic makes every decision more dangerous.

From here on, what happens should feel earned.
