## 1. Central Spine Service Hatch

**Location:**  
In the damaged service corridor just off the **Central Spine**, near the first cargo-transfer junction. The hatch sits between the Main Docking Spine access route and the deeper station systems.

**What is there:**  
A dark utility junction with a smeared handprint near the manual release wheel, a dropped cargo tether clip, scrape marks along the deck, and a still-functioning maintenance terminal with a cracked display.

The surrounding area shows signs of rushed movement: a cargo rail switch forced manually, warning tape torn away, and pressure-door markings overwritten by hand.

**What the players can recover:**  
A short cached terminal fragment:

> **TRANSFER STATUS:** SUSPENDED  
> **CARGO ACCESS:** LOCKED  
> **AUTHORITY STATE:** DISPUTED  
> **CREW STATUS:** SPLIT  
> **ROUTE CONFIRMATION:** PENDING  
> **NOTE:** Await command resolution before moving sealed cargo.

**What this clue establishes:**

- the conflict was internal, not an outside attack
- the station crisis centered on cargo access
- command authority had already broken down before open violence
- a sealed cargo item existed and was important enough to halt movement
- the route had not yet resolved when the system logged this entry

**GM use:**  
This is the first hard sign that Ghost Station broke over something specific.

It should not reveal where the container is. It should tell the players that cargo movement, authority failure, and the faction split are connected.

## 2. Survivor Hide Pocket

**Location:**  
In a sealed off-axis maintenance recess near the **spin-transfer threshold**, hidden behind a partially removed inspection panel. This area sits close enough to the Central Spine to be reachable early, but far enough from the main corridor that someone could hide there.

**What is there:**  
A cramped maintenance pocket containing one emergency blanket, two empty water pouches, a blood-stained sleeve patch, and **Talia Serrano**, weak but conscious.

A small handheld diagnostic unit nearby shows partial air-quality logs and a corrupted cargo-camera cache.

**What Talia says once the players get her stable enough to talk:**

> “It was supposed to move through the spine. Main transfer, then secondary route.”
> 
> “Arman ordered it locked down. Mira’s people said he was trapping everyone here.”
> 
> “Then the rail switched, the cameras dropped, and people started shooting.”
> 
> “I don’t think it reached the berth. I don’t think anyone knows where it ended up.”

**What this clue establishes:**

- the container was being moved through the Central Spine cargo-transfer system
- Arman’s Holdouts and Mira’s Breakaway Crew were already contesting control
- the route changed during the crisis
- the Secondary Spine / berth was part of the intended or contested route
- the container likely did not reach the freighter
- no single faction has the full truth

**GM use:**  
This is the humanizing clue.

It reframes the container dispute as a station rupture, not a simple theft. It also introduces the Volkov split without turning Talia into an exposition machine.

Talia should be useful but incomplete. She can point toward damaged camera recovery or cargo logs, not solve the search.

## 3. Secondary Transfer Record

**Location:**  
At the entrance to the **Secondary Spine transfer corridor**, before the actual freighter berth. This could be a cargo control alcove, damaged transfer junction, or pressure-door control station.

Do not place this clue inside the freighter hold by default.

**What is there:**  
A damaged cargo control panel beside a broken transfer frame. One pallet cradle is empty. A cargo clamp has been cut open. Two containers nearby are still secured, but the marked sealed slot is vacant.

The local camera node is cracked but not dead. It still stores a few seconds of corrupted footage.

**What the players can recover:**  
A final interrupted transfer record:

> **SEALED CONTAINER ACCEPTED**  
> **PLANNED ROUTE:** CENTRAL SPINE → SECONDARY TRANSFER → FREIGHTER BERTH  
> **TRANSFER STATUS:** INTERRUPTED  
> **ROUTE CHANGE:** MANUAL OVERRIDE  
> **AUTHORITY STATE:** CONTESTED  
> **LAST CONFIRMED POSITION:** SECONDARY TRANSFER APPROACH  
> **FINAL DESTINATION:** UNKNOWN  
> **LOG END:** weapons discharge / camera failure detected

Optional corrupted video frame:

> A sealed container moves along the transfer rail.  
> The frame shakes.  
> Someone in a Breakaway-marked suit reaches for the manual switch.  
> A second figure fires from outside the camera angle.  
> The rail shunts sideways.  
> The image dies before the container exits the frame.

**What this clue establishes:**

- the container was physically present and actively being transferred
- the planned route led toward the Secondary Spine / freighter berth
- the container did not complete that transfer
- a manual route change occurred during weapons fire
- the final container location is still unknown
- the next step is active reconstruction, not “go to the freighter hold”

**GM use:**  
This is the confirming clue.

From this point on, the players should understand that the container is real, central, and missing. They should also understand that the freighter matters, but that the artifact is not simply waiting aboard it.

This clue should push the crew into Act 3 investigation: cargo logs, sensor glitches, survivor testimony, physical traces, and faction pressure.

# Using the Three Clues Together

These clues create a default information path:

1. **Central Spine Service Hatch**  
    The station split over sealed cargo and broken authority.
2. **Survivor Hide Pocket**  
    The container moved through the spine, the Volkov conflict erupted, and the route changed.
3. **Secondary Transfer Record**  
    The container was real, the planned transfer failed, and the final location is unknown.

After all three clues, the players should know enough to make informed decisions:

- repair the Asterion and leave
- search for the container
- reach the freighter
- deal with Arman
- deal with Mira
- stabilize the station
- rescue survivors
- follow the route break deeper into danger

They should not automatically know where the container is.
