# Ghost Station – Flow

## Act 1 – Signal / Damage / Forced Approach
**Beats**
1. Routine aboard the **Asterion**
2. Weak distress signal
3. Approach / uncertainty
4. Debris impact / ship damaged
5. Station becomes the only viable option

**Players should understand**
- The signal is not clean
- The **Asterion** is no longer fully operational
- The station is necessary, not optional

**Triggers**
- Too much routine -> bring in the signal earlier
- Too much discussion -> another system failure aboard the Asterion
- Too much caution -> make it clear: shelter / stabilization / parts are only available there

---

## Act 2 – Entry / Dead Zones / First Contact
**Beats**
1. Docking / EVA entry
2. Dead outer sections
3. Transfer into inhabited areas
4. Signs of violence / fresh activity
5. First contact with **Old Guard** or **Recovery Team**

**Players should understand**
- The station is only partially controlled
- People are still living here
- There was an internal escalation
- At least two groups want incompatible things

**Triggers**
- Exploration drags -> fresh trace / radio fragment / corpse / open service hatch
- Too little pressure -> bad air / flickering power / blocked hatch
- Too much combat instinct -> first armed contacts talk before they shoot

---

## Act 3 – Relic / Factions / Decision
**Beats**
1. The conflict becomes clearer
2. Link between **relic / freighter / mutiny**
3. Factions want incompatible outcomes
4. Station condition worsens
5. Players must choose: side, deceive, negotiate, or flee
6. Final extraction / collapse / deal / loss

**Players should understand**
- The **relic** is the core of the conflict
- Both sides lie or withhold information
- Time makes every option worse

**Triggers**
- Players hesitate -> tick the clock, routes worsen, factions become more active
- Players only want out -> who comes with them, and what do they take?
- Players want a clear moral side -> show hard but understandable motives on both sides

---

## GM Questions for Any Scene
1. What is the immediate pressure?
2. What information is available here?
3. What decision is in front of the players?
4. What happens if they do nothing?

---

## Escalation Clock
Advance the clock when:
- time passes
- players fail
- they move loudly or destructively
- conflict openly escalates

### 1 – Unstable
- comms interference
- flickering lights
- bad air
- factions observing

### 2 – Critical
- local outages
- blocked routes
- slower movement
- rising mistrust

### 3 – Dangerous
- unstable rotation
- difficult navigation
- armed groups secure key areas
- first signs of panic

### 4 – Collapse Window
- sections fail
- fire / decompression / spin stress
- final decisions are forced

---

## 3 Mandatory Reveals
These three things must come out during play:

1. There was a **mutiny / internal break**
2. The **relic is real and valuable**
3. **Ghost Station is a habitat**, not just a hideout

---

## If the Session Stalls
Use one immediately:
- a wounded NPC appears
- a radio call asks for a meeting
- a hatch slams shut
- a data fragment names the relic directly
- an armed group forces a choice
- an environmental failure nearly splits the team


# Ghost Station – GM Cheat Sheet

## Page 2 – NPCs / Factions / Info

# Factions

## Old Guard
Wants to protect people and station.  
Needs time, help, access, and evacuation for some survivors.  
Fears purge, dispossession, and cover-up.

## Recovery Team
Wants to secure the relic, lock down the situation, and keep control.  
Needs access, information, compliance, or cooperation.  
Fears loss of control, leaks, and unpredictable outsiders.

## Asterion Crew
Wants to get out alive, avoid being used, possibly save people, and possibly secure proof, value, or leverage.

---

# Key NPCs

## Elena Markovic – Recovery Team
- calm, controlled, precise
- wants to secure, contain, control
- offers orderly cooperation
- becomes uncompromising under pressure

**Line:**  
“We can solve this in an orderly way. But not forever.”

---

## Arman Volkov – Old Guard
- rough, tired, credible
- wants to protect his people and not surrender the station
- offers local knowledge and context
- sacrifices space before people

**Line:**  
“For you, this is an operation. For us, this is where we live.”

---

## Talia Serrano – Survivor
- cautious, exhausted, opportunistic
- knows the freighter / mutiny / relic connection
- never tells everything at once
- wants to avoid being discarded by either side

**Line:**  
“Everyone is only telling you the part that helps them.”

---

# Motives in Short
- **Markovic:** secure, contain, control
- **Volkov:** hold, protect, avoid dispossession
- **Talia:** survive, avoid becoming expendable
- **Asterion crew:** get out alive without being used blindly

---

# What the Players Can Find

## Info 1 – Data Fragment
“Transfer suspended. Crew split. Cargo remains in secured hold.”

## Info 2 – Station Reality
Living niche, personal belongings, improvised utilities, child drawing  
-> People live here

## Info 3 – Escalation
Barricade, blood trail, sealed doors, bullet marks  
-> This was an internal break, not a clean external attack

---

# Relic at the Table
Do not play it as mystical.  
Do not play it as evil.  
Play it as: **extremely valuable, dangerous, politically sensitive**.

**Early clues**
- damaged manifest
- argument about cargo / hold / transfer / sell it
- unusual containment or transport hardware
- contradictory statements about its value

---

# Truth in 4 Lines
- A **freighter** brought or moved the **relic**
- This caused conflict over ownership, sale, or use
- The conflict escalated into **mutiny / violence / rupture**
- Now survivors, the recovery team, and outsiders are all fighting for control

---

# Emergency Finale
If time is running short:

1. Push the clock to **Collapse Window**
2. Both sides force a decision
3. Players choose:
   - who they leave with
   - who they take with them
   - whether they secure relic / data / proof
4. Run a hard extraction scene and end
