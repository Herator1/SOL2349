## Use at the Table

This page is the fast-reference version of **Ghost Station**.

Use it during play to track the situation, escalate pressure, reveal information, and keep the scenario moving.

Ghost Station is not a script.

It is a failing station, two desperate factions, a damaged ship, and a missing container.

---

# Core Setup

The crew of the **Asterion** is finishing a routine survey route in the Belt.

The final asteroid on the scan list contains a damaged station hidden inside an old hollowed mining body. The station’s communication array is broken and can only transmit corrupted signal fragments.

During approach, the Asterion is struck by debris from the station.

The ship is damaged.

The crew must board the station to find parts, data, shelter, another escape option, or answers.

---

# What Is Really Going On

The asteroid was mined out long ago by independent miner crews and later reused as a hidden station: part salvage depot, part pirate refuge, part black-market transfer point, part failing habitat.

Two pirate factions fought over a sealed container containing an [[AI Relic]] or AI-era technological fragment.

The conflict is also personal.

**Arman Volkov** leads the Holdouts and represents the old station order. He wants to keep the station alive and prevent the container from leaving.

**Mira Volkov**, his daughter, leads the Breakaway Crew. She believes the station is already dying and wants to escape with the container, the freighter, or enough leverage to survive elsewhere.

The fighting damaged the station, crippled the communication array, scattered survivors, and interrupted the container transfer.

The container is now missing.

Nobody knows exactly where it is.

It can only be found through active investigation.

# Major Locations

## Main Docking Spine

First visible access point from the Asterion.

Damaged, exposed, and surrounded by limited debris.

Use for docking, EVA entry, first hazards, and signs of recent fighting.

## Central Spine

Main internal axis and orientation hub.

Connects docking, cargo and transfer systems, maintenance, spin-transfer, old voids, and the later route toward the Secondary Spine.

Use this as the players’ spatial anchor.

## Cargo and Transfer Systems

Integrated along the Central Spine.

Use for manifests, empty cradles, scraped rails, cargo logs, lift failures, route-break clues, and signs that the container moved but did not reach its expected destination.

## Maintenance / Power / Life Support

Where technical work changes the station state.

Use for repairs, pressure control, air quality, power reroutes, camera restoration, and station stabilization.

## Rotating Habitat

Human core of the station.

Use for survivors, medical pressure, Arman’s Holdouts, faction tension, and moral choices.

## Secondary Spine / Freighter Berth

Offset docking structure hidden from first approach.

The docked freighter is here.

Use as a later discovery, alternate escape route, contested objective, and source of cargo records.

## Old Mining Voids

Older, rough, poorly mapped spaces.

Use sparingly for shortcuts, hidden storage, danger, old routes, or a possible container location.

# Factions

## Holdouts — Arman Volkov

**Strength:** 14–18 people  
**Position:** Rotating habitat, medical space, some life support access  
**State:** More numerous, less mobile, low on clean air, medical supplies, and power stability  
**Need:** air, power, medical help, protection from Breakaway movement toward the freighter

**Arman wants:**

- keep the station alive
- protect remaining locals
- stop Mira from taking the container or freighter
- force the crew to treat the station as inhabited, not dead salvage

**Arman can offer:**

- habitat access
- old station knowledge
- partial maps
- old access codes
- medical supplies or technicians
- old mining route knowledge

**Problem:** Arman may value the station’s survival over individual lives.

---

## Breakaway Crew — Mira Volkov

**Strength:** 8–11 people  
**Position:** Cargo areas, Secondary Spine route, freighter approach  
**State:** Smaller, more mobile, less secure air, under pressure to leave  
**Need:** container location, freighter access, parts, power, leverage

**Mira wants:**

- find the container
- secure the freighter or Asterion
- get her people out
- stop Arman from trapping everyone in a dying station

**Mira can offer:**

- cargo route information
- freighter access codes
- repair parts from cargo stores
- armed support
- a share of the container’s value

**Problem:** Mira may abandon people, break promises, or seize leverage if cornered.

---

## Unaligned Survivors

**Strength:** 3–6 people  
**Position:** scattered between faction zones  
**State:** frightened, injured, dependent on whoever controls air and doors  
**Need:** safe air, medical help, evacuation route, protection

Use them to complicate easy choices.

# Pressure Tracks

Use three 0–6 clocks.

Advance one clock whenever the crew spends significant time, fails loudly, forces access, ignores a warning, helps one faction, or pushes deeper into danger.

Reduce a clock when the crew solves a real problem.

## Asterion Condition

Tracks how safely the crew can leave with their own ship.

|Level|State|
|---|---|
|0|Stable after emergency response|
|1|Minor worsening; repairs still straightforward|
|2|One key system unreliable|
|3|Leaving requires repair, recalibration, or risk|
|4|Departure burn dangerous without major fix|
|5|Asterion vulnerable to seizure, overheating, or docking failure|
|6|Asterion cannot leave without sacrifice, tow, or emergency workaround|

Advance when:

- repairs are delayed
- debris shifts near the ship
- damaged docking systems are stressed
- power or heat problems worsen
- a faction approaches the ship

Reduce when:

- parts are found
- navigation is recalibrated
- radiator or maneuvering damage is patched
- safer departure data is recovered
- debris approach is cleared or avoided

---

## Station Collapse

Tracks how quickly the station is failing.

|Level|State|
|---|---|
|0|Bad but stable locally|
|1|Air quality worsens in one zone|
|2|Door, power, or pressure failures become common|
|3|Routes close; repairs require tradeoffs|
|4|Habitat or spin-transfer becomes dangerous|
|5|Survivors begin dying without intervention|
|6|Major collapse, decompression, fire, or system loss|

Advance when:

- life support degrades
- pressure zones fail
- power reroutes overload
- factions sabotage systems
- spin-transfer shudders
- fire, fumes, or decompression spread

Reduce when:

- scrubbers are restored
- pressure control is stabilized
- power is rerouted safely
- doors or pumps are repaired
- spin-transfer is stabilized
- a technician is rescued

---

## Faction Movement

Tracks how aggressively the factions act.

|Level|State|
|---|---|
|0|Factions cautious and scattered|
|1|Scouts observe the crew|
|2|Offers, warnings, or demands begin|
|3|Routes are blocked or guarded|
|4|One faction moves toward ship, freighter, or container|
|5|Hostages, sabotage, ambush, or forced bargain|
|6|Open faction crisis: firefight, betrayal, ship seizure, or rush for escape|

Advance when:

- the crew is seen
- the crew helps one side
- the crew approaches the container trail
- the crew reveals the Asterion may still fly
- the Secondary Spine becomes reachable
- the freighter becomes usable
- lies are exposed
- station conditions worsen

Reduce when:

- a temporary truce is made
- a shared survival system is repaired
- supplies or information are traded
- a faction is delayed, intimidated, or redirected
- survivors are protected in a way both sides value

# Core Reveals

Reveal these through clues, NPCs, logs, hazards, and player investigation.

Do not dump them all at once.

## Reveal 1 — The Signal Comes from a Station

The final asteroid contains a damaged station built into an old hollowed mining body.

## Reveal 2 — The Debris Comes from the Station

The Asterion was hit by station wreckage, not random natural debris.

## Reveal 3 — The Station Is Still Alive

Systems are failing, but people are still present or recently active.

## Reveal 4 — The Conflict Was Internal

The disaster was caused by fighting between Arman’s Holdouts and Mira’s Breakaway Crew.

## Reveal 5 — The Freighter Is Hidden at the Secondary Spine

It is not visible from the first approach. It is docked at an offset berth deeper in the station structure.

## Reveal 6 — A Sealed Container Caused the Break

The container was valuable enough to split the station and trigger violence.

## Reveal 7 — The Container Is Missing

It was moved during the fighting and did not reach the expected destination.

## Reveal 8 — The Artifact Is AI-Era but Not Active AI

It is valuable, dangerous, and politically sensitive, but not a conscious machine.

## Reveal 9 — Leaving Is Valid

The crew can survive without solving everything, but the consequences remain.

# Three Ready-to-Drop Clue Moments

Use these three clues as fixed anchor points during play.

They are not optional variants. They are the default information path of the scenario.

They do not reveal the final container location.  
They establish that the container existed, that it was moved, and that its route broke during the faction split.

## 1. Central Spine Service Hatch

**Location:**  
In the damaged service corridor just off the **Central Spine**, near the first cargo-transfer junction.

**What is there:**  
A dark utility junction with a smeared handprint near the manual release wheel, a dropped cargo tether clip, scrape marks along the deck, and a still-functioning maintenance terminal with a cracked display.

**Recoverable terminal fragment:**

**TRANSFER STATUS:** SUSPENDED  
**CARGO ACCESS:** LOCKED  
**AUTHORITY STATE:** DISPUTED  
**CREW STATUS:** SPLIT  
**ROUTE CONFIRMATION:** PENDING  
**NOTE:** Await command resolution before moving sealed cargo.

**Establishes:**

- the conflict was internal
- the crisis centered on cargo access
- command authority had broken down
- a sealed cargo item existed
- the route had not yet resolved

## 2. Survivor Hide Pocket

**Location:**  
In a sealed off-axis maintenance recess near the **spin-transfer threshold**, hidden behind a partially removed inspection panel.

**What is there:**  
A cramped maintenance pocket containing one emergency blanket, two empty water pouches, a blood-stained sleeve patch, and **Talia Serrano**, weak but conscious.

**Talia says:**

“It was supposed to move through the spine. Main transfer, then secondary route.”

“Arman ordered it locked down. Mira’s people said he was trapping everyone here.”

“Then the rail switched, the cameras dropped, and people started shooting.”

“I don’t think it reached the berth. I don’t think anyone knows where it ended up.”

**Establishes:**

- the container moved through the Central Spine cargo-transfer system
- Arman and Mira were contesting control
- the route changed during the crisis
- the Secondary Spine / berth was part of the intended or contested route
- the container likely did not reach the freighter
- no single faction has the full truth

## 3. Secondary Transfer Record

**Location:**  
At the entrance to the **Secondary Spine transfer corridor**, before the actual freighter berth.

Do not place this clue inside the freighter hold by default.

**What is there:**  
A damaged cargo control panel beside a broken transfer frame. One pallet cradle is empty. A cargo clamp has been cut open. Two containers nearby are still secured, but the marked sealed slot is vacant.

**Recoverable transfer record:**

**SEALED CONTAINER ACCEPTED**  
**PLANNED ROUTE:** CENTRAL SPINE → SECONDARY TRANSFER → FREIGHTER BERTH  
**TRANSFER STATUS:** INTERRUPTED  
**ROUTE CHANGE:** MANUAL OVERRIDE  
**AUTHORITY STATE:** CONTESTED  
**LAST CONFIRMED POSITION:** SECONDARY TRANSFER APPROACH  
**FINAL DESTINATION:** UNKNOWN  
**LOG END:** weapons discharge / camera failure detected

**Optional corrupted video frame:**

A sealed container moves along the transfer rail.  
The frame shakes.  
Someone in a Breakaway-marked suit reaches for the manual switch.  
A second figure fires from outside the camera angle.  
The rail shunts sideways.  
The image dies before the container exits the frame.

**Establishes:**

- the container was physically present and being transferred
- the planned route led toward the Secondary Spine / freighter berth
- the container did not complete that transfer
- a manual route change occurred during weapons fire
- the final location is still unknown
- the next step is active reconstruction

# Container Search

The container should not be found through one roll.

Use a three-step evidence chain.

## Step 1 — Planned Route

The crew learns where the container was supposed to go.

Sources:

- cargo manifest
- freighter transfer log
- station intake record
- camera footage
- Sera Nox
- Pavel Kest
- Talia Serrano

Example:

**SEALED CONTAINER ACCEPTED**  
**PLANNED ROUTE:** FREIGHTER LINK → CARGO HOLDING 3 → SECONDARY TRANSFER  
**TRANSFER STATUS:** INTERRUPTED

## Step 2 — Route Break

The crew learns the container did not follow the planned route.

Sources:

- mass discrepancy
- pressure-door log
- broken cargo rail switch
- access override
- camera blackout
- scrape marks
- Ilya Ro
- Talia Serrano
- Sera Nox

Example:

**ROUTE CHANGE AUTHORIZED**  
**AUTHORITY STATE:** DISPUTED  
**OVERRIDE SOURCE:** LOCAL MANUAL INPUT  
**DESTINATION:** [CORRUPTED]

## Step 3 — Final Location

The crew combines clues to identify where the container likely ended up.

Possible locations:

- jammed cargo lift
- sealed maintenance bay
- old mining service pocket
- spin-transfer buffer
- Secondary Spine transfer corridor
- decompressed cargo cell

The final reveal should feel earned.

# Escalation Events

Use one whenever pace slows, a roll fails loudly, or the crew spends too much time.

## Station Event

- air quality drops
- pressure door fails
- lights die
- fire or fumes spread
- cargo area decompresses
- spin-transfer jams
- habitat shudders

## Asterion Event

- heat rises
- power becomes unstable
- hull patch worsens
- docking clamp stress increases
- navigation drift appears
- debris shifts toward the ship

## Faction Event

- scout appears
- barricade moves
- demand is issued
- hostage situation develops
- one faction moves toward the Secondary Spine
- one faction tries to reach the Asterion
- firefight erupts elsewhere

## Freighter Event

- dock clamps shift
- freighter lock cycles unexpectedly
- emergency power drops
- hidden alarm activates
- a faction tries to board it
- Pavel says the undocking window is closing

## Information Event

- camera node briefly restores
- corrupted log resolves
- survivor contradicts earlier story
- sensor glitch narrows search area
- old map reveals hidden route

## Cost Event

A useful route remains open only if the crew sacrifices one of the following:

- time
- air
- power
- parts
- faction trust
- medical supplies
- access to another option

# Act Flow

## Act 1 — The Last Survey Target

Purpose: trap the crew in the situation.

Key beats:

- final asteroid on survey route
- corrupted signal
- station revealed
- debris strike
- Asterion damaged
- boarding becomes the practical option

End when the crew commits to entering the station.

---

## Act 2 — Boarding the Station

Purpose: turn the station into a physical, dangerous place.

Key beats:

- approach and entry
- dead systems, not a dead station
- Central Spine reached
- evidence of internal conflict
- Volkov split revealed through fragments
- first contact or first survivor
- container appears as missing cargo
- Secondary Spine / freighter becomes a lead
- players choose their next priority

End when the crew understands enough to choose what to risk.

---

## Act 3 — Pressure Sandbox

Purpose: let players decide what matters.

Possible objectives:

- repair the Asterion
- search for the container
- stabilize station systems
- deal with Arman
- deal with Mira
- reach the freighter
- rescue survivors
- salvage useful equipment
- leave

End when the crew escapes, the station state forces an outcome, or the major pressure tracks resolve.

# Possible Endings

## Asterion Escape

The crew repairs their ship and leaves.

## Freighter Escape

The crew uses or seizes the freighter.

## Container Recovered

The crew finds and removes the container.

## Container Destroyed

The crew denies the artifact to everyone.

## Container Abandoned

The crew leaves without it.

## Station Stabilized

Survivors remain alive and the station can last longer.

## Station Lost

The station collapses, burns, decompresses, or becomes unrecoverable.

## Arman Victory

The Holdouts keep control of the habitat, station systems, or the container.

## Mira Victory

The Breakaway Crew gains access to the freighter, container, Asterion, or escape route.

Neither side gains clean control. Survivors face a power vacuum.

## Hard Exit

The crew survives under fire, through debris, or with bad repairs and partial truth.

# GM Principles

## Keep the Central Spine Understandable

It is the main spatial anchor.

Players should know how to return to it, even if routes change.

## Make the Freighter a Discovery

Do not show it clearly on first approach.

Reveal it through records, scans, survivors, faction movement, or exploration.

## Every Route Costs Something

Time, air, stress, power, safety, faction attention, or another option.

## Technical Work Must Matter

Repairs, scans, logs, cameras, doors, cargo systems, and life support should all change the situation.

## Factions Bargain Before They Shoot

The station is too fragile for casual firefights.

Violence is possible, but pressure, leverage, and threats are usually stronger first.

## The Container Is Found by Evidence

No single roll solves it.

The crew finds it by connecting clues.

## Leaving Is Not Failure

Survival with partial truth is a valid Ghost Station ending.
