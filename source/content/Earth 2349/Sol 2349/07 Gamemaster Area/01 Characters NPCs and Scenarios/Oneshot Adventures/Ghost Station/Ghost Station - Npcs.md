## Purpose of This Page

This page provides the main NPCs for **Ghost Station**.

The NPCs are built for play, not exposition. Each one should create pressure, offer partial information, demand a cost, or force a decision.

No NPC knows the full truth.

Use them to reveal the station through fragments: what they saw, what they believe, what they need, and what they are hiding.

---

# Faction Overview

The station conflict is between two pirate factions that operated from the same hidden base before the fighting began.

Neither faction is a formal military force.  
Neither faction is a clean villain group.  
Both are damaged, desperate, armed, and operating with incomplete information.

The conflict is also personal.

**Arman Volkov** helped turn the abandoned mining hollow into a hidden Belt refuge. His daughter, **Mira Volkov**, now leads the Breakaway Crew trying to escape with the container, the freighter, or anything valuable enough to buy a future elsewhere.

The container did not create the split by itself.

It gave the split a reason to become violent.

---

# The Holdouts

The Holdouts are the remnants of the old station order.

They include long-term station residents, dock workers, smugglers, old pirate crew, technicians, injured fighters, and people who still believe the station can be held together.

They are loyal to the station first and to **Arman Volkov** second.

They want to keep the station alive, protect their remaining people, and stop the Breakaway Crew from taking the container or the freighter.

## Approximate Strength

**14–18 people total**

This includes:

- 5–7 armed fighters or former crew
- 3–4 technicians, dock workers, or system operators
- 1 medic or medical assistant
- 4–6 wounded, exhausted, elderly, or noncombatant survivors

## Current Condition

The Holdouts control parts of the rotating habitat, improvised medical space, and some life support access. They are low on clean air capacity, medical supplies, and power stability. Several people cannot move without assistance.

## They Urgently Need

- scrubbers or air quality stabilized
- medical supplies and evacuation capacity
- power restored to habitat sections
- help keeping pressure doors functional
- protection from Breakaway movement toward the freighter
- proof that the Asterion crew will not simply loot and leave

---

# The Breakaway Crew

The Breakaway Crew is led by **Mira Volkov**, Arman’s daughter.

They are younger, more mobile, more willing to cut losses, and more focused on getting out with the container, the freighter, or anything valuable enough to survive elsewhere.

They see the Holdouts as trapped by nostalgia, old loyalties, and a station that should have been abandoned years ago.

They are strongest around cargo handling, the Secondary Spine, and the freighter route.

## Approximate Strength

**8–11 people total**

This includes:

- 5–6 armed crew
- 1–2 cargo or ship-system specialists
- 1 freighter engineer or pilot-adjacent specialist
- 1–2 wounded or missing crew members

## Current Condition

The Breakaway Crew has less secure air and fewer safe fallback positions, but more freedom of movement near cargo systems. They are under pressure to find the container, reach the freighter, or seize another escape option before the station collapses.

## They Urgently Need

- the container location
- access to the freighter or Asterion
- repair parts and power for the Secondary Spine
- a safe route past Holdout-controlled systems
- medical care for injured crew
- leverage before Arman locks them down completely

---

# Unaligned Survivors

A few people do not fully belong to either side anymore.

They are scattered, frightened, and dependent on whoever controls air, doors, medicine, or escape routes.

## Approximate Strength

**3–6 people total**

This may include:

- Dr. Anika Vale and patients
- a trapped scrubber technician
- Niko Pell or another station local
- a freighter crew survivor
- frightened workers hiding between faction zones

## They Urgently Need

- safe air
- medical help
- a route out of contested sections
- protection without forced recruitment
- information about whether evacuation is possible

# Core NPCs

## Arman Volkov

**Role:** Old station patriarch / Holdout leader  
**Faction:** Holdouts  
**Likely Location:** Rotating habitat command nook, medical shelter, or life support-adjacent barricade

Arman Volkov is one of the last figures of the station’s older order.

He is not a formal governor, and the station was never legitimate enough to have one. But for years, people came to him for arbitration, protection, smuggling contacts, crew assignments, and hard decisions.

He helped keep the station alive when it was still useful.  
He may also be one of the reasons it became impossible to leave.

Arman is wounded, exhausted, and still dangerous. His authority is damaged but not gone. Some people follow him out of loyalty. Others follow him because he still controls air, access, and old station favors.

His daughter Mira now leads the Breakaway Crew.

He calls her reckless.  
She calls him already dead.

### Wants

- keep the station from collapsing completely
- protect the remaining Holdouts and station locals
- prevent Mira from leaving with the container
- keep control of life support and habitat access
- force the Asterion crew to recognize that the station is still inhabited, not just salvage

### Knows

- the container triggered the final break between him and Mira
- the container was supposed to move through cargo handling toward the Secondary Spine
- Mira’s crew tried to redirect or seize it
- the freighter is docked at the Secondary Spine
- the station was already close to failure before the fighting
- some Holdouts sabotaged routes to stop Mira from escaping

### Leverage

- access to Holdout-controlled habitat sections
- partial station maps and old access codes
- medical supplies, spare parts, or station personnel
- old mining route knowledge
- authority to make some Holdouts stand down

### Complication

Arman is not simply right.

He loves the station, but his decisions helped trap people there. He may value the station’s survival over individual lives. He may also refuse to admit that Mira’s rebellion began because the old order had no future.

If the crew helps Mira, Arman may treat them as thieves, collaborators, or fools.

## Mira Volkov

**Role:** Breakaway Crew leader / Arman Volkov’s daughter  
**Faction:** Breakaway Crew  
**Likely Location:** Cargo area, Secondary Spine access, or freighter route

Mira Volkov leads the Breakaway Crew.

She grew up inside the station’s old compromises: smuggling, salvage, piracy, favors, rationing, patched systems, and people calling decay “survival.” She knows the station better than most outsiders, but she no longer believes it deserves loyalty.

To Mira, the container is not just profit.

It is an exit.

With it, she can buy protection, passage, ship repairs, crew loyalty, or a future that is not tied to a dying rock.

She is practical, angry, and under pressure. She is not trying to destroy the station for fun. She is trying to stop being buried with it.

### Wants

- locate the container
- reach or secure the freighter
- get her people off the station
- prevent Arman from using station systems to trap her
- use the Asterion if the freighter cannot be made ready
- leave before station collapse makes every option meaningless

### Knows

- the container was not successfully loaded onto the freighter
- the planned route failed during the fighting
- someone changed the route manually
- the Secondary Spine is damaged but still usable
- Arman’s Holdouts control key system access
- some Breakaway crew members may have acted without her orders

### Leverage

- freighter access codes or partial docking data
- cargo transfer route information
- repair parts from cargo stores
- armed support
- a lead toward the route break in the container trail
- a promised share if the crew helps recover the container

### Complication

Mira is not simply wrong.

She may be the only person willing to admit that the station is finished. But she is willing to abandon people, break promises, and use the crew if that is what it takes to get out.

If the crew finds the container, Mira may become desperate enough to threaten, betray, or seize leverage.

## Talia Serrano

**Role:** Injured station technician / first credible survivor  
**Faction:** Holdout-aligned, but mainly trying to survive  
**Likely Location:** Near the Central Spine, maintenance access, or a sealed side corridor

Talia is one of the best early NPCs for Act 2. She gives the crew a human anchor inside the station without explaining everything.

She knows the station systems better than most surviving fighters, but she does not know where the container ended up.

### Wants

- medical help
- access to safer air
- protection from both factions
- someone to stabilize the station before more people die

### Knows

- the station was already in poor condition before the firefight
- the communication array was damaged during the conflict
- both factions used station systems against each other
- the container was moved after the shooting started
- the expected cargo route is no longer reliable
- the Secondary Spine leads toward the freighter berth

### Leverage

- can help restore a camera node
- can identify useful maintenance routes
- can reduce Station Collapse pressure if protected
- can explain which systems are most critical

### Complication

Talia has only partial information. She may misidentify who currently controls an area because faction positions have shifted since she last moved through the station.

She also knows that some repairs will save the station but cost the crew time they may not have.

## Ilya Ro

**Role:** Holdout systems operator  
**Faction:** Holdouts  
**Likely Location:** Improvised control station near habitat systems or life support

Ilya controls a patched-together set of station systems: doors, cameras, air quality, and local power. None of it works cleanly, but they can still influence the map.

Ilya is useful because they can help the crew investigate, but dangerous because they can also misdirect them.

### Wants

- keep Holdout territory pressurized
- stop Mira from reaching the freighter
- keep the crew away from evidence that makes the Holdouts look responsible
- recover enough system access to survive

### Knows

- which cameras are dead, corrupted, or partially recoverable
- some access logs from the Central Spine
- the container changed route during the firefight
- one camera blackout was not caused by damage alone
- the Secondary Spine still has intermittent power

### Leverage

- can restore limited camera footage
- can open or lock some doors
- can guide the crew around decompressed sections
- can provide pressure and air quality data
- can reveal one stage of the container route

### Complication

Ilya may selectively edit what they show. They do not want the crew seeing evidence that the Holdouts also sabotaged station systems during the fight.

---

## Sera Nox

**Role:** Breakaway cargo specialist  
**Faction:** Breakaway Crew  
**Likely Location:** Cargo transfer area, damaged lift, or Secondary Spine route

Sera handled cargo movement before the fighting. She is one of the few people who understands how the container was supposed to move through the station.

She is useful, but she is also directly connected to the failed transfer.

### Wants

- survive
- avoid being blamed by either faction
- recover or erase records that implicate her
- get off the station if a ship becomes available

### Knows

- the original planned route of the sealed container
- where the cargo records were altered
- which lift or transfer rail failed
- that the container did not reach the freighter
- that the current manifest is unreliable

### Leverage

- can reconstruct the first step of the container route
- can identify false cargo entries
- can operate old cargo systems
- can help move heavy salvage or the container
- can reveal that a manual override changed the destination

### Complication

Sera may try to blame the route change on someone else.

She may also try to destroy or steal evidence if she thinks the crew will hand her over.

---

## Dax Omari

**Role:** Breakaway gunhand / freighter access guard  
**Faction:** Breakaway Crew  
**Likely Location:** Secondary Spine, freighter lock, or cargo corridor

Dax guards access to the Secondary Spine or freighter route. He is not important because of what he knows, but because he controls a useful choke point.

He is a tactical obstacle, but not necessarily a combat encounter.

### Wants

- keep outsiders away from the freighter route
- protect remaining Breakaway assets
- survive long enough to leave
- avoid being abandoned by Mira

### Knows

- the freighter is still docked
- the route is damaged but passable
- the Breakaway Crew does not fully control the freighter
- the container never made it aboard

### Leverage

- can grant or deny access to the Secondary Spine
- can warn about damaged clamps or traps
- can provide route information if bribed or pressured
- may know where Breakaway supplies are stored

### Complication

Dax is afraid he will be left behind. If the crew offers a believable way off the station, he may defect, bargain, or panic.

---

## Dr. Anika Vale

**Role:** Unaligned medic / emergency caretaker  
**Faction:** None, tolerated by both sides  
**Likely Location:** Rotating habitat medical corner or emergency shelter

Dr. Vale treats whoever reaches her alive. Both factions have used her, threatened her, and protected her at different times.

She is a useful moral pressure point because she knows who is wounded, who is lying about casualties, and who still needs rescue.

### Wants

- medical supplies
- air and power for the treatment area
- evacuation of the worst wounded
- no more fighting in the habitat

### Knows

- both factions have wounded
- some survivors are noncombatants
- the station cannot support the injured much longer
- one injured survivor saw the container after the route changed
- the Holdouts and Breakaway Crew are both hiding casualties

### Leverage

- can identify survivors worth questioning
- can provide medical aid
- can reduce Stress or stabilize injured crew
- can reveal who recently came through the habitat

### Complication

Helping Dr. Vale costs time, supplies, and possibly ship capacity.

Saving people may make the container harder to recover.

---

## Niko Pell

**Role:** Young runner / station local  
**Faction:** Unaligned, connected to Holdout territory  
**Likely Location:** Habitat service routes, old mining cuts, maintenance crawlways

Niko knows the station by lived experience rather than official maps. They used to move messages, tools, food, or contraband through routes too small or unpleasant for most adults.

Niko can help the crew bypass danger, but only if they feel safer with the crew than with the factions.

### Wants

- get someone important to safety
- avoid both factions
- find out whether a missing relative or friend survived
- leave the station if possible

### Knows

- hidden routes around some barricades
- which doors were blocked after the fighting
- where people hid when pressure alarms started
- cargo was moved through an unusual route
- one possible way toward the Secondary Spine without using the main corridor

### Leverage

- can guide the crew through shortcuts
- can identify local markings and unofficial names
- can lead them to a survivor with key information
- can help avoid a faction patrol

### Complication

Niko may run, hide, or refuse to continue if the crew uses violence carelessly or ignores trapped survivors.

---

## Pavel Kest

**Role:** Freighter engineer  
**Faction:** Unclear; worked with whoever controlled the freighter  
**Likely Location:** Freighter access corridor, trapped compartment, or maintenance pocket near Secondary Spine

Pavel is the best NPC for making the freighter usable.

He knows the freighter’s condition, but he may not be trustworthy. He may have helped bring the container in, or he may only have been hired to keep the ship running.

### Wants

- reach the freighter
- avoid being executed by either faction
- get paid or escape
- keep the freighter from being destroyed

### Knows

- the freighter’s docking status
- what systems need repair before departure
- the container was expected but never arrived aboard
- who had access to the cargo route
- the freighter may still have useful logs

### Leverage

- can help undock or restart the freighter
- can identify hidden ship damage
- can recover cargo or route logs
- can provide compatible parts for the Asterion
- can reveal whether the freighter is a real escape option

### Complication

Pavel may be trying to preserve the freighter for himself.

He may also know more about the container’s buyer than he first admits.

---

## Hanne Kreel

**Role:** Old miner / station founder generation survivor  
**Faction:** Holdout sympathy, but mostly loyal to the station itself  
**Likely Location:** Habitat, old mining void access, or emergency shelter

Hanne remembers what the asteroid was before it became a pirate refuge. She is useful for grounding the station’s history and revealing older routes or forgotten spaces.

Do not use her for long exposition. Use her to provide one sharp piece of history or one practical route.

### Wants

- keep the station from being completely destroyed
- prevent outsiders from treating it as disposable salvage
- protect remaining locals
- make sure the old mining voids are not opened recklessly

### Knows

- the asteroid was hollowed by independent miner crews
- some old service pockets are not on current maps
- several later station additions were never properly integrated
- one old route may bypass a faction checkpoint
- the container could have been hidden in an older service pocket if someone knew the route

### Leverage

- can identify old mining access
- can warn about unstable voids
- can provide a hidden route
- can explain why station maps are unreliable
- can support or challenge salvage claims

### Complication

Hanne may resist any plan that treats the station as already dead.

She may withhold old route knowledge if the crew seems likely to loot and abandon everyone.

# Minor NPCs and Encounters

Use these NPCs when the station needs more life or pressure.

## Injured Holdout Fighter

Can provide partial truth about the first firefight, but blames everything on Mira’s Breakaway Crew.

Useful for faction bias, medical pressure, and route warnings.

## Breakaway Scout

Hides near cargo or Secondary Spine access.

Useful for ambush, negotiation, false information, or tracking the crew.

## Trapped Scrubber Technician

Can reduce Station Collapse pressure if rescued.

Useful for technical objectives, humanitarian choices, and station stabilization.

## Freighter Crew Corpse

Carries access tags, cargo notes, or a damaged personal recorder.

Useful for the freighter route, container transfer evidence, and tone.

## Panicked Survivor

May mislead the crew without intending to.

Useful for uncertainty, bad information, and pressure.

## Silent Camera Operator

Someone watches through restored camera nodes but does not speak immediately.

Useful for paranoia, later contact, or faction surveillance.

## Dead Courier

Carries a data fragment showing the container route changed.

Useful for investigation clues, old mining routes, or faction movement.

# NPC Use by Act

## Act 2

Use NPCs to establish ambiguity.

Good early choices:

- Talia Serrano as first survivor
- injured Holdout fighter as biased witness
- hidden observer through a camera node
- body with data before living contact
- Niko Pell glimpsed but not yet approached

Act 2 NPCs should reveal that people are still alive, the station is divided, and the container is missing.

## Act 3

Use NPCs to create choices.

Good Act 3 uses:

- Arman Volkov offers access in exchange for repairs or blocking Mira
- Mira Volkov offers freighter access or cargo data in exchange for help
- Sera Nox can reconstruct the container route but tries to protect herself
- Pavel Kest can make the freighter usable but may not be loyal
- Dr. Vale asks for evacuation or medical supplies
- Ilya Ro controls camera footage or door access
- Niko Pell offers a shortcut at a moral cost
- Hanne Kreel reveals an old route through mining voids

Act 3 NPCs should force the crew to choose what matters.

# Faction Tension Guidelines

The factions should create pressure, not constant combat.

Use them when:

- the crew spends too much time
- the crew gains access to a valuable system
- the crew approaches the container route
- the crew reveals the Asterion may still fly
- the crew helps one side
- station conditions worsen
- the Secondary Spine becomes reachable
- the freighter becomes usable

Faction NPCs should bargain before they shoot unless violence is already established.

The station is too fragile for everyone to treat firefights casually.

---

# Final GM Principle

NPCs in Ghost Station should never exist only to explain the plot.

Each NPC should do at least one of the following:

- offer a partial truth
- demand a cost
- create a decision
- change a route
- shift faction pressure
- make survival harder or more meaningful
