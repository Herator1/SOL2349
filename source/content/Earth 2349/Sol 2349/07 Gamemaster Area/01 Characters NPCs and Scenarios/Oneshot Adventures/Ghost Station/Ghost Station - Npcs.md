# 👥 NPCs — Ghost Station

At the time of the scenario, Ghost Station is effectively defined by three active human elements: the **Corporate Recovery Team**, the **Old Guard**, and **Talia Serrano**, a surviving freighter crewmember. Keep all NPCs lightweight. Combat should stay dangerous and short. Most people here prefer leverage, cover, delay, and survival over prolonged firefights.

---

# ⚔️ Corporate Recovery Team

A small, heavily armed deniable recovery unit, likely [[Archeron]]-linked or working in the same style. They are on the station to recover the relic, contain witnesses, and leave before the whole structure becomes untenable. They are not trying to occupy Ghost Station long-term. They are trying to complete a compromised mission.

They should feel disciplined, dangerous, tired, and controlled. They negotiate if that preserves mission control, but escalate quickly if secrecy, timing, or relic access is threatened. They use the station like an obstacle, not like home terrain.

## Elena Markovic

**Role:** Recovery Team leader, main negotiation anchor

Markovic is calm, precise, and practical. She treats every conversation as leverage. She wants the relic, control over witnesses, and access to a working extraction path. What she fears most is the relic disappearing, Talia talking, the players repairing a ship without her influence, or the station failing before she can get out.

**How to play her:** calm voice, exact wording, measured pressure. She makes offers that sound reasonable but mostly serve her side. The moment the relic or timeline is threatened, she becomes much harder.

**Simple GM line:**

> “Help me contain this properly, and all of us improve our chances of leaving alive.”

# 🏴 Old Guard

The Old Guard are the surviving station operators: smugglers, salvagers, ex-contractors, service hands, and defenders who know Ghost Station far better than the Recovery Team does. They are less equipped and less disciplined, but they understand the routes, patched systems, dead zones, and fallback paths of the station.

They are trying to keep their people alive, deny the Recovery Team a clean win, and get through the crisis without being erased. They avoid fair fights, use barricades and local knowledge, bargain when useful, and retreat when a position is not worth the cost.

## Arman Volkov

**Role:** Old Guard leader, negotiator, local authority

Volkov is the most stable voice on the station side. He is not ideological and not reckless. He knows he cannot win a clean fight. He wants his people alive, wants to stop the Recovery Team from leaving with both the relic and the story, and wants at least some station survivors to have a way out.

He fears being cornered into a final fight, losing people for nothing, the players cutting a deal with Markovic, or the relic leaving under Recovery Team control.

**How to play him:** plainspoken, tired, wary, direct. Easier to read than Markovic. He speaks like someone defending a place people actually lived and worked in.

**Simple GM line:**

> “They want the object and a clean exit. We’re the part they’d rather leave behind.”

# 👤 Talia Serrano

A surviving freighter crewmember caught in the failed transfer. Talia is not loyal to the Old Guard, does not trust the Recovery Team, and does not fully trust the players either. She is frightened, exhausted, and mainly wants to survive.

She matters because she directly connects the freighter, the failed transfer, the crew split, and the current stand-off without knowing the whole story herself. In play she works best as a witness, rescue burden, bargaining asset, liability, or practical lead toward the freighter hold.

Talia knows for sure that the freighter brought the cargo, that the transfer into station storage was interrupted, that authority broke down during the move, that the freighter crew split, and that the station was still inhabited when the Recovery Team pushed in. She suspects there was an outside buyer waiting on confirmation and that the Recovery Team cares more about the cargo than survivors. She does **not** know the full origin of the relic, the full corporate chain behind the operation, or the complete station-wide picture after the collapse.

She can confirm that the freighter is central, point the players toward the hold, provide limited technical help on freighter systems, and give a human perspective on the collapse.

**How to play her:** frightened, tired, suspicious, and useful in fragments rather than speeches. She becomes more helpful once protected and stabilized.

**Simple GM line:**

> “The cargo was still in the freighter when everything broke. If you want the center of this mess, start there.”

# 🔧 Combat and Difficulty

Combat should change the whole station mood immediately: trust drops, movement gets harder, negotiations narrow, and both sides assume worse intentions. Recovery Team fights with coordination and focused fire; Old Guard fights opportunistically through terrain, ambushes, and fallback routes.

For an easier version, reduce total NPC numbers, remove the Heavy Gunner, or make one faction more damaged and divided. For a harder version, add 1–2 Recovery Operatives, strengthen choke-point positioning, improve Old Guard barricades, or increase environmental pressure during fights.
