## Zone Overview

**Default route:**  
**Asterion Dock (Dock 1)** -> **Dead Corridors** -> **Spin-Transfer Lift / Neutral Threshold** -> **Inhabited Core**

**Freighter Dock (Dock 2)** lies elsewhere in the outer shell, farther from Dock 1, and is reached through the dead corridor network.

Ghost Station is **small**. Once inside the rotating core, the important question is not navigation complexity but **who controls which side of the habitat**.

---

## Core Spatial Logic

Ghost Station has two main layers:

1. **Outer shell**
   - docking points
   - dead corridors
   - maintenance access
   - cargo routes
   - little or no atmosphere
   - mostly zero-g

2. **Inner rotating core**
   - small inhabited pressure zone
   - partial life support
   - faction-controlled access
   - limited gravity from spin

The station is not a large habitat. It is a damaged hidden station with a **small surviving inner core**.

---

## Key Zones

### Dock 1 – Asterion Dock
- outer hull
- damaged improvised docking point
- zero-g
- entry point for the crew
- main fallback extraction point

### Dock 2 – Freighter Dock
- farther away in the outer shell
- tied to old cargo infrastructure
- associated with the freighter and relic conflict
- not close to Dock 1

### Dead Corridors
- abandoned access tunnels and cargo passages
- dark, unstable, mostly dead
- connect Dock 1, Dock 2, and the spin-transfer access
- main zero-g exploration space

### Spin-Transfer Lift / Neutral Threshold
- transition from outer shell into the rotating core
- awkward and dangerous threshold
- neither side fully owns it at all times
- best place for tense first contact, warning, or negotiation
- effectively the neutral edge of the inhabited station

### Inhabited Core
- small rotating inner section
- still pressurized in parts
- split into opposing faction territory
- not a maze: compact, tense, and politically divided

---

## Inhabited Core – Functional Split

### Neutral Threshold
- immediate area around the spin-transfer access
- unstable buffer zone
- can briefly function as meeting point or contested pause space
- nobody fully trusts it

### Spinward Side – Old Guard
- Old Guard fallback area
- defensive position closer to their holdouts
- rough, improvised, survival-oriented
- this is where they try to hold ground and protect what remains

### Counterspinward Side – Corporate Recovery Team
- recovery team operating zone
- cleaner, more tactical, more controlled
- used as forward containment and pressure point
- this is where organized external control is strongest

### Hidden Survivor Position
- **not in the middle of open faction territory**
- hidden in a **maintenance pocket / sealed utility crawlspace / service compartment**
- located **off the main inhabited axis**, near enough to overhear movement or faction activity, but not in direct line of sight
- best placement: **adjacent to the neutral threshold but slightly offset toward the damaged side of the core**
- close enough to have seen or heard key events
- hidden enough to have survived because both factions overlooked or bypassed the space

This works well because:
- the survivor is accessible before a full faction commitment
- the survivor does not unrealistically sit deep inside one faction zone
- the players can find her through caution, traces, or local disturbance rather than by random chance

---

## Relative Positioning

### Asterion
- docked at Dock 1
- outer shell
- closest fallback route

### Freighter
- docked at Dock 2
- outer shell, farther away
- physically separate from the inhabited core
- tied to the relic timeline, not to daily station life

### Spin-Transfer Lift
- main threshold into the rotating core
- most important choke point in the station

### Old Guard
- spinward side of the inhabited core

### Recovery Team
- counterspinward side of the inhabited core

### Lone Survivor
- hidden in a small offset compartment near the threshold zone, not openly inside either faction stronghold

---

## Simple GM Orientation

If players ask where they are:

- **At Dock 1:** “You are still in the dead outer shell, near the Asterion.”
- **In dead corridors:** “You are moving through abandoned outer station structure.”
- **At the spin-transfer lift:** “You are at the threshold between the dead shell and the inhabited rotating core.”
- **Spinward:** “You are moving toward Old Guard-controlled space.”
- **Counterspinward:** “You are moving toward Recovery Team-controlled space.”
- **At Dock 2 / freighter side:** “You are back in the outer structure, farther from the Asterion, near the cargo side of the station.”

---

## GM Use

Track only:
1. Are they in the **outer shell**, at the **threshold**, or in the **inhabited core**?
2. If in the core, are they drifting **spinward**, **counterspinward**, or staying near the threshold?
3. Is the next pressure **environmental**, **social**, or **armed**?
