# Asterion

The **Asterion** is an aging but well-maintained **Helios survey vessel** designed for long-range industrial survey work in the Asteroid Belt. It is built for prospecting, route verification, probe deployment, remote inspection, and limited sample recovery rather than combat. Old by current standards but heavily refitted over its service life, it remains a dependable contractor-grade ship: cramped, practical, and mechanically conservative.

The vessel normally operates with a **professional crew of five**, supported by extensive automation and robust industrial control systems. It is not luxurious, not fast by high-end corporate standards, and not intended to win fights. Its value lies in endurance, sensor quality, field reliability, and the ability to work independently for long periods far from major infrastructure.

## Technical Profile

**Type:** Belt survey and resource assessment vessel  
**Operator:** Helios  
**Role:** asteroid survey, prospect grading, route verification, probe deployment, limited sample recovery  
**Crew:** 5  
**Additional berthing:** 2 temporary bunks / mission overflow  
**Hull age:** 34 years  
**Last major refit:** 9 years ago  
**Condition:** old, reliable, repeatedly upgraded  

## Mass and Performance

**Dry mass:** 420 t  
**Typical loaded mass:** 760 t  
**Maximum departure mass:** 880 t  

**Drive:** fusion torch with magnetic nozzle  
**Reactor:** compact toroidal magnetic-confinement D–He3 fusion reactor  
**Auxiliary power:** compact backup fission unit with ship-scale battery reserve  

**Normal cruise acceleration:** 0.10 g  
**Sustained high cruise:** 0.18 g  
**Maximum short-burst acceleration:** 0.28 g  

**Loaded mission delta-v:** ~95 km/s  
**Light / ferry delta-v:** ~125 km/s  

## Operational Endurance

**Independent endurance:** 8 months for 5 crew  
**Emergency endurance:** up to 11 months under strict rationing  
**Typical survey deployment:** 10–14 weeks before full resupply becomes desirable  

The ship is designed to remain operational for extended Belt missions with limited external support. Consumables, spares, maintenance access, and survey probe storage all take priority over comfort.

## Survey and Mission Equipment

The **Asterion** carries a full industrial survey package optimized for asteroid and debris-field assessment:

- multiband passive sensor suite
- active lidar and radar mapping package
- gravimetric anomaly scanner
- spectrometric material analysis systems
- probe launcher and recovery support
- compact inspection and survey drones
- micro-sample handling and sealed storage
- onboard field lab for first-pass material characterization
- route hazard detection and navigation survey package

Its sensor systems are stronger than those of a typical small freighter, but less specialized than dedicated scientific deep-research platforms.

## Defensive and Utility Systems

The **Asterion** is not a combat vessel. Its protective systems are intended to survive routine Belt risk, not military action.

- light point-defense mount
- hardened comms and sensor shielding
- reinforced industrial hull sections
- compartmentalized pressure control
- emergency damage-control drones
- standard contractor EVA support and repair gear

## Internal Layout

The ship follows a thrust-axis industrial layout with compact pressure sections, accessible maintenance spaces, and modular work compartments. It is built to be serviced in the field and repeatedly patched rather than elegantly overhauled.

Main internal sections typically include:

- command and flight deck
- operations / sensor control
- engineering and reactor access
- small med / utility compartment
- crew berthing and galley
- survey stores and probe handling space
- EVA locker and repair workshop
- cargo and sample stowage

Accommodation is functional and tight. The ship is meant for working crews, not passengers.

## Protected Crew Core

The main crew compartment of the **Asterion** is built around a compact **protected core** that also serves as the vessel’s **storm shelter**. In normal operations, this section functions as crew berthing, galley, and emergency support space. During radiation events or major external exposure risk, it can be sealed and used as a hardened refuge volume with additional shielding from surrounding tankage, stores, and internal structure.

Like most small industrial torch ships, the shelter is practical rather than comfortable: cramped, overbuilt, and designed to keep the crew alive during bad hours, not to make them pleasant.

In its current survey loadout, the vessel is valuable less because of prestige than because it combines torch mobility, industrial survey capability, and long-endurance contractor independence in one proven hull.

## Current Scenario-Relevant Condition

At the start of **Ghost Station**, the **Asterion** remains habitable and partially operational after the debris strike, but its **magnetic nozzle containment and alignment assembly** has been critically damaged. The reactor remains live and the ship can still support local systems, shelter, and limited maneuvering, but it cannot safely perform a full departure burn until repaired.
