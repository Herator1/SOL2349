_Ghost Station_ is a **self-contained one-shot scenario** set in the hard, industrial reality of _SOL 2349_. It begins with a damaged survey vessel, a stranded crew, and a hidden station buried inside a mined-out asteroid that was never meant to appear on any chart.

This is not a story about heroic exploration. It is a story about survival inside a failing piece of Belt infrastructure, where salvage, secrecy, and deniable corporate interests collide. Ghost Station is not an abandoned ruin or a generic pirate base. It is an illegal retrofit: part smuggling node, part salvage site, part off-book transfer point, and now the scene of a recent and unfinished conflict.

The scenario focuses on **tension, uncertainty, and decision-making under pressure**. It is not primarily combat-driven, but violence is always close. Resources are limited, information is incomplete, and the station itself is becoming more dangerous by the hour. Life support is degrading, power is unstable, spin gravity is failing, and every delay makes the situation worse.

At the center of the scenario is a conflict already in motion. Two hostile groups remain on the station, neither in full control, both aware that time is running out. The players arrive after the decisive mistakes have already been made, but before the consequences have fully played out. Their role is not to follow a fixed plot, but to navigate an unstable environment, shifting loyalties, damaged ships, and a highly valuable [[AI Relic]] that should never have passed through a place like this.

The expected playtime is **3–5 hours**, depending on pacing and player decisions. The scenario can lean more into negotiation, technical problem-solving, and fragile alliances, or shift toward betrayal, survival pressure, and violence if events spiral.

There is no single correct outcome. Success depends on what the players choose to protect, what they are willing to risk, and what they are prepared to leave behind.
