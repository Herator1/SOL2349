These one-shots are designed as **full-session scenarios**.

Each of them is meant to represent a different side of what **SOL 2349** is supposed to be: hard science-fiction roleplaying built around pressure, believable environments, meaningful decisions, and conflicts that feel grounded in the wider setting. They are intended to tell the kinds of stories that should feel classic for SOL 2349, while also showing different locations, problems, and tones across the Solar System.

The goal is to make the entry point as easy as possible for both **players** and **Game Masters**. Every scenario is built to be playable without requiring large amounts of preparation or additional design work before the session. They come with the material needed to run them properly, including things like characters, NPCs, maps, locations, conflict structure, and practical GM support.

These scenarios are not written as rigid scripts with fixed scene order and predetermined outcomes. At the same time, they are not loose or undefined frameworks that leave the GM to invent half the session alone. Each one-shot starts from a clear situation: a place, a conflict, a set of pressures, and actors who already have goals and reasons to act. The players are then free to make meaningful choices inside that situation.

A good SOL 2349 scenario should give players real decisions to make and enough room for their actions to matter. At the same time, it should give the GM enough structure, pacing support, and concrete material to keep the session moving, maintain tension, and hold the group on a clear narrative line without forcing a specific ending.

In short, these one-shots are built to do two things at once:  
they should be easy to run, and they should still feel open, tense, and consequential in play.
