The following five characters are designed as the player-facing marine team for **Operation: Kestrel Landing**. They are not generic grunts. Each fills a clear operational role inside a small [[Jovian League]] rapid-response unit and gives the players a distinct perspective on the station crisis.

They should feel highly trained, professional, and capable — but still human. These are not super-soldiers. They are a compact intervention team operating with limited numbers, limited certainty, and no guarantee of reinforcement.

The characters are created using 80 pp, instead of the usual 50 to reflect their elite operative status.

## Notes

Talia is built as a **pressure commander**, not a frontline duelist. She is strongest when she can:

- keep the squad coordinated with **Command Uplink**
- push tense conversations with **Persuasion Suite**
- manipulate doors, locks, and local systems with **Systems Interface Node**
- stay viable in a firefight through solid Reflexes, Fitness, and Firearms
- maintain authority when the mission starts falling apart

Talia Serrin is the officer in command of the Kestrel landing team. She is disciplined, composed, and used to making decisions before the full picture is available. Her background is in station security response, boarding control, and small-unit crisis intervention. She is at her best when a situation is unstable but not yet fully broken.

Serrin is not charismatic in a warm sense. She leads through clarity, control, and competence. She does not waste words, and she does not give emotional reassurance she does not believe in. Her strength is that she can keep a team functioning under uncertainty. Her weakness is that she tends to hold responsibility too tightly, especially when the mission becomes morally unclear.

She is an excellent choice for players who want to steer the group, manage pressure, and decide what “professional conduct” really means once the official story begins to fall apart.

**Squad function:** decision-maker, tactical coordinator, mission authority  
**Typical gear profile:** command suit package, rifle, tactical uplink, compact sidearm  
**Player hook:** Do you stay inside the mission, or redefine it when the mission itself stops making sense?

Naima Rourke came into military service through emergency medicine rather than pure combat ambition. Before entering League service, she worked in civilian trauma response around industrial stations and transport nodes, where pressure loss, mechanical injury, and delayed evacuation were part of normal life. She has seen enough preventable death to distrust anyone who speaks too easily about acceptable losses.

Rourke is practical, observant, and emotionally harder to shake than most people first assume. She is not soft, but she is the one most likely to ask whether the team is still protecting people or merely following procedure. She is an excellent choice for players who want to be useful under pressure while also having a strong human angle on the mission.

## Team Dynamic

This team works best if the players lean into the natural tension between roles:

- **Serrin** pushes for mission coherence
    
- **Rourke** pushes for human cost to stay visible
    
- **Valek** anchors the team physically and tactically
    
- **Arden** pushes momentum and decisive action
    
- **Quist** pushes investigation, caution, and uncomfortable questions
    

That mix is intentional.  
**Operation: Kestrel Landing** works best when the squad is competent enough to survive, but divided enough to care about what survival means.

---

## GM Note

These characters should all feel like believable members of the same small professional response team. They are different in tone and outlook, but none of them should feel out of place on the **Kestrel**.

The scenario becomes stronger if each player quickly understands not only what their character is good at, but what that character is likely to care about once the mission stops being straightforward.
