The research sub carries a small, highly specialized crew. Most personnel assume the inspection is a routine audit. Only **Dr. Sia Renn**, the Biosecurity Officer, knows that the discrepancies in sample accounting are being treated as potentially serious.

The crew is competent, tired, and used to operating in confinement. Several are hiding something. Only some of those secrets matter.

## Prof. Dr. Lena Orlov

**Role:** Scientific Lead of the Europa Subsurface Program  
**Area:** Command / Science Oversight  
**First impression:** Controlled, formal, highly intelligent, difficult to read

Orlov is responsible for the research program as a whole. She does not manage every laboratory procedure directly, but nothing important happens on the sub without eventually passing through her priorities, schedules, or staffing decisions. She sees herself as the person holding the mission together between science, politics, and institutional pressure.

She wants the inspection to end quickly and cleanly. She does not want a shutdown, a scandal, or outside actors dictating what happens to the Europa program. She will cooperate, but in a narrow and disciplined way. She answers questions carefully and expects the players to behave like professionals.

Orlov is not involved in the thefts, but she has tolerated stress, weak documentation, and personnel decisions that made the system more fragile than it should be.

**Motivation:** Protect the mission, preserve authority, avoid escalation  
**Knows:** staffing pressures, contract extensions, research priorities, interdepartmental conflicts  
**Hides:** how much strain the program is under and how much bad process she has tolerated  
**Related clues:** Chen’s contract extension, pressure on publication and results, internal politics  
**Memorable feature:** Speaks with academic precision and still sounds like she is chairing a difficult seminar

## Captain Daren Vale

**Role:** Captain of the research sub  
**Area:** Command  
**First impression:** Dry, controlled, operational

Vale is not a scientist and has little patience for academic evasiveness. His priority is the vessel, the crew, and the continuation of operations within safe parameters. He views the audit as legitimate but potentially disruptive, and he expects the players to understand the difference between a real threat and institutional noise.

He is willing to provide logs, movement authority, and command access if the players behave credibly. If they speculate too freely or start accusing people without clear evidence, he becomes colder and more formal. He does not like drama, and he does not want the inspection to become one.

Vale has noticed stress among the crew, but not enough to identify the real causes.

**Motivation:** Maintain stability, keep the sub operational, avoid procedural chaos  
**Knows:** movement logs, docking schedules, who is trusted operationally  
**Hides:** his frustration with the scientific side of the mission and how fragile crew discipline has become  
**Related clues:** access to movement logs, transfer cycles, formal escalation if things go wrong  
**Memorable feature:** Braces automatically during maneuvers even when seated under stable gravity

## Dr. Sia Renn

**Role:** Biosecurity Officer  
**Area:** Transfer Spine / Controlled Access  
**First impression:** Alert, precise, visibly disciplined

Renn is the one who filed the report that triggered the audit. She noticed that the destruction volumes and collected sample volumes did not fully align and decided that the discrepancy was too consistent to dismiss. She does not know the full truth, but she knows something is wrong and that it may have biosecurity implications.

She is the players’ most reliable institutional ally at the start. At the same time, she is not warm. She expects caution, documentation, and professional restraint. She is very aware that pushing too early could trigger evidence loss.

If the players work methodically, Renn supports them. If they turn reckless, she becomes restrictive.

**Motivation:** Confirm the discrepancy, contain risk, prevent evidence loss  
**Knows:** audit trigger, destruction variance, access restrictions, safety procedures  
**Hides:** how worried she actually is about the implications if material has left the system  
**Related clues:** initial discrepancy, controlled material flow, restricted access logic  
**Memorable feature:** Checks seals, hatch lights, and lock indicators almost unconsciously before speaking

## Dr. Ilya Soren

**Role:** Senior Biochemist  
**Area:** Biochemistry Lab  
**First impression:** Precise, calm, technically convincing

Soren is one of the two actual perpetrators. He identified the possible long-term relevance of Europa-derived biochemical pathways for radiation resistance or mitigation, but chose not to push that line openly. Instead, he emphasized officially safer and more institutionally acceptable applications such as cryoprotection and immunomodulation. He did not falsify his work outright. He framed it.

Soren is convinced that if the real importance of the material becomes fully visible inside the official system, it will be locked down, patented, buried in politics, or otherwise kept out of reach of the people he believes need it most. He sees himself as acting against institutional capture, not as a thief.

He is difficult to crack because he is composed and morally convinced. He lies by omission, by emphasis, and by selective explanation rather than blunt denial.

**Motivation:** Prevent key discoveries from disappearing into institutional control  
**Knows:** the real significance of some biochemical pathways, the processing method, the transfer route via Tomas  
**Hides:** deliberate redirection of research emphasis and hidden processing of outgoing material  
**Related clues:** cartridge overuse, encapsulation equipment, suspicious lab workflow, transfer-linked return flow  
**Memorable feature:** Keeps his workstation almost unnaturally bare except for an old Belt research badge tucked behind a display

## Dr. Marek Chen

**Role:** Senior Analyst for Sequencing and Classification  
**Area:** Analysis / Sequencing Lab  
**First impression:** Intelligent, strained, permanently tired

Chen is the second perpetrator, though very different from Soren. He is not acting from conviction. He is being coerced. Before joining the Europa mission, he falsified or manipulated parts of earlier scientific work to secure a career path he did not fully deserve. Someone knows, and they have enough proof to ruin him.

Chen has been quietly diverting small samples from newly classified organisms or variants during shore leaves. He does not know the full identity of the network behind the theft. He only knows the process, the pressure, and the fact that he has not been allowed to walk away.

He appears sloppy because he is stressed, and because some of his documentation really is sloppy. That makes him a strong second-layer suspect.

**Motivation:** Avoid exposure, keep his career intact, survive the situation  
**Knows:** his own drop procedure, the pressure being applied, some rotation patterns, very little about the actual network  
**Hides:** manipulated records, implant misuse, coercion, previous academic misconduct  
**Related clues:** sequencing mismatches, pattern in errors, shore leave correlation, implant irregularities, contract anomaly  
**Memorable feature:** Uses old Shukra corporate lab phrases when stressed and apologizes before he is fully challenged

## Dr. Talia Neves

**Role:** Senior Researcher for Cultivation Systems  
**Area:** Cultivation Lab  
**First impression:** Fast, sharp, overworked, a little chaotic

Talia runs the cultivation side of the operation, which means she handles the messiest and most visibly imperfect part of the scientific workflow. There is biomass, turnover, partial loss, contamination pressure, and a lot of manual judgment. As a result, her lab is the most obvious false lead in the scenario.

Talia is not involved in the missing-sample operation, but she does have real problems. Her workflow is messy, her documentation is uneven, and she is using stimulants or similar substances outside medical protocol to keep up with the cycle. She is also in a hidden relationship with **Nika Venn**, which creates time gaps and awkward lies if players start looking too closely.

Talia is defensive because she knows how bad her lab looks under inspection.

**Motivation:** Keep the cultivation systems stable, get through the audit, protect Nika and herself  
**Knows:** cultivation problems, workflow strain, practical weaknesses in lab process  
**Hides:** substance misuse, relationship with Nika, just how close her lab is to becoming unsustainable under current pressure  
**Related clues:** false lead around sloppy records, high biomass handling, erratic behavior, contradictory whereabouts  
**Memorable feature:** Wears a small **Fivefold Covenant** life-charm under her collar and handles living cultures with quiet ritual care

## Dr. Jonas Krell

**Role:** Sample Custody and Chain-of-Handling Officer  
**Area:** Transfer Spine / Records / Material Flow  
**First impression:** Tense, procedural, protective of his systems

Krell sits at the point where sample movement becomes paperwork and accountability. He tracks intake, classification, transfer, and destruction records. That makes him immediately suspicious in the eyes of many players, especially because he is defensive and easily rattled.

Krell is not guilty of the thefts. He is guilty of having missed them.

That makes him fearful, territorial, and unpleasant under pressure. He will produce records, explain process, and insist that the system is sound, but every inconsistency the players find feels to him like a personal failure. If accused too aggressively, he becomes rigid and much less helpful.

**Motivation:** Prove the records still make sense, avoid becoming the scapegoat  
**Knows:** official material flow, destruction records, routing classifications, access points in the transfer spine  
**Hides:** his anxiety, how much he has started doubting his own control over the system  
**Related clues:** destruction variance, routing of reusable versus destroyable material, chain-of-custody records  
**Memorable feature:** Labels everything obsessively and hates it when people touch containers without announcing it

## Nika Venn

**Role:** Internal Service Technician  
**Area:** Engineering access / Transfer Spine / General maintenance  
**First impression:** practical, funny, slightly irreverent

Nika keeps the sub functional at the level where real life happens: panels, filters, fittings, locks, access points, seals, improvised fixes. She knows the ship physically better than most of the scientific staff. That gives her broad access and makes her a plausible false suspect if players start following movement rather than motive.

Nika is not involved in the sample thefts, but she is hiding several smaller things. She is quietly involved with Talia, and she diverts minor supplies or comfort items in ways that violate policy without threatening the mission. If players start digging, they can uncover real rule-breaking around her, but not the core crime.

She is often one of the easiest people for players to talk to until they push too far.

**Motivation:** Keep the sub running, protect Talia, avoid official scrutiny  
**Knows:** physical access routes, maintenance bottlenecks, who moves where and when  
**Hides:** relationship with Talia, minor supply diversion, how often she informally helps people outside protocol  
**Related clues:** access-related false lead, small inventory noise, hidden relationship  
**Memorable feature:** Marks tools and access tabs with old Belt pressure-hull blessing signs learned from dock crews

## Pavel Idris

**Role:** Systems and Operations Officer  
**Area:** Command / Systems Control  
**First impression:** focused, technical, already multitasking

Pavel manages the operational nervous system of the sub: cameras, environmental controls, movement logs, docking records, and the quiet constant flow of monitored machine behavior. He is not socially central, but he is structurally important because he can confirm or deny patterns in motion and access.

He is not hiding anything major. His main value is that he can provide evidence if the players know what to ask for. At the same time, he does not volunteer much because he is busy and sees most problems as request-routing rather than personal drama.

He is especially useful once the players start looking at Tomas Verik’s transfer routines or internal movement patterns.

**Motivation:** Keep systems stable, avoid disruption, keep the audit from turning into operational clutter  
**Knows:** camera access, movement logs, docking patterns, system event history  
**Hides:** mostly irritation at how many people ask for data without knowing what they want  
**Related clues:** cockpit camera footage, transfer timing, internal logs, access timestamps  
**Memorable feature:** Refuses to call any system “good,” only “within margin,” while drinking awful recycled-water coffee concentrate

## Dr. Keira Solheim

**Role:** Medical Officer  
**Area:** Med Bay  
**First impression:** observant, dry, professionally unsentimental

Solheim understands the crew as bodies under confinement, stress, poor sleep, stimulants, strain, and deferred maintenance. She is not central to the sample logic, but she is central to the human reality of the sub. She can reveal the difference between suspicious behavior and simple overwork.

She knows more than most about who is fraying, who is self-medicating, and who has avoided proper care. She does not like being used as an interrogation tool, but she can become a crucial ally if the players work carefully and respectfully.

She is the clearest path to the implant irregularities around Chen and the substance-use issue around Talia.

**Motivation:** Keep the crew functional, avoid panic, prevent preventable breakdown  
**Knows:** stress levels, undeclared treatment, implant maintenance irregularities, quiet medical compromises  
**Hides:** how much she has chosen not to formally escalate in order to keep the mission functioning  
**Related clues:** stimulant misuse, implant irritation, unscheduled maintenance evidence  
**Memorable feature:** Uses long-haul habitat triage shorthand and wastes nothing that can still safely be used

## Arun Salgado

**Role:** Drone Pilot and Field Technician  
**Area:** Drone Control / Sample Intake  
**First impression:** focused, quiet, more comfortable with machines than people

Arun operates the drones that collect samples from the ice-water interface and nearby upper-ocean zones. He understands where samples come from, how they are brought in, and what the timing of outside operations looks like. He is useful for reconstructing sampling windows and understanding what the vessel is actually doing at any given time.

He is not involved in the thefts. His main value is in helping the players understand the sample pipeline and ruling out some incorrect assumptions about the origin and timing of materials.

He is easier to engage through technical questions than social ones.

**Motivation:** Keep sampling operations stable, protect the exterior systems, avoid unnecessary interference  
**Knows:** intake timing, drone deployments, biofilm and ice-drill sampling routines, exterior access windows  
**Hides:** mostly resentment toward people who treat the drone side as background support rather than core science  
**Related clues:** how samples enter the system, timing of new material, realistic flow from outside to inside  
**Memorable feature:** Names the drones and trusts their telemetry more than most crew testimony

## Tomas Verik

**Role:** External Transfer Technician and Pilot of the resupply sub  
**Area:** Transfer Dock / Shuttle link to station  
**First impression:** friendly, grounded, easy to like

Tomas pilots the small transfer sub that connects the deeper research vessel with the station above. He appears to be exactly what he should be: a practical, socially competent operator who handles movement, cargo, and rough conditions without drama.

He is also one of the perpetrators, working with Soren to move selected material through the transfer chain. His motive is personal and Belter-rooted. He has seen the cost of radiation damage closely and believes that if the science below the ice has real potential, it should not disappear into institutional control.

Tomas is strong socially, which is why he works so well as an early trusted contact. He lies better through normality than through complexity.

**Motivation:** Help move valuable material out of the system for a cause he believes is justified  
**Knows:** transfer timings, handling routines, routing opportunities, Soren’s role  
**Hides:** cockpit absences, handling of suspicious returns, emotional investment in the Belt-side cause  
**Related clues:** camera footage, suspicious transfer behavior, altered return flow, personal link to Soren’s operation  
**Memorable feature:** Greets everyone like a longtime station regular and always brings some tiny off-manifest comfort item from topside

## Using the Crew in Play

Most NPCs should not feel like suspects first. They should feel like professionals under strain.

A good rule for the GM is:

- **Orlov** and **Vale** frame authority
- **Renn** and **Krell** frame system integrity
- **Talia**, **Nika**, and **Solheim** frame human weakness
- **Soren**, **Chen**, and **Tomas** frame the actual hidden crimes
- **Pavel** and **Arun** frame technical reality

That keeps the investigation readable.
