## Overview

The players arrive on the research sub and begin their audit.

The system remains fully operational.  
Crew members continue their work.  
No one expects a disruption.

The players are free to:

- move between sections
- request records
- inspect labs
- question personnel

Most information is accessible with the right approach.  
Checks refine or deepen understanding, not unlock everything.

---

# Sub Layout (Functional Overview)

The sub is compact and divided into three zones.

## Clean Section

Crew-facing spaces:

- Command (Captain Daren Vale, Systems Officer Pavel Idris)
- Crew quarters and mess
- Med Bay (Dr. Keira Solheim)

Primary use: conversations, background checks, social pressure.

---

## Contaminated Section

Scientific work areas:

- Sample Intake / Isolation
- Cultivation Lab (Dr. Talia Neves)
- Biochemistry Lab (Dr. Ilya Soren)
- Analysis / Sequencing (Dr. Marek Chen)

Primary use: technical investigation, records, inconsistencies.

---

## Transfer Spine (Central Corridor)

Controlled interface between systems:

- Decontamination
- Material handling
- Access to docking section
- Sample custody (Dr. Jonas Krell)

Primary use: material flow, bottlenecks, system logic.

---

# Real Leads (with Locations and Access)

## 1. Sample Destruction Variance

**NPC:** Dr. Sia Renn (Biosecurity Officer) / Dr. Jonas Krell (Sample Custody)  
**Location:** Biosecurity records, custody logs (Transfer Spine)

Players reviewing destruction and intake data notice:

- destroyed volume slightly lower than intake
- discrepancy small but consistent

Check (science/admin):  
→ confirms this is outside expected tolerance

This establishes the problem is real.

---

## 2. Cartridge Overuse in Biochemistry

**NPC:** Dr. Ilya Soren  
**Location:** Biochemistry Lab

Inspection of consumables shows:

- a specific microfluidic cartridge type used too frequently
- mismatch with declared experiments

Check (science/engineering):  
→ identifies cartridges as not strictly required for stated work

Soren explains this as optimization.  
It is not fully convincing.

---

## 3. Misaligned Equipment Use

**NPC:** Dr. Ilya Soren  
**Location:** Biochemistry Lab

Players notice:

- encapsulation equipment active
- not clearly required for cryoprotection or immunomodulation

Check (biochem/materials):  
→ confirms equipment is suited for containment/transport, not slow-release systems

This strengthens suspicion of hidden processing.

---

## 4. Suspicious Return Flow

**NPC:** Dr. Jonas Krell / Nika Venn  
**Location:** Transfer Spine (Decontamination / Material Handling)

Reviewing outgoing material shows:

- some components flagged as reusable instead of destroyed
- classification inconsistent

Check (logistics/biosecurity):  
→ identifies misclassification as systematic, not random

This reveals a pathway out of the system.

---

## 5. Transfer Sub Behavior

**NPC:** Pavel Idris (Systems), Tomas Verik (Transfer Pilot)  
**Location:** Command logs / external observation

Camera and flight logs show:

- Tomas Verik leaves the cockpit during solo runs

Check (operations/pilot):  
→ confirms this is unusual and unnecessary

At this stage, still explainable as routine behavior.

---

## 6. Analysis Record Inconsistencies

**NPC:** Dr. Marek Chen  
**Location:** Analysis / Sequencing Lab

Comparing sequencing data and sample IDs reveals:

- missing links between samples and results
- incomplete or mismatched entries

Check (science/data):  
→ confirms inconsistency beyond normal error

Chen attributes this to workload and backlog.

---

## 7. Pattern Recognition

**NPC:** none directly  
**Location:** cross-department comparison

Only after comparing multiple datasets:

- inconsistencies cluster around specific sample types or times

Check (investigation/data):  
→ confirms pattern is structured

This suggests deliberate interference.

---

## 8. Shore Leave Correlation

**NPC:** Prof. Dr. Lena Orlov / Pavel Idris  
**Location:** personnel and rotation logs (Command)

Cross-checking:

- sample discrepancies align with Dr. Marek Chen’s rotation schedule

Check (admin/investigation):  
→ confirms correlation

This connects data errors to physical movement.

---

## 9. Implant Irregularities

**NPC:** Dr. Keira Solheim / Dr. Marek Chen  
**Location:** Med Bay

Medical inspection or observation reveals:

- recent implant access
- not aligned with scheduled maintenance

Check (medical):  
→ confirms procedure occurred in suboptimal conditions

Chen downplays this as delayed maintenance.

---

## 10. Contract Extension Anomaly

**NPC:** Prof. Dr. Lena Orlov  
**Location:** Command / admin records

Reviewing staffing:

- Chen has remained assigned longer than expected
- not critical enough to justify extension

Check (admin/social):  
→ suggests quiet intervention

Implies external influence.

# GM Notes

Act 2 should feel like:

- too many small issues
- multiple plausible explanations
- no clear single cause

Players should be able to:

- suspect the wrong person
- “solve” part of the problem
- then realize something still does not fit

## Transition to Act 3

Move to Act 3 when:

- players identify Täter 1 (Soren + transfer chain)
- or discrepancies remain unresolved
- or the next transfer cycle forces action

At this point, the investigation becomes a decision problem.
