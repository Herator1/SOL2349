## Overview

Act 4 resolves the local case on Europa and determines whether the investigation ends there or continues offworld.

By this point, the players may have uncovered one theft operation, both, or only part of the truth. They may also have preserved evidence, damaged trust, or forced the station into formal lockdown.

The key question is no longer what happened. It is what the players do with what they know.

---

## Outcome 1 – Full Exposure

The players identify both theft operations and report everything through official channels.

**Dr. Ilya Soren**, **Tomas Verik**, and **Dr. Marek Chen** are exposed. The station enters formal review, records are frozen, and the audit becomes an official investigation.

This is the cleanest institutional outcome.

**Consequences:**  
The research program is partially suspended. The crew is reviewed. The Consortium secures all records and samples.

**Follow-up:**  
The players may be asked to continue the case offworld as official investigators or witnesses.

---

## Outcome 2 – Partial Resolution

The players uncover only one operation, or choose to focus on one while leaving the other unresolved.

The local problem appears mostly solved, but some discrepancy remains. This works especially well if the players catch **Soren and Verik** first, or expose **Chen** without understanding the wider pattern.

**Consequences:**  
One suspect is removed or detained, but the full case remains open. The station avoids full shutdown.

**Follow-up:**  
Strong. This is the best setup for a later investigation, because the players know they have not uncovered everything.

---

## Outcome 3 – Quiet Containment

The players uncover the truth but choose not to expose all of it.

They may decide that a full report would destroy the mission, bury the evidence in politics, or make it harder to identify the people behind the second theft route. They may quietly contain the local problem and keep part of the case unofficial.

**Consequences:**  
The station remains operational. The official report is incomplete. Some suspects may cooperate privately.

**Follow-up:**  
Excellent. This creates a strong offworld continuation, especially if one of the exposed suspects agrees to help.

---

## Outcome 4 – Breakdown

The players confront the wrong people, go public too early, or trigger evidence loss before securing the case.

This is not a failed ending, but an unstable one. Trust collapses, work becomes guarded, and the station may enter partial lockdown without a clean resolution.

**Consequences:**  
The local case remains unresolved or only partly resolved. The Consortium shifts toward damage control.

**Follow-up:**  
Strong, but messy. Best used if the next phase should focus on reconstruction, politics, and uncertain evidence.

---

## The Offworld Thread

The players should not be able to fully uncover the external network while still on Europa.

What they can learn is enough to prove that **Marek Chen** was acting for someone beyond the station. They may uncover:

- dead-drop deliveries on the orbital station
- suspicious contract extensions
- signs of outside influence over staffing or movement
- evidence that the recipient was not local

That is enough to continue the investigation, but not enough to close it.

---

## Cooperation from the Suspects

A continued investigation works best if at least one suspect cooperates.

**Chen** is the easiest to turn. If offered protection or leniency, he can provide drop procedures, timing, and what little he knows about the offworld contact.

**Soren** may cooperate if convinced that the real danger is no longer the audit, but the unknown network behind the second theft route.

**Verik** can help with logistics, station routines, and transfer habits if the players treat him as more than just a transport accomplice.

---

## Rewards

Rewards should fit the style of ending.

A clean official resolution may bring payment, formal recognition, and future Consortium work.

A deeper investigation may grant access to sealed records, sample data, personnel files, and offworld leads.

If the players handle the suspects well, the most useful reward may be continued cooperation from **Chen**, **Soren**, **Verik**, or **Dr. Sia Renn**.

---

## Recommended End-State

The strongest ending is usually this:

The players stabilize the local case, expose at least part of the truth, and learn that one theft route leads beyond Europa. The immediate problem is contained, but the larger network remains active.

That gives the one-shot a clear conclusion and leaves room for follow-up.
