## Overview

_Containment Breach – Europa_ is a closed-environment investigation scenario set aboard a long-duration research sub operating beneath the ice of [[Europa]]. The players arrive as an independent inspection team sent by the [[Jovian League Scientific Consortium]] after small but persistent discrepancies are detected in biological sample accounting.

Officially, the mission is a routine audit.

In reality, the discrepancy is real, biologically significant, and the result of **two separate theft operations** unfolding at the same time for completely different reasons.

Neither perpetrator knows the other is acting.

That is the core of the scenario.

Each theft route is small enough that it likely would have remained hidden on its own. The audit only happens because both operations overlap and push the total variance beyond acceptable tolerance.

The players are not entering a station in open crisis. They are entering a functioning system under strain, where procedural order still exists, work continues, and most crew members believe nothing more is happening than an inconvenient audit.

---

## The Core Truth

The missing material is not the result of one conspiracy, one saboteur, or one system failure.

It is the combined effect of:

- **Dr. Ilya Soren**, Senior Biochemist, who is quietly diverting selected biologically valuable material out of the Europa system through the transfer chain
- **Dr. Marek Chen**, Senior Analyst for Sequencing and Classification, who is separately stealing small samples under coercion during shore leaves

These two operations are unrelated in motive, method, and destination.

That difference matters. The scenario works best when the players first identify one explanation, believe they have solved the case, and only later realize that the remaining discrepancy still does not make sense.

---

## Theft Operation One – Ilya Soren and Tomas Verik

### What is happening

**Dr. Ilya Soren** has identified a line of biochemical potential in Europa-derived material that may have long-term applications related to radiation resistance, mitigation, or protection. He has not openly published or emphasized this interpretation. Instead, he has focused official attention on more acceptable and less politically explosive areas such as cryoprotection and immunomodulation.

He has not fabricated his work. He has framed it selectively.

At the same time, he has been preparing selected material for removal from the system.

He is assisted by **Tomas Verik**, the external transfer technician and pilot of the small resupply sub that moves people, supplies, and approved material between the station and the deeper research sub.

Together they exploit the return flow of sterilized or recoverable equipment. Material that should ultimately be destroyed is instead concealed within legitimate technical return pathways and moved upward through the system.

### Why Soren is doing it

Soren is not motivated by greed.

He previously worked with, trained with, or maintained strong ties to an **independent Belt-based research center**. He believes that if the real significance of this biochemical line becomes visible inside official channels, it will be restricted, patented, buried in review processes, or controlled by institutions that do not prioritize the people most affected by chronic radiation exposure.

He sees himself as preventing institutional capture.

### Why Tomas helps

**Tomas Verik** is Belt-born and has personal reasons to care about radiation damage. He has lost people to it and believes that if a useful line of research exists below the ice, it should not disappear into distant controlled systems that will delay access or price out the people who need it most.

He is not a criminal professional. He is a practical man helping a cause he believes is justified.

### Why this route is hard to detect

The material flow is small, distributed, and embedded in legitimate systems. The clues point toward:

- overuse of specific microfluidic cartridges
- questionable use of encapsulation-related equipment
- irregular classification of returnable equipment
- suspicious transfer-sub behavior by Tomas Verik

This is the cleaner and more coherent theft route. It is the one players are most likely to identify first.

## Theft Operation Two – Marek Chen

### What is happening

**Dr. Marek Chen** is independently stealing small quantities of newly classified biological material from the analysis and sequencing chain.

Unlike Soren, he is not acting from conviction. He is being coerced.

Earlier in his career, Chen falsified or manipulated research results in order to secure professional advancement. Someone knows this and has enough evidence to expose him. He does not know the full identity of the group behind the blackmail. He only knows that they have leveraged his fear, that he has been given clear collection instructions, and that he has not been allowed to stop.

He removes small samples over time and transfers them during shore leaves using dead-drop style delivery on the station above.

### Why he is hard to read

Chen looks like an overworked analyst with poor documentation discipline. That is partly true. He is exhausted, stressed, and error-prone. His logs are genuinely messy in places.

That makes him a strong secondary suspect only after the players begin comparing data carefully.

His route produces clues such as:

- mismatches between sample pulls and sequencing records
- patterns in documentation errors
- correlation with shore leave timing
- irregular signs of recent implant access
- suspiciously extended assignment duration on Europa

This route is smaller, dirtier, and harder to understand than Soren’s operation. It should feel wrong in a different way.

## Why the Audit Happens Now

The [[Jovian League Scientific Consortium]] notices a small but consistent discrepancy between collected and destroyed biological material.

On its own, either theft route likely would have remained hidden inside normal noise, sloppiness, or tolerated inefficiency.

But because both thefts happen at the same time, the numbers no longer close. The variance remains small, but it crosses tolerance often enough to trigger concern.

The report is filed by **Dr. Sia Renn**, Biosecurity Officer.

Renn does not know the full truth. She only knows that the destruction totals do not match what should have remained inside the system, and that any unexplained loss of Europa-derived biological material is unacceptable.

That is enough to justify a discreet inspection.

---

## What the Consortium Knows

At the start of the scenario, the [[Jovian League Scientific Consortium]] knows only the following:

- minor discrepancies exist in sample accounting
- the variance is outside acceptable tolerance when aggregated
- there is no confirmed contamination event
- there is no evidence yet of a full systems failure
- the matter should be handled quietly if possible

The Consortium does **not** know:

- that there are two separate theft operations
- that one route is ideological and Belt-linked
- that the second route is driven by coercion and tied to an external network
- that material may already have left the local system through more than one channel

The players therefore arrive with real authority, but incomplete understanding.

---

## What Most of the Crew Knows

Most crew members think the players are conducting a normal audit.

Only **Dr. Sia Renn** understands that the discrepancy may represent a serious biosafety issue. Even she does not know the real mechanism.

The rest of the crew falls into three broad categories:

**Innocent but stressed personnel** such as **Dr. Talia Neves**, **Nika Venn**, and **Dr. Jonas Krell**, who have real weaknesses, secrets, or procedural issues that can look suspicious.

**Institutional stabilizers** such as **Prof. Dr. Lena Orlov**, **Captain Daren Vale**, **Pavel Idris**, and **Dr. Keira Solheim**, who are not guilty but may withhold, narrow, or formalize access if the players become disruptive.

**Actual perpetrators** such as **Soren**, **Chen**, and **Tomas**, each of whom responds differently once pressure is applied.

## The Structure of the Investigation

The scenario is built as a sandbox. Players should be able to move freely, compare records, inspect rooms, question crew, and split up.

The investigation produces three kinds of information:

**Real leads** that point toward the two theft routes  
**False leads** that emerge from cultivation chaos, personnel stress, and bureaucratic defensiveness  
**Minor mysteries** such as substance misuse, hidden relationships, and supply diversion that create believable noise

The ideal progression is:

1. players understand the system
2. players identify one plausible culprit chain
3. players think the case is solved
4. one remaining inconsistency reveals that the first explanation was incomplete
5. confrontation forces decisions before the next transfer cycle

---

## The Role of the Ship Layout

The layout of the research sub reinforces the scenario logic.

The vessel is divided into:

**Clean areas** for command, crew life, and medical support  
**Contaminated areas** for intake and laboratory work  
**A central transfer spine** with biosafety checkpoints, changing rooms, and docking access

This means that movement, sample handling, and evidence destruction all pass through understandable physical bottlenecks. That helps the GM keep the scenario grounded.

Front is command and control.  
Back is transfer and dock.  
The midpoint between clean and contaminated sections is controlled through biosafety checkpoints and changing areas.

That spatial logic is important because the players are not solving an abstract mystery. They are tracing behavior through a functioning technical system.

---

## Pressure and Escalation

The scenario begins quietly. There is no immediate lockdown, no outbreak, and no visible emergency.

Pressure builds in three ways:

**Procedural pressure** from the audit itself  
**Social pressure** as crew members become defensive, embarrassed, or suspicious  
**Timing pressure** from the next transfer cycle and the possibility that evidence may be altered or removed

If the players move too slowly, the guilty adapt.

If the players accuse too early, wrong people close ranks, side secrets surface, and the actual perpetrators start adjusting their behavior.

The scenario should feel increasingly unstable once the players stop observing and begin confronting.

---

## What Act 3 and Act 4 Are Really About

Act 3 is not just about naming suspects. It is about testing whether the players understand the system well enough to apply pressure without collapsing the evidence base.

Act 4 is not just about solving the theft. It is about deciding how much truth to expose, what risks matter most, and whether the case should continue beyond Europa.

The larger offworld network behind Chen’s coercion should not be fully solvable within the one-shot. Europa should reveal that it exists, not resolve it completely.

That gives the scenario a contained ending with optional continuation.

---

## Strongest Intended Experience

The strongest version of the scenario usually looks like this:

The players arrive expecting a routine procedural problem.  
They uncover a coherent theft route in biochemistry and the transfer chain.  
They confront it and believe they have the answer.  
Then the remaining discrepancy still does not fit.  
A second, uglier theft route appears through analysis records and shore leave patterns.  
The station becomes unstable under pressure.  
The players must decide whether to expose everything, contain the problem quietly, or pursue the offworld thread.

That structure gives the GM a scenario with real movement, multiple truths, and a final decision that matters.

---

## GM Summary

At its core, _Containment Breach – Europa_ is a scenario about a functioning research system being compromised in two different ways at once.

One theft is driven by conviction.  
One theft is driven by fear.  
Both are small.  
Together they become visible.

The players are there because the numbers no longer close.

And by the time they understand why, they have already entered a system where every decision changes what remains possible.
