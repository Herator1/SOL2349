Use this page when you need an NPC fast.

A good improvised NPC usually only needs four things:

- **name**
- **role**
- **visible trait**
- **immediate motive**

That is enough to make them feel real in play.

## Quick NPC Formula

**[name] + [role] + [visible trait] + [motive]**

Example:

**Elena Okoye**

- **dock supervisor**
- **tired, controlled, never sits down**
- **needs this shift to end without outside attention**

That is already enough to run the character.

## Fast NPC Examples

### 1. Dock Pressure

**Malik Okoye**, **dock supervisor**, **smells of machine oil and keeps checking a damaged tablet**, **wants the problem contained before headquarters notices**.

### 2. Quiet Medic

**Hana Becker**, **medic**, **quiet, careful, observant**, **is trying to keep people calm while hiding that supplies are running out**.

### 3. Wrong Authority

**Tomas Walker**, **administrator**, **underdressed for the authority he claims**, **wants to shift blame before the inspection team arrives**.

### 4. Salvage Contact

**Yara Navarro**, **salvage operator**, **dry humor at the worst possible time**, **needs the players to recover something before someone else realizes what it is**.

## Fast Social Read

If you need an NPC reaction instantly, start here:

- **helpful but cautious**
- **professional and distant**
- **nervous and defensive**
- **cooperative if paid**
- **lying badly**
- **lying well**
- **angry but still useful**
- **trying to leave**

## Good Defaults for SOL 2349

When in doubt, NPCs should feel:

- pressured
- competent enough to matter
- limited by their role
- more complicated than their first impression

The best quick NPCs feel like people already stuck inside a problem before the players arrived.

## In One Sentence

A good improvised NPC in _SOL 2349_ needs a role, a visible edge, and a motive that already existed before the scene began.
