I deliberately keep this section a bit anonymous. Not because there is anything secret going on, but because this project is not about me as a public figure. It is about the setting.

That said, a bit of context probably helps.

In my spare time, I am very much what you would call a **nerd**.

Science fiction and fantasy have been a constant part of my life for as long as I can remember. I read, I play games, I watch films and series, I listen to audiobooks, and I spend far too much time thinking about fictional worlds that do not exist. I also play tabletop RPGs and pen-and-paper games, do tabletop wargaming, experiment with 3D printing, fly drones as a hobby, and occasionally still attend LAN parties — yes, those still exist.

In short: if it is even remotely nerdy, I am probably interested in it.

Professionally, things look a bit different, but not completely unrelated.

I am a scientist by training, with a background in biotechnology and a PhD in chemistry / genetic toxicology. I work at a very hands-on university institute that focuses on real-world problems, combining chemistry, toxicology, material science, environmental science, and other disciplines. It is a very interdisciplinary environment, and that way of thinking has definitely shaped how I approach worldbuilding.

These days, I am heading a research center, which mostly means writing project proposals, managing funding, and making sure people stay employed. That is my main responsibility — keeping the system running. I have published peer-reviewed papers, but I am also very aware that I am not on a path to a Nobel Prize. And that is fine. If anything, I would much rather see colleagues succeed at that level, even if that is not very realistic in the kind of applied science we are doing.

Outside of work, I have a fairly normal life: I am married, have two kids, and live in the suburbs of a major European capital. Which is also the point where this connects back to the project.

Time is limited.

On most days, I have maybe **around one hour** of spare time that I can realistically invest into **SOL 2349**. Without AI, this project would move at a completely different pace, and most of what you see here would simply not exist yet.

So this project sits right at the intersection of two things:

- a long-standing fascination with science fiction, systems, and worldbuilding
- and a very limited amount of actual time to turn those ideas into something structured and usable

Everything in this wiki comes out of that combination.
