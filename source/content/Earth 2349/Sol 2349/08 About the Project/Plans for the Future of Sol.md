Like everything else in this project, this page is less a polished master plan and more an honest look at where **SOL 2349** currently stands and where I would like to take it.

The short version is this:

**The project is still in a relatively early state.**  
A lot already exists, but a lot is still missing, unfinished, untested, or only half-connected. The basic direction is clear, but there is still a huge amount of work between “interesting project” and “fully usable game setting.”

So this page is partly roadmap, partly wish list, and partly reality check.

## 1. Current State: Alpha

Right now, I would clearly describe **SOL 2349** as being in an **alpha state**.

That does not mean there is nothing there — quite the opposite. A large amount of worldbuilding already exists, many core ideas are in place, and major parts of the setting are already written down in some form. But the whole thing is still far from complete.

A lot of initial worldbuilding is still incomplete.  
The rules are only partly tested.  
Many pages still need to be expanded, cleaned up, connected, crosslinked, and visually improved.  
A lot of concepts exist in rough or semi-finished form, but not yet in a version I would call complete.

Right now, the main goal is simple:

**complete the world.**

That does **not** mean documenting every single station, habitat, company, person, and corner of the Solar System. That would be impossible, and honestly not even useful. But I do want the setting to reach a point where the entire Solar System feels meaningfully covered — not in total detail, but in all the important areas.

That includes the obvious things:

- major planets and habitats
- major factions and corporations
- infrastructure
- military and economic actors
- daily life in different parts of the system
- technology
- politics
- travel
- conflict zones
- institutions

And yes, that also includes the less flashy stuff.

Sometimes a setting only feels alive when it includes things that seem boring at first glance — like regulatory authorities, oversight bodies, standards agencies, administrative systems, legal structures, and institutional machinery. Those things may not look exciting until they suddenly become very exciting because corporate agents are infiltrating them, laws are being changed behind the scenes, and players have to decide whether to prevent that, profit from it, or pretend not to notice.

That kind of thing matters to me.

So for now, the biggest task is not “add more epic lore.”  
It is: **finish the foundations**.

## 2. The Goal: a Complete New-Player Package

Once the world itself feels much more complete, the next big goal is making **SOL 2349** actually easy to play for people who are not already living inside my head.

That means I do **not** just want a lore wiki.  
I want a package that lets a random person land on the website, gather a few friends, and actually run a game.

That is still a long way off, but it is the target.

The ideal version would include:

- a clear introduction to the setting
- a usable rules core
- character creation that actually works at the table
- pre-made characters
- NPCs
- GM tools
- tables
- quick references
- starter material
- enough world information to support play without overwhelming new people

Most importantly, I want a set of **well-developed starting adventures**.

Right now, I have the rough beginnings of that, but they are not finished yet. The current plan is to eventually provide several strongly developed **one-shot intros**, probably something like **five to eight** in total, each built around a different kind of typical **SOL 2349** scenario.

That matters because the setting is broad, and I do not want every first session to feel the same.

A good intro package should show different sides of the world:

- station survival
- exploration
- research gone wrong
- cloud-city intrigue
- security or military action
- salvage
- industrial conflict
- political or corporate tension

At the moment, I have two early one-shot concepts — **Cloudfall** and **Ghost Station** — but neither is really finished. A third scenario is currently in development and is meant to be a bit more combat-oriented.

Beyond that, I would also like to build:

- around **two medium campaigns** designed to fill several evenings of play
- at least **one major campaign** that pushes forward larger plotlines in the setting

At the moment, the most likely candidate for that larger campaign would deal with the deeper history of the AI war, transhumanism, and human modification. That part of the lore is already important in the background, and it has the potential to support a campaign that feels both personal and system-wide at the same time.

A lot of the tools needed for this still only exist in my head.  
But that is clearly where I want the project to go.

## 3. Monetization: Probably Not

This one is simple.

I do **not** currently plan to seriously monetize **SOL 2349**.

Honestly, I chase funding and money for projects in my professional life all the time. I do **not** want to spend my spare time doing the same thing again. I do not want this project to turn into a second job built around product strategy, pricing, marketing, customer expectations, release pressure, and all the other things that come with monetization.

That is not what this is for.

So as things stand now, the plan is very straightforward:

**The core rules, the world, the lore, and the pre-made stories will remain free indefinitely.**

Or, more realistically:

They will remain free for as long as I keep the wiki online and do not accidentally let the whole thing disappear into internet oblivion.

Could that ever change?  
Technically, yes.

If the project somehow became much bigger than I realistically expect, I could imagine releasing additional material at some point — maybe novels, short stories, or extra sourcebooks. But I consider that extremely unlikely. I would put that at something like a **one percent scenario**, if that.

So no — this is not being built as a business plan.  
It is being built because I want it to exist.

## 4. Future Scenarios Beyond 2349

This is where things get more speculative.

The **2349** setting is meant to be the core version of the project. That is the main scenario, the foundation, and the place where most of the visible worldbuilding effort goes.

But the world behind it is bigger than that.

There is deeper lore in **SOL 2349** — including themes of cosmic scale that will remain mostly invisible, indirect, or only half-glimpsed in the core 2349 scenario. That is intentional. I do not want the main setting to collapse under the weight of its deepest mysteries.

At the same time, I already know that I do **not** want 2349 to be the only possible era forever.

Once the core scenario is actually finished, I would love to explore at least **two additional future settings**.

### Sol 2600

The first would be something like **Sol 2600**.

That scenario would probably begin to deal with:

- the first colonization of other star systems
- much larger-scale transhumanism
- deeper political and technological divergence
- the expansion of humanity beyond its old Solar System limits
- the first real confrontation with truths that are only hinted at in 2349

Even there, I suspect the **Solar System would still remain the main center of gravity**, at least for a long time. I am not interested in simply abandoning Sol and turning the setting into generic galactic sci-fi.

The focus would still be on the home system — but the edges of the map would begin to open, and the wider truth about the galaxy might begin to become partially visible.

That said, I already know what I **do not** want that revelation to be.

It is **not** a simulation.  
That would be boring.

It is also **not** just the Dark Forest.  
And it is not going to suddenly become **Star Trek**, **Star Wars**, or **Warhammer 40,000** in disguise.

If this future scenario happens, it still has to feel like a logical continuation of **SOL 2349**, not like a genre swap.

### Sol 3200

And then, eventually, there is the idea of a much later setting — something like **Sol 3200**.

This is where things could get much wilder.

If **2349** is the grounded industrial age, and **2600** is the age of first expansion beyond Sol, then **3200** could be the point where I deliberately test how far the setting can stretch without breaking.

That might be the place where hard science fiction, space opera, deep-time civilization, transhuman divergence, and cosmic horror all begin to collide in a more direct way.

Or, put less elegantly:

**let’s see whether hard sci-fi and space opera can be forced into the same room without murdering each other.**

That is a very long-term idea, obviously.  
Right now it is more a direction than a plan.

But it is there.

## Final note

For the moment, the priority is still very clear:

**Finish 2349 first.**

Build the world properly.  
Make it playable.  
Make it coherent.  
Make it usable for people who are not me.

Everything beyond that — campaigns, extra scenarios, future eras, cosmic revelations, far-future versions of the setting — only makes sense if the foundation is solid.

That is the real job right now.
