Megacorporations in SOL 2349 rarely rely on open force alone. Their visible power comes from contracts, infrastructure, patents, energy networks, logistics, and financial leverage. But across the solar system, most major corporate actors also maintain a second layer of influence: **unofficial, deniable, and deliberately obscured**.

These structures are commonly referred to as **Corporate Shadow Networks**.

They are not formal departments, and they rarely appear on organizational charts. Instead, they exist in the grey zone between corporate security, private intelligence, criminal subcontracting, and political interference. Some are built in-house through compartmentalized subsidiaries and security divisions. Others are outsourced to intermediaries who provide distance, plausible deniability, and the option to sever ties when necessary.

Their purpose is simple: to do what must be done when contracts, lawsuits, and formal pressure are no longer enough.

Corporate shadow networks are used for sabotage, coercion, industrial espionage, blackmail, labor disruption, targeted data theft, witness intimidation, and the quiet removal of inconvenient people, systems, or evidence. In some cases they also support smuggling, pirate intermediaries, or privateer-style proxy groups operating against competitors in the Belt or outer system. Their methods are rarely theatrical. The preferred result is not public fear, but **quiet correction**.
