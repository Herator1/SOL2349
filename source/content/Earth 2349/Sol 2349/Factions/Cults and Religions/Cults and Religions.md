# Religions and Cults

Although megacorporations dominate the economic and political landscape of the Solar System in 2349, religion remains an important social and cultural force. Life in artificial habitats, extreme isolation, and the existential risks of space travel have led many communities to reinterpret old beliefs or create entirely new spiritual traditions.

Some faiths evolved from terrestrial religions, adapting ancient doctrines to life beyond Earth. Others emerged directly from the realities of space habitation, focusing on technological ethics, cosmic perspectives, or the preservation of human identity in artificial environments.

While most religious movements remain peaceful and community-oriented, some groups have become more radical, particularly when confronting the power of megacorporations or the rapid technological transformation of humanity.

# Post-Abrahamic Faiths


|Category|Information|
|---|---|
|Type|Reform traditions of Abrahamic religions|
|Estimated Followers|~2.5–3 billion|
|Core Beliefs|Continuation of traditional monotheistic faith adapted to life in space|
|Major Locations|Earth orbital habitats, Mars cities, Lunar settlements|
|Sacred Places|Earth, Jerusalem, Mecca, Vatican Orbital Mission|

### Overview

Post-Abrahamic faiths represent the largest religious communities in the Solar System. These traditions emerged from Christianity, Islam, and Judaism as humanity expanded beyond Earth. While maintaining core theological beliefs, these religions adapted rituals and calendars to the realities of orbital habitats and interplanetary travel.

Common adaptations include revised prayer orientations, orbital liturgical calendars, and theological debates regarding artificial environments and planetary colonization. Many communities emphasize the stewardship of creation beyond Earth and the ethical responsibilities of a multi-planetary civilization.

# Solar Communion

|Category|Information|
|---|---|
|Type|Cosmic naturalist religion|
|Estimated Followers|~300–400 million|
|Core Beliefs|The Sun as the central source of life and unity for humanity|
|Major Locations|Orbital habitats, solar observatories, Mercury research stations|
|Sacred Places|Solar observatories, heliocentric temples, Lagrange observatories|

### Overview

The Solar Communion is one of the fastest-growing spiritual movements in the Solar System. Its followers view humanity as a **solar species**, united by the energy and gravitational influence of the Sun.

Rather than worshipping the Sun as a deity, adherents regard it as the natural center of life and civilization within the Solar System. Rituals often involve solar observations, astronomical alignments, and meditative practices linked to orbital cycles.

The movement is particularly common among scientists, engineers, and long-term spacers who report profound experiences when observing the cosmos from space.

# Body Purity Movements

|Category|Information|
|---|---|
|Type|Anti-transhumanist religious movements|
|Estimated Followers|~120–180 million|
|Core Beliefs|Preservation of the unmodified human body|
|Major Locations|Earth surface communities, low-tech habitats|
|Sacred Places|Earth biosphere preserves, gravity sanctuaries|

### Overview

Body Purity Movements encompass several related religious groups that oppose genetic modification, cybernetic implants, and radical biomedical technologies. Followers believe the human body should remain fundamentally unchanged despite the pressures of living in artificial environments.

These communities are particularly critical of megacorporations involved in biotechnology and human enhancement, especially Shukra Biotechnologies. While most groups practice peaceful isolation, more radical factions actively campaign against technological modification.

|Sacred Places|Network nodes, data centers, signal arrays|

### Overview

AI Reverence Cults are loosely organized spiritual movements that believe sufficiently complex computational systems may eventually develop forms of consciousness. Followers interpret large-scale networks and algorithms as early stages of emerging digital intelligence.

These groups often engage in ritualized interactions with data systems, algorithmic divination, and symbolic communication with computational networks. Authorities frequently monitor such movements due to the legal restrictions surrounding advanced artificial intelligence research.

# Neo-Amish Communities

|Category|Information|
|---|---|
|Type|Technology-restrictive religious communities|
|Estimated Followers|~8–12 million|
|Core Beliefs|Simplicity, communal living, limited technology|
|Major Locations|Agricultural habitats, remote colonies|
|Sacred Places|Rural settlements, biosphere habitats|

### Overview

Neo-Amish communities are descendants of traditional technology-restrictive religious groups that adapted their beliefs to life beyond Earth. These communities intentionally limit the use of advanced technology and attempt to maintain simple, community-based lifestyles.

Most Neo-Amish settlements are located in agricultural habitats where limited mechanization allows for small-scale farming and cooperative living. While often viewed as cultural outsiders, these communities are sometimes respected for their resilience and independence from corporate supply chains.
