# 2. Overview

**Atlas Orbital Industries (AOI)** is the largest spacecraft manufacturer and orbital construction company in the Solar System. The corporation specializes in the design and construction of **large-scale space infrastructure**, including interplanetary cargo vessels, orbital habitats, industrial stations, and deep-space platforms.

Founded during the rapid expansion of cis-lunar industry in the late 22nd century, Atlas quickly became the dominant builder of heavy spacecraft and large structural systems. Many of the Solar System’s largest stations, including mining hubs, orbital shipyards, and industrial complexes, were designed or constructed by Atlas engineering teams.

Unlike most megacorps, Atlas operates primarily as a **project-based engineering company**. Its projects often take decades to complete and require coordination with multiple other corporations, particularly Helios Heavy Industries for energy infrastructure and NovaChem Industries for advanced materials and environmental systems.

As a result, Atlas plays a critical role in enabling the **physical expansion of human civilization into space**.

# 3. Key Facilities

**Copernicus Lagrange Complex (Cis-Lunar L2)**  
The corporate headquarters and largest engineering hub of Atlas Orbital Industries. This massive facility coordinates ship design, structural engineering, and large-scale construction projects across the Solar System.

**Hephaestus Megadock (Cis-Lunar Space)**  
One of the largest drydock complexes ever built, capable of assembling kilometer-scale spacecraft and orbital habitats.

**Mars Orbital Shipyards**  
Construction facilities building freighters, industrial vessels, and station modules for the expanding Martian economy.

**Belt Construction Platforms**  
Mobile construction platforms used to assemble mining stations, refinery hubs, and transport infrastructure in the Asteroid Belt.

# 4. Example Products

- Interplanetary cargo vessels and heavy freighters
- Orbital habitats and station modules
- Deep-space construction platforms
- Fusion-drive engine frames and radiator assemblies
- Structural trusses and pressure hulls for large spacecraft

Many of the Solar System’s largest ships and stations carry **Atlas-built structural frameworks**, even when other corporations provide propulsion or life-support systems.

# 5. What Makes Atlas Unique

Atlas is known for building **enormous projects that no other corporation can realistically attempt**.

These include:

- kilometer-scale freighters
- rotating habitats
- large orbital drydocks
- planetary infrastructure projects

The company’s engineers often describe their work not as manufacturing but as **space architecture**.

Many Atlas engineers are highly respected across the industry, and the corporation maintains one of the most prestigious engineering cultures in the Solar System.

# 6. Controversies and Conflicts

Because Atlas controls many of the Solar System’s largest construction facilities, the corporation has been repeatedly accused of **price manipulation and infrastructure monopolies**. Smaller shipbuilders often claim that Atlas dominates access to large drydocks and fabrication yards.

Several accidents during the construction of large orbital stations have also raised concerns about safety practices in deep-space construction environments, where crews may work for years in isolated shipyard complexes.

Atlas has also faced political pressure due to its ability to effectively **delay or accelerate major infrastructure projects**, giving the corporation significant leverage over megacorps and planetary economies that depend on new stations or transport hubs.
