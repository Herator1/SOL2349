## 1. Overview

**Helios Heavy Industries** is one of the largest industrial megacorporations in the Solar System and a cornerstone of the interplanetary energy and resource economy. The company operates extensive solar energy infrastructure near Mercury, large-scale asteroid mining operations, and numerous orbital refining facilities that supply raw materials to industrial hubs across the system.

Helios presents itself as a stable and responsible provider of critical infrastructure. Its public image emphasizes reliability, technological competence, and long-term stewardship of the Solar System’s industrial expansion. As a result, the corporation maintains strong relationships with many habitats, stations, and other megacorps that depend on its energy and material supply.

Internally, however, Helios operates with a strictly pragmatic corporate philosophy where profitability and operational continuity take precedence above all other concerns. Management culture within the company is highly competitive and efficiency-driven, and decisions are typically guided by economic performance metrics rather than ideological or ethical considerations.

Despite this reputation for internal ruthlessness, Helios generally avoids overt conflicts and instead prefers **careful risk management, quiet settlements, and controlled damage containment** when problems arise.

## 2. Key Facilities

**Helios HQ (Earth Orbit)** – Corporate headquarters and financial center overseeing the company’s global operations.

**Mercury Solar Array** – Vast orbital solar collectors near Mercury that generate and transmit energy throughout the inner Solar System.

**Helios Refinery Rings** – Large industrial orbital facilities where raw asteroid materials are processed into industrial metals and fuel.

## 3. Example Products

- Large-scale solar energy transmission systems
- Refined metals from asteroid mining operations
- Industrial propellants and fuel for spacecraft
- Structural materials used in orbital construction

## 4. Controversies and Conflicts

Helios has occasionally faced criticism regarding labor conditions in remote mining installations and the environmental impact of large-scale orbital industry. Several accidents in asteroid mining operations have led to investigations and compensation disputes with contractor crews.

The corporation typically handles such incidents through internal investigations and negotiated settlements, which critics argue limits transparency. Helios maintains that its safety standards and operational procedures remain among the most advanced in the Solar System’s heavy industry sector.
