## 1. Overview

The **Kaiten Logistics Collective (KLC)** is one of the largest interplanetary transport networks in the Solar System. Originally formed as a union of freighter crews during the early asteroid mining era, Kaiten gradually evolved into a massive cooperative controlling significant parts of the **Solar System’s cargo infrastructure**.

Unlike traditional megacorporations such as Helios Interstellar or Shukra Biotechnologies, Kaiten operates as a **worker-owned transport guild**. Crews, captains, and dockworkers are collective members who share ownership and influence through the Route Council.

Kaiten’s philosophy is rooted in the **Spacer Solidarity Code**, an informal set of principles emphasizing mutual aid between crews, protection of workers, and independence from corporate exploitation.

Despite its cooperative structure, Kaiten is known for aggressively defending its routes and infrastructure. Strikes, blockades, and “navigation incidents” have historically been used to discourage competitors from entering Kaiten-controlled logistics corridors.

## 2. Key Facilities

**Ceres Free Port** – The administrative and logistical center of the Collective and the largest freight hub in the Asteroid Belt.

**The Spine** – A chain of Kaiten-operated fuel depots and service stations spread across major Belt transport routes.

**Outer Dockyards** – Large repair and retrofit facilities servicing freighters and long-haul cargo vessels.

## 3. Example Services

- Interplanetary bulk cargo transport
- Fuel and resupply services for long-range vessels
- Freight convoy coordination
- Emergency salvage and rescue operations

## 4. Controversies and Conflicts

Kaiten has frequently been accused by rival corporations of operating as a **de facto transport monopoly** in parts of the Asteroid Belt.

Several incidents involving damaged or missing competitor vessels have been informally linked to Kaiten’s rumored **Route Marshals**, though the Collective officially denies the existence of such enforcement units.
