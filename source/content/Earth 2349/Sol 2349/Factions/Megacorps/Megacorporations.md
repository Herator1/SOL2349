Macorporations have become the dominant political and economic actors of the Solar System**. As humanity expanded beyond Earth, traditional nation-states gradually lost their ability to directly control distant infrastructure, orbital industry, and off-world settlements. Into that vacuum stepped the interplanetary corporations. What began as commercial operators of transport lines, mining concessions, and industrial platforms evolved over time into something far more powerful: institutions that do not merely participate in civilization, but **operate large parts of it**.

In 2349, most stations, mining complexes, shipyards, orbital facilities, research platforms, and industrial habitats function under some form of corporate administration. Megacorps control energy production, freight movement, chemical supply chains, medical systems, network infrastructure, and large sections of the labor economy. In many parts of the Solar System, especially beyond Earth, they are not simply businesses. They are the entities that keep air flowing, ships moving, food arriving, and systems online.

As a result, megacorps function simultaneously as **employers, regulators, infrastructure owners, and governing authorities** within their domains. They maintain internal legal frameworks, private security forces, labor systems, customs procedures, and administrative hierarchies. In some cases, they provide comparatively stable and efficient governance. In others, they rule through dependency, contract law, and selective enforcement, caring less about public legitimacy than about uninterrupted throughput.

This does not mean the megacorps are unified or identical. Far from it. Each developed under different historical and economic conditions, and each projects a different culture into the worlds it influences. Some present themselves as responsible custodians of progress and stability. Others are openly profit-driven, aggressive, or deeply embedded in legally grey environments. Some are highly technocratic, while others rely on diffuse contractor webs, financial leverage, or political relationships. Their internal styles differ—but their scale ensures that all of them shape the lives of millions.

Competition between the megacorps is one of the defining political forces of the Solar System. They compete over **resources, contracts, patents, logistics corridors, labor access, industrial monopolies, and strategic infrastructure**. Some rivalries remain commercial. Others spill into sabotage, proxy action, black-budget operations, and indirect conflict through third parties. Open war between megacorps is rare, but continuous pressure, interference, and deniable confrontation are part of normal interplanetary life.

For ordinary people, megacorps are impossible to ignore. They determine where people work, what systems they depend on, what treatments they can afford, how they travel, and in many cases which laws apply to them. In some regions they are seen as engines of civilization. In others, as the structures that replaced governments without ever fully becoming accountable.

What is no longer in question is their importance.

The Solar System of 2349 does not merely contain megacorporations.  
It is, in many ways, **built around them**.

# List of Megacorporations

### [[Shukra]]

A leading biotech and pharmaceutical corporation headquartered on [[Mars]]. Shukra dominates sectors such as genetic engineering, advanced medicine, agricultural biotechnology, and planetary biosystems, and is one of the most influential scientific-industrial actors in human space.

### [[Helios]]

A major energy and resource conglomerate with critical operations on [[Mercury]] and across the inner system. Helios controls mining flows, power generation networks, transmission infrastructure, and large sections of the industrial energy economy.

### [[Kaiten]]

One of the most important transport and logistics corporations in the Solar System. Kaiten operates freight corridors, supply chains, shipping coordination systems, and interplanetary cargo networks that connect planets, stations, and industrial zones across vast distances.

### [[Archeron]]

A corporation specializing in arms manufacturing, private security, military systems, and high-risk contract operations. Archeron also maintains a significant presence in trauma medicine, veteran rehabilitation, and advanced limb replacement technologies.

### [[Solaris]]

A financial-services giant whose influence extends through insurance, credit systems, investment structures, and interplanetary risk management. Solaris shapes entire sectors of the economy not through direct production, but through capital allocation, debt, and financial dependency.

### [[NovaChem]]

A major chemical and life-support corporation with strong influence on [[Venus]] and [[Luna]]. NovaChem is central to atmospheric processing, industrial chemistry, pharmaceuticals, polymers, and the systems that keep large-scale human habitation functioning.

### [[Nyx Data Systems]]

A leading information infrastructure corporation specializing in networks, secure processing, communications architecture, datacenters, and high-level system coordination. Nyx forms part of the invisible digital backbone of the inner system.

### [[Atlas]]

A major space construction and orbital engineering corporation. Atlas builds ships, stations, modular habitats, drydock systems, and large-scale structural infrastructure, making it one of the most important industrial actors in orbital civilization.

# Role in the Solar System

Taken together, the megacorps form a second layer of sovereignty across human space. Planets and alliances still matter, but much of daily life is shaped by organizations whose authority comes not from elections or treaties, but from **ownership of infrastructure**.

They do not rule the Solar System openly as a single bloc. They compete, interfere, and constrain each other. But between them, they control enough of the systems that matter—energy, transport, medicine, finance, communications, construction, and security—that no major political or economic order can exist without them.

In practice, they are not above the system.  
They are part of the structure holding it together.
