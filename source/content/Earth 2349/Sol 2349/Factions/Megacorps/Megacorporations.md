By the mid-24th century, **megacorporations have become the dominant political and economic actors of the Solar System**. Following the gradual decline of traditional nation-states during the expansion into space, large interplanetary companies assumed responsibility for critical infrastructure such as energy production, transport networks, medical systems, and industrial supply chains. In practice, most space habitats, mining operations, research stations, and orbital industries operate under corporate administration.

Megacorps function simultaneously as **employers, regulators, and governing authorities** within their domains. They maintain internal legal systems, security forces, and administrative structures that regulate everything from labor contracts to access to life-support resources. While some corporations project an image of responsible stewardship and technological progress, others operate in legally ambiguous environments where oversight is limited and enforcement depends largely on corporate interests.

Despite their immense power, megacorps are not monolithic entities. They differ widely in culture, governance, and ideology: some operate as traditional profit-driven corporations, others as technocratic research institutions or cooperative industrial networks. Competition between these organizations—over resources, transport routes, research breakthroughs, and strategic infrastructure—forms one of the defining political dynamics of the Solar System in 2349.
