# 1. Overview

**NovaChem Industries (NCI)** is one of the most pervasive industrial corporations in the Solar System. While less visible than energy conglomerates or transport collectives, the company produces a vast range of **chemical systems and materials essential for maintaining artificial environments in space**.

NovaChem specializes in the chemistry that makes space habitation possible: atmospheric control compounds, water purification processes, industrial polymers, propellant additives, and nutrient synthesis systems used in closed-loop habitats. Many stations and colonies rely on NovaChem standards for air composition, environmental stabilization, and synthetic food production.

Because these systems operate largely in the background, NovaChem’s influence is often described as **structural rather than political**. The company rarely engages in open corporate conflicts, but its products are embedded in infrastructure across the Solar System.
This position has led to persistent speculation that NovaChem possesses the ability to **quietly influence or destabilize critical life-support systems**, although the corporation strongly denies any such capabilities.

# 2. Key Facilities

**Tycho Industrial Complex (Luna)** – Corporate headquarters and one of the largest chemical production centers in the inner Solar System.

**Aphrodite Atmospheric Platforms (Venus)** – Floating industrial refineries extracting and processing gases and sulfur compounds from the Venusian atmosphere.

**Kraken Chemical Fields (Titan)** – Large-scale hydrocarbon extraction and synthesis installations using Titan’s methane and ethane seas to produce advanced polymers and fuels.

**Mars Polymer Works** – Industrial plants supplying structural materials and environmental control chemicals for Martian infrastructure.

# 3. Example Products

- **NCI Atmos™** – Standardized atmospheric control mixtures used in orbital habitats
- **NutriBase™** – Synthetic nutrient compounds for closed-loop food production systems
- **SealFlex™** – High-performance polymers used in spacecraft and station hulls
- **CryoCool™** – Industrial cooling fluids used in reactors and propulsion systems
- **HydraCycle™** – Chemical water purification and recycling processes

NovaChem products are rarely branded for public visibility, but they form the **chemical backbone of most life-support and industrial systems** across the Solar System.

# 4. Controversies and Rumors

Although NovaChem maintains a reputation as a stable industrial supplier, its deep integration into critical infrastructure has led to ongoing rumors about its influence.

Several industrial accidents involving life-support malfunctions or chemical contamination have sparked speculation that NovaChem may possess the ability to **manipulate atmospheric systems or chemical supply chains** in corporate conflicts. None of these claims have been substantiated.

Critics argue that the corporation’s true power lies in the fact that **many stations cannot operate without NovaChem processes and compounds**, making the company one of the most quietly influential actors in the Solar System economy.
