## 1. Overview

**Solaris Financial Group (SFG)** is the largest financial and insurance provider in the Solar System. The corporation manages capital flows between megacorps, insures spacecraft and infrastructure, and provides loans for everything from asteroid mining operations to individual cargo vessels.

At its core, Solaris specializes in **risk modeling and predictive financial analytics**. By aggregating enormous quantities of economic, operational, and personal data, the company creates highly individualized insurance contracts and financial products tailored to specific clients, companies, or even individual crew members.

Solaris maintains an extremely polished public image centered on trust, stability, and opportunity. Massive marketing campaigns promote the company as the **financial backbone of interplanetary expansion**, offering flexible loans, cargo insurance, and investment services for both corporations and independent operators.

Behind this public image lies one of the largest data collection systems in human history. Solaris continuously gathers information from shipping records, trade networks, corporate contracts, station infrastructure logs, and personal financial histories to refine its predictive models.

## 2. Key Facilities

**Aurora Finance Hub (Venus Orbit)** – Corporate headquarters and one of the largest financial data centers in the Solar System.

**SolNet Analytics Core** – Massive distributed data processing network analyzing trade patterns, ship movements, and economic activity.

**Titan Risk Observatory** – Specialized modeling facility focused on long-term economic forecasting and planetary resource markets.

## 3. Example Products

- Interplanetary cargo insurance for freighters and transport companies
- Financing packages for asteroid mining operations
- Infrastructure investment funds for stations and habitats
- Personal insurance plans for long-duration space crews
- Predictive risk analytics used by megacorps and governments

Solaris also operates widely used **financial platforms and digital marketplaces** that allow corporations and individuals to trade commodities, contracts, and insurance instruments across the Solar System.

## 4. Controversies and Concerns

Solaris has been repeatedly criticized for the scale of its data collection operations. Many analysts believe the company possesses one of the most comprehensive economic intelligence networks in existence, allowing it to predict market movements and corporate activity with unusual precision.

Critics argue that the corporation’s ability to combine financial data with logistics and infrastructure information effectively makes Solaris a **central intelligence hub for the interplanetary economy**. The company denies these accusations, stating that its data systems are solely used to improve financial services and risk assessment.
