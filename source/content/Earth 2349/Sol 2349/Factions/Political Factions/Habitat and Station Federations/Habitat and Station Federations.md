# Habitat and Station Federations

Not all important political actors in **SOL 2349** are tied to planets. Many emerge from constellations of habitats, docks, stations, and orbital settlements that have learned to coordinate for survival, bargaining power, maintenance, or mutual defense.

These federations and compacts are shaped less by territory in the traditional sense than by pressure integrity, route access, repair capacity, and shared infrastructural dependence. In many parts of human space, they matter because they can keep air flowing, docking rights recognized, emergency support functioning, and local disputes from turning into systemic failure.

---

## Important Factions

### [[Cislunar Habitat League]]

A major association of larger civilian and mixed-use habitats across the **[[Earth]]–[[Luna]]** corridor. The League focuses on safety standards, maintenance norms, emergency response coordination, residency rights, and collective bargaining against monopolistic pressure from larger infrastructure actors.

Its members do not form a unified government, but together they represent one of the clearest civic voices in cislunar space. The League exists in constant tension with powerful actors such as **[[Earth]]**, **[[Luna]]**, **[[Atlas]]**, **[[Nyx]]**, and **[[NovaChem]]**, all of whom shape the corridor through infrastructure, regulation, or dependency. Where these powers think in terms of throughput and system stability, the League insists that habitats are not only nodes of movement, but places where people actually live.

---

### [[Belt Habitat Federation]]

A loose federation of major inhabited habitat complexes across the **[[Asteroid Belt]]**. It is one of the most important civil and political voices in the Belt beyond the direct influence of the megacorporations and major transport actors.

The Federation is primarily concerned with pressure safety, life-support continuity, labor mobility, emergency law, and dispute mediation between inhabited stations. It does not speak for every extraction site, industrial node, or free station in the Belt, but it does represent a large part of the permanently settled habitat population that depends on long-term stability rather than short-term contracts alone.

Its political importance lies in making the Belt legible as a lived region, not merely as an extraction zone.

---

### [[Free Stations Compact]]

A harder, more pragmatic alliance of independent stations, dock complexes, and service habitats spread across orbital and frontier corridors, especially in and around the **[[Asteroid Belt]]**. Compared with the more civic orientation of the [[Belt Habitat Federation]], the Compact is more contractual, operational, and openly transactional.

Its priorities include docking reciprocity, fuel access, salvage law, mutual defense, recognition of local claims, and practical coordination between independent stations that cannot rely on distant authorities to protect them. The Compact is less interested in ideal political legitimacy than in preserving enough order for stations to remain viable, defensible, and economically useful.

This makes it one of the most important grey-zone political actors in human space: neither lawless nor fully orderly, but essential to the functioning of regions where formal sovereignty remains thin.

---

### [[Outer Gate Network]]

A distributed alliance of refueling habitats, staging platforms, survey stations, rescue nodes, and long-range support facilities positioned along the routes leading toward the outer Solar System. It is not a government in any conventional sense, but an infrastructure federation built around continuity at distance.

The Network exists to keep long-haul movement possible: maintaining refueling access, emergency shelter, navigation support, rescue coordination, and the minimum conditions under which crews and cargo can continue outward toward **[[Jupiter]]**, **[[Saturn]]**, and beyond. Without it, the routes into the outer system would be far more fragile, expensive, and politically vulnerable.

Its power comes from a simple fact: in deep space, the actors who can keep a corridor alive often matter as much as those who formally claim to rule it.

---

### [[Sunward Station Accord]]

A loose compact of solar-near stations, relay habitats, industrial support docks, and research-service nodes operating across the harsh inner-system corridors closer to **[[Mercury]]**, **[[Venus]]**, and the high-energy traffic environment nearer the Sun.

The Accord focuses on extreme-environment standards, traffic coordination, solar hazard response, maintenance protocols, and the interoperability of stations operating under unusually severe thermal and radiation conditions. It is smaller and politically less visible than the larger cislunar and Belt federations, but it gives the inner system beyond the major planets a distinct political texture of its own.

Its existence reflects a recurring truth of **SOL 2349**: wherever humans build permanent life-support structures in difficult environments, some form of collective coordination eventually follows.
