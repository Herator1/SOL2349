The **Jovian League** is a loose political alliance of settlements and planetary states across the outer solar system, centered around the moons of Jupiter and Saturn. Formed during the later phases of human expansion beyond the asteroid belt, the League represents one of the few major political entities in the solar system that is not dominated by megacorporations or centralized planetary governments.

Rather than functioning as a unified state, the Jovian League operates more like a **federation of autonomous worlds**. Each member moon or settlement maintains a high degree of political independence while participating in shared institutions responsible for diplomacy, infrastructure coordination, and collective security.

The League’s founding principles emphasize **democratic governance, scientific cooperation, and political independence from corporate power**. This philosophy emerged partly in reaction to the increasing influence of megacorporations in the inner solar system. Many early outer-system colonies sought to create a political structure that would protect their autonomy and prevent corporate monopolies over resources and infrastructure.

As a result, the Jovian League maintains strict regulations on corporate ownership of critical infrastructure and natural resources. While corporations operate within League territory, large-scale economic control by megacorps is intentionally limited.

Militarily, the League maintains a **defense fleet** tasked primarily with protecting trade routes, infrastructure, and member worlds. This fleet is technologically advanced but relatively small compared to the military forces of the inner system. Its purpose is deterrence and security rather than power projection.

Economically, the League focuses heavily on **scientific research, exploration, and long-term infrastructure development** rather than short-term profit. Many of the solar system’s most important xenological, planetary science, and deep-space exploration programs originate from Jovian League institutions.

Life in the Jovian League is often considered stable and prosperous by outer-system standards. Although the region lacks the massive wealth and industrial scale of Earth or Venus, its citizens generally enjoy a **high standard of living, strong public institutions, and access to advanced education and healthcare**.

Politically, the League maintains diplomatic relations with all major factions of the solar system. Its position as a relatively neutral and scientifically focused alliance often allows it to act as a mediator in disputes between inner-system powers.

For many observers, the Jovian League represents one of the last strongholds of **democratic governance and cooperative exploration** in a solar system increasingly shaped by corporate interests and geopolitical rivalry.
