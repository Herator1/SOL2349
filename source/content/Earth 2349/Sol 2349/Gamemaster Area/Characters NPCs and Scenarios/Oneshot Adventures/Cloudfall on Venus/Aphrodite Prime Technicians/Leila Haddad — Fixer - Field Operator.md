## 🎭 Deception Module

**Cat:** Social  
**Type:** Neural / Cognitive  
**mSpace:** 2

A deception implant built for fast lies, plausible cover, and controlled misdirection under pressure.

### Cover Story

**Type:** Passive

You can rapidly construct a plausible false identity, explanation, or surface-level story that fits the immediate situation. This does not guarantee success, but it gives you a coherent false narrative instantly.

### False Signal

**Type:** Active  
**Cost:** 1 Action  
**Range:** 10 m  
**Load:** +1

Choose one visible target within range. Deliver a short, plausible false statement or misleading cue. If the lie is believable within the situation, the target treats it as true long enough for one immediate reaction, hesitation, or decision.

### Misdirection

**Type:** Augmented  
**Load:** +2

Until the start of your next turn, attempts to detect your deception, concealment, or false intent suffer **Setback**.

## 🖧 Systems Interface Node

**Cat:** Operations  
**Type:** Neural / Interface  
**mSpace:** 3

A neural control interface that lets you interact with nearby systems as if you were already plugged in.

### Remote Interface

**Type:** Passive

You may interact with a connected or exposed system within 20 m if you have line of sight, a valid access point, or a clear local interface path. This allows you to open or close doors, trigger panels, cycle locks, operate cameras, or activate simple mechanisms. It does **not** bypass security by itself.

### Fast Interface

**Type:** Augmented  
**Load:** +1  
**Limit:** 1× per round

Perform one simple system interaction without spending an Action. This may be used on your turn or reactively if a valid trigger exists.

### Extended Control

**Type:** Active  
**Cost:** 1 Action  
**Load:** +2

Take control of one simple connected system within range until the start of your next turn, such as a door, camera, lift control, local mechanism, or lightly secured sensor.

## 🧭 Notes

Leila is strongest when she can **shape the situation before anyone calls it a conflict**.

- she creates access rather than forcing entry
- she lies quickly and smoothly under pressure
- she can operate local systems without looking like a hacker
- she is excellent in controlled, social, urban environments like Aphrodite Prime

She is not built for brute force. She is built for **getting the team through the door, past the checkpoint, and out of the conversation before anyone realizes what really happened**.

## 📖 Optional Background Lore

Leila Haddad grew up inside the layered realities of Aphrodite Prime, where access matters more than ownership and where the difference between “allowed” and “possible” is often negotiable. She learned early that the city runs on procedure, but moves on favors, pressure, and timing.

She did not come out of formal corporate security or high administration. She grew in the spaces between them: contract mediation, local routing, service access, quiet logistics, and the kind of work that depends on knowing who can be convinced, who can be bypassed, and who should never be forced.

That makes her very good at moving through systems that were not built for openness.

It also means she knows exactly how fragile status is in a city like Aphrodite Prime. A wrong word in the wrong corridor can matter as much as a weapon.

Players stepping into Leila should focus on three things:

- speak like you belong
- keep every interaction under control
- use systems and people together, never separately
