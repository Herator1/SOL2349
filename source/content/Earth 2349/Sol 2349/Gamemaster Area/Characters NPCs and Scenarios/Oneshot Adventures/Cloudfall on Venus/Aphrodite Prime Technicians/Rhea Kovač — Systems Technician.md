## 🧭 Notes

Rhea is strongest when she can **understand the system before anyone else does**.

- excels at detecting inconsistencies in technical environments
- notices when system behavior and physical reality do not match
- best used to identify hidden logic, manipulated outputs, and unsafe assumptions
- not built for social leadership or direct confrontation

She is not there to overpower the scenario.

She is there to tell the group when the system is lying.

## 📖 Optional Background Lore

Rhea Kovač comes from the Belt, where systems are rarely elegant and never abstract. Life in those environments teaches a specific kind of technical thinking: if pressure, heat, load, or timing are wrong, the system may still look functional right up until the moment it stops being survivable.

That made her good at diagnostics early.

She built her career in the kind of jobs larger operators do not want to look at too closely — disputed maintenance, contradictory readings, unstable access infrastructure, systems that “should” be working according to the report but do not feel right in practice. She is not charismatic, and she does not try to be. What she has instead is the ability to sit with bad data longer than most people can tolerate and keep pulling on it until the pattern underneath becomes visible.

Aphrodite Prime is exactly the kind of place that bothers her. Not because it is unstable — but because it is sophisticated enough to make instability look intentional, acceptable, and clean.

Players stepping into Rhea should focus on three things:

- trust the discrepancy
- pay attention to what the system smooths over
- treat every “stable” output as something that still needs to be verified
