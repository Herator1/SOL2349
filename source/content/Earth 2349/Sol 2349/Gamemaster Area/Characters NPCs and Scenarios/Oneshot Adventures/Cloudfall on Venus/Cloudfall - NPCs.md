## 🧑‍💼 **Elena Varga**

_Infrastructure Optimization Director_

Elena Varga represents the system at its highest functional level. She is not an antagonist in the traditional sense, but the embodiment of system logic applied without compromise. She does not hide information. She does not manipulate emotionally. Instead, she reframes reality in terms of stability, efficiency, and necessity. To her, the system is not flawed — it is operating within constraints that cannot be ignored.

### 🎭 How to play her

- calm, precise, controlled
- never raises her voice
- never reacts emotionally
- answers directly, reframes instead of arguing

### 💬 Example tone

> “You’re looking for failure. There isn’t one.”  
> “There is only allocation.”

👉 **No combat stats needed** – she should never be fought.

## 🧑‍🏭 **Darian Velasco**

_Local Infrastructure Coordinator_

Velasco is the voice of the lower cluster. He understands the system well enough to know when something is wrong, but lacks the authority to change it.

He is pragmatic, tired, but still engaged. He has already tried the official route — and failed.

### 🎭 How to play him

- direct, grounded
- slightly exhausted
- respects competence

### 💬 Example tone

> “They already know. That’s the problem.”  
> “If you stop now, nothing changes.”

---

## ⚙️ Light Stat Profile (optional)

- **HP:** Low
- **Combat:** Minimal (improvised tools)
- **Strengths:** Knowledge, local network
- **Use in combat:** Avoid – not a fighter

## 🔧 **Yara N’Doye**

_Local Technician_

Yara is the first person to openly question the system. She has seen the data change while the problem remained.

She is cautious and aware of the risks, but not passive.

### 🎭 How to play her

- alert, quiet
- constantly observing surroundings
- builds trust slowly

### 💬 Example tone

> “The numbers changed. Not the system.”  
> “They didn’t fix it. They fixed what we see.”

---

## ⚙️ Light Stat Profile (optional)

- **HP:** Low
- **Skills:** Systems, Analysis
- **Combat:** None
- **Use:** Information, emotional anchor

# 🛡️ **Mid-Level Security Personnel**

_Corporate Security Units_

Security personnel are trained for control, not aggression. They are calm, efficient, and coordinated. Violence is a tool, not a reflex.

---

## ⚙️ **Statblock (Standard Unit)**

- **HP:** Medium (≈ 12–15)
- **Guard:** Medium
- **Armor:** Light Tactical (reduces incoming damage slightly)

---

### 🎯 Attributes

- **Combat:** +2
- **Physical:** +1
- **Systems:** 0
- **Presence:** +1

---

### ⚔️ Actions (3-Action Economy)

**1. Controlled Shot (1 Action)**

- Ranged attack
- Medium damage
- On hit: optional non-lethal (stun)

---

**2. Suppression (2 Actions)**

- Area denial
- Targets must reposition or take penalty
- reduces enemy actions next turn (–1 action)

---

**3. Restrain (1 Action)**

- Close range
- If successful: target slowed or immobilized

---

**4. Tactical Reposition (1 Action)**

- Move + defensive bonus
- gains Guard until next turn

---

### 🧠 Behavior

- focus fire
- avoid unnecessary risk
- disengage if inefficient

---

# 🤖 **Security Drone Units**

_Automated Enforcement Systems_

Drones are the ideal early or mid-combat encounter: controlled, scalable, and morally neutral.

---

## ⚙️ **Statblock (Light Drone)**

- **HP:** Low (≈ 6–8)
- **Guard:** Low
- **Armor:** None

---

### 🎯 Attributes

- **Combat:** +2
- **Systems:** +1
- **Physical:** 0

---

### ⚔️ Actions

**1. Stun Pulse (1 Action)**

- short range
- low damage
- applies “stunned” (lose next reaction / penalty)

---

**2. Tether Line (1 Action)**

- restrict movement
- target speed reduced or stuck

---

**3. Scan Target (1 Action)**

- marks target
- next attack gains bonus

---

**4. Evasive Hover (Reaction)**

- reduces incoming damage

---

### 🧠 Behavior

- prioritize closest target
- coordinate (focus marked target)
- retreat if heavily damaged

---

---

## ⚙️ **Statblock (Heavy Drone / Optional)**

- **HP:** Medium (≈ 10–12)
- **Guard:** Medium

---

### Additional Actions

**Pulse Burst (2 Actions)**

- area attack
- medium damage
- pushes targets slightly

---

👉 Use sparingly – escalation tool.

# 🧠 **Encounter Design Guidelines**

---

## 🎯 Early Combat (Recommended)

**1–2 Light Drones**

- fast
- low risk
- teaches system response

---

## 🎯 Mid-Level Combat

**2 Security + 1 Drone**

- tactical
- controlled
- players feel pressure

---

## 🎯 Escalation Combat

**3–4 Security OR Heavy Drone**

- only if players push hard
- should feel dangerous

---

---

# ⚠️ **Important GM Principle**

Combat is not the solution.

After every fight:

- system awareness increases
- access becomes harder
- surveillance intensifies

---

### 💬 System Feedback Example

> “Behavior flagged: hostile.”  
> “Access restrictions updated.”

---

---

# 🎯 **Design Intent**

These NPCs are not enemies in a traditional sense.

They are:

- extensions of the system
- responses to disruption
- tools to maintain control

---

> The system does not fight.
> 
> It contains.
