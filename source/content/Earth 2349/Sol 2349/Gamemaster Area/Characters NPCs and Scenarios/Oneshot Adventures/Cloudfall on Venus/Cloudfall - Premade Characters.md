# 👥 **Premade Characters**

## _Crew of an Independent Service Unit_

The characters in Cloudfall are not heroes, specialists from elite organizations, or representatives of major powers. They are part of a small, independent service crew operating within Aphrodite Prime — the kind of team that gets called when something falls outside standard procedures, but not far enough to justify escalation.

Their work sits somewhere between maintenance, diagnostics, security, and problem-solving. They are used to unclear assignments, incomplete information, and systems that do not behave exactly as they should. Most of their jobs end without incident.

This one does not.

Each character represents a different way of interacting with the environment of Aphrodite Prime:

- someone who understands conflict and control
- someone who knows how to navigate people and systems
- someone who reads the technical language of infrastructure
- someone who works through machines rather than direct presence

They are not a perfect team. They are a functional one.

They have likely worked together before, or at least long enough to trust each other’s roles, if not always each other’s decisions. They understand the rhythm of contract work, the pressure of continuous operations, and the reality that most systems are held together as much by experience as by design.

What connects them is simple:

They go where others don’t want to look.  
They are expected to fix what can be fixed.  
And to report what can’t.

---

## 🎯 **Using the Premade Characters**

These characters are designed to:

- provide a balanced skill set for investigation and interaction
- support multiple approaches (technical, social, procedural, physical)
- anchor the players inside the everyday reality of Aphrodite Prime

They are intentionally grounded. No one dominates the scenario alone. Progress comes from combining perspectives — technical insight, social navigation, field awareness, and controlled use of tools.

---

## ⚠️ **Tone Reminder**

These characters are not built for power fantasy.

They operate inside a system that is larger, older, and more complex than they are. Their strength lies in understanding how to move within it — and deciding when to stop doing that.
