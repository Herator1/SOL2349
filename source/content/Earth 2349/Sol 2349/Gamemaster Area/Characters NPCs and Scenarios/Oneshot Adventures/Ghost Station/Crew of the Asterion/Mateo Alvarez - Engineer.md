## 🛠️ Engineering Frame (Third Arm Rig)

**Cat:** Engineering  
**Type:** Mechanical  
**mSpace:** 3

A mounted auxiliary engineering arm built for fieldwork under pressure. It does not grant extra full actions, but it makes technical work possible in spaces where a normal two-handed operator would struggle.

### Integrated Tool Arm

**Type:** Passive

You have a functional third utility arm mounted to your body.

It can:

- hold or manipulate tools
- operate panels or controls
- perform simple engineering handling without occupying one of your main hands
- count as always having access to an integrated Multi-Tool

---

### Tool Override

**Type:** Active  
**Cost:** 1 Action  
**Load:** +1

Immediately perform one engineering interaction using the integrated arm:

- cut
- drill
- force open
- seal
- brace
- manipulate a panel or housing

This can be done even if both of your main hands are occupied, as long as the arm can physically reach the target.

## 🌬️ Adaptive Life Support

**Cat:** Survival  
**Type:** Bio / Regulatory  
**mSpace:** 3

A survival-regulation implant built to keep the body functioning through exposure, deprivation, and environmental shock.

### Environmental Shielding

**Type:** Active  
**Cost:** 1 Action  
**Load:** +1

Until the start of your next turn, ignore one ordinary environmental penalty caused by:

- heat
- cold
- toxic exposure
- low oxygen
- background radiation

This does not protect against catastrophic exposure, direct vacuum damage, or extreme high-intensity hazards by itself.

---

### Vacuum Tolerance

**Type:** Reactive  
**Trigger:** you are suddenly exposed to vacuum or severe atmospheric loss  
**Load:** +2

Remain functional for 2 turns without immediately suffering the full normal consequences of vacuum exposure.

During this time:

- movement is limited
- speech and precise action may be restricted
- this does not replace proper EVA gear

---

### Survival Recovery

**Type:** Passive

During a valid Short Rest, recover **+1 additional Health** if the rest takes place in a hostile, exposed, or resource-poor environment.

## 🧭 Notes

Mateo is built to keep systems alive when everything else starts to fail. His strength comes from **Coordination 4**, strong engineering skills, and two augment packages that make him exceptionally effective in damaged, hostile, and low-gravity environments. He is not a dominant combat character, but he is one of the most useful people on the ship once pressure, damage, and environmental failure become part of the scenario.

## 📖 Optional Background Lore

Mateo Álvarez grew up in the Belt, in the kind of family that measured time in shifts, maintenance windows, and contract cycles rather than holidays. Asteroids were never abstract places to him. They were worksites, pressure shells, dangerous transit routes, and the difference between getting paid and going hungry.

He did not become an engineer through prestige or academic ambition. He became one because in the Belt, technical competence is survival. If something breaks, someone either knows how to fix it or everyone starts negotiating with vacuum, pressure loss, or power failure.

Over time, Mateo turned that necessity into a profession. He learned how to patch damaged systems, keep old machinery running past its intended life, and improvise workable solutions out of bad tools, missing parts, and failing infrastructure. That made him useful everywhere from mining platforms to contract ships, and eventually useful enough for a [[Helios]] survey contract.

He is not attached to corporate structure, but he respects functional systems and the people who keep them alive. On the _Asterion_, he is the person most likely to notice that something is wrong before anyone else fully understands why.

Players who want to step into Mateo quickly should focus on three things:

- think in systems, not drama
- treat equipment as something that can always fail
- respond to danger by fixing, stabilizing, and improvising

He is not there to deliver speeches or lead from the front. He is there to make sure the ship, the air, the lights, and the doors keep working long enough for everyone else to matter.
