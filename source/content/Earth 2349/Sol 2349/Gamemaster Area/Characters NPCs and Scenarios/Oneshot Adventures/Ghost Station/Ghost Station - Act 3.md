# 🧭 Act 3 — Escalation

By the start of Act 3, the players are no longer just investigating Ghost Station. They are part of its internal balance.

They now know that the station is inhabited, that two hostile groups remain inside the rotating core, and that the crisis involved both an external recovery push and an internal break around a failed cargo transfer. They may also already have met **Talia Serrano**, the surviving freighter crewmember hidden near the threshold.

This act is not a fixed sequence. It is a **pressure sandbox** built around three linked problems:

- the station is getting worse
- the factions want incompatible outcomes
- the players need a ship

Act 3 begins once the players understand the broad shape of the situation and start making plans of their own.

---

## Scene Purpose

Act 3 exists to do four things:

- force real decisions
- escalate pressure without railroading
- make the ships and the relic materially relevant
- drive the scenario into a credible endgame

At this point, exploration gives way to leverage, repair work, bargaining, and commitment.

---

# ⚙️ Situation Overview

By the beginning of Act 3, the following should be clear:

- **Ghost Station is failing**
- **Old Guard** controls the spinward side of the inhabited core
- the **Corporate Recovery Team** controls the counterspinward side
- the **spin-transfer lift** is the unstable threshold between dead infrastructure and the rotating core
- the **Asterion** is damaged but still usable as shelter and fallback position
- the **freighter** is docked elsewhere in the outer shell
- the failed cargo transfer is central to the station’s collapse

The players do not need the full truth yet, but they should understand that the situation is now dynamic and that delay has costs.

---

# 🌐 Spatial Logic in Act 3

Act 3 works best if the GM keeps the station spatially simple.

## Default route
**Dock 1 (Asterion)** -> **Dead Corridors** -> **Spin-Transfer Lift / Neutral Threshold** -> **Inhabited Core**

## Core split
Inside the rotating section:

- **Spinward side** -> **Old Guard**
- **Counterspinward side** -> **Corporate Recovery Team**
- the threshold remains the only natural meeting, bargaining, or stand-off space between them

## Freighter location
The **freighter** is docked at **Dock 2**, elsewhere in the outer shell.

It is:
- farther from the Asterion
- reached through the dead corridor network
- tied to the cargo side of the station
- the physical center of the relic and transfer dispute

This keeps movement readable:
- the inhabited core is the social and political center
- the freighter is the main material objective
- the outer shell remains the dangerous space between them

---

# ⏳ Core Pressures

Act 3 moves because several pressures are active at once.

- **life support is degrading**
- **power failures are spreading**
- **spin stability is worsening**
- **both factions are watching the players**
- **every delay makes ship repair harder**

The station will not stabilize on its own. If the players hesitate too long, the environment and the factions begin making decisions for them.

---

# 🧪 The Relic

## What It Is

The relic is an [[AI Relic]]: a degraded machine-age signal-routing and processing node.

It is **not** an active intelligence, not a weapon, and not a mystical object. Its value comes from rarity, deniability, research potential, and the legal and political complications around possession.

That is enough to make it worth killing for.

---

## Where It Is

The relic is inside the **freighter**, hidden in a concealed secondary compartment near the secured hold.

It is:
- stored inside a shielded container
- separated from ordinary cargo
- protected against a hurried inspection
- physically tied to the failed transfer that triggered the crisis

By Act 3, the players may already strongly suspect that the freighter contains the real center of the conflict. This is where that suspicion can become certainty.

---

## How It Feels

The relic should feel strange, but not supernatural.

- compact
- dense
- irregular but engineered
- difficult to identify at a glance
- no obvious interface or visible function

Its effects remain subtle:
- sensor desynchronization
- inconsistent readings
- timing discomfort
- instruments behaving almost correctly, but not quite

Do not let it become a spectacle. It is a destabilizing asset, not a showpiece.

---

# 🚀 Ship Repair Situation

Escape is the practical core of Act 3.

The players should quickly understand that there are **two real paths out**, and that both depend on access, time, and faction pressure.

---

## The Asterion

The **Asterion** suffered critical damage in the debris strike.

Its **magnetic nozzle containment and alignment system** is compromised. The reactor can still support local ship systems, shelter, and limited operations, but the ship cannot safely perform a full departure burn in its current state.

A launch without repair risks drive instability, loss of thrust control, and possible destruction of the ship.

### To repair the Asterion, the players need:
- drive-control components from the freighter
- time for major engineering work
- access to tools, work space, and power
- relative freedom from faction interference

This is the **harder but better** option.

If successful:
- the players keep their own ship
- departure is dangerous, but credible
- they leave with more control and better long-term survival odds

If rushed or only partially successful:
- the ship becomes barely spaceworthy
- escape is possible, but comes with real risk

---

## The Freighter

The **freighter** is battered, but in much better propulsion condition.

It has:
- random structural damage
- local hull failures
- damaged sensors and control routing
- minor internal system faults

Its drive section, however, is largely intact.

That means the freighter is much easier to patch into emergency flight condition than the Asterion is to fully restore.

### To fly the freighter, the players need:
- moderate repair work
- patching of structural damage
- restored basic flight control
- limited tools, components, and calibration support from the Asterion

This is the **easier but worse** option.

If successful:
- the freighter becomes the fastest escape route
- the players can leave sooner
- they abandon the Asterion

If rushed or incomplete:
- it is still launchable, but compromised
- navigation, sensors, and survivability are poor
- it feels like an emergency escape, not a clean solution

---

## Repair Logic at a Glance

### Option 1 — Repair the Asterion
- harder
- slower
- needs parts from the freighter
- preserves the players’ ship

### Option 2 — Patch the Freighter
- easier
- faster
- needs support from the Asterion
- means abandoning their own ship

This choice should stay visible throughout the act. It is one of the main drivers of movement, negotiation, and conflict.

---

# 🧩 Talia in Act 3

If the players found **Talia Serrano** in Act 2, decide early how she matters now.

She should not become background furniture. In Act 3 she is one of four things:

- a **witness**
- a **burden**
- a **bargaining asset**
- a **liability**

## What Talia Knows
Talia can confirm:
- the freighter brought the cargo
- the transfer to an internal hold was interrupted
- command authority collapsed during the attempt
- the freighter crew split
- the station was still inhabited when the recovery push began

She does **not** know the full origin of the relic or the full corporate chain behind the operation.

## How to Use Her
If protected, she can:
- confirm player suspicions
- push them toward the freighter
- ask for extraction
- make the human cost of delay visible

If exposed, she can become:
- a target for Recovery Team pressure
- someone Old Guard wants protected
- a reason for immediate negotiation or confrontation

Keep her specific and limited. She is a witness to the collapse, not a full exposition device.

---

# 📦 Freighter Hold Discovery

The freighter is where suspicion becomes proof.

If the players reach the secured hold area, they can recover the clearest confirmation yet that the crisis centered on a contested transfer and an intended outside handoff.

## What Is There
- a damaged cargo control panel still running on emergency power
- a breached transfer frame
- one broken containment bracket
- two dead freighter crew in vac-suits
- the concealed secondary compartment containing the relic

## Recovered Transfer Record

> **CARGO TRANSFER ABORTED**  
> **DESTINATION:** GHOST STATION INTERNAL HOLD  
> **EXTERNAL BUYER CONFIRMATION:** PENDING  
> **COMMAND OVERRIDE:** CONTESTED  
> **SECURED ITEM REMAINS IN HOLD**  
> **LOG END:** manual interruption / weapons discharge detected

## What This Establishes
- the relic was physically present
- there was an intended outside buyer
- the mutiny and violence began during the transfer dispute itself

This is the strongest confirming discovery in the scenario. After this point, the players should understand what the conflict is really about.

# ⚔️ Conflict Triggers

Act 3 works best when escalation feels earned. Use the station and the factions as reactions to player behavior, not as arbitrary punishment.

---

## Trigger 1 — The Players Start Visible Repair Work

Once the players openly begin work on either ship, both factions react.

Why:
- a working ship means escape
- escape means leverage
- leverage means someone may lose the relic, the station, or witnesses

Likely consequences:
- demands for transport
- demands for access
- sudden negotiation attempts
- threats or obstruction

---

## Trigger 2 — The Relic Is Found, Moved, or Mentioned

If the players discover the relic, relocate it, or openly discuss it, tension rises immediately.

Likely consequences:
- both factions refocus on the players
- trust hardens into demands
- hidden agendas become urgent

---

## Trigger 3 — Talia Changes Hands

If Talia is handed over, moved, exposed, or openly protected, the situation shifts.

Likely consequences:
- Old Guard may treat her as proof of what happened
- Recovery Team may want to secure or silence her
- the players become harder to treat as neutral outsiders

Use this only if Talia is still active in the scenario.

---

## Trigger 4 — The Players Clearly Back a Side

Once the players visibly help one faction, the other changes posture.

Likely consequences:
- access closes
- bargaining narrows
- patrols or watch positions shift
- intimidation or open hostility increases

---

## Trigger 5 — Time Passes Without Resolution

Use this when the table slows down or the players become too comfortable.

Examples:
- pressure drops in one section
- power fails at the threshold
- spin-transfer access becomes unreliable
- dead corridors become harder to traverse
- ship repair becomes more difficult or urgent

This should feel inevitable, not punitive.

---

## Trigger 6 — A Lie or Theft Collapses

If the players deceive either faction and are exposed, the situation sharpens quickly.

Likely consequences:
- trust evaporates
- offers disappear
- both sides may treat the players as an independent threat

---

# 🛠️ High-Value Pressure Points

These are the most useful things the players can fight over, bargain over, or use as leverage.

---

## The Spin-Transfer Lift
The threshold between dead infrastructure and inhabited core.

Useful because:
- it is the clearest choke point in the station
- meetings, ambushes, and stand-offs make sense here
- control of it shapes movement between factions and ships

---

## The Freighter
Not just transport, but the center of the relic story and the best source of critical parts.

Useful because:
- it contains the clearest proof
- it matters to both factions
- it can become either escape route or salvage source

---

## The Asterion
Still valuable even damaged.

Useful because:
- it is the players’ fallback shelter
- it contains tools, systems, and calibration support
- it is part of either repair path

---

## Local Systems
A surviving power, air, or access control node can buy time.

Useful because:
- stabilizing it may shift faction behavior
- it can support repair work
- it offers non-combat leverage

Do not overcomplicate this. One functioning local systems node is enough.

---

# 🧠 Player Approaches

No path should be framed as the “correct” one.

The most likely approaches are:

### Negotiate
Trade access, repair help, transport, or temporary stabilization.

### Choose a Side
Back Old Guard or Recovery Team and accept the consequences.

### Stay Independent
Focus on ship repair, avoid committing fully, and use information carefully.

### Escalate
Threaten, sabotage, attack, or seize a critical zone.

### Exploit Chaos
Take the relic, finish a ship, and run.

Each should remain viable as long as the players act decisively.

---

# 🎯 Endgame Structure

Act 3 tips into endgame once one of the following becomes true:

- one ship becomes launchable
- the relic changes hands
- the factions openly clash
- the threshold fails
- the players commit to a final plan

Once that happens, stop broadening the situation. Narrow it.

The final phase should answer three questions:

1. **Which ship leaves, if any?**
2. **Who goes with the players, if anyone?**
3. **What happens to the relic?**

Those answers determine the ending.

---

# 🧠 GM Guidance

## Keep it visible
Do not let the act become abstract. Keep reminding the players of:
- ship condition
- route distance
- faction posture
- station deterioration

## Keep it specific
Use concrete obstacles:
- blocked access
- missing components
- demanded transport
- failing pressure
- armed stand-offs

## Keep it moving
If the session stalls, advance one pressure:
- worsen a system
- move a faction
- escalate around Talia
- force a repair choice
- make the threshold unsafe

## Do not force a preferred ending
Let:
- the players decide
- the factions react
- the station keep failing
- consequences emerge from action, not script

---

# 🎯 Summary

Act 3 is about pressure, repair, leverage, and consequence.

The players now know enough to act. The station is getting worse. The factions want different outcomes. The ships give the conflict material urgency. The relic makes every decision more expensive.

From here on, what happens should feel earned.
