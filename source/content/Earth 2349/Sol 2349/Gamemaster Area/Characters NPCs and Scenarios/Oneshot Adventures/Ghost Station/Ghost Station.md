_Ghost Station_ is a **self-contained one-shot scenario** set in the hard, industrial reality of _SOL 2349_. It begins with a damaged survey vessel, a stranded crew, and a hidden station inside a mined-out asteroid that was never meant to appear on any chart.

This is not a story about heroic exploration. It is a story about survival inside a failing piece of Belt infrastructure, where salvage, secrecy, and deniable corporate interests collide. The station is not just an abandoned ruin or a generic pirate base. It is an illegal retrofit built into the shell of an exhausted asteroid: part smuggling node, part salvage site, part off-book transfer point, and now the scene of a recent and unfinished conflict.

The adventure focuses on **tension, uncertainty, and decision-making under pressure**. It is not primarily combat-driven, but violence is always close, and every practical problem is tied to another one. Resources are limited, information is incomplete, and even the station itself is working against the players. Life support is degrading, power is unstable, spin gravity is failing, and every delay makes the situation worse.

The tone is grounded, slow-burning, and atmospheric. _Ghost Station_ leans into the strengths of _SOL 2349_: physical realism, environmental hazards, fragmentary authority, and the logic of a world held together by infrastructure, contracts, and fragile systems. The central conflict is not just about who survives, but about who controls what remains — ships, systems, salvage, and a highly valuable [[AI Relic]] that no one should have been moving through a place like this.

At its core, the scenario is about entering a situation already in motion. The players arrive after the decisive mistakes have already been made, but before the consequences have fully played out. Two hostile groups remain on the station, neither in full control, and both aware that time is running out. The players are not there to follow a fixed plot. They are there to navigate an unstable environment, shifting loyalties, and a conflict shaped as much by economics and infrastructure as by guns.

The expected playtime is **3–5 hours**, depending on pacing, player decisions, and how quickly the station tips from tense standoff into open collapse. The scenario is designed to stay flexible. It can lean more into negotiation, technical problem-solving, and fragile alliances, or shift toward survival, betrayal, and violence if events spiral.

There is no single correct outcome. Success is defined by what the players choose to prioritize, what they are willing to risk, and what they are prepared to leave behind.
