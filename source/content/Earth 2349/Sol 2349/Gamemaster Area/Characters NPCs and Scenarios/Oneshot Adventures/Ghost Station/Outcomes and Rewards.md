# 🧭 Outcomes & Rewards — Ghost Station

There is no single correct ending to _Ghost Station_. Every resolution reflects what the players chose to protect, what they were willing to risk, and how much of the station remained functional by the end.

In _SOL 2349_, success rarely means a clean victory. More often, it means leaving alive with something useful, something costly, or something that will matter later.

The best outcomes in this scenario should feel like **survival with consequences**.

---

# 🟢 Ending 1 — Fragile Cooperation

## Summary

The players broker a temporary truce between the Old Guard and the recovery team. Resources are pooled just long enough to stabilize a ship, preserve life support, or evacuate survivors before the station deteriorates further.

This is usually the closest thing to a “good ending,” but it should never feel comfortable. The players have not solved the underlying conflict. They have only forced it to stop long enough to survive.

---

## 🎙️ Read Aloud

> The departure is not orderly, and it is not clean, but it holds together. People who were trying to kill each other only hours ago now move through the same spaces with weapons lowered, watching, waiting, but not firing. No one mistakes this for trust. It is simply the temporary discipline of people who understand that dying here would benefit no one.
> 
> As the ships pull away from the station, no one celebrates. Survival was not won. It was negotiated. Behind you, the damaged structure continues to drift, still carrying the remains of a conflict that has been paused, not resolved.

---

## Rewards / Consequences

**Potential rewards**

- a professional reputation for keeping a crisis from collapsing into total disaster
- access to shared salvage, emergency supplies, or technical support
- possible future goodwill from survivors on both sides

**Likely consequences**

- both factions survive and may reappear later
- neither side fully trusts the players
- the players may become known as useful intermediaries in places where neutrality is dangerous

---

## Hook

- one or both factions reach out later for help, transport, or discreet negotiation
- the unresolved ownership of the relic resurfaces
- the players gain a reputation as people who can operate between hostile interests

---

# 🟡 Ending 2 — Recovery Team Alignment

## Summary

The players back the recovery team, help secure the relic, and either evacuate with them or enable their mission to succeed. The station and its surviving operators become secondary to extraction.

This ending is efficient, practical, and morally cold — very much in keeping with how a deniable operation might actually conclude.

---

## 🎙️ Read Aloud

> Once the decision is made, everything narrows. The station becomes less a place than a problem to be passed through efficiently. Routes are secured, priorities reduced, and hesitation removed wherever it threatens the timetable. By the time the final systems begin to fail, the outcome has already been decided.
> 
> As you leave, the station recedes into the black with no ceremony at all. What mattered was never the habitat, the people, or the wreckage. What mattered was the object at the center of it. That has been secured, and the rest is now someone else’s loss.

---

## Rewards / Consequences

**Potential rewards**

- payment or off-book compensation in **SOL**
- access to higher-tier corporate contacts, directly or indirectly
- possible contract offers tied to retrieval, transport, or containment work

**Likely consequences**

- [[Helios]] may become interested in why the _Asterion_ crew ended up inside a deniable recovery operation
- surviving station personnel will remember what the players chose
- the players are now “visible” to people who track assets, not stories

**Cost**

- survivors may be abandoned
- the players may be associated with the destruction of the station, whether fairly or not

---

## Hook

- follow-up recovery mission
- containment, transport, or handoff operation
- corporate audit, questioning, or indirect surveillance
- a new job offer that is clearly too well informed to be accidental

---

# 🔴 Ending 3 — Old Guard Alignment

## Summary

The players side with the Old Guard, neutralize or evade the recovery team, and escape with the freighter or with station-aligned survivors. This usually means embracing the Belt logic of survival, pragmatism, and grey-market loyalty over formal power.

This is not a heroic ending, but it is a very believable Belt ending.

---

## 🎙️ Read Aloud

> The escape is rough and hurried, shaped more by necessity than planning. Systems are pushed hard, checks are skipped, and the distance between you and the station grows just quickly enough to matter. Behind you, the damaged habitat remains where it is, carrying whatever is left of the fight without you.
> 
> The people leaving with you are not allies in any official sense, and no one pretends otherwise. But they are alive, and that counts for something. Out here, survival creates its own forms of loyalty — temporary, conditional, and more honest than most contracts.

---

## Rewards / Consequences

**Potential rewards**

- access to a smuggling, salvage, or black-market contact network
- practical control, partial control, or claim over the freighter
- salvageable cargo, tools, or station-grade resources

**Likely consequences**

- recovery-team backers or their proxies may pursue the players later
- the players become linked to an illegal Belt operation
- any relic-related claim now becomes harder to hide

**Cost**

- corporate hostility increases
- future docking, inspection, or contract interactions may become more complicated

---

## Hook

- smuggling job or discreet transfer mission
- Belt network politics
- pursuit by a deniable recovery force
- dispute over who actually owns the freighter, cargo, or relic

---

# ⚫ Ending 4 — Independent Escape

## Summary

The players avoid committing to either faction, secure a ship, and leave while the station’s conflict remains unresolved. They may save themselves, and possibly their own crew, but leave the larger outcome behind.

This is one of the most realistic endings in setting terms. It is also one of the most morally ambiguous.

---

## 🎙️ Read Aloud

> In the end, you leave the way many people survive in the Belt — by recognizing the point at which a problem stops being yours. The station remains behind, still inhabited, still divided, and still moving toward an ending that you will not see.
> 
> Nothing about the departure feels triumphant. It feels practical. Whatever happens next inside that damaged shell will happen without you, and that may be the most honest outcome of all.

---

## Rewards / Consequences

**Potential rewards**

- clean escape with minimal direct entanglement
- possible possession of ship, cargo, or relic if the players acted carefully
- fewer immediate obligations to survivors or factions

**Likely consequences**

- missed opportunities for alliance, leverage, or payment
- delayed consequences instead of immediate ones
- someone may still be tracking the ship, the relic, or the players’ sensor logs

**Cost**

- the players may have saved themselves at the expense of everyone else
- later fallout may arrive without warning

---

## Hook

- a delayed claim dispute
- relic-related anomalies or pursuit
- unexpected message from a survivor
- a future job shaped by information the players thought they had escaped

---

# 💥 Ending 5 — Collapse / Violent Resolution

## Summary

The standoff breaks completely. One or both factions are destroyed, the station suffers catastrophic damage, and escape becomes a matter of surviving the collapse rather than controlling the situation.

This is the harshest ending and should feel costly even if the players survive.

---

## 🎙️ Read Aloud

> The situation does not resolve. It breaks. Tension becomes action, action becomes damage, and damage becomes failure spreading faster than anyone can control it. The station gives way in pieces — pressure, power, structure, rotation — until what remains is no longer a place anyone can meaningfully hold.
> 
> By the time you leave, there is very little left behind that still resembles a working habitat. The outcome is final, but not clean. Whatever was hidden here is now buried under wreckage, silence, and the kind of debris field that creates its own future problems.

---

## Rewards / Consequences

**Potential rewards**

- emergency salvage, if the players move quickly
- relic possession by force of circumstance
- elimination of immediate rivals or pursuers

**Likely consequences**

- ship damage, injuries, or crew losses
- [[Helios]] penalties, contractual consequences, or scrutiny if the _Asterion_ survives
- unstable reputation among future employers or contacts
- loss of future opportunities tied to the station’s survivors

**Cost**

- this is usually the most expensive ending in terms of equipment, trust, and future stability

---

## Hook

- investigation into the incident
- salvage rights dispute over the destroyed station
- search for survivors, wreckage, or missing cargo
- pressure from interested parties who believe something valuable was lost but not destroyed

---

# 🧪 Optional Relic Add-On

If the players leave with the relic, add this to almost any ending.

## 🎙️ Read Aloud

> It is only after departure, once the immediate danger has begun to flatten into routine, that the first inconsistencies become noticeable. They are small enough to dismiss individually — a timing mismatch, a delayed system response, a sensor reading that resolves a fraction too late.
> 
> Nothing dramatic. Nothing immediately dangerous.
> 
> But enough to notice, enough to repeat, and enough to suggest that whatever was taken from the station did not remain behind in any simple sense.

---

## Hook

- anomaly investigation
- quiet scientific interest
- recovery attempt by a third party
- growing suspicion that more than one actor was looking for the same object

---

# Final Note for the GM

No ending should feel perfectly clean or fully resolved.

Even the best outcome should leave behind:

- debt
- suspicion
- leverage
- survivors
- claims
- unanswered questions

That is the right tone for _Ghost Station_, and for _SOL 2349_ more broadly.
