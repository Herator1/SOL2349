### GM Notes

- Purists are dangerous because of **angles, tight interiors, shotguns, and improvisation**, not because of inflated stats.
    
- Corporate Recovery is dangerous because of **discipline, clean positioning, and objective pressure**.
    
- If the marines are already worn down, remove **one enemy** rather than inflating survivability.
    
- The scenario stays sharper if enemies are **dangerous and fast**, not spongey.

## OKL – NPCs

The scenario works best when its NPCs feel like people under pressure rather than quest-givers or combat tokens. Most of them should be tired, overstretched, and forced into positions they did not fully plan for. Even the most dangerous among them should still feel human.

This section focuses on the **key NPCs** most likely to shape the scenario.

---

## Kestrel Crew

These NPCs remain aboard the **Kestrel** during the station operation. They do not enter the outpost, but they matter throughout the mission as remote support, practical voices, and the last connection to normal procedure.

### Marek Ionescu — Pilot

Marek is the **Kestrel’s** pilot: calm, economical, and difficult to rattle. He speaks little, flies precisely, and does not indulge panic. He is the kind of pilot who inspires confidence not through charm, but through the complete absence of wasted motion.

He sees the mission in terms of vectors, burn windows, positioning, and exit problems. If the players want reassurance, he will not give it. If they want the cleanest possible insertion or pickup window, he will try to find it.

Use Marek for:

- practical reminders that extraction is not guaranteed
    
- calm comments during the burn and approach
    
- matter-of-fact warnings about timing and exposure
    
- subtle reinforcement that the players are operating on a very short window
    

Marek is not cynical. He is simply realistic.

---

### Lina Haddad — Comms / Sensor Operator

Lina handles the **Kestrel’s** communications and sensor picture. She is fast, sharp, and slightly more talkative than the rest of the ship crew, mostly because she is the one translating incomplete data into something usable.

She is very good at saying **what the ship knows**, **what it does not know**, and **what people are pretending to know anyway**.

Use Lina for:

- jammer confirmation
    
- updates on station emissions and heat signatures
    
- corporate traffic detection
    
- relaying inbound signals or partial comm bursts
    
- letting players ask sensible technical questions without getting magical certainty in return
    

Lina should feel useful without becoming an all-seeing mission control voice. Her role is to narrow uncertainty, not remove it.

---

### Tobias Krämer — Engineer

Tobias is the ship’s engineer: dry, exact, and mildly pessimistic in the way that competent engineers often are. He thinks in structural logic, load paths, seals, power routing, and failure points. He is not dramatic because he does not need to be.

Use Tobias for:

- entry-point analysis
    
- hull and docking damage interpretation
    
- breach risk warnings
    
- suit prep and equipment integrity checks
    
- reminding players that space infrastructure does not forgive improvisation forever
    

He is especially useful if the players start getting clever with tools, pressure boundaries, or forced access methods. Tobias should reward smart technical thinking while making bad ideas sound expensive.

## Scientists

These are the core surviving members of the research team. They are not identical in outlook, and they should not all react to the crisis in the same way.

---

### Dr. Lyra Voss — Lead Scientist

Dr. Lyra Voss is the principal investigator and the intellectual center of the project. She is calm, articulate, and far too controlled for the situation she is in. Even under pressure, she speaks like someone used to being the most composed person in the room.

She is not a mad scientist and should never be played that way.

Voss believes the project is dangerous, but necessary. In her view, the real moral failure would be abandoning a system that could redefine long-term adaptation in space simply because its first serious crisis was ugly. She does not think she is innocent. She thinks she is right enough to continue.

Use Voss for:

- clear, persuasive defense of the research
    
- refusal to use the words others want her to use
    
- attempts to steer the players toward extraction rather than judgment
    
- moments where competence becomes unsettling
    

She should be hardest to argue with when the players are tired, hurried, or trying to remain “professional.”

#### What Voss wants

- extraction
    
- retention of the research
    
- survival of key staff
    
- prevention of uncontrolled disclosure
    
- preservation of decision-making authority
    

#### What she fears

- losing control of the data
    
- external actors defining the project before she can
    
- being judged by people who do not understand what the system can become
    

---

### Dr. Kiano Reyes — Systems Specialist

Reyes is one of the technical minds behind the interface architecture and the closest thing the scenario has to an internal conscience that did not act soon enough. He is brilliant, exhausted, and very bad at pretending this is all still manageable.

Unlike Voss, Reyes can be pushed off balance.

He knows the hidden control architecture exists. He knows the risks are real. He also knows that none of this was supposed to escalate like this. He is the kind of person who kept telling himself there would still be time to correct course later.

Use Reyes for:

- partial technical explanations
    
- nervous contradictions of official research language
    
- revealing too much under pressure
    
- moments of visible guilt
    

Reyes is not a heroic whistleblower. He is someone who understood too much too late and is still trying to decide what cowardice looks like.

#### What Reyes wants

- to survive
    
- to reduce the damage
    
- to avoid total loss of the work
    
- to stop pretending the players do not deserve the truth
    

#### What he fears

- that the Purists are right in the one way that matters
    
- that Voss will keep rationalizing forward
    
- that corporate recovery will make everyone else irrelevant
    

---

### Dr. Sera Volkova — Behavioral Analyst

Volkova is one of the most unsettling figures on the station, not because she is overtly hostile, but because she is too composed. She studies behavior, stress, and adaptation under controlled conditions. At this stage, she has either been exposed to the system deeply enough to show subtle effects or has simply spent too long rationalizing its outcomes. In play, the difference should remain unclear.

She is measured, intelligent, and disturbingly difficult to read.

Use Volkova for:

- elegant moral reframing
    
- responses that sound rational but feel wrong
    
- subtle dismissal of ordinary human instability
    
- making the players doubt their own emotional reactions
    

She should never chew scenery. The more controlled she is, the more unsettling she becomes.

#### What Volkova wants

- continuity of the research
    
- maintenance of internal discipline
    
- prevention of emotional overreaction
    
- proof that the system works under real stress
    

#### What she fears

- uncontrolled collapse into panic, vengeance, or simplistic judgment
    
- the destruction of a system she sees as a necessary corrective to human instability

## Purists

The Purists should never feel like generic anti-tech raiders. They are under pressure, morally compromised by events, and increasingly desperate, but they still believe they are the only people on the station acting before it is too late.

---

### Nadiya Vale — Purist Cell Leader

Nadiya Vale leads the strike team currently trapped inside the station. She is committed, controlled, and running on far less certainty than she wants others to think. She did not come here to die pointlessly, but she is prepared to if it prevents the wrong people from walking away clean.

She is the best face of the Purist argument because she is not frothing, theatrical, or cruel for its own sake. She is angry in a disciplined way.

Use Vale for:

- clear warnings without melodrama
    
- refusal to collapse into “terrorist villain” logic
    
- pressure on the players to look before acting
    
- sharp lines about systems, deniability, and institutional failure
    

She should make sense, especially when she is talking about what happens if the project leaves the station intact.

#### What Vale wants

- to stop the project permanently
    
- to keep the scientists from leaving with usable control
    
- to preserve proof of what happened here
    
- to deny corporate cleanup a clean outcome
    

#### What she fears

- that the players will choose procedure over evidence
    
- that corporate response will erase everything
    
- that her own people will fracture before the truth can survive
    

---

### Ivo Saran — Former Station Technician / Purist Insider

Ivo is the scenario’s most useful Purist-adjacent bridge between technical reality and ideological action. He is not a polished revolutionary. He is a former insider who recognized too late that the system was doing more than it claimed, tried formal channels, and ended up helping bring the Purists in.

He knows the station layout better than most of the cell. He also knows exactly how badly things have gone.

Use Ivo for:

- technical detail from the Purist side
    
- confirming that reports were ignored or buried
    
- emotionally grounded anger rather than ideology alone
    
- showing that the attack did not begin as a simple kill operation
    

Ivo should feel like someone who still remembers the station as a workplace.

#### What Ivo wants

- the project stopped
    
- the station’s truth preserved
    
- proof that he was not imagining the danger
    
- some version of this ending without total meaningless slaughter
    

#### What he fears

- that the station will become a sealed incident file
    
- that the players will dismiss him as radical noise
    
- that he helped unleash something he cannot now contain
    

---

## Corporate Recovery

These NPCs should feel clean, efficient, and professionally dangerous. They are not there to debate. They are there to end uncertainty in favor of control.

---

### Adrien Vossler — Aegis-3 Team Lead

Vossler commands the corporate recovery element. He is polished, controlled, and never louder than he needs to be. He does not threaten people unless he has already decided the threat is useful. He frames force as procedure and cleanup as responsibility.

He is not cartoonishly sinister. He is much worse than that: he sounds reasonable.

Use Vossler for:

- cold procedural authority
    
- “we will take over from here” energy
    
- quietly making the players understand that corporate support is not neutral
    
- turning cooperation into submission one sentence at a time
    

Vossler should feel like a man whose job is to make bad outcomes administratively invisible.

#### What Vossler wants

- control of the site
    
- control of the data
    
- control of survivors
    
- elimination of unmanaged witnesses if necessary
    
- a final story that can be contained
    

#### What he fears

- uncontrolled disclosure
    
- League interference that becomes public
    
- evidence leaving the station outside corporate control
    

---

### Tamsin Heller — Corporate Technical Recovery Specialist

Heller is the technical member of the recovery team, the person most likely to speak in terms of systems, assets, integrity, and contamination. She is not the field commander, but she may be the one who best understands what the research actually is worth.

Use Heller for:

- technical calm under pressure
    
- unsettlingly detached valuation of data over people
    
- explaining why the project must be secured, not merely destroyed
    
- making clear that the corporate team has prepared for this kind of incident before
    

Heller works well as the NPC who confirms, without meaning to, that the corporation already understood more than it publicly admitted.

#### What Heller wants

- intact retrieval if possible
    
- controlled destruction if not
    
- zero uncontrolled leakage
    
- preservation of whatever can still be monetized or weaponized later
    

---

## Using the NPCs at the Table

Not every NPC needs equal screen time.

If the scenario is moving quickly, prioritize:

- **Dr. Lyra Voss**
    
- **Nadiya Vale**
    
- **Adrien Vossler**
    

These three form the main pressure triangle of the scenario.

Use Reyes, Volkova, Ivo, and Heller to deepen the picture:

- Reyes adds guilt and technical truth
    
- Volkova adds unease and ideological distortion
    
- Ivo adds lived evidence and insider context
    
- Heller adds the cold logic of corporate containment
    

The **Kestrel** crew should remain useful throughout, but never solve the mission from afar. Their role is to keep the players anchored to procedure while the station itself tries to pull them into ambiguity.
