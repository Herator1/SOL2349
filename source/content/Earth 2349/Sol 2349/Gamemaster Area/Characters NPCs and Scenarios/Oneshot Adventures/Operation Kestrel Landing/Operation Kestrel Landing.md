Space is quiet—until it isn’t.

You are part of a Jovian League Marine rapid response unit, deployed to the outer Saturn system after a remote research outpost goes dark following a fragmented distress signal. Communications are unreliable, the situation unclear, and your team is the first—possibly the only—force to arrive on site.

This is not a large-scale military operation. There is no support fleet, no orbital dominance, no second wave waiting behind you. Just your squad, your equipment, and whatever you find inside the station.

_Kestrel Landing_ is a grounded, combat-focused scenario set in a hard science fiction environment. Engagements are tight, tactical, and consequential. Ammunition, positioning, and decision-making matter. The environment is as much a threat as any opponent: pressure, structure, and system integrity can turn even a small mistake into a critical situation.

At the same time, not every problem is solved with a trigger pull. The situation inside the station is unstable and incomplete. Information is fragmented. Different perspectives exist, and not all of them align. Players are free to approach the mission in their own way—careful, aggressive, methodical, or improvised. The scenario rewards coordination, awareness, and initiative. It does not assume a single correct path.

And yes—you are space marines. No, you are not 3-meter-tall superhuman monsters in indestructible power armor with unresolved parental issues.

Your suit leaks. Your ammo runs out. Your body is still very much human. There is no heroic last stand where you shrug off physics—because physics will not shrug you off. You are professionals, not legends. Highly trained, well-equipped—and entirely mortal.

What matters is not only how you fight, but when, why, and for whom. You are not here to win a battle. You are here to enter, assess, and decide.
