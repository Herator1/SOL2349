Mars keeps its dead.

Machines fail, crews move on, contracts close, and dust does the rest. Out on the surface, value is rarely dramatic. Most of the time it lies half-buried, broken, and barely worth the fuel it takes to reach it. That is the work: long drives, thin margins, cold routines, and the constant question of whether a site is worth the risk.

**Voices Below Mars** is a one-shot scenario for **SOL 2349** about salvage, pressure, and a discovery that turns a routine recovery job into something far stranger. The player characters are the crew of the **MCRV Dustbreaker**, a working Mars salvage rover owned by **Tharsis Recovery & Leasing**. They are not soldiers, chosen heroes, or explorers on a grand mission. They are a professional crew on a low-priority contract, sent to recover a handful of small unmanned assets from a remote surface cluster that does not look important.

It should have been simple.

Instead, the crew finds a pattern that does not quite make sense. The wrecks are real. The contract is real. But the site hides something older, deeper, and far more valuable than the job paperwork suggests.

This scenario is built as a **hard-science-fiction dungeon crawl**: not with monsters or supernatural horror, but with buried infrastructure, machine logic, vertical spaces, bad angles, fragile safety margins, and the growing sense that some places were never meant to be entered by human beings. The danger comes from environment, escalation, and the cost of staying too long. The deeper the crew goes, the more the job stops being about routine salvage and starts becoming a question of what they are willing to risk for knowledge, profit, and the chance to get back out alive.

**Voices Below Mars** works best for groups who enjoy:

- grounded hard-sci-fi atmosphere
- salvage and exploration under pressure
- claustrophobic non-human spaces
- mystery through fragments and inference
- meaningful trade-offs instead of clean victories

At its core, this is a scenario about a working crew finding something too big for the contract they signed — and having to decide, under pressure, what part of it they can actually carry back to the surface.

---
## Scenario Tone

Expect:

- industrial Mars surface realism
- tight interiors and awkward traversal
- pressure, fatigue, and limited margins
- machine systems that still do something, even if no one fully understands what
- costly decisions with no perfectly safe answer

Do not expect:

- heroic power fantasy
- military action
- supernatural haunting
- clean ownership of what is found
- a simple “win”
