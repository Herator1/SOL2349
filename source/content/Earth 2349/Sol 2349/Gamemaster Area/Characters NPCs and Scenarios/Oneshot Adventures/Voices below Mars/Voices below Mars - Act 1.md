## ACT 1 — THE JOB

## Scene Frame

The crew of the **MCRV Dustbreaker** is en route to a clustered recovery site flagged by a low-confidence orbital scan.

The job is routine. The terrain is not.

---
## Read Aloud — Opening

> The Dustbreaker crawls across the Martian surface, its mass carried on wide pressure wheels that grind through dust and rock alike.
> 
> Inside, everything hums. Air systems. Thermal loops. Drive control.
> 
> Nothing here is optional.
> 
> Outside, the world is silent, thin, and lethal.
> 
> Inside, five people and a machine keep each other alive.
>

# The MCRV Dustbreaker

**Type:** Medium Pressurized Salvage Rover  
**Owner:** Tharsis Recovery & Leasing  
**Crew Capacity:** 5  
**Operational Endurance:** ~10–14 days active field operation

---
## Purpose

The Dustbreaker is not a transport vehicle.  
It is a **mobile recovery platform**.

Built for:

- locating lost assets
- field stripping
- short-term salvage operations
- operating far from infrastructure

---
## What It Carries

### Core Systems

- pressurized crew compartment
- life support (recycled air + water loop)
- thermal management (critical for Mars surface ops)
- navigation + terrain mapping systems
- short-range comms + burst transmission capability

---

### Salvage Equipment

- external manipulator arm
- cutting tools (thermal + mechanical)
- portable winch system
- modular tool racks
- drone interface (scout drones, optional use)

---

### Sensor Suite

- ground-penetrating radar
- short-range lidar
- thermal imaging
- signal analysis / comm scan

---

### Storage

- limited internal cargo space
- external clamp mounts for bulky salvage
- fragile items must be secured manually

👉 The Dustbreaker can recover **parts**, not whole wrecks.

---

## Limitations

- not fast
- not armored
- not designed for combat
- cannot lift large structures
- interior space is tight and functional

---

## GM Note

The Dustbreaker should feel like:

- a machine that works
- but not perfectly
- and not forever

---

# Contractor

## Tharsis Recovery & Leasing

A mid-tier Mars surface contractor specializing in:

- refurbished salvage vehicles
- low-margin recovery contracts
- distributed crew operations

---

### What They Care About

- contract completion
- recoverable value
- minimal loss

---

### What They Don’t Care About

- crew comfort
- long-term site exploration
- anything not on the contract

---

## Contract Summary

**Objective:**  
Recover, identify, and strip three unmanned assets.

**Site:**  
Low-confidence orbital anomaly  
Clustered debris (~2 km radius)

**Expected Value:**  
Low–moderate

---

## Company Contact (Optional)

**Channel:** delayed comms / burst transmission

---

### Tone

- dry
- efficient

# Gameplay — Scan Phase

## Initial Scan

The Dustbreaker detects:

- 3 separate objects
- spread across ~2 km
- partially buried
- no active signals

---

## Skill Checks

### Locate Objects (Operations / Piloting)

**Success:**

- refine positions
- estimate distance
- safe approach routes

**Failure:**

- longer travel time
- inefficient route
- possible terrain complication

---

### Sensor Analysis (Operations / Science)

**Success:**

- confirms artificial origin
- estimates size and depth
- detects inconsistent patterns between objects

**Strong Success:**

- identifies different generations of tech

**Failure:**

- unclear readings
- possible misclassification

---

### Subsurface Scan (Tech / Engineering)

**Success:**

- detects burial depth
- identifies orientation
- confirms objects are intact but inactive

**Failure:**

- incomplete scan
- excavation required to confirm

---

## What Feels Off (Early Hint)

If players succeed well:

- objects are **not impact clustered**
- spacing feels **too even or too unrelated**
- no clear crash signature

👉 Not alarming yet — just unusual

---

# Movement Between Objects

Travel is not instant.

Use:

- terrain navigation
- obstacle avoidance
- time pressure (light escalation seeds)

Optional minor complications:

- uneven ground
- traction issues
- sensor interference

---

## Interaction

### Accessing the Drone (Engineering)

**Success:**

**Failure:**

- casing damage
- slower access
- minor escalation trigger possible

---

### System Check (Tech / Operations)

**Success:**

**Failure:**

- corrupted logs

## What’s Wrong

Subtle only:

- orientation doesn’t match a crash
- no impact signature
- system shutdown unclear
