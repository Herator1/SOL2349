## ACT 2 — THE SIGNAL

---
## Scene Frame

The crew continues the recovery operation.

The second and third assets confirm a pattern.  
Not valuable.  
But not random.

---
# Second Wreck — Survey Probe

## Read Aloud

> The second signal sits lower.
> 
> Buried deeper.
> 
> You don’t see it at first — only the scan confirms it’s there.
> 
> When you clear enough dust, the shape emerges.
> 
> Cleaner than the first.  
> Newer.
> 
> And intact.

---

## Description

- compact survey probe
- partially buried
- minimal external damage
- sensor array partially deployed

---

## Interaction

### Access (Engineering)

- standard casing, easier than the drone
- intact internals

---

### Data Extraction (Tech / Operations)

**Success:**

- partial sensor logs recovered
- mapping attempts visible

**Strong Success:**

- detects repeated scan focus on **same subsurface point**
- multiple failed attempts to resolve it

**Failure:**

- fragmented logs
- unclear result

---

## What’s Wrong

- probe repeatedly tried to scan **one specific location**
- data inconsistent, overlapping, unresolved
- system did not shut down properly — it stalled

👉 first clear anomaly

---

# First Signal Detection

Triggered when:

- crew runs active scan
- or deeply analyzes probe data

---

## Read Aloud

> Your systems pick it up clean.
> 
> Not noise. Not interference.
> 
> A signal.
> 
> Fixed position. Below the surface.
> 
> Stable. Repeating.
> 
> Structured — but wrong.
> 
> It does not change.
> 
> It does not respond.
> 
> It just… runs.

---

## Signal Properties

- constant origin point below ground
- no drift
- stable repetition
- structured data format
- no known protocol

---

## Skill Checks

### Signal Analysis (Science / Operations)

**Success:**

- confirms artificial origin
- confirms **non-standard protocol**

**Strong Success:**

- identifies structure resembling communication
- but internally inconsistent

**Failure:**

- unclear classification

---

### Subsurface Scan (Tech)

**Success:**

- detects **void / structure below**
- confirms non-natural geometry

**Failure:**

- weak or incomplete return

---

## What It Means

The crew now knows:

- something artificial is below
- it is still running
- it is not part of the known network

👉 potential high-value find

---

# Third Wreck — Autonomous Crawler

## Read Aloud

> The third signal is easier to see.
> 
> Something sits against a rock formation, half exposed.
> 
> Smaller than expected.
> 
> Twisted.
> 
> But recent.

---

## Description

- small autonomous crawler
- structurally damaged
- partially exposed

---

## Interaction

### Access (Engineering)

- damaged casing
- internal systems accessible with effort

---

### Data Logs (Tech / Operations)

**Success:**

- movement logs recovered
- pathing behavior visible

**Strong Success:**

- repeated re-routing toward **same location as signal**
- system attempted to reach it multiple times

**Failure:**

- partial logs only

---

## What’s Wrong

- crawler wasn’t just operating
- it was **trying to get somewhere**

👉 same point as probe scans  
👉 same point as signal

---

# The Pattern

At this stage, the crew can conclude:

- all three objects interacted with the same location
- none succeeded in resolving it
- none show clear failure cause

👉 this is not a random debris field

---

# The Hook — Potential Value

- unknown active system
- buried structure
- unregistered site
- no claim markers

👉 **this could be extremely valuable**

# The Access Shaft

## Read Aloud

> The ground gives way slowly.
> 
> Not a hole. Not a crater.
> 
> A structure.
> 
> Partially buried.
> 
> Edges too straight. Materials too uniform.
> 
> Something vertical.
> 
> Going down.

---

## Description

- collapsed cooling / service shaft
- partially filled with regolith
- structural frame still intact
- no markings, no identification

---

## Clearing the Entrance

### Actions:

- digging / clearing
- cutting through debris
- stabilizing access

---

### Checks (Engineering / Physical)

- stable descent path

- loose material
- increased risk on descent
- Escalation +1 (first trigger)
