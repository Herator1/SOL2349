## 💪 Titan Frame

**Cat:** Physical  
**Type:** Muscular / Reinforcement  
**mSpace:** 3

A reinforced upper-body frame built for force, control, and overpowering physical interaction.

### Brute Force

**Type:** Passive

Gain **Tactical Edge** on actions involving raw force, such as:

- forcing doors
- lifting heavy objects
- holding position against pressure
- breaking restraints
- overpowering physical resistance

### Power Strike

**Type:** Active  
**Cost:** 1 Action  
**Load:** +1

Make 1 melee attack.

On a hit, choose one:

- if the target still has Guard, deal **+1d6 damage**
- push the target up to **2 m**
- in microgravity or zero-g, push the target up to **5 m** instead

### Lift & Throw

**Type:** Active  
**Cost:** 1 Action  
**Load:** +2

Lift and throw:

- a heavy object
- or a target you can physically control

The thrown object or target becomes a ranged attack that deals **2d6 damage** on a hit.

At GM discretion, the target may also be:

- knocked prone
- repositioned
- slammed into cover, a wall, or another target

## 🧭 Notes

Ilya is a direct-action combat specialist built around pressure, force, and threat projection. He is strongest when he can:

- seize tempo with **Reflex Spike**
- dominate close range with **Titan Frame**
- push enemies out of position
- absorb pain and stay functional after taking real damage

He is not subtle, but he is extremely effective in tight corridors, boarding actions, and short violent engagements.

## 📖 Optional Background Lore

Ilya Sokolov came out of the lower tiers of Venus, where protection, intimidation, and violence often meant the difference between being used and being erased. He worked as an enforcer in places where contracts were informal, authority was local, and people learned quickly what strength looked like when the official systems stopped mattering.

He was good at it.

Not loud, not reckless, and not theatrical — just efficient. The kind of man people noticed only once it was already too late to change their minds.

At some point, that life narrowed down to the same cycle it always does: pressure, repayment, punishment, escalation. Kaito offered him a way out, or at least a different kind of work, and Ilya took it. He did not become softer. He simply became more selective about who he was willing to use violence for.

On the _Asterion_, he fills a role the ship is not really designed to need until everything goes wrong. He is there to keep threats contained, to make bad situations less chaotic, and to stand between the crew and whatever breaks through first.

Players who want to step into Ilya quickly should focus on three things:

- speak little, observe first
- act decisively once a line has been crossed
- stay physically calm, even when the situation is dangerous

He is not charming, and he is not diplomatic. But he is reliable in the one way that matters most under pressure: when things become violent, he does not hesitate.
