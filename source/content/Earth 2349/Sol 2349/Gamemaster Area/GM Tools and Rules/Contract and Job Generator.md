Use this page when you need a job fast.

A good _SOL 2349_ contract usually has five parts:

- **who is hiring**
- **what they want**
- **why it matters now**
- **what the hidden problem is**
- **what makes the job dangerous**

Roll or choose freely.

## Quick Contract Formula

**[client] + [job] + [urgency] + [hidden problem] + [risk]**

Example:

**[[Nyx Data Systems]] liaison**

- **carry sensitive data to another station**
- **another party is already moving on the same target**
- **the job is really about access, not the stated objective**
- **hidden surveillance**

That is already enough to build a session.

## Fast Contract Examples

### 1. Quiet Retrieval

A [[Helios]] representative hires the crew to recover a missing shipment before the next outbound transport leaves. The cargo is misdeclared, another team is already looking for it, and station security must not get involved.

### 2. Route Test

An [[Interplanetary Science Authority]] researcher wants the crew to test a route or system under real conditions. The route is only briefly usable, the supporting data is incomplete, and the real purpose of the job is access to something the players were not told about.

### 3. Escort Under Pressure

A [[Venus Administrative Compact]] official hires the crew to escort a technician to a nearby habitat before inspection teams arrive. Officially it is routine. In reality, the technician knows something politically dangerous.

### 4. Dirty Emergency Delivery

A [[Smuggling Rings]] broker needs urgent supplies moved before lockdown. The manifest is wrong, the route is unsafe, and the people waiting at the destination are not the only ones expecting the shipment.

## Good Defaults for SOL 2349

When in doubt, a good contract should be:

- practical
- pressured
- partly dishonest
- more complicated than it first appears

A job becomes interesting when the stated task is clear, but the real situation is not.

## In One Sentence

A good _SOL 2349_ contract starts as paid work and turns into a problem with pressure, layers, and consequences.
