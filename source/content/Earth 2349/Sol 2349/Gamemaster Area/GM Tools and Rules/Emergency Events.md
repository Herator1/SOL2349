Use this page when you need sudden pressure.

An emergency event should do one of three things:

- force immediate action
- change priorities
- make the current situation worse

Roll when the scene needs escalation, when the players are stalling, or when a routine situation suddenly stops being routine.

## Quick Emergency Formula

**[event] + [location] + [escalation]**

Example:

**a hatch fails to seal**

- **docking ring**
- **civilians are trapped nearby**

That is already enough to start a scene.

## Fast Emergency Examples

### 1. Bad Docking

A shuttle comes in too fast and clips the docking frame. Seals fail, internal traffic stops, and station security is more concerned with blame than rescue.

### 2. Hidden Contamination

Something toxic gets loose in a cargo handling bay. The obvious response is evacuation, but the cargo records were altered and nobody wants to admit what is actually in the containers.

### 3. Lockdown Failure

A section goes into automatic lockdown after a thermal event. The system works, but it cuts off medbay staff, traps civilians, and seals the only route to a failing coolant manifold.

### 4. Crowd Collapse

A power failure hits a public concourse during a tense labor dispute. Lights die, rumors spread, people start pushing toward the wrong exits, and someone uses the chaos to cover a different operation.

## Using Emergency Events Well

Emergency events work best when they do not feel random.

Even if rolled, they should connect to the current situation, location, and pressure. A good emergency should force choices. What gets saved first? Who gets left waiting? What system gets sacrificed to protect another? Who is telling the truth, and who is using the crisis?

An emergency becomes memorable when it changes the shape of the scene instead of just adding noise.

## Good Defaults for SOL 2349

When in doubt, an emergency should be:

- immediate
- physical
- confusing
- harder to solve than it first appears

The best emergencies create both technical and human problems at the same time.

## In One Sentence

A good emergency in _SOL 2349_ is not just danger — it is danger that forces decisions under pressure.
