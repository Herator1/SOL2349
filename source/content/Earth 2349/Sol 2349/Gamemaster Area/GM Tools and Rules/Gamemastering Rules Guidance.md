This page is about using the rules quickly and well.

Rules in _SOL 2349_ are tools, not obstacles. Use them when they create clarity, tension, and meaningful choices. Simplify them when they would slow the game down without adding anything interesting.

## Roll Only When It Matters

Call for a roll when success is uncertain and failure would change the situation.

If a competent character is doing something routine, and failure would not create anything useful, do not roll. Let them succeed and keep the game moving.

## Failure Should Create Pressure

A failed roll should usually do more than stop progress.

It should cost time, create danger, worsen the situation, attract attention, damage equipment, reduce options, or force a harder choice. Empty failure is usually less interesting than changed circumstances.

## Make Fast Rulings

If you do not remember an exact rule, make a reasonable call and continue.

Do not stop the session to solve every rules question in real time. A fast ruling now and a correction later is usually better than killing pacing. This is also a common principle in other GM advice: make the call, keep play moving, review later if needed.

## Let Competence Show

Player characters should feel capable in their areas of strength.

Do not bury specialists under unnecessary rolls and penalties. The world is dangerous enough already. Let the tension come from pressure, cost, and consequence rather than constant incompetence.

## Use the Right Amount of Rules

Not every scene needs full mechanical weight.

Use more detail when the moment is dangerous, uncertain, or expensive. Use less detail when the point of the scene is movement, flow, or information. Keep the rules where they help most.

## Be Consistent, Not Perfect

You do not need flawless rulings. You need understandable rulings.

Try to stay consistent with earlier decisions. If you later realize something worked differently than intended, adjust it openly and move on. Good GM advice in other systems makes the same point: consistency matters more than total mastery.

## A Good Default

When in doubt:

- roll when uncertainty matters
- let competence succeed
- make failure costly, not empty
- make a quick ruling and continue

## In One Sentence

Use the rules of _SOL 2349_ to support pacing, pressure, and consequences — never to slow the session down more than they help.
