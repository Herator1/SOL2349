Use this page when you need a station problem fast.

A good station problem in _SOL 2349_ usually has three parts:

- something is wrong
- someone is hiding, denying, or exploiting it
- if nothing changes, the situation gets worse

You can roll or choose freely.

## Quick Station Problem Formula

**[problem] + [person under pressure] + [hidden factor] + [escalation]**

Example:

**Cooling systems are failing in one section**

- **maintenance chief**
- **the station cannot afford a real repair**
- **a ship is about to arrive or depart**

That is already enough for a session start.

## Fast Station Problem Examples

### Failing Habitat Loop

A habitat ring is slowly overheating because cooling capacity was quietly rerouted weeks ago. The maintenance chief knows, but the station administrator will not approve a shutdown while a high-value transport is docked.

### Missing Cargo, Wrong Lockdown

A dangerous cargo pallet is missing, but security is searching the wrong people because the real trail leads into a protected contractor network.

### Water Problem

Water recycling output is collapsing. Officially it is a technical issue. In reality, someone has been diverting clean reserves for private sale.

### Sealed Section

A sealed old section has been opened. The station claims it was a mapping error. It was not.

## Good Defaults for SOL 2349

When in doubt, make station problems:

- practical
- layered
- partially hidden
- worse under delay

A station problem becomes interesting when it is not just a malfunction, but a social and operational crisis.

## In One Sentence

A good station problem in _SOL 2349_ is never just a broken system — it is a broken system with people, pressure, and consequences attached.
