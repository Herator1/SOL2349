Use this page when you need a fast travel estimate at the table.

These are **GM-facing benchmark values**, not exact orbital calculations. They are meant to help you decide quickly how long a trip takes, what it roughly costs, and what kind of risk or ship stress comes with pushing for speed.

In _SOL 2349_, travel is usually a trade between **time, cost, and how hard the ship is being pushed**. The current rules already support broad bands instead of precise simulation.

## Travel Profiles

Use these three travel modes as defaults:

- **Slow** — cheap, cautious, lower wear, longer exposure
- **Medium** — standard commercial or professional transfer
- **Fast** — expensive, stressful, hard on ship and crew

## In-System Orbital Travel

These are trips **within the same planetary system**.

|Start|Destination|Slow|Medium|Fast|Typical Cost|
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|[[Earth]] orbit|[[Luna]] orbit|2–5 days|1–2 days|under 1 day|1–3 SOL|
|[[Mars]] orbit|[[Phobos]] / [[Deimos]]|6–24 hours|3–12 hours|under 3 hours|under 1 SOL|
|[[Jupiter]] system|major inner moon transfer|3–10 days|1–4 days|under 1 day|2–6 SOL|
|[[Saturn]] system|major inner moon transfer|4–12 days|2–5 days|1–2 days|2–8 SOL|

**GM note:**  
For Jupiter and Saturn, exact moon-to-moon times vary a lot, so use these as **system-level default bands** unless the exact route matters.

## Reading the Table

Use these numbers as **campaign-scale estimates**, not exact flight plans.

A **slow** trip is usually:

- cheaper
- easier on ship systems
- more vulnerable to delays, inspections, and onboard tension

A **fast** trip is usually:

- expensive
- hard on the ship
- harder on the crew
- more likely to justify follow-up maintenance, risk, or complications

That matches the existing travel rules, which already frame speed as a tradeoff rather than a free upgrade.

## In One Sentence

Travel in _SOL 2349_ should usually be estimated by **region**, not exact orbital math: in-system trips take hours to days, inner-system trips take weeks, Belt traffic takes weeks to months, and the outer system takes months.
