This may honestly be the most important note in this whole GM section:

_SOL 2349_ is my version of this universe.  
But the moment your group starts playing in it, it becomes **your version** too.

And that means you are absolutely allowed to break it.

Not by accident.  
On purpose.

## The Setting Is a Foundation, Not a Prison

I put a lot of thought into the tone, the hard sci-fi logic, the political structure, the hidden lore, and the overall shape of the world. That version of _SOL 2349_ is the one I personally settled on, and it is the one this wiki is mainly written to support.

But that does **not** mean it is the only good version.

Quite the opposite.

A roleplaying setting should be something you can use, reshape, test, twist, and occasionally throw into complete chaos if that creates a better campaign for your table.

## I Thought About the “Broken” Versions Too

A lot of the things you might think of as “breaking” _SOL 2349_ are not random ideas that never crossed my mind.

I actively played around with many of them while building the setting.

First contact.  
Alien intelligence.  
Zombie outbreaks on stations.  
A much more action-heavy style.  
Returning AI.  
Weirder relics.  
Softer sci-fi turns.  
Even humanity eventually pushing beyond the Solar System.

I genuinely like those ideas.

A lot.

They are cool, they are fun, and they can work extremely well with the rules and with the wider structure of the universe.

They are simply **not the version I chose as the default baseline for SOL 2349**.

That is an important distinction.

Not wrong.  
Not banned.  
Just not the main version I settled on.

## So Yes — Please Break It If That Makes It Better

If your group wants to run a **first contact** campaign where humanity encounters alien intelligence for the first time:  
amazing. Do it.

If you want to turn the setting into a **combat-heavy space dungeon crawler** full of boarding actions, derelicts, loot, gunfights, and constant pressure:  
go for it.

If a **zombie virus** breaks out on a station and the campaign becomes survival horror in pressure corridors and sealed habitat sectors:  
that sounds great.

If the big campaign twist is that humanity invents the first **FTL drive** and suddenly starts pushing toward Alpha Centauri:  
honestly? That is cool as hell.

None of that is forbidden.  
None of that is “playing it wrong.”  
None of that needs my permission.

But you have my blessing anyway.

## It Is Still SOL — Just Not This Version of SOL

That is really the core idea.

You are not destroying the setting by changing it.  
You are creating a different branch of it.

The written version of _SOL 2349_ is just one timeline, one interpretation, one chosen tone, one settled form. It is the hard-sci-fi, human-centered, fragmented, lower-flightscale version that I wanted to build first and make coherent.

But there are absolutely other directions hidden inside it.

And over time, I fully expect that the wider **SOL** universe may grow into later eras and alternative developments where some of those softer sci-fi elements enter more openly. Alien contact, stranger phenomena, more speculative technology, and a gradual widening of the setting’s boundaries may all have a place there.

Just not here.  
Not yet.  
Not in this baseline version.

## Keep What You Love

If you love the hard-sci-fi tone, keep it.  
If you love the political fragmentation, keep it.  
If you love the hidden lore, station pressure, salvage stories, and constrained human scale, keep all of that.

But if your group finds something else in the setting and wants to follow that path instead, that is not a betrayal of the project. That is exactly what roleplaying games are for.

The setting should serve the table.  
Not the other way around.

## There Is No Lore Police

Really. There is not.

You are allowed to contradict things.  
You are allowed to escalate things.  
You are allowed to introduce things I deliberately kept out.  
You are allowed to say: “This campaign starts as normal SOL 2349, and then goes completely off the rails.”

That is not failure.  
That is play.

## One Honest Recommendation

The only thing I would suggest is this:

Know whether you are **playing close to the intended version of SOL 2349** or whether you are **using it as a launch platform for your own version**.

Both are great.  
Both are valid.  
But it helps to know which one you are doing.

That way you can break the setting deliberately, not accidentally.

And deliberate breaking is often where the best campaigns begin.

## In One Sentence

Break _SOL 2349_ whenever it creates a better campaign — because I explored many of those paths myself, I still love them, and once the setting reaches your table, it belongs to your group too.
