_SOL 2349_ is a classic pen-and-paper roleplaying game set in a hard science-fiction future.

At the table, it combines character-driven decision-making, flexible problem solving, grounded worldbuilding, and a rules framework based on **d20 and d6 rolls**. Characters operate in a dangerous but believable Solar System shaped by physics, distance, fragile infrastructure, political fragmentation, and human conflict.

This is not a heroic power fantasy, and it is not space opera built around destiny, chosen ones, or universe-saving plotlines. _SOL 2349_ works best when played as a grounded science-fiction RPG in which pressure, consequences, uncertainty, and difficult tradeoffs matter as much as combat.

## Core Table Experience

At the table, _SOL 2349_ is built around a few key ideas:

- **Characters matter.** Human choices, competence, fear, loyalty, greed, and compromise drive the story.
- **The world pushes back.** Space is hostile, infrastructure is fragile, and every decision has practical consequences.
- **Problems are rarely simple.** Most situations are shaped by technical limits, competing interests, incomplete information, and human pressure.
- **Violence is possible, but never trivial.** Combat exists, but it is only one part of play and rarely the cleanest solution.
- **Tone matters.** The setting works best when it feels grounded, industrial, tense, and human.

## What Players Actually Do

A typical _SOL 2349_ session may involve:

- negotiating a dangerous contract
- investigating sabotage, disappearance, or system failure
- surviving a station or ship crisis
- moving cargo or people through politically sensitive space
- exploring abandoned infrastructure or relic sites
- handling faction pressure, labor tension, or corporate interference
- making hard choices under time, resource, or moral pressure

The game supports combat, but most strong sessions are built around **stress, decisions, competing motives, and consequences**, not constant firefights.

## What Kind of Setting This Is

_SOL 2349_ is a **hard sci-fi setting**. That means the world should feel constrained by believable technology, logistics, energy limits, travel time, maintenance, and environmental danger. The broader wiki already reflects this through dedicated sections on technological concepts, energy and drive systems, radiation, heat management, travel in space, and space combat.

The setting is also **fragmented rather than epic**. The Solar System is full of factions, corporations, habitats, transport systems, legal frameworks, and local conflicts, but it is not built around a single grand war or a simple good-versus-evil struggle. The history and lore sections likewise emphasize the AI War, the Departure, postwar reconstruction, factional development, and local political complexity rather than a unified empire-style setting.

## What the Rules Support

The rules structure of _SOL 2349_ already suggests the intended play style:

- character creation is built around **Attributes**, **Skills**, **Base Stats**, and progression systems
- core play includes **Difficulty Checks**, **Time & Actions**, **Load**, **Mental Strain & Psychological Pressure**, and survivability rules
- combat is present, but so are environment, stabilization, movement, and consequences
- the game also supports **Space Combat** and **Travelling in Space**, which helps anchor adventures in the larger realities of the setting

This makes _SOL 2349_ best suited for groups who enjoy a mix of:

- roleplay
- grounded problem solving
- meaningful risk
- investigative or scenario-based play
- and mechanical support for danger without requiring constant tactical combat

## What SOL 2349 Is Not

_SOL 2349_ is not primarily about:

- cinematic superhero action
- easy moral clarity
- endless loot escalation
- soft-science miracle technology
- chosen-one narratives
- or solving every problem through combat alone

Players can absolutely be capable, dangerous, and highly skilled. But even strong characters remain part of a larger world that is difficult to control and rarely fully fair.

## Best Use at the Table

_SOL 2349_ works especially well when the group treats the setting as:

- a lived-in industrial future
- a world of contracts, systems, and pressure
- a setting where local stories matter
- and a place where human decisions remain more important than spectacle

The best sessions usually come from situations where characters are competent, but not comfortable; informed, but never fully certain; powerful enough to matter, but never powerful enough to ignore the world around them.
