## 🧠 **Resolution**

By the end of the scenario, the players have made a decision that affects not only AP-LC-47, but how Aphrodite Prime responds to instability itself. None of the available outcomes represent a perfect solution. Each reflects a different way of interacting with the system — and a different willingness to accept its logic.

The consequences unfold quickly, but not dramatically. As with everything in Aphrodite Prime, change is absorbed, redistributed, and integrated.

The system continues.

The difference lies in how.

---
# 🅰️ **Outcome: Maintain Stability**

The players accept the system’s logic, or choose not to interfere further. The data remains controlled. The instability remains contained.

---
### 🎙️ Read-Aloud

> The system settles back into its rhythm without resistance. Data flows cleanly, without deviation. Displays remain stable, consistent, unquestioned.
> 
> AP-LC-47 continues to operate, just as before. The fluctuations remain — small, controlled, unacknowledged.
> 
> Life does not stop. It adjusts. Quietly.
> 
> Nothing changes.
> 
> And that is exactly the point.

---

## 🧠 **Consequences**

- The cluster continues to degrade slowly
- No large-scale intervention is triggered
- The system remains stable across all levels

Velasco understands what has happened, even if nothing is said. Yara, if still present, continues her work — but without expectation that anything will improve.

The players leave with the knowledge that they made a decision in favor of stability over intervention.

---

## 🎁 **Rewards**

- continued access to corporate-level contracts
- increased trust from system-aligned actors
- reputation as reliable and compliant operators

👉 future jobs may involve similar “optimization scenarios”

---

---

# 🅱️ **Outcome: Expose the Truth**

The players force the system to reveal what it has been hiding. Data becomes visible beyond controlled layers.

---

### 🎙️ Read-Aloud

> For a moment, the system stops pretending.
> 
> Data fractures across interfaces, no longer aligned, no longer controlled. Values spike, drift, contradict each other.
> 
> AP-LC-47 appears in the noise — unstable, unresolved, real.
> 
> The response is immediate. Not silence, but sound. Not control, but reaction.
> 
> Systems begin to move. People begin to notice.
> 
> The city does not collapse.
> 
> But it is no longer quiet.

---

## 🧠 **Consequences**

- alarms and automated responses are triggered
- partial evacuation protocols may begin
- mid-level stability is briefly disrupted

Some lives are saved. Others are not. The system regains control, but not before the truth becomes visible.

Velasco becomes an active organizer. Yara survives in most cases, now fully aware of how the system operates.

The players are no longer invisible.

---

## 🎁 **Rewards**

- strong reputation among lower-level populations
- access to underground networks and informal contacts
- potential long-term consequences with corporate actors

👉 future interactions with the system may be more restricted

---

---

# 🅲 **Outcome: Controlled Resolution**

The players engage with the system and influence it without breaking it. A compromise is reached.

---

### 🎙️ Read-Aloud

> The system adjusts — not fully, not honestly, but with purpose.
> 
> Values shift, but do not collapse. Warnings appear, but remain contained.
> 
> Movement begins below. Not chaos, but direction.
> 
> Some leave. Some remain.
> 
> The structure holds.
> 
> Not because it was saved.
> 
> But because it was… managed.

---

## 🧠 **Consequences**

- partial evacuation of AP-LC-47
- selective allocation of resources
- controlled stabilization of remaining systems

The outcome is efficient, but uneven. Those deemed more valuable or easier to relocate are prioritized.

Velasco accepts the result, but without satisfaction. Yara survives, but with a clearer understanding of the system’s limits.

The players have changed the outcome — but not the rules.

---

## 🎁 **Rewards**

- balanced reputation across different factions
- continued access to both corporate and informal networks
- potential future leverage within system-level decisions

👉 the players are seen as pragmatic operators

---

---

# 🅳 **Outcome: System Disruption**

The players push beyond control and force instability into the system itself.

---

### 🎙️ Read-Aloud

> The system reacts too late.
> 
> For a moment, everything desynchronizes. Data no longer aligns, responses no longer match inputs.
> 
> Then the structure shifts. Not gradually. Not predictably.
> 
> AP-LC-47 appears one last time — not as data, but as movement.
> 
> The cluster drops.
> 
> The system does not fail.
> 
> It adapts.
> 
> But something has already been lost.

---

## 🧠 **Consequences**

- rapid destabilization of the cluster
- large-scale casualties likely
- broader system impact possible

The system absorbs the shock, but the cost is immediate and visible.

Velasco may not survive. Yara’s fate depends on timing and player action. The event becomes a recorded incident — contained, but not forgotten.

---

## 🎁 **Rewards**

- access to fragmented or restricted system data
- potential leverage in unstable or fringe environments
- reputation as unpredictable and dangerous actors

👉 future interactions with structured systems become more difficult

---

---

# ⚠️ **Final Note for the GM**

No outcome should feel like a victory.

Each path represents a different relationship with the system:

- acceptance
- disruption
- compromise
- or escalation

The system continues in all cases.

The players do not decide whether it survives.

They decide:

> **what kind of system it becomes — at least for a moment.**
