# 🧭 Act 2 — Arrival at the Station (Part 1)

## Scene Purpose

- establish location
- reinforce vulnerability (EVA, damage)
- introduce aftermath of conflict
- build tension before first contact

---

# 🎙️ Read Aloud — Approach

As the station slowly grows in the forward view, it becomes clear that this is not a place meant to be found. The structure is embedded deep into the asteroid, its outer ring still rotating, though unevenly, as if struggling to maintain stability. Large sections remain dark, while others flicker with unstable light. There are no running signals, no guidance systems, and no response to your presence. By all expectations, this place should be dead — but it isn’t.

# 🎙️ Read Aloud — Docking

> The ship closes the final distance in slow, controlled bursts, each maneuver slightly delayed by the damage sustained in the debris field. The docking structure comes into view, partially torn open and visibly scarred, but still intact enough to attempt a connection. There are no lights to guide you and no automated systems to assist. # 🧭 Act 2 — Part 2: Spin Gravity & First Contact

## Scene Purpose

- introduce artificial gravity (rotation)
- create physical discomfort / realism
- transition from “dead station” → “inhabited space”
- set up first encounters

---

# 🎙️ Read Aloud — Transfer Shaft

> The corridor does not continue straight into the station. Instead, it narrows into a cylindrical access shaft that cuts inward, perpendicular to the docking structure. There is still no gravity here, only handholds and structural ribs running along the walls. As you move forward, the station begins to change in a way that is not immediately visible. At first it is only a sensation — a subtle shift in orientation, as if your body is trying to decide where “down” should be.

---

## 🧠 What Happens

- players move from **zero-G into rotating section**
- gravity increases gradually
- station rotation is **unstable**

---

## 🎲 Suggested Checks

### Low-G Movement / Coordination

**Success:**

- controlled transition
- no major issues

**Failure:**

- disorientation
- short loss of control / collision

---

---

# 🎙️ Read Aloud — First Gravity

> As you continue deeper into the shaft, the effect becomes stronger. Your boots begin to make contact with the surface beneath you, at first lightly, then with increasing consistency. The station’s rotation asserts itself, not as a clean, stable pull, but as something uneven, slightly misaligned. Movements that felt natural moments ago now require adjustment. Turning your head too quickly creates a brief but noticeable sense of imbalance, as if your inner sense of direction no longer matches what you see.

---

## ⚠️ Effects

- mild nausea possible
- fast movement = harder
- perception slightly off

👉 emphasize:  
**this is not comfortable gravity**

---

---

# 🎙️ Read Aloud — Entering Atmosphere

> The inner hatch shows signs of pressure. Frost patterns cling to the edges of the seal, and a faint movement of particles suggests that some atmosphere remains beyond it. When the door opens, the transition is subtle but immediate. The absolute silence of vacuum gives way to a muted, enclosed soundscape — distant creaking metal, low vibrations, and the barely audible hum of systems still running somewhere within the station.

---

## 🧠 Environment

- thin but breathable atmosphere
- unstable systems
- sound returns (muted)

---

## GM Notes

- helmets stay on (recommended)
- atmosphere is **not safe long-term**

---

---

# 🎙️ Read Aloud — Inside the Ring

> The corridor beyond is no longer weightless. Objects rest against surfaces instead of drifting, and dust settles slowly along the curved structure of the station. The gravity is weak and inconsistent, shifting just enough to make balance uncertain. The space feels used, not abandoned — patched sections, repaired panels, and improvised fixes suggest that someone has been trying to keep this place functional despite the damage.
