# 🧭 Outcomes & Rewards — Ghost Station

There is no single “correct” ending. Each outcome reflects the players’ decisions and priorities under pressure.

---

# 🟢 Ending 1 — Fragile Cooperation

## Summary

The players manage to negotiate a temporary truce between both factions. Resources are shared, and a joint evacuation becomes possible.

---

## 🎙️ Read Aloud

> The departure is not clean, and it is not orderly, but it works. People who were trying to kill each other only hours ago now move through the same space with weapons lowered, watching, waiting, but not firing. The tension never disappears; it simply becomes something controlled, something contained long enough to matter.
> 
> As the ships separate from the station, no one celebrates. There is no sense of victory, only the quiet understanding that survival was negotiated, not earned. Behind you, the station continues to drift, its systems failing one by one, carrying with it the remains of a conflict that never truly ended.

---

## 💰 Rewards / Consequences

- - reputation (neutral / professional)
- possible shared resources or salvage
- both factions survive → future complications

---

## 🔗 Hook

- both factions may contact players later
- unresolved conflict resurfaces
- players are known as **mediators**

---

---

# 🟡 Ending 2 — Corporate Alignment

## Summary

The players support the security team and help secure the artifact.

---

## 🎙️ Read Aloud

> The operation concludes with efficiency. Decisions are made quickly, and hesitation is removed wherever it appears. By the time the final systems fail, the outcome is already determined. The station is left behind with little regard for what remains on it, and the focus shifts entirely to extraction.
> 
> As you move away, the damaged structure fades into the distance, reduced to just another object in space. What mattered was never the station itself, but what was taken from it. And that is now secured.

---

## 💰 Rewards / Consequences

- payment from corporate entity (SOL payout)
- access to higher-tier contacts
- Helios becomes interested (or suspicious)

⚠️ moral cost:

- survivors left behind

---

## 🔗 Hook

- corporate follow-up mission
- retrieval / containment operations
- players now “on record”

---

---

# 🔴 Ending 3 — Pirate Alignment

## Summary

The players side with the pirates, eliminate or evade security, and escape with them or their ship.

---

## 🎙️ Read Aloud

> The escape is fast and uneven, driven more by necessity than planning. Systems are pushed beyond safe limits, and the distance between you and the station grows just quickly enough to matter. Behind you, whatever remains of the conflict continues without you.
> 
> The people you leave with are not allies in any formal sense, but they are alive, and for now that is enough. Out here, survival creates its own kind of loyalty — temporary, uncertain, but real.

---

## 💰 Rewards / Consequences

- access to pirate network
- freighter ownership (or shared control)
- salvage opportunities

⚠️ consequence:

- corporate hostility

---

## 🔗 Hook

- smuggling / black market missions
- hunted by corporate forces
- pirate politics

---

---

# ⚫ Ending 4 — Independent Escape

## Summary

The players avoid both factions, secure a ship, and leave without committing.

---

## 🎙️ Read Aloud

> In the end, you leave the way you arrived — not as part of the conflict, but as something moving through it. The station remains behind you, still occupied, still unresolved, its internal tensions continuing without your involvement.
> 
> There is no clear sense of what will happen there, only the certainty that it will not end cleanly. Whatever conclusions are reached will not include you.

---

## 💰 Rewards / Consequences

- clean escape
- possible artifact possession
- minimal reputation impact

⚠️ consequence:

- missed opportunities
- unknown future threats

---

## 🔗 Hook

- delayed consequences
- unknown faction tracking players
- artifact-related anomalies

---

---

# 💥 Ending 5 — Collapse / Violent Resolution

## Summary

The situation escalates into full conflict. One or both factions are destroyed, and the station may partially collapse.

---

## 🎙️ Read Aloud

> The situation does not resolve — it breaks. What begins as tension becomes action, and action becomes something that cannot be contained. Systems fail faster than they can be repaired, and the station itself begins to give way under the strain.
> 
> By the time you leave, there is little left to return to. Whatever was built here, whatever was fought over, is reduced to debris and silence. The outcome is final, but not clean.

---

## 💰 Rewards / Consequences

- salvage (if time allows)
- possible loss of ship / heavy damage
- injuries / crew losses

⚠️ consequence:

- Helios penalties (ship damage, mission failure)
- unstable reputation

---

## 🔗 Hook

- investigation into incident
- legal / corporate consequences
- search for survivors or wreckage

---

# 🧪 Optional Twist (Artifact-Based)

If players take the artifact:

---

## 🎙️ Add-On Outro

> It is some time after departure that the first inconsistencies begin to appear. Small at first — a delay in a system response, a mismatch in timing that cannot be easily explained. Nothing critical, nothing immediately dangerous.
> 
> But enough to notice.
> 
> Enough to question.
> 
> And enough to suggest that whatever was taken from the station did not remain behind.

---

## 🔗 Hook

- anomaly investigation
- corporate retrieval team
- scientific analysis arc

# 🎯 Final Note for GM

No ending should feel:

- perfectly clean
- fully resolved

---

👉 The best outcome is:

**survival with consequences**
