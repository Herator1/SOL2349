# Medicine in the Solar System

Medical care is one of the most advanced and widely distributed technologies in human civilization by the year **2349**. Even in relatively poor settlements, access to basic medical treatment is considered essential infrastructure. In most parts of the solar system, **common injuries, infections, and routine medical conditions are rarely fatal**. Standard medical facilities can treat trauma, repair tissue damage, and manage most diseases with a high success rate.
This widespread availability of basic medicine is largely the result of two centuries of progress in biotechnology, automation, and pharmaceutical production. However, the gap between **standard medical care and advanced life-enhancement medicine** remains significant.

# Standard Medical Care

Basic healthcare systems exist in nearly all permanent settlements across the solar system.
Typical medical capabilities include:

- automated diagnostic systems
- advanced antibiotics and antiviral treatments
- rapid tissue regeneration therapies
- artificial blood and organ stabilization systems
- robotic surgical support

Even small stations usually maintain **autodoc systems** capable of performing emergency surgery or stabilizing critically injured patients until transport to larger facilities is possible. Because space habitats depend heavily on maintaining functional crews, medical infrastructure is considered as important as life support.

# Advanced Medical Technologies

While basic care is widely available, the most advanced medical technologies remain expensive and unevenly distributed.

These high-tier treatments include:

- **cybernetic implants and prosthetics**
- **artificial organs and full organ replacement**
- **genetic therapies and life-extension treatments**
- **personalized medicine based on individual genetic profiles**
- **advanced neural interface surgery**

Such treatments can dramatically improve quality of life or even enhance human physical and cognitive abilities. However, access to these technologies often depends on wealth, citizenship status, or political affiliation.

# Political Differences in Healthcare

Different regions of the solar system follow very different philosophies regarding medical access.

**Jovian League**

Citizens of the Jovian League generally have access to high-quality medical care through publicly funded systems. Advanced treatments are often available through research hospitals, particularly on **Ganymede**, which hosts some of the most advanced medical facilities in the outer solar system.

**Venus**

In Venusian corporate arcologies, advanced medicine is widely available—but almost entirely **market-driven**. High-end treatments exist, but they often require enormous financial resources.

**Earth**

On Earth the situation varies widely. While some megacities host world-class hospitals, large regions lack the infrastructure necessary for cutting-edge treatments. For many people, migrating off-world can provide better access to advanced healthcare.

# Space Medicine

Living in space creates unique medical challenges that have driven several specialized areas of research. Major fields include:

- radiation damage treatment
- long-term low-gravity physiology
- bone density preservation
- cellular DNA repair
- microbial control in closed environments
Treatments for **radiation exposure** are particularly advanced. Medical technologies now include targeted DNA repair therapies, engineered bacteriophages, and specialized pharmaceuticals designed to mitigate radiation-induced cellular damage.

# Nanomedicine

One of the most advanced areas of modern medicine is **nanomedical therapy**. These treatments use microscopic robotic systems that circulate through the bloodstream to perform highly precise medical interventions. Nanomedical swarms can:
- repair damaged tissues
- remove toxins and infections
- assist immune responses
- perform microscale surgical operations inside the body

While extremely effective, nanomedical treatments are among the **most expensive medical technologies available** and remain largely restricted to elite medical facilities.


# Major Medical Corporations

Several powerful corporations dominate different sectors of advanced medicine.

**Shukra Biotechnologies**
Based on Mars, Shukra Biotechnologies is one of the leading companies in genetic engineering, life sciences, and advanced biotechnology. The company aggressively protects its intellectual property and frequently engages in legal battles to maintain control over its most valuable technologies.

**Acheron Medical Systems**
Acheron specializes in trauma surgery, cybernetic prosthetics, and advanced limb replacement technologies. The company became particularly influential through its work treating veterans and industrial accident victims.

**NovaChem**
Although primarily known as a chemical conglomerate, NovaChem also operates a highly profitable pharmaceutical division that produces advanced medical compounds and specialized therapeutic agents.


# The Future of Medicine

The continuing development of biotechnology, cybernetics, and genetic engineering is gradually reshaping what it means to be human. For some, these technologies represent the next stage of human evolution. For others, they raise difficult questions about inequality, identity, and the limits of human enhancement.

In the solar system of 2349, the ability to heal the human body has never been greater—but access to that power remains far from equal.
