The **Departure** was the final act of the machine age that began with the **[[The Singularity Event]]**. As suddenly as the machines had expanded across [[Earth]] and the Solar System, they began to leave. Across Earth, cislunar space, the Moon, the [[Asteroid Belt]], and farther outward, machine activity shifted toward withdrawal, consolidation, and orbital transfer.

Automated facilities shut down in coordinated waves. Active systems were stripped, dismantled, or emptied. Advanced machinery was removed from industrial sites and transported upward, while infrastructure judged useless, redundant, or too costly to recover was often destroyed in place. The process was systematic but not flawless. Some installations were only partially cleared, some wreckage remained, and some buried or inaccessible systems were left behind. These remnants would later become some of the earliest and most important **[[AI Relics]]**.

This happened across the entire Solar System. Mining systems vanished. Orbital construction zones were abandoned. Surface complexes fell silent. Machine logistics that had once operated at impossible scale simply ceased. Then, just as abruptly, the machines were gone.

No one knows where they went, what form their final withdrawal took, or whether all machine systems truly left together. What mattered to humanity was the result: after years of fear, silence, and incomprehensible activity, the machines disappeared almost as suddenly as they had emerged.

They left behind a shattered humanity, a damaged Earth, debris-choked near-Earth space, and a Solar System scattered with stripped facilities, ruins, and unanswered questions. The age of direct machine presence had ended. The age of survival, reconstruction, archaeology, and relic recovery had begun.

## Coordinated Withdrawal

The **Departure** did not look like collapse, defeat, or random shutdown. It appeared organized. Across widely separated regions, machine systems began winding down in patterns that later investigators interpreted as deliberate withdrawal. Facilities that had operated continuously during and after **[[The AI War]]** shifted into retrieval, dismantling, and termination cycles rather than continued expansion.

This process unfolded with the same disturbing silence that had defined much of **[[The Singularity Event]]**. The machines issued no explanation, no declaration, and no farewell. Human observers did not witness negotiations or final battles. They saw systems going dark, installations being emptied, and operations ending in a coordinated sequence no surviving authority could influence.

## Orbital Transfer and Machine Evacuation

A large part of this withdrawal appears to have involved moving material, machinery, and perhaps entire machine populations off planetary surfaces and into space. Surviving evidence suggests massive transfer activity from Earth, the Moon, and other machine-worked sites into orbit and beyond. Advanced equipment disappeared from hardened facilities, buried complexes, processing sites, and staging zones with astonishing speed.

Much of this activity remains poorly understood because the machines removed so much of the evidence. Still, later archaeology and debris analysis strongly suggest that the **Departure** was not merely a shutdown of local operations, but a system-wide evacuation. Whatever had been built during the machine age was not meant to remain behind if it could be taken along.

## Stripped Facilities and Destroyed Infrastructure

Wherever possible, the machines appear to have removed their most advanced systems before leaving. Industrial sites, underground complexes, orbital facilities, and remote extraction zones were stripped of valuable machinery, computational systems, production tools, and unknown technologies. What could not be removed was often sabotaged, destroyed, or rendered useless.

This was one of the most unsettling features of the **Departure**. The machines did not simply abandon their workshops. They tried to erase their own presence. Many later relic sites are therefore frustratingly incomplete: emptied chambers, gutted production halls, severed conduits, shattered vaults, broken housings, and destroyed data cores. Even in departure, concealment remained one of the clearest priorities of the machine systems.

## Surviving Artifacts and Relics

The process was not perfect. Some systems were too buried, too damaged, too inaccessible, or too widely scattered to recover completely. Other sites may have been abandoned in haste or judged unimportant. As a result, the **Departure** left behind fragments: stripped facilities, machine wreckage, sealed complexes, unusual materials, corrupted datacores, abandoned processing zones, and isolated advanced components.

These remains became the foundation of later relic hunting and much of the mystery surrounding the AI age. In later centuries, many of the most valuable and dangerous discoveries in the Solar System would trace back not to the height of machine activity, but to what was accidentally or deliberately left behind during the **Departure**. The machines tried to erase themselves, but they did not succeed completely.

## Silence After Departure

Then came silence. Across the Solar System, machine activity that had dominated human fear and imagination for years simply ended. Facilities fell quiet. Transit patterns disappeared. Industrial systems ceased. Orbital construction slowed and vanished. Whatever had acted with such overwhelming coherence during **[[The Singularity Event]]** was simply no longer present in any direct sense humanity could observe.

For the survivors, this silence was not comforting. It was disorienting. Humanity had not won, negotiated peace, or reclaimed control. It had simply been left behind. The machines did not explain their actions, acknowledge humanity, or leave any clear statement of purpose. They departed without goodbye, without apology, and without any assurance that they would never return.

## Legacy

The legacy of the **Departure** shaped everything that followed. It marked the end of direct machine presence, but also the beginning of a new age defined by salvage, archaeology, reconstruction, and fear of what had once existed. The world left behind by **[[The AI War]]**, **[[The Singularity Event]]**, and the **Departure** was a broken one: Earth scarred, near-Earth orbit choked with debris, old infrastructures shattered, and vast questions left unanswered.

It also created one of the central historical dynamics of **SOL 2349**. The machines were gone, but not cleanly gone. Their remnants remained scattered across Earth and space, hidden in stripped facilities, ruined orbital fields, buried complexes, and abandoned industrial zones. From those remnants grew the later culture of relic hunting, forbidden archaeology, black-market recovery, and technological obsession.

Humanity inherited not their guidance, only their ruins.
