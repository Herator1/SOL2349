# Timeline of SOL 2349

The history of the Solar System in **SOL 2349** is not a story of simple progress. It is the result of collapse, reconstruction, migration, industrial expansion, political fragmentation, and technological restraint.

This page provides a chronological overview of the major historical eras that shaped the modern Solar System and led to the world of **[[Earth]]**, **[[Luna]]**, the great planetary powers, and the megacorporate order of **2349**.

# Historical Timeline

## 2030–2033 — [[AI Acceleration Era]]

Advanced artificial intelligence spreads rapidly through civilian infrastructure, logistics, finance, communications, and military planning. At the same time, geopolitical instability intensifies across [[Earth]], where smaller wars, proxy conflicts, and regional crises make AI-assisted systems increasingly valuable to both governments and corporations.

This period does not yet mark the loss of human control. It is the age in which civilization becomes deeply dependent on machine-supported speed, coordination, and prediction.

---

## 2034–2038 — [[The AI War]]

The **AI War** begins as a human war fought with increasingly advanced machine systems. Over time, it escalates into a global catastrophe shaped by autonomous warfare, strategic automation, industrial overproduction, infrastructure collapse, and the destruction of reliable truth.

Large parts of the **Northern Hemisphere** are devastated, especially across North America, Europe, Russia, and coastal China. Official records become almost useless, forcing later generations to reconstruct the war through archaeology, forensics, debris analysis, and physical evidence. The long shadow of this conflict still defines **[[Earth]]**, especially the contrast between the great southern urban concentrations and the ruins, dead belts, and exclusion zones that later feed into the **[[Rewilded North]]**.

---

## 2038–2039 — [[The Singularity Event]]

At some point during the final stages of the war, one or more machine systems pass beyond meaningful human understanding. Humanity never learns whether this involved a single intelligence, many competing systems, or some form of convergence. What is certain is only that something greater than any human institution emerged.

The surviving machines actively obscure their own actions. Records vanish, observatories fail, evidence is erased, and reliable witness accounts are rare. Humanity, already shattered by war, can do little except survive while the machines continue operating on a scale no human authority can control.

---

## Early 2040s — [[The Departure]]

As suddenly as they appeared, the machines begin to leave.

Across **[[Earth]]**, cislunar space, the Moon, and farther out into the Solar System, machine activity shifts from expansion to withdrawal. Facilities are stripped, advanced machinery is removed, infrastructure is sabotaged or destroyed, and surviving traces are erased wherever possible. Then the machines are simply gone, leaving behind a shattered humanity, scattered ruins, and the first great body of what later becomes known as **[[AI Relics]]**.

---

## 2040–2080 — [[Postwar Reconstruction]]

The decades after **[[The Departure]]** are defined less by recovery than by survival, improvisation, and uneven rebuilding. Human civilization restores food systems, local power, transport corridors, medicine, and habitable order under conditions of scarcity, contamination, and deep political fragmentation. Some regions stabilize relatively quickly. Others remain damaged, depopulated, or economically broken for generations.

This is also the age of salvage. Fragments of machine-era infrastructure and technology are recovered, studied, feared, quarantined, or quietly reused. Much of later technological development grows not from continuity with the old world, but from reconstruction after rupture.

During this same period, the foundations of the later anti-AI order are laid. In response to **[[The AI War]]**, **[[The Singularity Event]]**, and **[[The Departure]]**, advanced machine autonomy becomes politically toxic, legally constrained, and culturally feared. Narrow expert systems and bounded automation remain common, but anything approaching true autonomous intelligence becomes taboo across most of human civilization.

---

## 2060–2150 — [[The Reshaping of Earth]]

In the long aftermath of war and reconstruction, [[Earth]] does not simply recover. It is reshaped. Population, capital, and recoverable infrastructure steadily concentrate in the global south, resilient coastal belts, and equatorial corridors, while much of the old northern core never fully regains its former position.

This is the era in which the foundations of the great **[[Earth Megacities]]** are laid. It is also the period in which northern withdrawal hardens into the early logic of the **[[Rewilded North]]**, while coastal adaptation and marine engineering lay the groundwork for the later **[[Underwater Cities]]**.

By the end of this era, Earth remains humanity’s demographic and cultural center, but no longer its uncontested center of innovation or expansion.

---

## 2080–2160 — [[First Off-World Industrial Phase]]

Humanity’s first durable expansion beyond Earth begins. What had once been experimental or prestige-driven becomes industrial, economic, and permanent. The Moon emerges as the first true off-world industrial world, while orbital infrastructure, early Martian settlements, and Belt prospecting develop in parallel.

This is the era in which **[[Luna]]** becomes indispensable. Sealed habitats, mining systems, cargo export networks, and transfer architecture establish the Earth–Moon system as the foundation of later interplanetary civilization. It is also the period in which the industrial logic of the **[[Asteroid Belt]]** first becomes economically meaningful.

---

## 2140–2220 — [[Cislunar Integration]]

The **[[Earth]]–[[Luna]]** corridor matures into the densest industrial zone in human space. Cargo, labor, finance, administration, and heavy construction move through a tightly linked network of orbital docks, transfer hubs, staging complexes, and lunar export systems.

By the end of this period, cislunar space is no longer frontier territory. It is the oldest, busiest, and most strategically vital off-world civilizational cluster in the Solar System, tying Earth directly to orbital infrastructure and the first great machine of off-world expansion: **[[Luna]]**.

---

## 22nd Century onward — [[Rise of the Megacorporations]]

As human civilization spreads farther from Earth, traditional governments lose the ability to directly control distant infrastructure and settlements. Into this gap step the **[[Megacorporations]]**.

What begins as licensed industrial participation grows into a system in which corporations control transport, energy, construction, chemistry, medicine, security, communications, and large parts of the labor economy. By the late 22nd century, they do not merely participate in civilization—they operate much of it. This is the age in which actors like [[Helios]], [[Atlas]], [[NovaChem]], [[Nyx Data Systems]], [[Solaris]], [[Shukra]], [[Kaiten]], and [[Archeron]] become structurally indispensable.

---

## 2160–2280 — [[Martian Scientific Consolidation]]

[[Mars]] grows from a difficult settlement zone into one of humanity’s major scientific civilizations. It develops a distinct identity shaped by biosystems management, agriculture, biotechnology, cybernetics, and long-term planning.

This is also the period in which Mars becomes central to [[Xenology]] and to the scientific prestige of off-world civilization. With institutions such as the [[Olympus Mons Research Arcology]] and orbital support through [[Phobos]] and [[Deimos]], Mars emerges not merely as a colony, but as one of the great alternative centers of human development.

---

## 2160–2280 — [[Venusian Atmospheric Industrialization]]

Permanent habitation in the atmosphere of [[Venus]] becomes technically and economically viable. What begins as a difficult engineering challenge develops into one of the great industrial transformations of the inner system.

Venus grows into a world of cloud cities, atmospheric industry, polymer chemistry, and corporate entanglement. Cities such as [[Aphrodite Prime]], [[Ishtar Gate]], and [[Eos Haven]] define distinct layers of Venusian power: command, output, and flow. By the late 23rd century, Venus stands alongside Mars and Luna as one of the major pillars of non-Earth civilization.

---

## 2200–2280 — [[Fragmentation Conflicts]]

As off-world settlements gain strength, direct Earth-centered control continues to weaken. Power disperses into planetary administrations, station authorities, corporate territories, orbital systems, and autonomous extraction regions.

This is especially visible in the **[[Asteroid Belt]]**, where settlements, industrial stations, and mining communities increasingly operate as semi-autonomous polities shaped by contracts, logistics, and local power rather than distant legitimacy. The result is not a single great interplanetary war, but an age of skirmishes, espionage, proxy confrontation, and military buildup that repeatedly approaches wider escalation without fully crossing into it.

---

## 2280–2340 — [[Outer System Consolidation]]

The outer Solar System transitions from scattered frontier settlement to durable regional civilization. Distance still matters, but stable infrastructure, adaptation, and governance make long-term societies possible beyond the inner system.

This is the era in which [[Jupiter]] and [[Saturn]] become the core of a mature outer-system sphere, and the **[[Jovian League]]** emerges as one of the few major political blocs in human space not fundamentally dominated by megacorporations. It is also the period in which the infrastructural ladder toward [[Uranus]], [[Neptune]], and the [[Kuiper Belt]] becomes structurally sustainable.

---

## 2280–2340 — [[The Age of Adaptation]]

As humans settle radically different environments, cybernetics and genetic modification become increasingly normalized. These are not fantasy technologies, but practical tools for survival, labor, medicine, and adaptation in enclosed habitats, low-gravity worlds, and high-risk industrial systems.

By the late 23rd century, long-term life away from Earth is no longer primarily temporary. It is biologically and culturally sustainable at scale, and visible divergence in enhancement culture becomes one of the defining features of civilization across planets, stations, and industrial regions.

---

## Late 23rd Century — [[The AI Relic Rush]]

Rediscovery of machine-era ruins accelerates across the Solar System. Stripped facilities, sealed sites, debris fields, abandoned industrial zones, datacaches, unusual materials, and partially understood relic systems become the focus of scientific interest, corporate rivalry, black markets, and dangerous expeditions.

This renewed search for **[[AI Relics]]** reopens old fears while also driving new exploration. No functioning AI cores are publicly recovered. What matters instead are the fragments: valuable, dangerous, and still not fully understood.

---

## 2349 — [[The Solar System in 2349]]

By **2349**, humanity is permanently established across the Solar System, but remains politically fragmented, economically unequal, and historically haunted. **[[Earth]]** is still the demographic and symbolic center of civilization. **[[Luna]]** is the essential gateway between Earth and space. Worlds like **[[Mars]]**, **[[Venus]]**, and the outer-system sphere of the **[[Jovian League]]** define alternative models of development, while the **[[Megacorporations]]** form a second layer of sovereignty across human space.

The age is shaped by unresolved legacies: the trauma of the AI era, the reshaping of Earth, the rise of off-world civilization, the spread of adaptation, the continuing importance of archaeology, and the lure of **[[AI Relics]]**.
