Some phenomena surrounding the AI era remain poorly understood and are often the subject of rumors, conspiracy theories, or classified research programs. These include:

### Partially Functional AI Artifacts

Occasionally, explorers or corporations recover relics dating back to the AI era. Most of these systems are damaged or incomprehensible, but some exhibit limited functionality.

Examples include:
- autonomous navigation systems that outperform modern equivalents
- exotic energy devices with unknown internal architecture
- fragments of computational systems that cannot be replicated with current technology
Such artifacts are extremely valuable and frequently traded on the black market.

### The “Quiet Pattern”

Historians and data scientists have noted an unusual statistical pattern in human history since the end of the AI War. Large-scale conflicts between major factions **almost never escalate into full wars**, even in situations where geopolitical conditions would historically have made war highly likely.

Most scholars attribute this to:
- economic interdependence  
- the fragility of space infrastructure
- improved conflict mediation mechanisms

However, some researchers believe the pattern may indicate **unknown stabilizing mechanisms within interplanetary systems**.

### Smooth Technological Progression

Another topic of discussion is the unusually stable development of technology since the AI War. Major breakthroughs continue to occur, but catastrophic technological failures—once common during earlier periods of rapid innovation—have become surprisingly rare.

Some researchers speculate that this stability may be linked to unknown constraints or self-regulating processes in advanced technological systems. No consensus explanation currently exists.
