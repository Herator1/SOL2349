# ⚙️ Augmentations & Equipment

In _SOL 2349_, survival and effectiveness are not defined by supernatural abilities, but by **access to technology**. Characters do not gain powers in the traditional sense—instead, they rely on **equipment, implants, and engineered systems** to extend what they are capable of. Technology replaces abilities. It allows characters to act faster, overcome limitations, and interact with the world in ways that would otherwise be impossible.

At its core, technology serves two roles: it **expands what a character can do** and **improves how effectively they can do it**. Skills represent training and experience, while technology provides **new functions and system-level advantages**. A well-timed implant activation can decide a fight, bypass an obstacle entirely, or create opportunities that would not exist otherwise.

---

## 🧬 Implants (Core System)

Implants are the central augmentation system in _SOL 2349_. They are **active, controllable systems** that integrate directly with the character’s body and mind. Their use is tightly connected to the **Action Economy** and constrained by two key mechanics: **Load** and **Mod Space**.

Most implant abilities require either a normal Action or are used as **Augmented Actions**. Every activation generates Load, representing the strain on the nervous system and internal systems. As Load increases, so does the risk of instability. Pushing beyond safe limits can trigger **Overload**, leading to reduced performance, system failure, or worse.

---

## 🔁 Augmented Actions

Some implants allow abilities to be used **outside the normal action flow**. These are called Augmented Actions. They do not consume an Action and can be used both during a character’s turn and reactively in response to events. However, they are never “free”: they always generate Load and are subject to timing and usage limits.

Augmented Actions represent a fundamental trade-off in the system. Instead of spending Actions, a character spends **system stability**. This creates a constant tension between acting faster and pushing too far.

---

## 🔒 Specialization

Implants are organized into distinct categories such as Combat, Engineering, Survival, or Operations. Each character may only access **two categories**, forcing meaningful specialization. Hybrid implants exist at the intersection of two categories and require access to both.

This structure replaces traditional class systems. Characters are defined by their **technological profile**—what systems they run, how they combine them, and what risks they are willing to take.

---

## 🧬 Genetic Modifications

Genetic modifications represent a different form of enhancement. They are **permanent, passive adaptations** that are always active and do not generate Load. In exchange, they come with inherent drawbacks and cannot be switched off or adjusted.

While implants offer control and flexibility, genetic modifications represent **long-term adaptation**. They define how a character has been shaped by life in extreme environments.

---

## 🔫 Equipment, Tools, and Systems

Beyond implants, characters rely heavily on equipment. Weapons and tactical gear determine damage output, engagement style, and combat options, interacting directly with positioning, cover, and the Action Economy. Tools and devices extend a character’s capabilities outside combat, enabling engineering work, medical treatment, scanning, and system interaction. In many cases, these tools are not optional—they are required to even attempt certain actions.

Drones and robotic systems further extend a character’s reach. They provide remote presence, support functions, and additional flexibility, but require attention and coordination. Whether directly controlled or operating in a limited autonomous mode, they remain tools—not independent actors.

Consumables represent short-term advantages. Stims, emergency treatments, and other single-use items provide immediate effects, often at a cost. They are powerful, but limited, and frequently come with side effects.

---

## ⚖️ System Limits

Technology in _SOL 2349_ is powerful, but always constrained. **Load** limits how frequently systems can be used in a short timeframe, creating immediate pressure and the risk of Overload. **Mod Space** limits how many implants a character can install, defining long-term capacity and forcing meaningful choices.

Power is never free. Every advantage is tied to **risk, limitation, or trade-off**.

---

## 💰 Economy & Availability

Access to technology is shaped by the economic and political reality of the setting. Advanced systems are not just rare—they are expensive, controlled, and often restricted. Typical individuals earn between **1 and 15 SOL per year**, while advanced implants can cost **10 SOL or more**, and spacecraft range into the hundreds or thousands.

Availability varies strongly by location. Core worlds and major stations offer broad access, while remote installations are limited. Corporate and military-grade technology is often restricted entirely. The Game Master may use cost, legality, and faction access as meaningful constraints.

---

Technology defines what characters can do—but it never removes the need for judgment. Knowing **when to use it, how far to push it, and when to hold back** is what separates professionals from casualties.
