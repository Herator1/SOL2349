Consumables are single-use items that provide **immediate utility, short-term medical support, or temporary tactical advantage**. They are often the fastest way to solve a problem under pressure, but they are limited, expendable, and sometimes dangerous.

Unless explicitly stated otherwise, using a consumable requires **1 Action**.

There are **no bonus actions** in _SOL 2349_. Using a consumable always competes with:

- attacking
- moving
- activating implants
- reacting

## Core Rules

- Consumables are usually **single-use**
- Standard use costs **1 Action**
- If an item requires longer application, this is listed as **Multi-Action**
- Consumables do **not** normally require a test unless the item or situation says otherwise
- If a consumable creates an ongoing effect, its duration is listed in the item entry
- If a consumable affects an area, the GM determines exact placement and who is affected

## Medical Use and Multi-Action Items

**Multi-Action** items require extended handling and are not resolved with a single quick interaction.

As a baseline:

- the first Action begins use
- additional Actions complete treatment, repair, or setup
- the GM decides whether those Actions must be consecutive

Typical guidance:

- **Field Repair Kit:** usually **3–4 Actions**
- **Advanced Repair Kit:** usually **4+ Actions**
- **First Aid Kit:** usually **2–3 Actions**
- **Trauma Kit:** usually **1 Action to begin stabilization**, but full treatment takes longer

This keeps emergency use possible while preventing heavy kits from becoming instant reset buttons in combat.

See [[Medicine & Stabilization]] for medical details.

Combat stims are legal, controlled, or semi-regulated chemical enhancers designed for emergency use. They are powerful, but always come with side effects or delayed cost.

## Duration Rules

Consumable effects follow these general rules:

- **Immediate effects** happen when the item is used
- **Next roll** effects expire once used
- **This turn only** effects end at the end of the current turn
- **X turns** means the current turn counts as the first turn unless the GM rules otherwise
- Delayed drawbacks apply immediately when the listed duration ends

## Consumables in Combat

Consumables are strongest when:

- they solve a specific problem quickly
- they buy time
- they shift position or pressure
- they keep a character operational for one more turn

They should not replace core systems such as medicine, movement, or equipment specialization.

## Design Notes

Consumables should remain:

- **fast**
- **limited**
- **situational**
- **worth carrying**
- but never mandatory in every single turn

Stims in particular should always feel like a trade:

- immediate gain
- delayed cost
- possible physiological or psychological consequence
