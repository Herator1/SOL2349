# Drones and Robotics

Drones are **remote-operated or semi-autonomous systems** that extend a character’s reach into combat, hazardous environments, and technical operations.

They are not independent characters. They are **tools with limited agency**: mobile platforms that can scout, assist, repair, observe, or provide controlled support under the operator’s direction.

## Core Concept

A drone usually operates in one of two modes:

- **Direct Control** — the operator actively spends Actions to command it
- **Autonomous Mode** — the drone follows simple standing instructions with limited effectiveness

Drones are useful because they extend presence and capability. They are limited because they still require control, bandwidth, and tactical attention.

## Direct Control

Under **Direct Control**, the operator actively commands the drone.

**Rule:**  
Spend **1 Action** → one drone performs **1 meaningful action**

This action usually uses the operator’s:

- relevant **Attribute**
- plus **Robotics**

Examples:

- attack
- scan
- reposition
- manipulate a simple tool
- begin a repair task
- maintain observation on a target

### Direct Control Rules

- each meaningful drone action costs **1 Action**
- the drone acts as part of **your** action economy
- direct drone use competes with your attacks, movement, implants, and other choices
- if the drone makes attacks, those count as **attacks for your round economy** unless a specific rule says otherwise
- drones do not gain their own separate full turn economy

This prevents drones from becoming free extra characters.

## Autonomous Mode

If not actively controlled, a drone may operate in **Autonomous Mode**.

Autonomous drones can follow only simple instructions, such as:

- hold position
- follow a designated target
- continue a simple non-complex task
- maintain overwatch or observation
- return to the operator
- keep a route or patrol pattern

### Autonomous Limitations

Autonomous drones normally:

- do **not** attack
- do **not** make complex tactical choices
- do **not** improvise well
- do **not** perform precise multi-step operations without supervision

If precise judgment, combat timing, or complex problem-solving is required, autonomy is not enough.

## Drone Durability

Drones can be targeted, damaged, disabled, and destroyed.

Use the same basic damage flow as other units:

**Guard → Integrity → destroyed**

Drones do **not** take Wounds.

### Notes

- **Guard** reflects movement, positioning, and limited protective design
- **Integrity** reflects structural durability and internal systems
- once Integrity is reduced to 0, the drone is destroyed or rendered inoperable

## Drone Actions

Common drone actions include:

### Attack

Requires **Direct Control** unless a specific drone rule says otherwise.  
Uses the operator’s relevant **Attribute + Robotics**.

### Scan

Detect enemies, anomalies, movement, signals, or environmental data.

### Repair

Perform repair or support work using tools, attached modules, or built-in systems.  
Actual results depend on drone type, equipment, and context.

### Reposition

Move the drone to a new position, angle, or zone.

### Support

Provide a specialized benefit such as shielding, carrying, relay support, or sensor assistance.

## Drone Control Limits

A character may control more than one drone, but each meaningful drone action still costs **1 Action**.

This means:

- one Action controls one drone’s meaningful action
- multiple drones can be used in the same round only if the operator spends multiple Actions
- autonomous drones remain limited to simple non-complex behavior

This keeps drones useful without replacing the operator.

## Signal, Range, and Interference

Drones usually require a stable control link unless operating autonomously.

The GM may apply limits, interference, or signal loss due to:

- bulkheads
- dense station structure
- jamming
- radiation
- EMP effects
- long distance
- severe environmental disruption

In uncertain cases, treat drone control as possible unless the scene gives a clear reason why it should be difficult or impossible.

## Individual Units

### Micro Drone

**Cost:** 0.2–0.5 SOL  
**Stats:** Integrity 4 / Guard 1

A small stealth platform used for surveillance, scouting, and observation. Hard to detect, but fragile.

### Survey Drone

**Cost:** 0.5–1.0 SOL  
**Stats:** Integrity 6 / Guard 1

Used for mapping, anomaly detection, and sensor sweeps. Better at analysis than stealth.

### Utility Drone

**Cost:** 0.5–1.5 SOL  
**Stats:** Integrity 8 / Guard 2

A general-purpose work platform for carrying gear, operating tools, or assisting with routine tasks.

### Repair Bot

**Cost:** 1–3 SOL  
**Stats:** Integrity 10 / Guard 2

Designed for engineering support, damage stabilization, and repair tasks in hostile or unstable conditions.

### EVA Drone

**Cost:** 1–2 SOL  
**Stats:** Integrity 8 / Guard 2

A vacuum-capable drone with thruster support and good mobility in external operations.

### Combat Drone (Light)

**Cost:** 2–5 SOL  
**Stats:** Integrity 8 / Guard 2

A mobile support drone equipped for ranged engagement under active operator control.

### Combat Drone (Heavy)

**Cost:** 5–10 SOL  
**Stats:** Integrity 12 / Guard 3

A larger and more durable combat platform with heavier weapons and slower repositioning.

### Shield Drone

**Cost:** 2–4 SOL  
**Stats:** Integrity 6 / Guard 2

Projects defensive support to one visible ally.

**Support Rule:**  
When directly controlled, the Shield Drone may grant **+2 Guard** to one target within line of sight.  
This costs **1 Action** and the bonus normally lasts until the start of your next turn.  
The effect does not stack with itself unless a rule says otherwise.

## Drones in Combat

Drones are valuable in combat because they:

- extend line of sight
- provide remote presence
- threaten angles
- support allies
- enter dangerous zones without risking the operator directly

But they are limited because:

- they consume Actions
- they can be destroyed
- they are vulnerable to signal loss and hazards
- autonomy is intentionally weak in tactical situations

A drone should feel like a force multiplier, not an extra full party member.

## Drones and Hazards

Drones are often especially useful in:

- vacuum
- toxic atmosphere
- fire zones
- unstable gravity
- radiation exposure
- structural collapse risk
- contaminated engineering spaces

They are still vulnerable to damage, interference, and environmental destruction, but they can often enter places humans cannot safely reach.

## Design Principle

Drones in _SOL 2349_ should support:

- remote problem-solving
- tactical support
- hazardous-environment gameplay
- engineering and reconnaissance scenes

They should not replace player action. They should extend it.
