# Genetic Modifications

Genemods are **permanent biological adaptations** engineered to help humans survive and function in the harsh environments of space. Unlike [[Implant Overview|Implants]], they are always active, generate **no [[Load]]**, and cannot normally be switched off or removed.

They are not conventional upgrades, but **biological trade-offs**. Every Genemod improves performance in one area while creating a meaningful drawback somewhere else. A body adapted for one environment, one stress pattern, or one operational role often becomes worse at something else.

In many parts of the Solar System, Genemods are not exotic. They are part of how populations adapted to life away from Earth: station-born low-g bodies, radiation-tolerant industrial workers, controlled emotional profiles for confined habitats, or specialized alterations for scientific, medical, or military roles.

Because of this, Genemods shape not only capability, but identity. They influence how a character moves, reacts, recovers, senses danger, and copes with pressure.

## Rules

- Maximum: **3 total**
- At character creation: **up to 2**
- Genemods are **always active**
- Genemods require **no Actions**
- Genemods generate **no Load**
- Genemods do **not** use **Mod Space**
- Identical Genemods do not stack

## Genemods vs Implants

Genemods are not mini-implants.

- **Genemods** are passive, permanent, and biological
- **Implants** are active, configurable, and system-driven
- Genemods usually change how a body or mind functions all the time
- Implants create discrete moments of increased capability

A Genemod should feel like part of who a character **is**, not just part of what they can activate.

## Design Note

Genemods should remain **clear, passive, and double-edged**. They are strongest when they:

- create a distinct biological profile
- change how a character behaves in play
- reflect upbringing, adaptation, or engineered purpose
- offer real advantages without becoming strictly better than implants

A good Genemod should feel like a lasting physiological truth about the character.
