Implants are the defining edge of modern operators in SOL 2349. They are not passive upgrades or background enhancements—they are active systems designed for moments where human capability is no longer enough.

Every implant represents a deliberate trade-off. Space inside the body is limited, and so is the strain a system can endure. Choosing implants is not about stacking power, but about defining how a character performs under pressure—what they are capable of when it matters most.

Some implants enhance reflexes and combat efficiency. Others provide tools for engineering, medicine, or deep-space operations. Some interface directly with machines, while others alter perception, cognition, or social interaction. No implant stands alone—true strength comes from how they are combined and when they are activated.

But all implants share one fundamental limitation: they push the human body beyond safe operating thresholds. Every activation builds strain. Every system has limits. And exceeding those limits comes at a cost.

This creates a core tension at the heart of the system:

- Precision or improvisation
- Control or escalation
- Safety or performance

Used with discipline, implants turn skilled individuals into highly effective specialists. Used recklessly, they become a liability—overloading systems, disrupting actions, and potentially taking their user out of the fight.

The following categories group implants by their primary function and field of application. Each offers a different approach to problem-solving, survival, and control in the harsh environments of the solar system.
