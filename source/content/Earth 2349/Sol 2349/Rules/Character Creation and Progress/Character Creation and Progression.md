Characters in **SOL 2349** are defined by a combination of **attributes, skills, and enhancements**, allowing for a high degree of flexibility without adding unnecessary complexity at the table. The system is designed to represent **capable professionals**, not superheroes—individuals who are skilled in their field, but still vulnerable in a hostile environment.

At the core of character creation is a **point-based system**. Players receive a pool of **Progress Points (PP)**, which can be freely allocated to build their character. These points are used to improve **skills**, increase **attributes**, and enhance **base capabilities** such as health, stress tolerance, or augmentation capacity. Costs increase with higher levels, making specialization increasingly expensive while still allowing for broad competence.

Skills are organized into clearly defined categories linked to attributes, ensuring that characters develop in a structured but flexible way. Attributes provide a broad foundation, while skills represent specific training and expertise. Together, they determine how effective a character is in different situations.

In addition to training and experience, characters can be enhanced through **augmentations**. These include cybernetic implants and genetic modifications, which provide powerful abilities but are limited by system capacity and resource constraints.

Progression continues throughout the campaign using the same system. Characters earn additional Progress Points through play and can invest them to improve their abilities over time. This creates a consistent and transparent advancement system where early improvements are accessible, while mastery requires significant investment.
