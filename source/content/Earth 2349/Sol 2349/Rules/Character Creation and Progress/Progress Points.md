Characters in SOL 2349 improve through **Progress Points (PP)**. These represent experience gained through missions, problem-solving, and meaningful actions during play.

Progress Points are used to improve **skills, attributes, and base stats**, allowing characters to grow over time in a consistent and transparent way.

---
## Advancement

Improving a value costs a number of Progress Points equal to the **next level**.

Cost = next level

This creates a **progressive cost system**, where early improvements are cheap, but higher levels require significant investment.

---
### Skill Advancement

|Level|Total Cost|
|---|---|
|0 → 1|1|
|0 → 2|3|
|0 → 3|6|
|0 → 4|10|
|0 → 5|15|
|0 → 6|21|
|0 → 7|28|

---

### Attribute Advancement

Attributes follow the same progression, but cost **double**.

Attribute cost = 2 × next level

This reflects their broad impact across multiple skills and derived stats.

---

## What Can Be Improved

Progress Points can be spent on:

- **Skills**
    
- **Attributes**
    
- **Base Stats** (Health, Stress, Load, Wounds, Mod Space)
    

This allows players to decide between:

- becoming more skilled
    
- improving overall capability
    
- increasing survivability or system capacity
    

---

## Gaining Progress Points

Progress Points are awarded at the end of a session.

The exact amount depends on:

- mission success
    
- difficulty of challenges
    
- roleplay and decision-making
    
- discoveries and problem-solving
    

---

### Typical Rewards

|Session Type|PP Gained|
|---|---|
|Short / low intensity|2–3 PP|
|Standard session|3–5 PP|
|Major mission / milestone|5–8 PP|

---

### Additional Awards (optional)

The GM may grant small bonuses for:

- exceptional roleplay
    
- creative problem-solving
    
- critical discoveries
    
- completing personal objectives
    

Typical bonus:

+1 PP

---

## Progression Pace

At this rate:

- Small improvements (e.g. skill +1) can happen every **1–2 sessions**
    
- Reaching higher levels requires **long-term investment**
    
- Mastery (level 5+) is a significant achievement
    
- Levels 6–7 represent **exceptional individuals**
    

---

## Summary

The Progress Point system ensures steady and meaningful growth.  
Characters improve gradually, with increasing specialization over time, while maintaining flexibility in how they develop.
