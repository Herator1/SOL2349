# ⚙️ **Time & Actions**

Time during combat and other high-intensity situations is measured in **rounds**.  
One round represents roughly **five seconds** of in-world time.

During each round, all characters act once.

---
## 🎲 **Actions**

On their turn, a character has **3 Actions**.

Actions can be used freely for:

- movement
- attacks
- abilities
- interactions

👉 There are **no action types** (no bonus actions, no separate reactions).

---

## 🔁 **Reactive Actions**

Actions do not need to be used immediately.

- Any unused Action can be used **later in the round**
- including **outside the character’s turn**

These are called **Reactive Actions**

---

### Examples:

- Dodging an incoming attack
- Firing at a moving target
- Activating an implant in response to danger

---

### Rules:

- Reactive Actions must be triggered by a **clear event**
- The GM decides if an action is valid in the moment
- Complex or slow actions cannot be used reactively

---

## ❗ **Action Limit**

- A character can use up to **5 Actions per round**
- (via implants, abilities, or overdrive effects)

---

## ⚖️ **Diminishing Returns**

Repeated use of the same type of action becomes less effective.

Each action category tracks its own usage per round.

---

### 🟥 Attack

|Use|Modifier|
|---|---|
|1st|0|
|2nd|−2|
|3rd|−4|
|4th|−6|
|5th|−8|

---

### 🟪 Implant / Ability

|Use|Cost|
|---|---|
|1st|no additional cost|
|2nd|+1 Load|
|3rd|+2 Load|
|4th|+2 Load|
|5th|+3 Load|

---

### 🟦 Movement

|Use|Distance|
|---|---|
|1st|10 m|
|2nd|5 m|
|3rd|3 m|
|4th|3 m|
|5th|3 m|

---

### ⚫ Utility Actions

Some actions do not scale and can be performed without penalties:

- pressing buttons
- opening doors
- basic interactions

These are considered **Utility Actions**. Some examples include:

### **Take Aim**

- Use 1 Action
- Gain **+2 to next attack**
- Improve critical range by 1 (e.g. 19-20)


---

## 🧠 **Load & Overuse**

Using more actions or pushing systems increases **Load**.

- High Load introduces risk
- Overuse may cause system instability or failure
- Load may lead to **mental strain** over time
