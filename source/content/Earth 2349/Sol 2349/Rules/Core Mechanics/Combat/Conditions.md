Conditions are temporary states that affect a character’s **actions, survivability, control, or effectiveness**. They may be caused by combat, environmental hazards, wounds, overload, equipment problems, or psychological pressure.

This page collects the most important recurring conditions in one place. Unless a rule says otherwise, conditions apply immediately when triggered and remain in effect until their duration ends or they are removed.

Conditions are grouped into:

- **Physical Conditions**
- **Mental Conditions**
- **System Conditions**

## Using Conditions

A condition should always answer three questions clearly:

- **What causes it?**
- **What does it do?**
- **How long does it last?**

If multiple conditions affect the same character, apply all relevant effects unless two conditions clearly overlap and the GM rules that only the stronger one applies.

## Physical Conditions

## Crouched

A crouched character presents a smaller target and is harder to hit at range.

**Effect:**

- **+1 DC vs ranged attacks**

**Duration:**

- until the character changes position

**Notes:**  
Changing to or from crouched is usually part of movement, but may cost **1 Action** in restricted or unstable situations.

## Prone

A prone character is low to the ground or pressed against a surface.

**Effect:**

- **+2 DC vs ranged attacks**
- **−2 DC vs melee attacks** against them

**Duration:**

- until the character changes position

**Notes:**  
Going prone may be voluntary or caused by impact, suppression, failed movement, or other effects. Standing up is usually part of movement, but may cost **1 Action** in difficult conditions.

## Incapacitated

An incapacitated character cannot act effectively.

**Effect:**

- cannot take Actions
- cannot take Reactive Actions
- cannot meaningfully defend themselves unless a rule says otherwise

**Duration:**

- depends on the cause

**Common causes:**

- taking a **Wound**
- reaching maximum Wounds and entering **Dying**
- extreme hazard effects
- some overload or system failure results

**Notes:**  
A character who takes a Wound is usually incapacitated **until the end of their next turn**. A dying character remains incapacitated until stabilized or dead.

## Dying

A dying character has reached their **maximum Wounds** and is at immediate risk of death.

**Effect:**

- the character is incapacitated
- cannot act
- cannot take Reactions
- will die if not stabilized in time

**Duration:**

- until stabilized, dead, or otherwise saved by a specific effect

**Notes:**  
A dying character usually survives for a limited number of turns equal to their **maximum Wounds**, unless another rule says otherwise. See [[Medicine & Stabilization]] and [[Survivability: Guard, Health & Wounds]].

## Restrained

A restrained character has their movement significantly limited by physical force, a tether, debris, a grapple, or a similar restriction.

**Effect:**

- movement is reduced or impossible
- may be unable to [[Evade]]
- may suffer **Setback** on physical actions
- may be easier to hit or control, depending on the cause

**Duration:**

- until freed

**Notes:**  
The exact effect depends on the source. A loose tether and pinned limbs are both restraint, but not equally severe.

## Unstabilized

An unstabilized character is drifting, off-balance, tumbling, or otherwise unable to control their position properly.

This condition is most common in **microgravity** and **zero-g**.

**Effect:**

- may suffer **Setback** on precision-based and physical actions
- counts as moving for targeting purposes where relevant
- may be unable to [[Evade]] effectively
- may require a test to stop, turn, or regain control

**Duration:**

- until stabilized through a surface, tether, controlled thrust, handhold, mag boots, or similar means

**Notes:**  
See [[Movement]].

## Mental Conditions

Mental strain is tracked through four escalating conditions.

## Unsettled

The character is distracted, shaken internally, or struggling to maintain focus.

**Effect:**

- **−1 to concentration-based actions**

**Duration:**

- until reduced through rest, support, or other recovery

## Shaken

The character is under stronger mental pressure and has trouble staying composed.

**Effect:**

- **−2 to concentration-based actions**

**Duration:**

- until reduced through rest, support, or other recovery

## Strained

The character is mentally overloaded and functioning poorly across the board.

**Effect:**

- **−2 to all actions**

**Duration:**

- until reduced through rest, support, or other recovery

## Breaking Point

The character temporarily loses control under extreme psychological pressure.

**Effect:**

- immediate loss of control
- the GM determines the short-term reaction
- after the effect resolves, reduce the condition by **1 level**, usually to **Strained**

**Possible reactions:**

- panic movement
- freezing
- withdrawal
- irrational action
- emotional collapse

**Duration:**

- immediate crisis, then reduced by 1 level

**Notes:**  
See [[Mental Strain & Psychological Pressure]].

## System Conditions

## Low Ammo

A weapon under **Low Ammo** is running inefficiently, overheating, depleting its feed, or otherwise losing sustained fire capability.

**Effect:**

- **−2 to all attack rolls** with that weapon

**Duration:**

- until **Reload** is used

**Common cause:**

- automatically triggered by certain **Full Auto** fire modes

**Notes:**  
Low Ammo is tracked **per weapon**, not per character, unless a specific rule says otherwise.

## Implant Lock

A specific implant becomes unusable.

**Effect:**

- the affected implant cannot be activated

**Duration:**

- usually for the rest of the encounter, unless another rule says otherwise

**Common cause:**

- severe [[Implants & Load|Overload]] result

## System Shutdown

Implant systems stop responding temporarily.

**Effect:**

- the character cannot activate implants

**Duration:**

- usually until the end of their next turn or as specified by the triggering effect

**Common cause:**

- overload or critical system failure

## Suppressed

A suppressed character is under active threat from incoming fire and is penalized if they expose themselves carelessly.

**Effect:**

- usually suffers **Setback** on attacks while suppression is active
- may trigger a follow-up attack if they leave cover or attack, depending on the suppression effect

**Duration:**

- usually until the start of the attacker’s next turn, unless another rule says otherwise

**Notes:**  
Suppressed is only used if your table wants suppressive fire to create a tracked state. Otherwise, resolve it directly from the fire mode effect and do not track it as a separate condition.

## Condition Duration

Most conditions last in one of the following ways:

- **until position changes**  
    such as Crouched or Prone
- **until the end of the next turn**  
    such as temporary incapacity after a Wound
- **until removed by action or recovery**  
    such as Low Ammo, Mental Conditions, or Restrained
- **until the end of the encounter**  
    such as some implant failures
- **until the cause ends**  
    such as suppression, unstable gravity, or environmental exposure

If a rule does not specify a duration, the GM should assign the shortest duration that makes sense.

## Removing Conditions

Conditions are usually removed by one of the following:

- spending Actions
- changing position
- reloading or resetting a system
- receiving medical treatment
- taking a Short Rest or Extended Rest
- regaining stable footing or control
- ending the source of the hazard
- specific abilities, gear, or implants

## Stacking and Overlap

Conditions with the same name do not normally stack unless a rule says otherwise.

If two different conditions affect the same kind of action:

- apply both if they clearly come from different sources
- avoid excessive stacking if both represent essentially the same hindrance
- when in doubt, apply the stronger effect rather than piling on multiple near-identical penalties
