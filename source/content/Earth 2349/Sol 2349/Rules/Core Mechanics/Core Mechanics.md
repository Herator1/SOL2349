_SOL 2349_ is a **hard science fiction roleplaying game** grounded in plausible technology, physical constraints, and believable human limits. It is designed to capture the danger, tension, and fragility of life in space without turning play into a full technical simulation. Whenever realism and playability conflict, **clarity, speed, and table flow take priority**.

The system uses a **simple d20-based core mechanic** to resolve most actions quickly and consistently. Characters still benefit from meaningful specialization, equipment matters, and the environment can be as dangerous as any opponent, but the rules are built to keep the focus on **decisions, teamwork, and consequences** rather than heavy calculation.

At the heart of the game is a **flexible action economy**. Players are not locked into rigid action types, but decide how much effort to commit in a given moment. Acting more aggressively can create advantages, but it also increases risk, inefficiency, and system strain. This makes each additional action a meaningful choice and reinforces the pressure of operating in hostile, high-risk environments.

## Core Principles

**Plausible, not perfectly simulated.**  
The setting and mechanics are informed by real science, engineering, and human physiology, but simplified where needed for smooth play.

**Fast core resolution.**  
Most actions are resolved through a clear and consistent d20 system that is easy to use at the table.

**Meaningful trade-offs.**  
Speed, aggression, exposure, and resource use must constantly be balanced. Pushing harder is possible, but rarely free.

**Equipment and environment matter.**  
Tools, protection, injuries, system strain, and hostile surroundings all shape what characters can realistically do.

**Teamwork over complexity.**  
The rules support coordination, problem-solving, and tactical decision-making without excessive bookkeeping.

_SOL 2349_ is a game about capable people working under pressure in environments that do not forgive mistakes._
