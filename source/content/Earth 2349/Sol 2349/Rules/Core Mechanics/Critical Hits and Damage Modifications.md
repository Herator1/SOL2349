# 💥 **Damage & Armor**

Combat in _SOL 2349_ is designed to be **dangerous but predictable**.  
Damage is influenced by weapon power, armor, and positioning—but critical hits can quickly shift the outcome of a fight.

---

## 🎯 **Critical Hits**

On a **natural 20**, an attack becomes a **critical hit**.

Critical hits represent:

- perfectly placed shots
- exposed weak points
- system overloads or structural failure

---

### ⚙️ **Effects of a Critical Hit**

A critical hit has two effects:

1. **Ignore Armor**
2. **Double weapon damage dice**

---

### 🎲 **Double Dice (Not Result)**

Critical hits do **not** double the final damage.

👉 Instead:  
**Roll twice the number of weapon damage dice**

---

### Example

Weapon: **2d6**  
Critical → **4d6**

Situational modifiers are **not doubled**

---

### Example (with bonus)

Weapon: **2d6 + 2**  
Critical → **4d6 + 2**

---

## 🛡️ **Guard**

Guard represents **immediate protection**:

- cover
- positioning
- defensive movement

Guard absorbs damage **before Health**.

---

### Key Rules

- Guard is depleted first
- Guard is restored through actions (e.g. taking cover)
- Guard does **not reduce damage**, it **absorbs it**

---

## 🦾 **Armor**

Armor provides **flat damage reduction**.

👉 **Final Damage = Damage − Armor**

Armor represents:

- physical protection
- material resistance
- energy dispersion

---

### Important

- Armor applies **only to Health damage**
- Armor cannot reduce damage below **0**
- Armor is **ignored on critical hits**

---

## 🔁 **Damage Resolution**

Damage is resolved in the following order:

---

### 1. Roll Damage

(including critical effects)

---

### 2. Apply Guard

- Guard absorbs damage first
- Any remaining damage carries over

---

### 3. Apply Armor

- Only to damage affecting Health
- Ignored on critical hits

---

### 4. Apply to Health

- Remaining damage reduces Health

---

### 5. Check for Wounds

- If Health falls below 0
- or takes heavy impact (GM discretion)

---

## 🧪 **Examples**

---

### **Normal Hit**

Damage: 6  
Guard: 2  
Armor: 1

Resolution:

- Guard absorbs 2 → 4 remaining
- Armor reduces 1 → 3
- Health takes **3 damage**

---

### **Critical Hit**

Weapon: 2d6 → **4d6 = 12**  
Guard: 2  
Armor: 2

Resolution:

- Guard absorbs 2 → 10 remaining
- Armor ignored
- Health takes **10 damage**

---

## ⚙️ **Situational Modifiers**

Damage may be modified by:

- abilities
- implants
- environmental effects
- weapon traits

---

### Important

- Modifiers are added **after the roll**
- Modifiers are **not doubled on critical hits**

---

## 🔗 **Interaction with Actions & Combat Flow**

Damage output is influenced by the **Action Economy**:

- Multiple attacks become less accurate
- Reactive attacks are less reliable
- Positioning and timing matter as much as raw damage

👉 This ensures that combat is driven by **decisions, not just numbers**
