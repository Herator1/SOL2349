Combat in SOL 2349 is designed to be **dangerous but predictable**. Damage is influenced by weapon power, armor, and situational factors—but critical hits can turn the tide of a fight.

---

## Critical Hits

On a **natural 20**, an attack becomes a **critical hit**.

Critical hits represent:

- perfectly placed shots
    
- exposed weak points
    
- catastrophic system failures
    

---

### Effects of a Critical Hit

A critical hit has two effects:

1. Ignore Armor  
2. Double weapon damage dice

---

### Double Dice (Not Result)

Critical hits **do not double the final damage**.

Instead:

Roll twice the number of damage dice

Example:

Weapon: 2d6  
  
Critical → 4d6

This keeps damage:

- more consistent
    
- less random spike-based
    
- still clearly more powerful
    

---

### Important

Only the **base weapon damage dice** are doubled.

Situational bonuses are NOT doubled

---

### Example

Weapon: 2d6  
+2 bonus (e.g. ability)  
  
Critical:  
→ roll 4d6 + 2

---

## Armor

Armor provides **flat damage reduction**.

Final Damage = Damage − Armor

Armor represents:

- physical protection
    
- energy dispersion
    
- ballistic resistance
    

---

### Important

- Armor always reduces damage **after Guard is depleted**
    
- Armor cannot reduce damage below **0**
    
- Armor is **ignored on critical hits**
    

---

## Damage Flow

Damage is resolved in the following order:

1. Apply damage to Guard  
2. Remaining damage → Health  
3. Apply Armor reduction  
4. Check for Wounds (if Health < 0)

---

### Example (Normal Hit)

Damage: 6  
Guard: 2  
Armor: 1

Resolution:

Guard absorbs 2 → 4 remaining  
Health takes 4  
Armor reduces 1 → 3 final damage

---

### Example (Critical Hit)

Weapon: 2d6 → critical → 4d6 = 12  
Guard: 2  
Armor: 2

Resolution:

Guard absorbs 2 → 10 remaining  
Armor ignored  
Health takes 10

---

## Situational Damage Modifiers

Situational modifiers may affect damage:

- abilities
    
- implants
    
- environmental effects
    
- weapon traits
    

These modifiers:

are added after the dice roll

and

are NOT doubled on critical hits
