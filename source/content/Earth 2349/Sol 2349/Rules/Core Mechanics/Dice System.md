The rules of _SOL 2349_ rely on only two types of dice: **d20** and **d6**. The **d20** is used for almost all action tests, while **d6** are primarily used for **damage, random tables, and other small effects**. 
When a character attempts an action where the outcome is uncertain, the player rolls **1d20 and adds the relevant Attribute and Skill modifiers**. The result is compared to a **Difficulty (DC)** determined by the Game Master. If the result meets or exceeds the DC, the action succeeds.

Certain circumstances may provide a **Tactical Edge** or impose a **Setback**.  
When a character has a **Tactical Edge**, the player rolls **two d20 and keeps the higher result**.  When a character suffers a **Setback**, the player rolls **two d20 and keeps the lower result**.

In some situations, abilities, equipment, or narrative advantages may allow a **reroll**. A reroll must always replace the original result completely — **the new roll must be used even if it is worse**. A character can only **reroll once per test**.

Two results on the d20 are always special:
- **Natural 20 — Critical Success**  
    The action succeeds automatically and produces an exceptional or advantageous outcome. The exact effect is determined by the Game Master.
    
- **Natural 1 — Critical Failure**  
    The action automatically fails and creates a complication or negative consequence appropriate to the situation. The exact effect is determined by the Game Master.
