When a character attempts an action with an uncertain outcome, the GM assigns a **Difficulty (DC)**.

The player rolls:

**1d20 + Attribute + Skill**

- If the result is **equal to or higher than the DC**, the action succeeds
- Otherwise, it fails or succeeds with complications, depending on the situation and GM ruling

This is the standard resolution method for most actions in _SOL 2349_.

## Typical Total Bonuses

These ranges give a rough guideline for how capable a character is on a standard action test:

- **+2** — basic training
- **+4** — competent specialist
- **+6** — highly skilled
- **+8** — elite professional
- **+10** — exceptional expert

These are **total test bonuses**, usually combining **Attribute + Skill**, before situational effects.

## Difficulty Benchmarks

|Difficulty|DC|Example|
|---|---|---|
|Routine|8|Basic repair under calm conditions|
|Standard|10|Typical professional task|
|Challenging|13|Difficult maneuver, complex system|
|Hard|16|Emergency repair, hazardous situation|
|Extreme|19|Success requires strong skill or good conditions|
|Near Impossible|22+|Experimental, desperate, or borderline impossible task|

These values are guidelines, not absolutes. The GM should choose a DC that reflects both the task itself and the conditions under which it is attempted.

## Setting Difficulty

A good approach is:

1. decide how difficult the task would be under normal conditions
2. adjust for the actual situation
3. roll only if the outcome is uncertain and failure matters

Difficulty may change because of:

- environmental conditions such as zero-g, vacuum, smoke, damage, or poor visibility
- time pressure and stress
- proper tools, preparation, or planning
- assistance from other characters
- injuries, unstable footing, or system strain

In intense situations, even simple tasks may become difficult.

## Adjusting Difficulty Cleanly

Do not overcomplicate difficulty with too many tiny modifiers. In most cases, it is better to make **one meaningful adjustment** than several minor ones.

As a rule of thumb:

- use **DC changes** when the task itself becomes easier or harder
- use **[[Tactical Edge and Setback]]** when circumstances strongly favor or hinder the attempt
- use existing penalties from [[Time & Actions], [[Load]], injuries, or equipment when those rules already apply

This keeps resolution fast and predictable.

## Actions and Difficulty

Most attempts require **1 Action**.

Other factors may affect the test:

- repeating the same kind of action in a round may impose penalties or additional cost
- high [[Load]] may reduce reliability or increase risk
- hostile conditions may raise the DC or impose Setback

Success in _SOL 2349_ depends not only on skill, but also on **timing, preparation, positioning, and resource management**.
