## 🎲 **Action Resolution**

When a character attempts an action with an uncertain outcome, the Game Master assigns a **Difficulty (DC)**.

The player rolls:

👉 **1d20 + Attribute + Skill**

- If the result is **equal to or higher than the DC**, the action succeeds
- Otherwise, it fails or succeeds with complications (GM discretion)

---

## ⚙️ **Typical Modifiers**

These ranges provide a general guideline for character capability:

- **+2** — basic training
- **+4** — competent specialist
- **+6** — highly skilled
- **+8** — elite professional
- **+10** — exceptional expert

---

## 📊 **Difficulty Examples**

|Difficulty|DC|Example|
|---|---|---|
|Routine|8|Basic repair, simple negotiation|
|Standard|10|Typical professional task|
|Challenging|13|Difficult maneuver, complex system|
|Hard|16|Emergency repair, hazardous situation|
|Extreme|19|Only experts succeed reliably|
|Near Impossible|22+|Experimental or borderline impossible task|

---

## 🧠 **When to Adjust Difficulty**

Difficulty is not static. The GM should adjust DCs based on:

- environmental conditions (zero-g, vacuum, damage)
- time pressure and stress
- available tools or preparation
- assistance from other characters

👉 In high-intensity situations, even routine tasks can become difficult.

---

## 🔗 **Interaction with Actions**

Each attempt typically requires **1 Action**.

- Repeating the same type of action in a round may impose penalties or additional cost
- High **Load** or adverse conditions may further increase difficulty

👉 Success depends not only on skill, but on **timing, positioning, and resource management**
