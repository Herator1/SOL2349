# 🧬 **Implants & Load**

In _SOL 2349_, implants are what separate trained professionals from truly dangerous operators. A full list can be found under [[Implants (Lists)]].

They are not passive upgrades.  
They are **active systems** designed to push the human body beyond its natural limits for brief, decisive moments.

---

## ⚙️ **What Implants Do**

Implants allow characters to:

- act faster
- improve accuracy
- manipulate systems
- survive extreme conditions

Most implants require **Actions** and often interact directly with the **Action Economy**.

---

## 🔥 **Load**

Every implant activation generates **Load**.

Load represents:

- neural strain
- system instability
- biological stress

---

### Key Rules

- Load increases when using implants or pushing systems
- Load has a **maximum value**
- As long as Load stays within safe limits, systems operate normally

---

## ⚠️ **Overload**

When a character exceeds their Load limit, they risk **Overload**.

---

### Trigger

Overload is checked **immediately after an ability is used** that pushes Load above its maximum.

The action still resolves.

---

## 🎲 **Overload Roll**

Roll:

👉 **1d6 + Overload Value**

**Overload Value = current Load − max Load**

---

## 🔥 **Overload Effects**

Apply the result immediately:

|Result|Effect|
|---|---|
|**1–3**|⚠️ **Minor Glitch** – next Action suffers **−2**|
|**4–5**|⚠️ **System Lag** – lose **1 available Action next turn**|
|**6–7**|⚠️ **Instability** – next implant costs **+1 Load**|
|**8–9**|⚠️ **Motor Disruption** – physical actions suffer **Complication** for 1 turn|
|**10–11**|⚠️ **Targeting Glitch** – attacks suffer **−2** for 1 turn|
|**12–13**|🔥 **Neural Burn** – take **1d6 Health damage**|
|**14–15**|🔥 **System Shutdown** – cannot use implants next turn|
|**16–17**|🔥 **Implant Lock** – triggering implant disabled for the encounter|
|**18–19**|💀 **Critical Feedback** – suffer **1 Wound**|
|**20+**|☠️ **Cascade Failure** – suffer **1 Wound and lose all Actions next turn**|

---

## 🧠 **Design Principles**

Implants are built around:

- **short bursts of extreme performance**
- not sustained superiority

They reward:

- timing
- positioning
- risk management

---

## ⚖️ **Risk vs Reward**

Every activation creates a decision:

- stay efficient and controlled
- or push beyond limits for immediate advantage

---

## 🔗 **Interaction with Action Economy**

Implants directly interact with actions:

- they consume Actions
- repeated use increases cost (Load scaling)
- they compete with attacks and movement

👉 Using implants means **not doing something else**
