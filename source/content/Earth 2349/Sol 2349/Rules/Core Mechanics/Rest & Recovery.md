Recovery in _SOL 2349_ is never completely free. Time, safety, medical support, and environmental stability all affect how well characters recover from injury, strain, and system stress.

This page covers the baseline recovery rules for:

- **Guard**
- **Health**
- **Mental Conditions**
- **Load**
- long-term recovery from **Wounds**

Recovery is divided into **Short Rest** and **Extended Rest**. More serious injury or system damage may require medical care, downtime, or specialized facilities.

## Core Principle

A rest only provides recovery if the character actually has:

- enough time
- enough safety
- enough stability
- enough freedom from immediate pressure

A short pause in the middle of a crisis is not automatically a valid rest.

## Short Rest

A **Short Rest** usually takes **10–15 minutes**.

It requires:

- relative safety
- no immediate active threat
- only minimal exertion
- a brief chance to regroup

During a valid Short Rest, a character may:

- fully restore **Guard**
- recover **1d6 Health**
- reduce **Mental Condition by 1 level**
- reduce current **Load to 0**

A Short Rest cannot be chained repeatedly without consequence. A character only benefits from another Short Rest if meaningful time has passed, the situation has changed, or they were exposed to new pressure, danger, or strain.

## Extended Rest

An **Extended Rest** usually takes **6–8 hours** of safe downtime.

It requires:

- a safe environment
- no active immediate threat
- enough time for real recovery
- tolerable environmental conditions

During a valid Extended Rest, a character may:

- fully restore **Guard**
- recover **all Health**
- remove **all Mental Conditions**
- reduce current **Load to 0**

An Extended Rest may also clear temporary penalties from stims or minor system instability, if the GM judges that the conditions are good enough.

## Wounds and Recovery

**Wounds do not heal automatically through normal rest alone.**

Rest may improve a wounded character’s general condition, but a Wound itself usually requires:

- medical treatment
- extended downtime
- surgery
- advanced facilities
- or a specific implant, consumable, or special rule

As a baseline, **Wounds require more than rest**.

See [[Medicine & Stabilization]].

## Poor or Interrupted Rest

If rest conditions are poor, recovery may be reduced or prevented.

Common reasons include:

- environmental danger
- pain
- noise
- unstable gravity
- toxic atmosphere
- exposure
- poor shelter
- repeated interruptions
- psychological stress
- unresolved hazard conditions

The GM may:

- reduce Health recovery
- reduce Mental recovery
- reduce Load recovery
- allow only partial benefit
- or prevent the rest entirely

Use the mildest reduction that fits the situation.

## Recovery Support

Recovery can be improved through proper care, gear, and support.

Examples include:

- medical treatment
- psychological support
- supervised rest
- proper facilities
- implant diagnostics or recalibration

These benefits are usually handled through [[Medicine & Stabilization]], [[Mental Strain & Psychological Pressure]], or specific gear and implant rules.

## Recovery During Combat

Characters do **not** gain rest-based recovery during combat or under immediate threat.

However:

- **Guard** may be restored by specific actions or effects
- **Health** may be restored through first aid, consumables, or medical implants
- **Mental strain** may be reduced only in unusual cases
- **Load** may be reduced only by specific rules, if any

Rest is not the same as healing in the middle of a fight.

## Downtime Recovery

Outside combat and immediate mission pressure, recovery may be handled more broadly.

Over downtime, characters may:

- fully restore ordinary injury
- receive treatment for Wounds
- recover from severe mental strain
- clear stim side effects
- repair or recalibrate implants
- regain readiness for the next mission

This is where the GM resolves the difference between rough field recovery, safe station recovery, and proper clinical care.

## Example: Short Rest After Combat

After a short firefight, the group seals the room and confirms no immediate pursuit.

They take a **Short Rest**.

Each character:

- restores **Guard**
- recovers **1d6 Health**
- reduces **Mental Condition by 1**
- resets current **Load to 0**

## Example: Wounded Recovery

A character with one Wound and low Health reaches a secure clinic.

After an Extended Rest:

- Health is restored
- Mental strain is removed
- Load resets
- the Wound remains until properly treated

The character feels better, but is not fully healed.
