Recovery in _SOL 2349_ is never completely free. Time, safety, medical support, and environmental stability all affect how well characters recover from injury, strain, and system stress.

This page covers the baseline recovery rules for:

- **Guard**
- **Health**
- **Mental Conditions**
- **Load**
- long-term recovery from **Wounds**

Recovery is usually divided into **Short Rest** and **Extended Rest**. More serious injury or system damage may require medical care, downtime, or specialized facilities.

## Core Principle

A rest only provides recovery if the character actually has:

- enough time
- enough safety
- enough stability
- and enough freedom from immediate pressure

A short pause in the middle of a crisis is not automatically a valid rest.

## Short Rest

A **Short Rest** usually takes **10–15 minutes**.

It represents:

- catching breath
- regaining tactical control
- quick first aid
- basic system checks
- emotional decompression
- limited recalibration or cooldown

A Short Rest requires:

- relative safety
- no immediate active threat
- only minimal physical exertion
- at least a brief opportunity to regroup

## Short Rest Recovery

During a valid Short Rest, a character may recover:

### Guard

- **fully restore Guard**

This represents regaining control, breathing space, footing, and tactical stability.

### Health

- recover **1d6 Health**

This may represent:

- basic first aid
- pain management
- wound dressing
- limited treatment
- short-term stabilization

### Mental Conditions

- reduce **Mental Condition by 1 level**

This reflects:

- regaining composure
- grounding
- breathing control
- brief emotional stabilization

### Load

- reduce current **Load to 0**, unless a rule says otherwise

This represents:

- cooldown
- neural recovery
- system reset
- regaining control over implant strain

If you want Load to feel harsher in your campaign, you could later change this to “reduce Load significantly” instead, but with the current rules, a full reset after a valid Short Rest is the cleanest baseline.

## Limits on Short Rests

A Short Rest cannot simply be repeated over and over without consequence.

A character only benefits from another Short Rest if:

- meaningful time has passed
- the situation has changed
- they returned to meaningful activity
- or they were exposed to new pressure, danger, or strain

In other words:  
**no chaining Short Rests back-to-back for free recovery.**

## Extended Rest

An **Extended Rest** usually takes **6–8 hours** of safe downtime.

It represents:

- proper sleep
- recovery in a secure environment
- deeper medical treatment
- emotional reset
- full system cooldown
- real physical rest

An Extended Rest requires:

- a safe environment
- no active immediate threat
- enough time for real recovery
- tolerable environmental conditions

Poor shelter, interrupted sleep, exposure, noise, pain, or stress may reduce its effectiveness.

## Extended Rest Recovery

During a valid Extended Rest, a character may recover:

### Guard

- fully restore Guard

### Health

- recover **all Health**

### Mental Conditions

- remove **all Mental Conditions**

### Load

- reduce current **Load to 0**

### Other Effects

An Extended Rest may also:

- remove temporary penalties from stims
- clear short-term instability
- restore basic functionality after minor system stress
- prepare a character for long-term treatment

## Wounds and Recovery

**Wounds do not heal automatically through normal rest alone.**

A rest may:

- stabilize a wounded character
- reduce fatigue around the injury
- support survival
- improve general condition

But a Wound itself usually requires:

- medical treatment
- extended downtime
- surgery
- advanced facilities
- or a specific implant, consumable, or special rule

At GM discretion, exceptionally light Wounds in a well-supported environment may begin healing through downtime, but as a baseline, **Wounds require more than rest**.

See [[Medicine & Stabilization]].

## Poor or Interrupted Rest

If rest conditions are poor, recovery may be reduced or prevented.

Common reasons include:

- environmental danger
- pain
- noise
- unstable gravity
- toxic atmosphere
- exposure
- poor shelter
- repeated interruptions
- psychological stress
- unresolved hazard conditions

The GM may reduce recovery by:

- denying Health recovery
- denying Mental recovery
- reducing Load recovery
- preventing the rest entirely
- allowing only partial benefit

Use the mildest reduction that fits the situation.

## Recovery Support

Recovery can be improved through proper care, gear, and support.

## Medical Support

During a rest, medical care may:

- improve Health recovery
- stabilize serious injury
- reduce immediate complications
- prepare a patient for long-term treatment

See [[Medicine & Stabilization]].

## Psychological Support

During a **Short Rest**, a trained character may support an ally psychologically.

On a successful appropriate test, the ally may reduce Mental Condition by **1 additional level**, unless a rule says otherwise.

See [[Mental Strain & Psychological Pressure]].

## Technical and Implant Support

During a rest, implant users may:

- run diagnostics
- recalibrate systems
- cool down implants
- clear minor instability
- prepare for renewed use

This is part of why Load resets so effectively during real rest.

If a character is suffering from severe implant effects such as **Implant Lock** or major system damage, additional repair or treatment may still be required.

## Recovery During Combat

Normally, characters do **not** gain rest-based recovery during combat or under immediate threat.

However:

- **Guard** may be restored by specific actions or effects in combat
- **Health** may be restored through first aid, consumables, or medical implants
- **Mental strain** may be reduced only in unusual cases
- **Load** may be reduced only by specific rules, if any

Rest is not the same as healing in the middle of a fight.

## Downtime Recovery

Outside combat and outside immediate mission pressure, recovery may be handled more broadly.

Over downtime, characters may:

- fully restore ordinary injury
- receive treatment for Wounds
- recover from severe mental strain
- clear stim side effects
- repair or recalibrate implants
- regain readiness for the next mission

This is where the GM can resolve the difference between:

- rough field survival
- safe station recovery
- proper clinical care
- long-term rehabilitation

## Stims and Recovery

Some stims delay consequences rather than remove them.

After the immediate effect ends, a character may suffer:

- Health loss
- Mental Conditions
- fatigue
- impaired judgment
- dependence or rebound effects

These usually become relevant **after the encounter**, during rest, treatment, or the next scene.

A rest may reduce some stim penalties, but not automatically all of them.

See [[Consumables]] and [[Medicine & Stabilization]].

## Group Recovery and Pacing

Recovery should matter to pacing.

If the group pushes forward without resting:

- injuries accumulate
- Mental Conditions worsen
- implant users become riskier
- Wounds become more dangerous

If the group secures time and safety:

- they recover efficiently
- specialists become valuable
- survival feels earned rather than automatic

Recovery should create meaningful tempo decisions, not just bookkeeping.

## Example: Short Rest After Combat

After a short firefight, the group seals the room and confirms no immediate pursuit.

They take a **Short Rest**.

Each character:

- restores **Guard**
- recovers **1d6 Health**
- reduces **Mental Condition by 1**, if applicable
- resets current **Load to 0**

The medic also provides support to one injured ally, improving their recovery further.

## Example: Interrupted Extended Rest

A crew tries to rest in a damaged service section during a reactor incident.

The GM rules that the rest is unsafe and repeatedly interrupted.

Result:

- Guard restores
- Load resets
- Health recovery is reduced
- Mental recovery is only partial
- no Wound recovery occurs

The characters rested, but not well.

## Example: Wounded Recovery

A character with one Wound and low Health reaches a secure clinic.

After an Extended Rest:

- Health is restored
- Mental strain is removed
- Load resets
- the Wound remains until properly treated

The character feels better, but is not fully healed.
