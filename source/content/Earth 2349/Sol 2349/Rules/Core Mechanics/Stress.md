Life in space places constant psychological pressure on the human mind. Isolation, confined environments, dangerous machinery, vacuum exposure, and the knowledge that small mistakes can be fatal all contribute to mental strain. Long missions, equipment failures, sudden decompression alarms, or violent encounters can push even experienced crew members beyond their limits. In _SOL 2349_, this pressure is represented by **Stress**.

Each character has a **Stress Level** and a **Maximum Stress Threshold**. Stress increases through dangerous situations, fear, exhaustion, psychological attacks, or traumatic events.

### Exceeding Stress Limits

When a character’s Stress rises **above their maximum Stress Threshold**, the character must immediately roll **1d6**.

Add the amount of **excess Stress** (the amount above the threshold) to the result.  
The final value is then checked on the **Stress Reaction Table**.

Example:  
A character with a maximum Stress of **8** currently has **10 Stress**.  
They are **2 points over the limit**.

They roll **1d6** and get a **3**.
Final result: **3 + 2 = 5**

The result **5** is applied on the Stress Reaction Table.

### Stress Reaction Table

|Result|Reaction|
|---|---|
|**1–3**|**Adrenaline Spike** – The character reacts instinctively and immediately performs **one action** (attack, move, interact, etc.). Afterwards, **reduce Stress by 1**.|
|**4–6**|**Shaken** – The character loses focus. They suffer **−2 on all tests during the current and next round**. Reduce Stress by **1**.|
|**7–8**|**Panic Movement** – The character instinctively tries to create distance from the perceived threat. They must **move away from the danger using their full movement** on their next turn and **cannot move toward it during that turn**. Reduce Stress by **2**.|
|**9–10**|**Drop and Cover** – The character drops prone or takes cover instinctively. They are **knocked prone and lose their next action**. Reduce Stress by **2**.|
|**11+**|**Severe Panic** – The character loses control completely. They **lose all actions, bonus actions, and reactions for their next round**. Reduce Stress by **3**.|
Stress reactions represent **momentary psychological overload**, not long-term conditions. After a reaction resolves, the character regains control and continues acting normally unless Stress rises above the threshold again.

### Stress Recovery

Stress can be reduced through rest, support from allies, or specialized equipment and abilities.
Certain **items, implants, or abilities** may reduce Stress directly. In addition, a character trained in **Psychology** can attempt to stabilize an ally. A successful **Psychological skill test** reduces the target’s Stress by an amount determined by the ability, item, or situation.

Taking a **short break (about 10 minutes)** allows a character to recover slightly, reducing their Stress by **2 points**.
During a **long rest**, when a character has sufficient time to sleep, recover, and decompress in a safe environment, **all Stress is removed**.
