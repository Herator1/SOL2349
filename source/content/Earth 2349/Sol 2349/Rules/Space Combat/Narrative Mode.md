Narrative Space Combat provides a fast and accessible way to resolve battles between spacecraft in **SOL 2349**. In this mode, a ship is treated as a single entity whose effectiveness is shaped by the actions of its crew. The system focuses on tension, teamwork, and key decisions rather than detailed simulation.

Narrative combat is designed to be easy to run at the table. It uses the same core dice mechanics as the character system and avoids complex calculations. A more detailed **Tactical Space Combat** system will be introduced in a later version of the game.


## Core Principles

Narrative space combat follows a few simple principles:

- A **ship acts as a single character-like entity**.
    
- **Crew actions improve the ship’s performance** during combat.
    
- Guided weapons such as missiles are dangerous but can be stopped by **Point Defense (PD)**.
    
- Damage reduces the ship’s **Hull Integrity** and may cause complications.
    
- When Hull Integrity reaches **0**, the ship becomes **combat ineffective**.
    

Combat usually ends when one ship is disabled, forced to retreat, or surrenders.

# [[Ship Statistics]]

Ships in Narrative Combat use a small number of statistics.

**Thrust**  
Determines maneuverability and the ability to change range.

**Sensors**  
Used for detection, targeting, and electronic warfare.

**Armament**  
Represents the ship’s offensive weapon systems.

**Defense**  
Represents armor, electronic countermeasures, and point-defense effectiveness.

**Systems**  
Represents the quality and reliability of internal ship systems.

**Hull Integrity**  
The structural condition of the ship. Damage reduces this value.

When Hull Integrity reaches **0**, the ship is disabled.


# [[Weapons]]

Each ship has a number of **Hardpoints**, which determine how many weapon systems it can mount.

Typical classes:

|Ship Class|Hardpoints|
|---|---|
|Patrol Boat|2|
|Corvette|3|
|Frigate|4|
|Light Cruiser|5|
|Cruiser|6|
|Battleship|8|

Each weapon system occupies one hardpoint.

In Narrative Combat, weapons are divided into two categories:

### Guided Weapons

- Torpedoes
    
- Missiles
    
- Light Missiles
    
- Interception Pods
    

These weapons track their targets after launch.

### Direct Fire Weapons

- Railguns
    
- Laser Arrays
    

These weapons require a normal attack roll.


# [[Range Bands]]

Space combat takes place across large distances. Engagements are divided into four range bands.

|   |   |
|---|---|
|Range|Distance|
|Extreme|5,000 – 20,000 km|
|Long|500 – 5,000 km|
|Medium|50 – 500 km|
|Close|0 – 50 km|

Different weapons are effective at different ranges.

- **Torpedoes** operate at Extreme and Long range.
    
- **Missiles** operate at Long and Medium range.
    
- **Light missiles** operate at Medium and Close range.
    
- **Railguns and lasers** are most effective at Medium and Close range.
    

Range usually changes gradually as ships maneuver during combat.

# [[Crew Actions and Combat Rounds]]

Each combat round represents a short period of intense activity aboard the ship.

A round usually includes several **ship actions**, each of which should involve at least one player roll.

Typical actions include:

- Targeting and sensor operations
    
- Maneuvering
    
- Weapon fire
    
- Point defense interception
    
- Engineering support
    

Not every crew role must act every round. The importance of different roles depends on the range and situation.


# Guided Weapon Attacks

Guided weapons such as missiles do **not require an attack roll**.

If a weapon can reach the target and a launch is declared, the missiles are considered **incoming threats**.

Each missile has a **Threat Value**, representing its tracking capability and lethality.

Example:

Dragonbreath Missile  
Threat: **12**

A successful **Target Lock** from the Sensors operator may increase the Threat value.

Example:

Threat 12 + Target Lock (+2) = **Threat 14**


# [[Point Defense]]

Point Defense systems attempt to intercept incoming missiles.

Each PD system may fire a limited number of times per round.

Example:  
Gatling PD System — **maximum 4 shots per round**

Each additional shot suffers a cumulative penalty.

|   |   |
|---|---|
|Shot|Penalty|
|1|0|
|2|−4|
|3|−8|
|4|−12|

## Resolving Missile Defense

Missiles are resolved **one at a time**.

For each incoming missile:

1. A PD system may attempt an interception roll.
    
2. If it fails, another available PD system may attempt to intercept.
    
3. Continue until:
    
    - the missile is destroyed, or
        
    - all PD systems have fired and failed.
        

## Interception Result

Roll:

**d20 + Defense + situational bonuses**

Situational bonuses may include:

- Pilot maneuver actions
    
- Sensor support
    
- engineering assistance
    

Compare the final result to the missile’s **Threat Value**.

### Intercept

If the PD roll **equals or exceeds the Threat**, the missile is destroyed.

### Near Hit

If all interception attempts fail but the **best failed roll is greater than half the Threat**, the missile detonates close to the ship.

The ship takes **reduced damage**.

### Direct Hit

If the best failed roll is **equal to or less than half the Threat**, the missile strikes the ship directly.

The ship takes **full damage**.


# Damage

Damage depends on the weapon’s warhead.

Example:

Dragonbreath Warhead

Direct Hit: **2d6 damage**  
Near Hit: **1d6 damage**

Damage reduces the ship’s **Hull Integrity**.


# Disabled Ships

When a ship’s Hull Integrity reaches **0**, the vessel becomes **combat ineffective**.

The ship may:

- lose propulsion
    
- lose power
    
- suffer major structural damage
    

At this point the outcome is determined by the victor.

Possible results include:

- surrender
    
- boarding
    
- salvage
    
- destruction
    

The deliberate destruction of a disabled ship is considered a serious escalation in most parts of the Solar System.
