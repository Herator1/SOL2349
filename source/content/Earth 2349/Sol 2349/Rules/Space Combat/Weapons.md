# Hardpoints and Weapons

Each ship has a number of **Hardpoints**, which determine how many major weapon or defense systems it can mount.

Each installed weapon system normally occupies **1 Hardpoint** unless a specific ship or weapon rule says otherwise.

Typical classes:

| Ship Class | Hardpoints |
|---|---:|
| Patrol Boat | 2 |
| Corvette | 3 |
| Frigate | 4 |
| Light Cruiser | 5 |
| Cruiser | 6 |
| Battleship | 8 |

## Weapon Systems

This section provides **baseline weapon templates** for space combat.

These are not the only weapons available in the setting. Different factions may use specialized variants, alternative warheads, unusual tracking logic, or experimental systems.

The weapons listed here serve as **standard templates** that define:
- usable **Range Bands**
- typical **damage values**
- **Threat** values for guided weapons
- basic combat role

GMs and players may freely create new variants using these templates as a foundation.

## Weapon Categories

Ship weapons fall into three major categories.

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| **Guided Weapons** | Missiles, torpedoes, and other tracked threats resolved through **Threat** and interception |
| **Direct Fire Weapons** | Weapons that require an attack roll |
| **Defensive Weapons** | Systems designed to intercept incoming guided threats |

---

# Guided Weapons

Guided weapons do **not** usually require a direct attack roll at the moment of impact.

Instead, they create an incoming **Threat** that must be intercepted using **Point Defense**.

As a baseline:
- **1 launcher or rack fires 1 guided salvo per round**
- each salvo is resolved as **one incoming threat**
- larger ships remain dangerous by mounting multiple launchers, not by tracking huge numbers of individual missiles

Damage depends on the warhead and hit quality.

## Torpedo Launcher

Torpedoes are large, expensive guided weapons designed for **very long-range engagements**.

They are slower and easier to intercept than smaller missiles, but carry heavier warheads.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| **Range** | Extreme, Long |
| **Threat** | 11 |
| **Salvos per Round** | 1 |
| **Ammo per Launcher** | 2–3 |
| **Direct Hit Damage** | 3d6 |
| **Near Hit Damage** | 2d6 |

Typical Use:
- long-range opening strikes
- anti-capital ship combat
- strategic deterrence

---

## Missile Rack

Missiles are the most common ship-to-ship guided weapon.

They are smaller and more maneuverable than torpedoes and are used in standard military combat.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| **Range** | Long, Medium |
| **Threat** | 12 |
| **Salvos per Round** | 1 |
| **Ammo per Rack** | 3 salvos |
| **Direct Hit Damage** | 2d6 |
| **Near Hit Damage** | 1d6 |

Typical Use:
- standard ship-to-ship combat
- pressuring enemy Point Defense
- general-purpose attack loadout

---

## Light Missile Launcher

Light missiles are smaller guided weapons with shorter range and lighter warheads.

They are often used by lighter craft or as supplemental close-range attack weapons.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| **Range** | Medium, Close |
| **Threat** | 10 |
| **Salvos per Round** | 1 |
| **Ammo per Launcher** | 4 salvos |
| **Direct Hit Damage** | 1d6 |
| **Near Hit Damage** | 1 |

Typical Use:
- close engagement pressure
- lighter combat craft
- short-range attack support

---

## Interception Pod Launcher

Interception pods are defensive guided munitions designed to destroy incoming threats.

They are rarely used offensively.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| **Range** | Close |
| **Salvos per Round** | 1 |
| **Ammo per Launcher** | 4 |

**Effect:**  
An Interception Pod Launcher may be used to support one **Point Defense** attempt in the current round.

That interception gains **Tactical Edge**.

# Direct Fire Weapons

Direct fire weapons require an attack roll.

In most cases, this roll is:

**1d20 + Reflexes + Gunnery**

The result is compared against the target ship’s direct-fire target value:

**Base DC 10 + Defense**

## Railgun

Railguns fire extremely high-velocity kinetic projectiles.

They are powerful but often require good ship alignment and firing opportunity.

Small ships often mount **keel-aligned railguns**, which work best when pilot and gunner are coordinated.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| **Range** | Medium, Close |
| **Long Range Use** | Limited |
| **Shots per Round** | 1 |
| **Damage** | 3d6 |

Typical Use:
- anti-ship direct fire
- finishing blows
- medium- to close-range decisive strikes

At **Long Range**, a railgun attack is usually **Limited** and may suffer **Setback** or require special setup, such as successful alignment.

---

## Laser Array

Laser arrays fire concentrated energy beams.

They are more flexible than railguns, but usually less destructive per hit.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| **Range** | Long, Medium, Close |
| **Long Range Use** | Effective |
| **Shots per Round** | 1 |
| **Damage** | 2d6 |

Typical Use:
- precision fire
- sustained engagement
- close and medium combat
- point-target damage

At **Long Range**, a laser array remains usable but may still be less decisive than guided weapons.

# Defensive Weapons

Defensive weapons are used to intercept incoming guided threats.

## Point Defense System

A Point Defense system protects the ship from incoming missiles, torpedoes, and similar guided threats.

Examples include:
- Gatling defensive cannons
- defensive laser arrays
- close-intercept batteries

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| **Range** | Defensive / local |
| **Intercepts per Round** | 1 per installed PD system |

A Point Defense attempt uses the ship’s **Point Defense** value, not a separate weapon attack roll.

See [[Point Defense]].

# Weapon Customization

The weapons above are **generic templates**.

Variants may differ by:
- faction technology
- warhead design
- propulsion and tracking logic
- sensor integration
- treaty restrictions
- stealth or electronic warfare packages

Possible variants include:
- armor-piercing railgun rounds
- electronic warfare missiles
- stealth torpedoes
- decoy-heavy missile salvos
- treaty-restricted nuclear torpedoes
- high-focus laser batteries

GMs are encouraged to create new weapons by modifying:
- **Threat**
- **Damage**
- **Range Bands**
- **Ammo**
- or **special effects**

without changing the core combat structure.
