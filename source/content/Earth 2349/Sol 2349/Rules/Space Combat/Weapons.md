# Hardpoints and Weapons

Each ship has a number of **Hardpoints**, which determine how many weapon systems it can mount.

Typical classes:

| Ship Class    | Hardpoints |
| ------------- | ---------- |
| Patrol Boat   | 2          |
| Corvette      | 3          |
| Frigate       | 4          |
| Light Cruiser | 5          |
| Cruiser       | 6          |
| Battleship    | 8          |
Each weapon system occupies one hardpoint.

# Weapon Systems

This section provides **baseline weapon templates** for Narrative Space Combat.

These are not the only weapons available in the setting. Many factions develop specialized variants, experimental systems, and different **warhead types**.

The weapons listed here serve as **standard templates** that define:

- usable **range bands**
    
- typical **damage values**
    
- number of **shots per round**
    
- basic **Threat values** for guided weapons
    

Game Masters and players can freely create new weapon variants using these templates as a foundation.

---

# Weapon Categories

Ship weapons fall into three major categories.

|Category|Description|
|---|---|
|Guided Weapons|Self-guiding weapons such as missiles and torpedoes|
|Direct Fire Weapons|Weapons that require an attack roll|
|Defensive Weapons|Systems designed to intercept incoming missiles|

---

# Guided Weapons

Guided weapons do **not require attack rolls**.

Instead they create an incoming **Threat** that must be intercepted using **Point Defense**.

Damage depends on the weapon's warhead type.

---

## Torpedo Launcher

Torpedoes are large, expensive guided weapons designed for **very long-range engagements**.

They are slower and easier to intercept than smaller missiles but carry heavier warheads.

|   |   |
|---|---|
|Parameter|Value|
|Range|Extreme, Long|
|Threat|11|
|Shots per Round|1|
|Ammo per Rack|2–3|
|Direct Hit Damage|3d6|
|Near Hit Damage|2d6|

Typical Use:

- long-range opening strikes
    
- anti-capital ship combat
    

---

## Missile Rack

Missiles are the most common ship-to-ship weapon.

They are smaller and more maneuverable than torpedoes and usually launched in **salvos**.

|   |   |
|---|---|
|Parameter|Value|
|Range|Long, Medium|
|Threat|12|
|Shots per Round|2|
|Ammo per Rack|3 salvos|
|Direct Hit Damage|2d6|
|Near Hit Damage|1d6|

Typical Use:

- standard ship-to-ship combat
    
- overwhelming enemy point defense
    

---

## Light Missile Launcher

Light missiles are smaller guided weapons with limited range.

They are often used as **hybrid offensive and defensive weapons**.

|   |   |
|---|---|
|Parameter|Value|
|Range|Medium, Close|
|Threat|10|
|Shots per Round|2|
|Ammo per Rack|4 salvos|
|Direct Hit Damage|1d6|
|Near Hit Damage|1|

Typical Use:

- supplemental point defense
    

---

## Interception Pod Launcher

Interception pods are defensive missiles designed specifically to destroy incoming threats.

They are rarely used offensively.

|   |   |
|---|---|
|Parameter|Value|
|Range|Close|
|Threat|—|
|Shots per Round|2|
|Ammo per Rack|4|

Effect:

Interception pods grant **+3 bonus to a PD roll** against a missile.

---

# Direct Fire Weapons

Direct fire weapons require an **attack roll**.

Attack roll:

```
d20 + Gunnery + Armament
```

Compared against the target ship's **Defense**.

---

## Railgun

Railguns fire extremely high velocity kinetic projectiles.

They are powerful but require accurate targeting and proper alignment.

Small ships usually mount **keel-aligned railguns** that require pilot assistance.

|   |   |
|---|---|
|Parameter|Value|
|Range|Medium, Close|
|Attack Modifier|−4 at Long|
|Shots per Round|1|
|Damage|3d6|

Typical Use:

- anti-ship direct fire
    
- finishing blows at close range
    

---

## Laser Array

Laser arrays fire concentrated energy beams.

They are accurate and effective at shorter distances.

|   |   |
|---|---|
|Parameter|Value|
|Range|Long, Medium, Close|
|Attack Modifier|−2 at Long|
|Shots per Round|1|
|Damage|2d6|

Typical Use:

- precise strikes
    
- close combat
    

---

# Defensive Weapons

Defensive weapons are used to intercept incoming missiles.

---

## Gatling Point Defense System

A rapid-fire kinetic weapon designed to destroy incoming missiles.

|   |   |
|---|---|
|Parameter|Value|
|Range|Close|
|Maximum Shots per Round|4|
|Modifiers|−4 per additional shot|

Example:

|   |   |
|---|---|
|Shot|Modifier|
|1|0|
|2|−4|
|3|−8|
|4|−12|

Point Defense roll:

```
d20 + Defense + modifiers
```

Compared against the missile's **Threat value**.

# Weapon Customization

The weapons above represent **generic templates**.

Many variations may exist depending on:

- faction technology
    
- warhead design
    
- propulsion systems
    
- targeting software
    

Examples of possible variants include:

- armor-piercing railgun rounds
    
- electronic warfare missiles
    
- nuclear torpedoes (restricted by international treaties)
    
- stealth torpedoes
    
- high-velocity laser arrays
    

Game Masters are encouraged to create new weapons using these templates as a foundation.
