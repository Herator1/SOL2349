# Overview

16 Psyche is one of the most economically valuable objects in the Asteroid Belt and among the largest known **metal-rich asteroids** in the solar system. Unlike most asteroids, which are composed primarily of rock or ice, Psyche consists largely of iron and nickel, making it an enormous natural deposit of industrial metals.

For this reason, Psyche became one of the earliest large-scale mining targets during the expansion into the asteroid belt.

Today the asteroid hosts extensive excavation networks, automated refineries, and industrial processing facilities. Vast mining pits cut deep into its metallic surface, while orbital platforms manage cargo transport and resource processing before shipment to manufacturing centers across the inner solar system.

---

# Mining Operations

Industrial activity on Psyche operates on a massive scale. Gigantic automated drills and cutting systems carve metal directly from the asteroid’s body, while smelting and processing facilities convert raw material into transportable ingots and structural alloys.

The extracted metals are used across the solar system for:

- spacecraft construction
    
- orbital infrastructure
    
- large-scale industrial manufacturing
    

Because the asteroid already consists of refined metal rather than ore embedded in rock, extraction is comparatively efficient compared to many other mining targets in the belt.

---

# Corporate Influence

Like much of the Asteroid Belt, Psyche lies outside strong centralized political control. Most operations are run by **corporate mining consortia** and private industrial contractors.

Unlike the more regulated environments of Mars or the Jovian League, economic life in the belt is often driven by competition between corporations, independent operators, and small industrial collectives.

Labor conditions and governance vary widely between installations, ranging from highly organized corporate arcologies to rough industrial settlements run by worker cooperatives or private security forces.

---

# Settlements

Psyche hosts several scattered industrial habitats carved into the asteroid’s surface or constructed as modular structures attached to excavation zones.

Unlike major population centers such as Ceres, Psyche lacks large arcologies or major civilian cities. Most residents are industrial workers, engineers, or logistics crews involved in mining and processing operations.

Orbital cargo platforms constantly move refined metal toward manufacturing centers throughout the inner solar system.

---

# Role in the Asteroid Belt

Within the chaotic political landscape of the Asteroid Belt, 16 Psyche represents one of the region’s most important industrial assets. Its vast metal reserves have made it a cornerstone of interplanetary construction and heavy industry.

However, the asteroid’s immense value also makes it a frequent source of economic conflict, corporate rivalry, and occasional sabotage within the belt’s fiercely competitive mining economy.
