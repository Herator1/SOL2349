## Overview

The inhabited asteroids of the Solar System form one of the most **dynamic, fragmented, and economically vital regions** of human civilization. Spread across the [[Asteroid Belt]], these settlements are not unified by a single political system or cultural identity. Instead, they consist of a loose and often unstable network of mining colonies, industrial habitats, logistics stations, independent enclaves, and corporate-controlled extraction zones.

Unlike the more structured societies of [[Mars]] or the regulated institutional systems of the **Jovian League**, asteroid settlements exist in a far more **decentralized and competitive environment**. Control is often shaped less by formal legitimacy than by ownership, resource access, contracts, security capacity, and economic leverage.

Life in the Belt is defined by extraction, movement, trade, and pressure. Some habitats are highly organized industrial centers, others are improvised frontier settlements or long-standing independent stations with weak outside oversight. Together, they form one of the most important resource and transport regions in human space.
