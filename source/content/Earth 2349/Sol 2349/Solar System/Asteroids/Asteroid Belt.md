## Overview

The inhabited asteroids of the solar system form one of the most **dynamic, chaotic, and economically vital regions** of human civilization. Spread across the Asteroid Belt and beyond, these settlements are not unified by a single political system or cultural identity. Instead, they represent a fragmented network of mining outposts, industrial facilities, independent habitats, and corporate territories.

Unlike the structured societies of Mars or the regulated environments of the Jovian League, asteroid settlements operate in a far more **decentralized and competitive landscape**, where control is often determined by ownership, force, or economic leverage.
