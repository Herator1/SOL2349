# Overview
![[Ceres trade district.png|450]]
Ceres is the **largest inhabited body in the asteroid belt** and serves as one of the most important diplomatic and commercial hubs in the Solar System. Due to its strategic location and abundant water reserves, it became a natural center for trade, logistics, and interplanetary transit during the early expansion into the Belt.

Over time, Ceres developed a reputation as **neutral ground** where competing megacorporations conduct negotiations, sign contracts, and maintain diplomatic offices. Most major corporations operate embassies or corporate delegations on the dwarf planet, making the city districts of Ceres a dense network of trade offices, arbitration courts, and discreet intelligence operations.

Although espionage is widespread, open conflict is extremely rare. Ceres maintains one of the **most sophisticated security systems in the Solar System**, combining corporate security forces, automated surveillance, and strict neutrality laws. Violence within the habitat zones is harshly punished, ensuring that business and diplomacy can proceed without disruption.

# Habitat Structure

Most of Ceres’ population lives **inside the dwarf planet rather than on its surface**. Vast cavern systems were excavated over centuries and converted into pressurized habitat networks.

These interior cities consist of:

- rotating cylindrical habitats for artificial gravity
- large hydroponic agriculture zones
- industrial sectors and water processing plants
- diplomatic districts and corporate headquarters

The outer layers of the dwarf planet provide **natural radiation shielding**, making interior habitats safer than many orbital stations.

Orbiting Ceres are several large **trade docks and logistics stations**, where cargo from across the asteroid belt is transferred between freighters.

# Life in Low Gravity

The extremely low gravity of Ceres shapes everyday life in unusual ways. Surface gravity is only about **3% of Earth’s**, making uncontrolled movement potentially dangerous.

Common adaptations include:
- magnetic boots in surface areas
- internal rotating habitats that generate partial gravity
- architecture designed around vertical movement and handholds
Long-term residents often develop **elongated body proportions and reduced bone density**, a common trait among Belt-born populations.

Sports and recreational activities on Ceres often take advantage of the low gravity environment. Popular activities include three-dimensional racing, zero-gravity acrobatics, and slow-motion aerial sports.

# Political and Economic Role

Ceres plays a unique role in the political structure of the Solar System. While most regions are heavily influenced by megacorporations, Ceres operates under a **neutral governance charter** maintained by a coalition of trade organizations and independent institutions.

This neutrality has made Ceres the preferred location for:

- corporate arbitration courts
- diplomatic negotiations
- financial clearinghouses
- major trade exchanges

Despite its reputation for neutrality, the city districts of Ceres are widely considered one of the most active **centers of corporate espionage** in the Solar System. Intelligence operatives, data brokers, and corporate agents operate quietly throughout the station districts.

Nevertheless, the strict enforcement of security laws ensures that these conflicts remain **covert rather than violent**, preserving Ceres’ reputation as the safest place in the asteroid belt to conduct business.

### Ceres – Role in the Game

In **SOL 2349**, Ceres serves as one of the primary **quest hubs and social centers** of the Solar System. Its political neutrality and concentration of corporate offices, embassies, and trade institutions make it a natural meeting point for factions that would otherwise avoid direct contact.

Many missions on Ceres revolve around **information rather than violence**. Players may be hired for espionage, corporate negotiations, data retrieval, discreet surveillance, or diplomatic escort operations. Rival megacorps frequently test each other’s influence here through covert actions, legal maneuvering, and financial pressure rather than open confrontation.

Due to the station’s extremely strict security systems and neutrality laws, **violent conflict is almost impossible to resolve openly**. Weapons are heavily restricted, surveillance is omnipresent, and security forces respond rapidly to disturbances. As a result, players operating on Ceres must rely on **stealth, social engineering, deception, and investigation** rather than combat.

For many campaigns, Ceres functions as the place where deals are made, secrets are traded, and the hidden struggles between megacorporations quietly unfold.
