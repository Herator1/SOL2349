## Overview

Hygiea is one of the largest bodies in the Asteroid Belt, yet it remains one of the least developed. Its distance from major traffic routes and lack of early industrial investment have left it comparatively underutilized. Unlike more densely populated asteroids, Hygiea offers **space—both physically and economically**. Large sections remain only partially developed, with a mix of surface installations and slowly expanding internal habitats.

The asteroid has become a preferred location for long-term storage, bulk material handling, and low-cost expansion projects. Infrastructure is functional but sparse, and growth is steady rather than explosive.

Hygiea does not compete with the Belt’s major hubs.  
It waits.
