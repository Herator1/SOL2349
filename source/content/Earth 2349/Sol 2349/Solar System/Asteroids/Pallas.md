## Overview

Pallas is one of the most difficult major bodies in the Asteroid Belt to operate on. Its highly inclined orbit and irregular structure make access and development significantly more complex than on other asteroids. As a result, Pallas has never attracted large-scale population growth. Instead, it has developed into a hub for **specialized operations** that benefit from its challenging conditions.

Facilities on Pallas are built for resilience. Reinforced habitats, redundant systems, and highly trained crews are the norm. The asteroid hosts engineering testbeds, experimental infrastructure, and projects that require isolation and control. Pallas is not a place for expansion. It is a place for precision.
