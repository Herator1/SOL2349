Beyond the structured regions of the Kuiper Belt and the chaotic motion of the Scattered Disc, the outer solar system contains a number of **isolated objects**—dwarf planets, minor bodies, and distant remnants that exist on the fringes of known orbital patterns.

These objects do not form a continuous region. Instead, they appear as **widely separated points in deep space**, often following extreme orbits that carry them far beyond the typical boundaries of the outer system. Some briefly pass through the outer edges of the Kuiper Belt before disappearing again into darkness for centuries or longer. What defines them is not their composition, but their **isolation**.

Distances between these objects are vast, and each one exists almost entirely on its own. There are no natural trade routes connecting them, no shared infrastructure, and no meaningful sense of proximity. Reaching one of these bodies is always a deliberate decision—never something encountered by accident.
Because of this, they occupy a unique role in the solar system. They are not part of any network. They are not part of any system. They are places that must be **specifically sought out**.

---

# Role in the Setting

In SOL 2349, isolated deep objects function as **natural story anchors in otherwise empty space**. Their extreme distance and lack of oversight make them ideal locations for anything that requires separation from the rest of human civilization.

A single object can host a research station, a hidden installation, or the remains of a long-forgotten expedition—completely disconnected from any larger structure.

In a region defined by emptiness, these objects become rare points of focus. Not because they are important by themselves —  but because there is nothing else around them.
