## Venus (2349)

In the year 2349, Venus has become one of the most important industrial hubs of the inner solar system. Roughly **240 million people** live within vast networks of **floating cloud cities**, suspended high in the planet’s atmosphere where pressure and temperature allow human habitation. These aerostat metropolises drift through the dense golden clouds, connected by cargo platforms, atmospheric aircraft, and orbital elevators linking them to space.
Far below, the surface of Venus remains utterly hostile. Temperatures hot enough to melt lead and crushing atmospheric pressure make sustained human presence impossible. Only heavily shielded **unmanned scientific probes** occasionally descend to the surface for brief research missions before being destroyed by the extreme environment.
The true life of Venus exists in the skies. The planet’s atmosphere provides abundant **carbon dioxide and immense solar energy**, enabling massive chemical industries, polymer production, and atmospheric mining. As a result, Venus has grown into a critical **manufacturing and trade center** supplying materials, fuel precursors, and industrial components throughout the inner solar system.
Political authority on Venus is fragmented. While nominally part of Earth’s broader political sphere, real power lies with powerful **megacorporations**, as well as shadow networks of **cartels and syndicates** that dominate logistics, smuggling, and black-market trade between the cloud cities. In the endless haze of the Venusian skies, corporate ambition, organized crime, and industrial expansion coexist in a fragile balance.

## Industry and Manufacturing

Industry forms the backbone of Venusian society. The planet’s dense **carbon dioxide atmosphere** and abundant solar energy enable large-scale **chemical processing and manufacturing** in the upper cloud layers. Atmospheric refineries extract carbon, oxygen, nitrogen and trace compounds directly from the air, supplying a vast network of industrial facilities.
Venus has therefore become the **primary center for polymer production and advanced materials** in the inner solar system. Factories produce high-performance plastics, composites, structural foams and corrosion-resistant components used in spacecraft, orbital habitats and atmospheric platforms across the system.
Beyond chemical production, the cloud cities host extensive **high-tech manufacturing**, including electronics, sensors, drones and specialized industrial equipment, often built in modular facilities using large-scale industrial 3D fabrication systems.
At the same time, Venus supports a large **grey economy**. Corporate monopolies coexist with cartel networks and syndicates involved in smuggling, unlicensed manufacturing and illegal materials processing, particularly in the lower industrial tiers of the cloud cities where oversight is weak.

# Major Cloud Cities of Venus

## [[Aphrodite Prime]]

**Population:** ~95 million

The largest city of Venus and the political and economic heart of the planet. Aphrodite Prime is a vast interconnected network of aerostat platforms stretching across dozens of kilometers of the upper atmosphere. Massive chemical plants, orbital shipping hubs and corporate headquarters dominate the skyline. Most major Venusian megacorporations maintain their primary offices here, and much of the planet’s financial and logistical coordination happens within its floating districts.

---

## [[Ishtar Gate]]

**Population:** ~65 million

Located above the highlands of **Ishtar Terra**, Ishtar Gate is the primary **industrial and manufacturing center** of Venus. The city is dominated by vast fabrication complexes, polymer refineries and industrial megaplatforms that produce structural materials for orbital habitats and spacecraft across the inner solar system. Much of the heavy industrial workforce of Venus lives here.

---

## Eos Haven

**Population:** ~40 million

Eos Haven developed as a **trade and logistics hub** connecting Venus with Earth, Mercury and the inner-system shipping routes. Enormous docking aerostats and orbital transfer ports handle a constant flow of cargo and passenger traffic. The city is known for its dense commercial districts, free trade zones and the strong presence of shipping syndicates and transport guilds.

# Secondary Cloud Cities

These smaller cities are often specialized industrial or logistical centers.

**Helios Array** – ~18 million  
Energy generation and atmospheric processing platforms supplying power and chemical feedstocks.

**Zephyria** – ~12 million  
Research city specializing in atmospheric science, climate engineering and high-altitude exploration technologies.

**Carthage Drift** – ~10 million  
A semi-lawless trade city known for smuggling, cartel activity and black-market manufacturing.
