# Overview

Luna is one of the most important and densely developed off-world locations in the solar system. As Earth’s closest celestial neighbor, it serves as the **primary gateway between planetary civilization and space infrastructure**.

Unlike the more distant colonies, Luna benefits from relatively short travel times and well-established transport routes to Earth. This accessibility has allowed it to develop into a **highly urbanized, economically powerful environment** with advanced infrastructure and a large permanent population.

The moon is heavily influenced by **NovaChem**, whose headquarters and industrial complexes dominate large parts of the lunar economy.

---
# Gateway to Space

Luna’s greatest advantage is its position. It functions as the main transfer point for:

- cargo leaving Earth
- passengers traveling off-world
- industrial goods entering space-based supply chains

Compared to launching directly from Earth, transporting materials from Luna is significantly cheaper due to its low gravity and lack of atmosphere. Because of this, many interplanetary missions originate from lunar orbit or surface facilities.

---

# Transport Infrastructure

The Earth–Moon system hosts some of the most advanced transportation infrastructure in human space.

### Mass Drivers

Luna is equipped with large-scale **mass driver systems**—electromagnetic launch tracks that accelerate cargo into orbit without the need for chemical rockets.

These systems are used to:

- launch raw materials
- transport industrial goods
- supply orbital stations

Mass drivers are one of the key reasons Luna became an industrial hub.

---

### Earth–Luna Transfer Systems

Regular transport between Earth and Luna is maintained through:

- **rotating shuttle networks**
- high-efficiency transfer ships
- orbital tug systems

Some experimental and partially operational systems include:

- **rail-assisted launch infrastructure on Earth**
- **orbital tether concepts** (limited deployment)
- synchronized transfer windows enabling near-continuous traffic flow

As a result, Luna is one of the **few off-world destinations accessible at relatively low cost**, even for non-elite travelers.

---

# Economy and Industry

Luna specializes in **low-gravity manufacturing and industrial processing**.

Key advantages include:

- reduced structural stress during production
- easier handling of large components
- efficient launch of finished products into orbit

Major industries include:

- advanced materials manufacturing
- chemical processing (NovaChem)
- spacecraft components
- orbital construction materials

Mining operations extract regolith and subsurface materials, which are processed locally or exported.

---

# Habitat Complexes

Most of Luna’s population lives in massive **subsurface or enclosed habitat complexes**, designed to protect against radiation and temperature extremes.

These habitats range from:

- dense industrial worker settlements
- corporate arcologies
- high-end residential environments

Some of the largest habitat complexes rival small cities in scale.

---

# Tourism

Luna is one of the few places in the solar system where **tourism is a major economic factor**.

Visitors from Earth are drawn by:

- the low-gravity experience
- views of Earth from the lunar surface
- relatively short and affordable travel

Luxury hotels, observation domes, and controlled surface excursions make Luna a popular destination for wealthy travelers—and occasionally for middle-class visitors.

---

# Corporate Power — NovaChem

Luna is the primary power base of **NovaChem**, one of the most influential megacorporations in the solar system.

NovaChem controls:

- major industrial zones
- chemical production facilities
- parts of the transport infrastructure

Its dominance has shaped Luna into a **corporate-driven environment**, where economic efficiency and industrial output often take priority over social considerations.

---
# Society

Lunar society is highly stratified.

- corporate elites and specialists live in high-end arcologies
- industrial workers inhabit dense habitat zones
- transient populations move between Earth and orbital infrastructure

Despite corporate influence, Luna maintains a relatively high standard of living compared to many other industrial regions.

---

# Role in the Solar System

Luna is not just a colony—it is a **critical interface between Earth and space**.

It connects:

- Earth’s population and economy
- orbital infrastructure
- interplanetary trade routes

Without Luna, large-scale human activity in space would be far more expensive and far less efficient.
