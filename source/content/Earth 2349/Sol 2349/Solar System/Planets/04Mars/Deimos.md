# Overview

Deimos is the **quiet counterpart to Phobos**. Orbiting further away from Mars, it serves as a low-traffic support node for research, storage, and long-term infrastructure.

Where Phobos is dense, industrial, and constantly active, Deimos is **sparse, controlled, and slow-paced**.

---

# Infrastructure

Deimos hosts a smaller number of facilities, including:

- long-term storage depots
- scientific research stations
- communication relays
- navigation and observation platforms

Its stable orbit and low activity make it ideal for installations that require minimal disturbance.

---

# Research and Operations

Deimos is often used for:

- orbital observation of Mars
- deep-space communication experiments
- long-duration system monitoring

Some facilities operate with minimal crews, relying heavily on automation.

---

# Strategic Use

Because of its position, Deimos is occasionally used as a **staging point for outbound missions**, particularly those heading deeper into the solar system.

It is also a useful location for:

- fuel storage
- emergency fallback infrastructure
- low-profile operations

---

# Society

The population of Deimos is small and highly specialized.

Residents are typically:

- researchers
- system engineers
- long-duration station crews

Life here is quiet and isolated, with far less traffic than Phobos.

---

# Role in the Mars System

Deimos serves as a **secondary orbital layer** of Martian infrastructure.

If Phobos is the gateway, Deimos is the **back-end system**—handling storage, observation, and long-term operations away from the intensity of primary traffic routes.
