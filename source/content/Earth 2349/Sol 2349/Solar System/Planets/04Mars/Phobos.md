# Overview

Phobos is the **primary orbital hub of Mars** and one of the most strategically important locations in the inner solar system. Orbiting extremely close to the planet, it serves as the main staging point for traffic between the Martian surface and interplanetary space.

Nearly all cargo and passenger transport to and from Mars passes through Phobos at some stage. Its proximity allows for efficient transfer operations, making it the natural location for large-scale **docking infrastructure and orbital industry**.

---

# Infrastructure

Phobos is heavily industrialized and almost entirely artificial in its functional layout.

Key infrastructure includes:

- massive docking rings
- cargo transfer stations
- orbital shipyards
- fuel depots

Much of the asteroid’s interior has been hollowed out and reinforced, creating pressurized environments for workers and long-term operations.

---

# Shipyards

Phobos hosts some of the most important **ship construction and maintenance facilities** in the Martian system.

Advantages include:

- extremely low gravity
- immediate access to Mars orbit
- efficient launch and departure trajectories

Many interplanetary vessels are assembled or refitted here before departure.

---

# Role in the Mars System

Phobos acts as the **interface between Mars and space**.

It connects:

- surface settlements
- orbital infrastructure
- interplanetary trade routes

For many travelers, Phobos is the first and last stop when entering or leaving Mars.

---

# Society

Life on Phobos is highly industrial and transient.

The population consists largely of:

- engineers
- dock workers
- ship crews
- logistics personnel

Most residents do not stay permanently. Instead, Phobos functions as a **high-traffic working environment**, constantly cycling people and cargo.
