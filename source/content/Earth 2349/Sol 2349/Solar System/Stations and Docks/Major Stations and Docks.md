Space stations and docking facilities are the **connective tissue of human civilization beyond planetary surfaces**. They serve as hubs for transportation, trade, maintenance, and daily life, linking distant worlds through a vast network of orbital infrastructure. From small refueling platforms to massive multi-ring habitats, stations provide essential services such as life support, cargo handling, ship repair, and crew rotation. In a solar system where travel is slow and infrastructure is everything, stations and docks are not just transit points—they are lifelines.

Broadly speaking, orbital infrastructure in SOL 2349 falls into two major categories. **Orbital stations** are built directly around planets, moons, or industrial worlds, where they support logistics, trade, energy transfer, manufacturing, and administration. **Lagrange stations**, by contrast, occupy positions of gravitational stability between large bodies and often serve as transfer hubs, staging areas, observation sites, or long-range communication nodes. Together, these two systems form the practical framework that keeps interplanetary civilization functioning.

# Orbital Stations

Orbital stations are built where traffic, industry, and energy demand are highest. Some exist primarily to handle cargo and docking operations, while others function as corporate headquarters, industrial command centers, or large-scale habitats housing permanent populations. In many regions of the solar system, orbital stations are more important than the planetary surface below them. They allow goods to move between gravity wells, support ship construction and maintenance, and provide the infrastructure necessary for trade, communication, and administration. In heavily industrialized systems, they often serve as the true centers of power.

## Helios Prime

|Parameter|Value|
|---|---|
|Type|Corporate orbital headquarters|
|Location|Earth Orbit|
|Primary Role|Resource coordination, energy logistics|
|Controlled By|[[Helios]]|
**Helios Prime** is the main orbital headquarters of **Helios**, one of the most powerful resource and energy corporations in the inner solar system. Positioned in Earth orbit, it functions as both an executive command center and a major logistics hub linking Earth, Luna, Mercury, and the industrial corridors of the inner system.

The station itself is not primarily a habitat, though it contains substantial residential and administrative sectors. Its true importance lies in coordination. Helios Prime manages transport contracts, resource flows, energy distribution agreements, and the complex scheduling architecture that connects mining operations, transmission arrays, fuel networks, and refining platforms across multiple worlds. In practice, much of Helios’ power does not come from owning raw resources alone, but from controlling how and when those resources move.

## Nyx Relay Nexus

|Parameter|Value|
|---|---|
|Type|Data and network coordination hub|
|Location|Mercury Solar Orbit|
|Primary Role|Datacenters, routing, secure processing|
|Controlled By|[[Nyx]]|
The **Nyx Relay Nexus** is one of the most important information infrastructure sites in the inner solar system. Located in **Mercury solar orbit**, it draws directly on the enormous energy surplus generated by Mercurian solar arrays and orbital transmission systems. This makes it an ideal location for large-scale datacenters, encrypted routing nodes, industrial control networks, and high-density secure processing architecture.

Unlike more visibly industrial stations, Nyx Relay Nexus is quiet, controlled, and heavily hardened. Much of its significance is invisible from the outside. It handles traffic management, industrial synchronization, secure financial data exchange, and network control functions that support everything from transmission relays to orbital logistics. In a civilization where energy and information are inseparable, the Nyx Relay Nexus is one of the places where that relationship becomes concrete.

## Aurora Finance Hub

| Parameter     | Value                                       |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Type          | Financial and administrative orbital hub    |
| Location      | Venus Orbit                                 |
| Primary Role  | Capital flows, contracts, corporate finance |
| Controlled By | [[Solaris]]-linked financial institutions   |

The **Aurora Finance Hub** is the primary orbital financial center above Venus and one of the most influential transaction nodes in the entire solar system. Suspended above the industrial giant below, it acts as a clearing center for contracts, investment flows, insurance structures, and intercorporate financing tied to Venusian manufacturing, energy-intensive production, and long-haul trade.

Where Helios Prime coordinates resources and Nyx manages information, Aurora Finance Hub specializes in **abstraction and control through capital**. It is a place where industrial output becomes debt instruments, where shipping schedules become derivatives, and where future production is bought, sold, and leveraged long before it physically exists. The station itself is clean, highly regulated, and deliberately insulated from the harsher realities of Venusian industrial life. It embodies the upper layer of the system: the level where numbers decide what factories below will build.

## Ceres Exchange Ring

|Parameter|Value|
|---|---|
|Type|Diplomatic and trade orbital hub|
|Location|Ceres Orbit|
|Primary Role|Trade clearing, embassies, neutral docking|
|Controlled By|Ceres Administrative Authority|

The **Ceres Exchange Ring** is the principal orbital gateway of Ceres and one of the most politically sensitive stations in the Asteroid Belt. While the surface and internal habitats of Ceres serve as the neutral diplomatic center of the Belt, the Exchange Ring is where that neutrality becomes operational. Cargo is inspected, delegations arrive, commercial agreements are brokered, and disputes are often settled before they ever reach the surface below.

Unlike the harsher industrial docks of the Belt, the Exchange Ring is deliberately structured to appear orderly, balanced, and neutral. Its docking sectors are divided between commercial, diplomatic, and high-security traffic, while internal zones host trade offices, arbitration chambers, secure meeting suites, and long-term mission support facilities. For many travelers, the station is the real first contact with Ceres—not a frontier port, but a carefully managed interface between rival powers.

## Vesta Control Station

|Parameter|Value|
|---|---|
|Type|Security and convoy coordination hub|
|Location|Vesta Orbit|
|Primary Role|Route monitoring, escort staging, anti-piracy operations|
|Controlled By|Vesta Route Authority|

The **Vesta Control Station** is one of the most important security nodes in the Asteroid Belt. Positioned above one of the Belt’s most structured and tightly governed bodies, it functions as a central coordination point for convoy routing, escort deployment, inspection protocols, and high-value cargo monitoring.

Its architecture reflects its purpose: practical, hardened, and heavily surveilled. Traffic entering the Vesta sphere of influence is logged and assessed, and vessels moving along major Belt corridors often pass through its control grid. The station is not a diplomatic space and not a commercial showcase. It exists to impose order on a region where order is often difficult to maintain. For traders it represents protection. For pirates and smugglers, it represents complication.

## Ares Transfer Terminal

|Parameter|Value|
|---|---|
|Type|Planetary transfer and export station|
|Location|Mars Orbit|
|Primary Role|Passenger transit, agroexport, research logistics|
|Controlled By|Martian planetary authorities and licensed operators|

The **Ares Transfer Terminal** is the primary orbital gateway of Mars and one of the busiest civilian stations in the inner solar system. Nearly all passenger movement, agricultural export, scientific cargo, and orbital transfer traffic between Mars and the wider system passes through this station in some form. If Phobos is the industrial interface of Mars, Ares is its public-facing transit hub.

The station’s internal structure reflects the priorities of the Martian economy. Cargo sections are optimized for biosensitive shipping, controlled environments, and high-throughput food logistics, while passenger sectors are cleaner, more regulated, and strongly integrated with Martian customs and biosecurity systems. Scientific transport is also prominent, with dedicated docking capacity for research missions, xenology programs, and biotech logistics. Ares feels distinctly Martian: efficient, technical, and highly managed.

## Ganymede Assembly Station

|Parameter|Value|
|---|---|
|Type|Diplomatic and administrative orbital hub|
|Location|Ganymede Orbit|
|Primary Role|Jovian League governance, diplomacy, intermoon coordination|
|Controlled By|Jovian League|
The **Ganymede Assembly Station** is the principal orbital institution of the **Jovian League** and the seat of its **Diplomatic Assembly**. Located above the most populous moon of the Jovian system, it functions as the political and administrative heart of the League, hosting delegates, committees, technical councils, and coordination bodies representing the major Jovian and Saturnian member worlds.

Unlike the corporate headquarters of the inner system, the station is designed to project legitimacy through transparency and shared governance. Its halls contain chambers for debate, mediation suites, administrative offices, and secure intersystem communications infrastructure linking the League’s member states. Though not a large industrial dock, it remains an important transport node, especially for diplomatic traffic, League security vessels, scientific delegations, and long-range coordination missions. In practical terms, the Assembly Station is where the outer solar system speaks with one voice—when it can.
