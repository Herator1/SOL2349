Space stations and docking facilities are the **connective tissue of human civilization beyond planetary surfaces**. They serve as hubs for transportation, trade, maintenance, and daily life, linking distant worlds through a vast network of orbital infrastructure. From small refueling platforms to massive multi-ring habitats, stations provide essential services such as life support, cargo handling, ship repair, and crew rotation. In a solar system where travel is slow and infrastructure is everything, stations and docks are not just transit points—they are lifelines.
