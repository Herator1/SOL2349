## ⚙️ Technology in SOL 2349 — Survival by Design

**You don’t need to understand any of this to run a great game — but if you’re looking for inspiration for creative, catastrophic failures, this is exactly where things can start to go wrong.**

In SOL 2349, technology is not about convenience — it is about survival. Energy is not just a resource; it is the thin line between a functioning system and total loss. Fusion powers the Solar System. From the smallest deep-space tug to the largest orbital habitat, controlled fusion is the beating heart of civilization. It sustains life support, drives industry, and enables ships to cross the vast distances between worlds. Fusion torch drives turn that energy into motion, converting fuel into thrust with unmatched power — and at equally unmatched cost.

Because nothing about this is easy.

Fusion systems are complex, temperamental, and fundamentally unforgiving. They operate at the edge of what materials, control systems, and human oversight can handle. Containment must remain stable, heat must be continuously managed, and every subsystem must function within tight tolerances. A minor calibration error, a delayed maintenance cycle, or a single compromised component can escalate within seconds — from a manageable anomaly to a cascading failure. In the vacuum of space, such failures are rarely survivable.

Running a fusion-powered vessel is therefore not simply a matter of advanced technology — it is a constant act of control, discipline, and risk management. Every burn is calculated. Every system is monitored. Every decision carries consequences in heat, stress, and wear. Crews do not rely on their machines because they are perfect, but because they have no alternative. This is the foundation of life in space.

Systems that make civilization possible, while always remaining one failure away from catastrophe.
