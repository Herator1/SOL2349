Not every civilization leaves its mark through monuments. In **SOL 2349**, many of the greatest achievements of humanity are not beautiful, symbolic, or even widely loved. They are systems built because the Solar System is too large, too hostile, and too materially demanding to function without infrastructure on an almost incomprehensible scale.

**Megaprojects** are the largest organized works of human civilization: energy networks, orbital construction systems, transfer infrastructures, industrial corridors, planetary engineering programs, and other undertakings so vast that they reshape how entire regions of human space operate. Some are fixed structures. Some are distributed networks. Some are not “structures” in the conventional sense at all, but coordinated systems of ships, stations, reactors, depots, relays, habitats, and industrial sites that together function as a single civilizational machine.

What unites them is not form, but scale.

A megaproject in **SOL 2349** is not simply a large building. It is a concentration of capital, labor, logistics, engineering, and political will so great that it alters the strategic reality of the Solar System around it. These projects take decades to plan, decades to expand, and often never become truly finished. They consume resources on a planetary scale, bind corporations and governments into uneasy cooperation, and create whole secondary economies in their shadow.

They are also among the clearest expressions of what humanity has become in 2349. A civilization still constrained by physics, distance, and cost—but capable of organizing effort at a scale that would have seemed almost impossible in 2026.

Some megaprojects keep worlds alive.  
Some move industry across millions of kilometers.  
Some define entire regions of space.

Together, they form the largest machinery humanity has ever built.
